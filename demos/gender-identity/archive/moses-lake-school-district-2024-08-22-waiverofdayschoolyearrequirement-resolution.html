2024-25 Resolution
Waiver of 180-Day School Year Requirement

Whereas, the State Legislature requires that the school year consist of 180 school days, Moses
Lake School District #161 is requesting waivers for Kindergarten, seventh, eighth, tenth,
eleventh, and twelfth grade students of the minimum 180-day school year for the school year
2024-25; and

Whereas, the Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction is authorized to approve a waiver of
this requirement, conditional upon the District providing adequate evidence that it is improving
student learning; and

Whereas, the Moses Lake School District is committed to the social and emotional wellbeing of
students and recognizes its importance in relation to positive academic outcomes; and

Whereas, the Moses Lake School District values the direct correlation between smooth
transitions for students as they enter new school settings with positive, long-term educational
outcomes; and

Whereas, the Moses Lake School District will continue to meet the basic minimum instructional
hours requirements under RCW 28A.140.220 under the waiver plans;

Now, therefore, be it resolved that the Board of Directors of the Moses Lake School
District does hereby petition the Office of the Superintendent of Public Instruction for waivers
of the 180-day school year requirement so that one (1) full school day can be devoted to
transition and/or slow start programs for incoming kindergarten, sixth, ninth, tenth, eleventh
and twelfth grade students.

ADOPTED this 22nd day of August 2024 at a special meeting of the Board of Directors, Moses
Lake School District.

Board of Directors, Moses Lake School District #161
Grant County, State of Washington
