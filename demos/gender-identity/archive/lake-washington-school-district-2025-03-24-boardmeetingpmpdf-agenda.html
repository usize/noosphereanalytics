Public Content

Land acknowledgement and commitment to native students and families:

The board of directors of Lake Washington School District acknowledges that our district is on the
Indigenous Land of the Coast Salish Peoples, who have reserved treaty rights on this land, including the
Duwamish (dxdwab) and the Snoqualmie Indian Tribe (sdukalbix). We thank these caretakers of this land
who have lived here since time immemorial. We commit to continue collaborating with our native
community, to teach the indigenous history of this land and these local tribes to all children of the district.
We also commit to provide the best possible education for all children of native descent who attend our
schools.

Subject : 6. International Alliance Of Stage Employees Performing
Arts Center Managers Collective Bargaining Agreement,
2024-2027

Meeting : Mar 24, 2025 - Board Meeting - 7:00 p.m.

Type : Action (Consent)

Recommended Action : Approve the IATSE Performing Arts Center Manager
2024 2027 Collective Bargaining agreement, as
presented.

Public Content

SITUATION

The current International Alliance of Stage Employees (IATSE) Theater Manager Collective Bargaining Agreement ran from September 2021
through August 2024. Bargaining for a successor agreement has been occurring since June 2024. The District and IATSE recently reached
a tentative agreement on a successor Collective Bargaining Agreement.

Within this agreement, IATSE represents all Performing Arts Center Managers within the Lake Washington School District.

The settlement is within the parameter discussed with the Board. The following issues were addressed in this bargain:

Duration: September 1, 2024 August 31, 2027 (three-year agreement).

Wages:

2024-25: Wages will be increased by the state determined inflationary adjustment for classified school district employees, which is three and
seven tenths percent (3.7%).

2025-2026: Wages will be increased by one half of a percent (0.5%) or the state determined inflationary adjustment for classified school
district employees, whichever is higher.

2026-2027: Wages will be increased by one half of a percent (0.5%) or the state determined inflationary adjustment for classified school
district employees, whichever is higher.

Other Items:


Job Title: Job title was changed from Theater Manager to Performing Arts Center (PAC) Manager, to more accurately reflect the roles duties
and responsibilities.

Article 6 Work Duties: This new article describes the roles duties and responsibilities.

Professional Development: Increased the annual funds from $350.00 to $500.00. This will benefit the District and the professional
development of the bargaining unit.

Holidays: added Juneteenth as a paid holiday.

Legal Compliance: Added Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) and Washington State Paid Family Medical Leave (PFML) language in
accordance with the law and to provide consistency across District employee groups.

Attached is a copy of all language/provisions for which the parties have reached a tentative agreement.

The tentative agreements identified above were accepted and ratified unanimously by the members of the IATSE Performing Arts Center
Managers on Monday, March 3, 2024.

RECOMMENDATION

The Board of Directors approves the IATSE Performing Arts Center Manager 2024 2027 Collective Bargaining agreement.

shortage categories on a conditional certification. If approved by the appropriate board of directors, OSPI will issue staff identified by the

district a conditional certificate, which then deems the individual appropriately certified by the state. The conditional certificate may be issued
to individuals in all endorsement areas.
