Good afternoon! I hope you’re doing well, here are my reports for the night:)
SHS:
We are happy to be welcoming students back to in-person learning this week!
Friday, March 5 is a no school day for students.
Sports this week –
Wednesday 3/3 is Volleyball and Soccer both at Chimacum
Friday 3/5, Football at home, 6pm
OPA:
At OPA we welcomed our HS students today. Staff are so excited to have our students on campus. Tuesday will be the first time in nearly a year OPA will have our K- 12 students on campus at the same time. Go Otters!
Students started at SOS today as well. Mr. Thorson has been collaborating with students, staff, and families in an effort to help our SOS kids reach their graduation and after HS goals. Thank you Mr. Thorson, your passion and connections with students is outstanding. We appreciate you.
SMS:
With students back in our Hybrid model, they are seeing their teachers twice a week. Both the teachers and student have shown are beginning to find their feet and are easing into a routine. SMS has a number of staff and classes that are doing some new things and will continue to do so as they adapt to meet the needs of our students.
We have several staff the have already begun to record and post selections from their In-Person Hybrid sessions each do to benefit all. We have one teacher that has begun to test the waters with Live Streaming of each Hybrid session and plans to do the same on Wednesday’s. Their premise is that once they learn how to it well, it will feel a lot more like regular teaching know all their students have access to them every day.
Among our classes, you might remember the Leaderships first two efforts at Some Good News SMS. The class now together more often in Hybrid are taking their learning to the next level by shifting to a model the includes and may quickly become exclusively a video production.
With the district engaged in an instructional paradigm shift toward Universal Design for Learning, our sixth grade science classes are shifting into high gear with what happens when Universal Design for Learning meets Next Generation Science Standards. OpenSciEd lessons and materials target our state standards, capitalize on and promote students' natural curiosity, and build on innate problem-solving skills. If you visit the campus or look through Canvas online, you will find students in action with engaging science that leaves many saying that science is one of their favorite classes.
