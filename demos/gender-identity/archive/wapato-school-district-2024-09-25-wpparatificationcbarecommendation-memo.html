RE: 2024 Negotiations Summary (for Board Ratification), WPPA

The District has concluded negotiations with WPPA and reached a tentative agreement to finalize a
new 2024-2027 Collective Bargaining Agreement. The members of WPPA have voted to ratify this
tentative agreement, so it is now ready for Board review and action.

This memorandum is to review the financial components of the tentative agreement with WPPA and
recommend to the Board that they approve the new Collective Bargaining Agreement during the
regular Board Meeting on September 25th. This item will be placed on the Consent Agenda.

In total, four bargaining sessions took place: three between late April and mid June and one on
August 27th. The District Team consisted of Sylvia Bazan, Kerry McGraw, and Leti Salazar. Lorraine
Wilson from Porter Foster Rorick, our external legal counsel, was the District's lead negotiator. I
provided support to Lorraine and District oversight to our bargaining team. .

The WPPA Team consisted of Lollie Thierrault, Aurora Rodriguez, Gloria Romero, and Cynthia Ibarra.
They were represented by WEA-Midstate Director La Vasha Murdoch, who served as their lead
negotiator.

The parties have agreed to a new three year contract to run between September 1, 2024 and August
31, 2027 to cover the 2024-25, 2025-26, and 2026-27 school years. Beyond financial considerations,
most of the agreements between the parties were minor, including clarifying edits to existing
language, grammatical fixes, formatting errors, and other updates.

The parties further agreed (separate from the fiscal considerations below) to form a joint working
committee during the 2024-2025 school year to review and update the evaluation process for
members of the bargaining unit. The current process is one-size-fits-all regardless of job title and is
out of date. We anticipate new evaluative processes in place for 2025-2026.

A. Wages

Throughout bargaining, the parties proposed a variety of wage models for two, three, and four year
agreements. Generally, the District prefers the stability that comes with longer-termed Agreements,
while Associations typically favor shorter agreements to allow for more bargaining opportunities,
unless fiscal guarantees meet their predetermined interests.


Article VI, Section 7A of the Collective Bargaining Agreement already guarantees that any
“inflationary raise”, such as IPD (the Implicit Price Deflator set by the State), is automatically passed
through to employees. This means that prior to any bargaining, employees were guaranteed an IPD
raise of 3.7% for 2024-2025. While IPD for 2025-2026 and 2026-2027 have not been formally defined
at the state level, we anticipate that they will be set at approximately 1.9% and 2.2% for those years.
As a result, before bargaining even began, employees are guaranteed approximately a 7.8% increase
over three years’. This set the floor for any negotiated wage package.

After several rounds of bargaining, the parties settled on a three year salary schedule with minimal
increases (less than 1.0%) beyond IPD in each year of the agreement. This was a bargaining
compromise - the District wanted a four year agreement, the Association proposed only two years.
The specific financial terms are as follows:

2024-2025: 4.5% (3.7% of this is IPD and “new money” is 0.8%)
2025-2026: IPD + 0.75% (currently 1.9% IPD so 2.65% total increase)
2026-2027: 3% or IPD, whichever is greater

(currently projected to be 2.2% IPD + 0.8% “new money”)

By August, 2027, we will encumber an estimated $151,373 of new payroll obligations. However,
$116,127 (77%) of that money is already guaranteed. The District's new, ongoing financial commitment
beyond IPD at the end of this Agreement in August, 2027 is approximately $35,247.

Hourly wages will increase between $2.74 and $3.61 per hour at the end of the three year agreement,
depending on the specific job title. Again, with IPD already guaranteed, the “new money” in this
agreement accounts for a net increase of between $0.62 and $0.81 per hour of that total. While not a
zero increase, we continue to prioritize minimizing wage growth over time due to the salary levels in
this bargaining unit.

B. VEBA

VEBA® luntar mployees’ Beneficiary Association) is a funded health reimbursement
arrangement program for school districts in the State of Washington. It is managed by a trust
authorized under Section 501(c)(9) of the federal Internal Revenue Code. VEBA allows employees to
access a tax advantaged health reimbursement plan where the individual employee, the employer, or
both contribute to the VEBA Plan. There is a tax benefit for employees as money invested into VEBA
is not taxed, meaning that the employee gets full dollar-for-dollar benefit when funds are accessed.

Classified employees have had the ability to contribute their own earnings into VEBA for several
years if their specific bargaining unit decides as a unit to allow VEBA contributions. This differs from
teachers and administrators, who can both contribute their own earnings as well as receive a
contribution from the District as part of their financial compensation package.

We have agreed with WPPA to begin a monthly district contribution to VEBA. The specific language
is that the District will contribute $15/month per full-time employee defined as working 1440 hours
in a contract year and prorated otherwise. This itemizes to an annual cost to the District of $6,300
based on thirty-five (35) full-time employees.

This is a big step for the District, which has declined employer contributions to classified units over
the past several bargaining cycles. We anticipate similar proposals and movement with our other
classified units in their next open bargaining (e.g., WAEOP in 2025 and PSE in 2026).

We attempted to bargain moving two small stipends already codified in the contract into VEBA,
specifically a $2,000 professional development stipend in Article III, Section 9(B) and a $5,000 health
stipend in Article VI, Section 6(H) but were ultimately unsuccessful even after pointing out that
VEBA is a tax benefit for employees they don't otherwise get with the current stipend structure.

III. Recommendation

The District's Bargaining Team recommends that the Board approve the WPPA 2024-2027 Collective
Bargaining Agreement during their meeting on September 25th. Thank you for your continued
support of the negotiations process.
