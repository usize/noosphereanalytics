SERVICE DIRECTIVE

This SERVICE DIRECTIVE (“SD”) NO. 001, effective as of May 1, 2024, supplements and modifies the
terms of the Professional Services Agreement between OAC Services, Inc., a Washington Corporation
(“OAC”) and Bremerton School District (“Client”) dated May 1, 2024 (the “Agreement’). This SD modifies,
supplements, and extends the Agreement, as follows:

Jeff Jurgensen, Senior Vice Principal

Phil lverson, Senior Project Manager
Cynthia Balzarini, Project Controls Manager
Elizabeth Rosenbeck, Senior Project Coordinator

Il. SCOPE: OAC’s scope of services for this Project (the “Services”) is set forth in Exhibit A dated
5/10/2024.

Ill. COMPENSATION: The Services performed under this SD will be compensated at the hourly rates set
forth in Exhibit A up to a Maximum Allowable Not to Exceed ("NTE") price of $3,116,522.00. Reimbursable
expenses will be invoiced consistent with the Agreement, and such expenses will be included in the NTE
price stated above.

IV. SCHEDULE: The schedule for completion of the Services is estimated to be as set forth in Exhibit A.
V. ADDITIONAL TERMS:

1. The compensation under this SD includes all costs of labor, materials, equipment, overhead,
profits, and all other costs or expenses associated with the subject matter of this SD.

2. All terms, conditions, and obligations of the Agreement remain in full force and effect except as
expressly modified by a fully executed SD. This SD, together with the Agreement, constitute the entire
understanding of the parties with respect to this subject matter and supersede all prior or contemporaneous
statements, representations, or promises, whether written or oral.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have caused their authorized agents to execute this SD to
the Agreement effective on the date set forth above.

OAC SCOPE OF SERVICES AND FEE
(revised May 10, 2024)

Scope of Services: Based upon the information currently available about the Bremerton School
District (BSD) 2024 Bond Program, OAC proposes to provide comprehensive project management
services in support of the 2024 Bond program, including:

GENERAL DUTIES
e CONSULTANT SELECTION. Assist BSD in the selection of all necessary special consultants

(such as commissioning, special inspection) required for each project and preparation of
Professional Services Agreements, including recommended language pertaining to scope,
budget, and schedule adherence, and Project Manager/BSD interface requirements. OAC will
assist BSD with fee, scope, and terms for the contract with District-provided legal consultants.

e QOAC will review the scope and appropriateness of cost for District consultants and make
recommendations for payment to the District consultants.

e CONTRACT DEVELOPMENT. Coordinate efforts between the District and their attorneys in the
development of all Design-Builder and/or other District consultant contracts.

e PROJECT BUDGET. Review the budget for the project in comparison to site constraints,
schedule, scope, and budget data in existence, and develop a project budget based on this data
and the Project Manager's historical cost data. OAC will prepare tailored reports for the BSD
detailing overall budget adequacy.

e COST MANAGEMENT PROCEDURES. Provide BSD with updated cost summary reports for the
project throughout the construction progress with information gathered from all contracted
parties. Provide BSD with cost summaries of sources of budget status, changes, issues, and any
recommended actions.

e CASH FLOW PROJECTION REPORTS. Incorporate and compare the District's cash flow
projects projections and that of the Contractor's schedule of values. OAC will prepare and
distribute cash flow projection reports. These will be adjusted to reflect cash flow based on actual
bid figures in comparison to the budget.

e INFORMATION FLOW AND REPORTS. The Project Manager will become knowledgeable of
BSD’s reporting needs and, in conjunction with BSD, determine the type of information
necessary, the reporting format, the frequency of various reports, and the distribution
requirements for information related to the projects.

e MASTER SCHEDULE. The Project Manager will prepare, maintain, and update the District's
Master Schedule by incorporating all in-progress adjustments and will distribute the updated
Master Schedule to the BSD, design-build teams, and/or other contractors and designers and
other appropriate parties.

e DOCUMENT CONTROLS. The Project Manager will develop a project specific document
controls plan with the District, Design-Builder and/or Architect and General Contractor so that a
complete project record is compiled, audited, and provided to the District at project closeout.

PROCUREMENT/PREDESIGN PHASE

e PROGRAM DEVELOPMENT & COORDINATION. OAC will collaborate with Bremerton School
District to develop a schedule, budget, and strategy at the outset of each major project.

e CONSULTANT SELECTION. Assist Bremerton School District (BSD) in the selection of all
necessary special consultants (such as wetlands, commissioning, constructability review, survey,
and geo-technical) required for each project and preparation of Professional Services
Agreements, including recommended language pertaining to scope, budget, and schedule
adherence, and Project Manager/BSD interface requirements. OAC will assist BSD with fee,
scope, and terms for the contract with the Design-Builder.

e CONTRACT DEVELOPMENT. Coordinate efforts between the District and their attorneys in the
development of the Design-Builder contract.

e PROJECT BUDGET. Review the general budget for each project in comparison to site
constraints, schedule, scope, and budget data in existence, and develop a project budget based
on this data and the Project Manager's historical cost data. The Project Manager will prepare a
report for WSD detailing overall budget adequacy.

e REQUEST FOR QUALIFICATIONS/REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS. Develop the Request for
Qualifications (RFQ) and Request for Proposals (RFP), and coordinate the necessary supporting
documentation to solicit the required Progressive Design Build (PDB) project teams and/or
Architect/Engineering (A/E) team as required and authorized by the Project Review Committee
(PRC).

e ADVERTISEMENTS. Verify the notices and advertisements and notices are correct and accurate
relative to intent to solicit contractor interest in the project.

e PROJECT REVIEW CONFERENCE(S). In conjunction with the BSD, Project Manager will
coordinate and facilitate necessary project review conference(s). These conferences will be a
forum for the BSD and the Project Manager to present the project to prospective PDB teams
and/or A/E team to support submission of their Statements of Qualifications (SOQs).

e COORDINATION AND INQUIRIES. Monitor communications related to solicitation inquiries and
seek resolution from the appropriate party and assist the BSD with formulation of
responses/information to responsive teams in a timely manner. Coordinate with the BSD to issue
addenda when necessary.

e ADDENDA REVIEW. Provide a review of each addendum during the procurement phase for time,
cost, or constructability impact, and make appropriate comments or recommendations.

e STATEMENT OF QUALIFICATIONS/PROPOSAL EVALUATION. Assist the BSD in the review
of Statements of Qualifications (SOQs) and proposals from prospective PDB Teams and/or A/E
teams, including evaluation of SOQs and proposals for completeness, responsiveness, and price,
including alternate prices and unit prices.

DESIGN PHASE

e DESIGN REVIEW. The Project Manager will coordinate with the A/E team and the BSD to
facilitate the design review process and ensure that the design meets the criteria established by
the BSD.

e EXPEDITE AGENCY REVIEWING AND APPROVALS. Collaborate with the designers of record
as they will lead the effort with agency review.

BIDDING PHASE (AS NECESSARY) If Design Bid Build these tasks will take place:

e BID ADVERTISEMENTS. Verify the notices and advertisements and notices are correct &
accurate relative to intent to solicit contractor interest in the project.

e PRE-BID CONFERENCE(S). In conjunction with the Architect and the BSD, Project Manager will
attend pre-bid conference(s). These conferences will be a forum for the BSD, the Project
Manager, and Architect to present the project.

e COORDINATION AND INQUIRIES. Monitor communications related to bidder inquiries, seek
resolution from the appropriate party, and assist the Architect with formulation of
responses/information to bidders in a timely manner. Coordinate with the Architect to issue
addenda when necessary.

e ADDENDA REVIEW. Provide a review of each addendum during the bid phase for time, cost, or
constructability impact, and make appropriate comments or recommendations.

e BID EVALUATION. Assist the BSD and the Architect in the bid process, including bid opening (as
required); evaluation of bids for completeness, responsiveness, and price, including alternate
prices and unit prices.

e PRE-CONSTRUCTION CONFERENCE(S). In conjunction with the BSD, the contractor, and the
Architect, attend a pre-construction orientation conference(s) for the benefit of the successful
contractor(s) and will serve to orient the contractor(s) to the various reporting procedures and
site rules prior to the commencement of actual construction.

CONSTRUCTION PHASE

e CONTRACT ADMMISTRATION. The Project Manager will administer the construction contract
on behalf of the BSD and with the BSD’s concurrence as provided in the General Conditions of
the contract for construction.

e SUBMITTAL PROCEDURES. The Project Manager will coordinate with the A/E to establish and
implement procedures for submittals, requests for information, payment requests, and other
procedures; and maintain logs, files, and other necessary documentation which will be followed
by all parties to the contract. As the BSD's representative at the job site, Project Manager will be
the party through which change orders, payment requests, review of selected Owner-required
submittals, and information will be processed from Contractor to BSD and/or Architect, and from
the Architect and/or BSD to the Contractor

e JOB SITE MEETINGS. The Project Manager will participate in all job-site progress meetings with
the Contractors, Architect, and BSD.

e COORDINATION OF TECHNICAL INSPECTION AND TESTING. Project Manager will
coordinate the technical inspection and testing provided by the Architect or other third parties. All
technical inspection reports will be in a format approved by the Project Manager and the requiring
authorities.

e CONSTRUCTION OBSERVATION. Project Manager will observe the progress and quality of the
Work in comparison to contract documents, educational specifications, and schedule. The
Project Manager's observation duties shall include reasonable diligence to discover work that is
not in compliance with the contract documents and take photos to document the findings and
progress of work.

e NON-CONFORMING WORK. The Project Manager will make recommendations to the BSD and
the Architect in instances where Project Manager observes work that, in his/her opinion, is
defective or not in conformance with the Contract Documents.

e CONSTRUCTION SCHEDULE. The Project Manager will receive and review the _ initial
construction schedule from the Contractor and, after review, distribute with comments to the BSD,
Architect and other appropriate parties.

e MONITOR THE CONSTRUCTION SCHEDULE. The Project Manager will use the Contractor's
critical path network as data for the detailed construction schedule report. This information will
be the basis for all future reports reflecting actual project progress during the construction phase.

e SCHEDULE OF VALUES. Project Manager will review and reconcile the contractor's Schedule
of Values for each of the activities included in the construction schedule and will use this
information as initial data. Project Manager will initialize the progress payment schedule for the
construction phase. This report will then be used as the basis for cash flow projections and future
progress payments during the construction phase and closeout phases.

e CONSTRUCTION PROGRESS REVIEW. The Project Manager will review the progress of
construction with the Contractor, observe work in place and safely stored materials monthly, and
evaluate the percentage complete of each construction activity as indicated in the construction
schedule.

e MONTHLY CONSTRUCTION SCHEDULE UPDATES. The Project Manager will prepare and
distribute a report on the monthly construction schedule updates. The report will reflect actual
progress as compared to schedule progress, noting variances (if any) as negative float. This
report will also be the basis for determining implementation of certain BSD prerogatives
concerning the progress of the Project, when required.

e MONTHLY CONTRACTOR PAYMENT. The Project Manager will review and make
recommendations to the BSD pertaining to monthly payment to each Contractor. However, if it
should later be found that a Contractor has failed to comply with the provisions of its contract with
the BSD in any way or detail, such failures and subsequent compliance will be the sole
responsibility of the Contractor, which the Project Manager will help facilitate.

e MONTHLY CONSTRUCTION PAYMENT REPORTS. The Project Manager will prepare and
distribute the monthly construction payment reports that will be an integral function of the monthly
schedule report. This report will reflect the total construction contract price, Contractor's payment
to date, current payment requested, retainage, and actual amounts owed for the current period.

e SCHEDULE REVISION. The Project Manager will monitor all revisions to the construction
schedule and report to the BSD on all findings.

e EVALUATE PROPOSAL COST. The Project Manager will evaluate the Contractor's proposal
cost and will make a formal recommendation to the BSD regarding acceptance of the proposal
for a Change Order.

e NEGOTIATION OF CHANGE ORDER COSTS AND TIME EXTENSIONS. The Project Manager
will assist the BSD to negotiate change order costs and time extensions when appropriate. The
Project Manager will advise the BSD of acceptability of price and time extension prior to the
execution of any change order.

e CONTRACTOR CLAIMS. During the construction phase, the Project Manager will receive all
notices of claims by Contractors against the BSD for additional cost or time due to any alleged
cause. The Project Manager will promptly inform the BSD of the claim and perform an evaluation

e CLAIMS ANALYSIS. The Project Manager will analyze the claims for extension of time and cost,

impact on the schedule. The report will also provide a narrative including a recommendation for

e CONTRACTOR CLAIMS NEGOTIATIONS. The Project Manager will negotiate claims with the

CLOSEOUT PHASE

e INSTRUCTION MANUALS/WARRANTIES. The Project Manager will be the recipient of all
written material such as operations and maintenance manuals, warranties, and guarantees for
all equipment installed in the Project. The architect will review these documents for completeness
and forward them to the BSD for future reference.

e AS-BUILT DOCUMENTS. The Project Manager will perform coordination and expediting
functions in connection with the Contractor's obligation to provide "as-built" documents.

e TRAINING SESSIONS. Project Manager will assist to coordinate and schedule training sessions
for the BSD’s maintenance and operational personnel and will assure that the Contractor's
obligation in providing this training is fulfilled.

FF&E PHASE

e FURNITURE/FIXTURES/EQUIPMENT. The Project Manager will assist the owner in the
procurement and delivery of FF&E. The BSD will be the lead agency in purchasing and arranging
payment of all FF&E.

e MOVE IN. We will coordinate the move in and compile a punch list with the owner and the
contractors etc.

WARRANTY PHASE

e WARRANTY MANAGEMENT. The Project Manager will assist the owner in the management
and tracking of all warranty claims to ensure items are corrected in accordance with contract
documents, and any necessary extended warranties as allowable by contract documents are put
in place. Assumed 3-4 months support per project.

e ENHANCED COMMISSIONING. Not included.
