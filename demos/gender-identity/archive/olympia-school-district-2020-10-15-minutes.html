  MEETING SUMMARY
  1. OPENING
1.1 Welcome and Introduction
President Seidel welcomed all in attendance at 6:04 p.m.
  PURPOSE OF WORK SESSION: OSD School Board to be presented information from district staff on Racial Equity Planning updates for all staff, and updates on the district's Reopening Plan including the Return to Work Safety Plan.
    2.1 REGULAR AGENDA
Racial Equity Update
The Olympia School District recognizes that a commitment to equity is an ongoing, daily practice that must be implemented by all staff of the district. In partnership with the Puget Sound Educational Services District (PSESD), the district is developing plans to help provides support and education on equity for all staff addressing  persistent educational and opportunity gaps faced by students based on race, ethnicity, gender, language, or disability. The district goal is to seek the cultural competency of teachers and staff, access to high-quality instruction, and access to culturally-relevant curriculum content. Together, the School Board and district leaders seeks to strengthen the District's equity lens in its analysis of the impact of policy and practice on historically underserved, underrepresented, and marginalized individuals and groups and resulting barriers and roadblocks to success.
  Eileen Yoshina, Director of Equity Education with PSESD, briefly spoke about the framework for the district in regard to racial equity mentioning the importance of shifting the equity from the student onto staff and educators. Timing also plays a huge role in racial equity often times being heightened when inequitable situations occur.  Ms. Yoshina presented the A-B-C (Adult Behavior Change)-CLRE:
Awareness & Shared Analysis - “Racial Equity Lens”
Shared Purpose “Deepening our Why”
Skill “Adaptive Leadership Tools”
Capacity “Tolerance for Heat & Ambiguity”
Evidence of Adult Behavior Change & Student Impact “Racial Equity Evaluation”
Staff found the CLRE course as well as other equity tools and resources provided by PSESD valuable for both themselves and educators. Racism is complex. Many are afraid to even approach the subject. It often feels easier and safer to avoid the topic altogether. Participants in the work session discussed how silence and inaction reinforce the situation.  Avoidance speaks volumes. The group continued to discuss addressing these problems and finding solutions.  Educators have moments of opportunities to constructively and productively address race. But these opportunities need to be thoughtfully created, seized, planned and managed.  Conversation about making race conversations normal, constructive, and successful by creating a safe work space was discussed. 
  2.2 District Reopening Plan Update
Updates to the district’s Reopening Plan were shared including the Return to Work Safety Protocol for all employees.  This plan is very much part of the equity lens ensuring that the district accommodates all health and safety needs for both staff and students of the district upon returning to hybrid or full-time in-person model.
