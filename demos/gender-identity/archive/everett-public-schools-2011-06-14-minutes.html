9.01 Community Outreach Planning
President Petersen reconvened the meeting at 6:40 p.m. in the board room.

The board of directors discussed at several meetings hosting another series of community engagement sessions to follow the adoption of the strategic plan. Suggestions have been offered to consider morning coffees, evening gatherings, another series of study sessions, and presentations of the strategic plan.  This time was allocated for board discussion of the subject.  President Petersen opened the floor for discussions.

Director Olson stated that it is a good idea to make the board approachable and available in an informal setting to listen to ideas and have informal discussions. She shared ideas on frequency, venues, and board attendance at the proposed Community Outreach meetings.  Follow-up conversations included the requirement for minutes and meeting announcements.

Vice-President Russell spoke of crafting a more focused engagement event involving feedback from the student representatives and thought the board should develop a process for topic selection.

Director Dutton suggested the board consider what their objectives are in holding these meetings:  What do directors want to offer to the community?  What does the board expect by engaging the community? She said that it should be a two-way street so the board is able to use the information to help in its work. It's important to serve as a conduit since the board directors are liaisons between the district and the community.

President Petersen stated he is interested in themed sessions that are open and free-flowing with topics related to the Strategic Plan and aligning them with the Strategic Goals. He also asked that the board preserve as much time as possible for policy consideration.

Director Andrews stated that the board represents such a broad spectrum of constituents, from students to grandparents, that she was not clear how the board could decide which group to target. She shared her experiences with the large number of community meetings the board hosted during the superintendent hiring process, all of which had very poor attendance. She felt the board is available through email and many other avenues that had not been available just a few years ago. Time is scarce and this commitment of time may not reap results for board.

Director Olson believes focused or themed meetings may be intimidating to the community. Bowling with the board night or shooting pool with the school board at a tavern where folks can chat would make the board more approachable.

Director Dutton suggested investigating the use of existing forums such as the City of Everett's neighborhood meetings. Perhaps getting on the agenda of one of these meetings would be a way to guarantee an audience.

President Petersen summarized that the purpose of this discussion was to get the board thinking about community outreach and to set the stage for future conversations during the August board workshop.

11. Policy Review
11.01 Policy 1140, Student Representation to the School Board, Proposed New Policy (second reading)
This is a new policy to establish student representatives to the school board, and was presented for second reading and adoption. The policy would allow for two student representatives to be appointed to the board of directors in a non-voting advisory role. Student representatives would be appointed from two of the district’s four high schools for two-year terms. A new student would be selected annually from one of the four high schools on a rotating basis. The policy would foster student and board communication on issues of mutual interest. A committee will be established by the superintendent to select a student for each year, for board appointment to serve the following two years.

The directors are eager to add student representatives to the board and they shared how valuable the students' perspectives will be to board discussions.
  Recommendation:  That the board of directors approve for adoption upon second reading Policy 1140, Student Representation to the School Board.  (E:289/11)

  11.03 Policy 3204, Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying (first reading)
At 7:05 p.m. Director Dutton excused herself from the meeting due to illness.

Dr. Molly Ringo shared information on Policy 3204, Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying.  (E:291/11)  The policy was provided for first reading with second reading and adoption to be recommended at a future board meeting. Proposed revisions to this policy made during the 2010 legislative session are the result of amendments and additions to RCW 28A.300.285, Washington State’s anti-bullying law. The new law is focused on a greater awareness and reporting of harassment, intimidation and bullying (HIB) in the school environment. The focus of the legislation was a directive to the Office of the Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) to work with the Washington State School Directors’ Association (WSSDA) and the Office of the Education Ombudsman (OEO) to enhance existing school district policies and procedures to ensure that greater protections are provided to students. The legislation states that by August 1, 2011, each school district in the state is required to adopt or amend its existing policy and procedure to incorporate the changes to the law.

Director Olson asked that "subject to board approval" be inserted in the final sentence of the policy, and in the paragraph regarding district core values, that the word "respect" be replaced with "courtesy".  Vice-President Russell suggested that respect is a modifier of treatment.  Dr. Cohn offered that another way to say it would be to treat one another respectfully.  Discussion followed regarding the board refraining from involvement in administrative procedures.

  14. Work/Study Session
14.01 Study Session: Standards-Based Learning Environment
Throughout the nation, school districts are beginning to shift to implementation of standards-based learning environments, including grading for learning. In standards-based learning environments the goal of education is to ensure that each student attains and/or exceeds specified standards. Everett Public Schools has completed considerable work in this area, focusing upon coordination, articulation, and alignment of practice, and is looking ahead to next steps in the process. The report provided board members information on the direction that the district may be heading, understanding the benefits of moving in this direction, and an opportunity to envision the possible future of education for the students in Everett Public Schools. Presenters Matt McCauley and Karin Manns responded to board questions throughout the presentation.  (E:299/11)
