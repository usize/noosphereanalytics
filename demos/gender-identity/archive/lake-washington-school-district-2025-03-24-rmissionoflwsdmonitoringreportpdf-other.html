Executive Summary:

R-1 Monitoring Report, dated March 24, 2025, evaluates our district's mission and vision fulfillment based
on the 2023-2024 academic year. The Lake Washington School District achieved an on-time graduation rate
of 93.3%, placing us fifth out of our ten benchmark districts. We have seen a slight decline in the graduation
rate for all students. Students from low-income households showed a positive annual trend. We continue to
work to address specific subgroups, including Black/African American students and students eligible for
multilingual or special education services, who all show lower graduation rates compared to other groups.
As students leave our system, we do our best to track the reason(s) why as shown in Appendix C. Of the 97
students that dropped out, two of the reasons provided were significantly higher than the class of 2023:
Unknown — no reason given, which went from 15 students in 2023 to 36 students in 2024, and Transfer
Unconfirmed, which went from 22 students in 2023 to 45 students in 2024.

Our data on dropout rates align with the graduation trends. Of the Class of 2024, 95.6% did not drop out.
We see a positive annual trend for our students from low-income households. Black/African American,
Hispanic/Latino students, and students receiving multilingual services show a negative annual trend.

Our extended graduation rate is 95.3%, which is approaching the target of 98% and places us sixth out of ten
benchmarking districts. Our Black/African American students show a positive annual trend.

While we see a negative trend for all students enrolling in post-secondary institutions within two years, we
have a positive annual trend for Black African American students in this category.

94.2% of graduates completed a dual credit course with a B or higher, which is just below the target of
>95%. The past 5 years show a slight positive trend for all students. There are positive annual trends for 5
student groups: Black/African American, Hispanic/Latino, students eligible for multilingual services, students
from low-income households, and students receiving Special Education services.

Superintendent Interpretation:

Students must have opportunities to learn a rich curriculum in inclusive classrooms and have access to learning
opportunities that encourage ongoing growth and development. Every student learns and achieves at high levels
and it is our responsibility to help Each student learn, grows and be challenged while they acquire knowledge and
skills that will help them to be successful in an ever- changing world. Students must be equipped to lead a life
that is personally rewarding and professionally meaningful.

Further, | define these terms to mean the following:

° Each Student: Every child enrolled in Lake Washington School District school or program

° Graduate: Student who completes the required course of study in Lake Washington School District

° Prepared: Equipped to move to the pursuit of their choice for school, work, and life

° Rewarding Life: Able to participate in activities that are of interest and add value personally and globally

° Responsible Life: Ready to assume obligations and duties with reliability and support the ability to bea
self-sufficient adult

° Contributing Member: Able to participate in meaningful activities that promote well-being, community
involvement, and an awareness of being part of the greater good in society

° Community: The broader group of people, places, and activities that include common interests, work,
identity, and location opportunities

° Greater Society: The connections of multiple communities and mindset that understands the
interconnectedness of one’s own existence with the world around them

° College: Place of higher learning after high school that provides education and grants degrees or
certification in an area of interest and aptitude

° Global: The broad scope of mindset and skills that include all places rather than a limited view of place and
connection

° Workplace: A place where people are employed and expected to possess the skills necessary for success

° Personal: Important to the individual interests, skills, and development of every person

° Success: The achievement of something desired or worked for that aligns with personal and professional

goals and aspirations

Data Summary and What the Data Tells Us: Results 1

The on-time (4-year) graduation rate for the Class of 2024 was 93.3% (see Table 1). This was a decline from the
previous year. Compared to last year, the district benchmark ranking dropped from 3 to 5" out of 10 districts.
See Appendix B for further details on historical rankings.

On-time graduation continues to remain high for most student groups with the highest rate for student groups of
Asian (97.0%) and Two or More Races (96.4%). There was a positive annual trend for students from low-income
households (+1.1%). Other target student groups had a negative annual trend over the past four years.

Further analysis of graduation rates for the Class of 2024 shows a significant difference in the on-time rates for
students that began in the Lake Washington School District since the start of 9° grade. Students that enrolled in
LWSD by the start of 9* grade graduated within 4 years at a rate of 95.1%. Students that enrolled in LWSD after
the start of 9" grade graduated at a rate of 75.6%.

The percent of students not dropping out of school during high school shows a similar pattern to the data in the
on-time graduation data (see Table 2). 95.6% of students in the Class of 2024 did not drop out. Students eligible
for multilingual services and students identifying as Hispanic/Latino had the highest percentage of students
dropping out. There was a positive annual trend for students from low-income households (+0.2%). Other target
student groups had a negative annual trend over the past four years.

97 students in the Class of 2024 dropped out. Table 14 in Appendix C provides information on the classification
of these students. A large number of these (45/97) were classified as “unconfirmed transfer.” 36/97 were
“unknown-no reason given”. 1 student graduated after the data was submitted to OSPI. The other 15 left due to
lack of academic progress/attendance, career education options, or pursuit of a GED.

51 students in the Class of 2024 are continuing their education. 36/51 are receiving Special Education Services.
13/51 are multilingual learners. 8 students are receiving both Special Education services and multilingual
services.

The extended (7-year) graduation rate for the Class of 2021 was 95.3% (see Table 3). This shows a negative trend
over the last four years. The district ranking is 6th out of 10. This is a decrease from last year’s ranking of 2nd.

Post-secondary enrollment within two years of graduation continues to remain high for LWSD graduates at 79.2%
for the Class of 2022 (see Table 4). Though this is approaching the target of “>85%”, there has been a negative
trend over the past four years (-1.1%). Black/African American students, Hispanic/Latino students, multilingual
learners, students from low-income households, and students receiving Special Education services all enroll at a
lower rate than other groups with negative trends for all groups except for Black/African American. It is
important to note that post-secondary enrollment only includes students that are pursuing education through
two- and four-year colleges and universities and collected through the National Clearinghouse database. A
senior intent survey was completed for the Class of 2024 with a 76% return rate. Of those that completed the
survey, 84% said they were planning to attend a four year college or university. 9% said a two year community
college or technical/vocational school. 4% said they were taking a gap semester or year. 3% said they were
going to be working or doing an apprenticeship. Less than 1% responded as entering military or unsure. The
survey will be given again this spring to the Class of 2025 with additional efforts to increase the response rates.
FAFSA completion rates for the Class of 2024 was at 59% which was a slight decline from the Class of 2023 (61%).
(Source: Washington Student Achievement Council Data). The district participates in the Guaranteed Admissions
Program (GAP) and submitted 1130 Class of 2024 students for that program. This program provides guaranteed
admission to Central Washington University, Eastern Washington University, The Evergreen State College,
University of Washington Tacoma, Washington State University and Western Washington University for students

Appendix A

Business Rules for Indicators

To ensure consistency and transparency, the business rules for R-1 are shown in Table 7.

Table 7. Business Rules for Results-1 Calculations

Business Rules and Notes

Business rules for P210 graduation rate for OSPI.

Numerator: Number of students graduating in four years, based on their ninth-grade
cohort. Cohorts are based on the year a student entered ninth grade for the first time.
Denominator: Number of students in the ninth-grade cohort

Students with negative withdrawal code are considered “drop outs”. Students with
continuing status are not included as a “drop out”.

Numerator: Number of students who were reported as graduates or

continuing students on the P210 Report.

Denominator: Number of students who were reported as graduates, continuing students,
or dropouts on the P210 Report.

Business rules for P210 graduation rate for OSPI.

Numerator: Number of students graduating in seven years, based on their ninth-grade
cohort. Cohorts are based on the year a student entered ninth grade for the first time.
Denominator: Number of students in the ninth-grade cohort

Numerator: Number of on-time graduates (as reported in P210) who enrolled in a post-
secondary institution within 2 years* of high school graduation (as reported in National
Clearinghouse).

Denominator: Number of on-time graduates.

*Within two years is defined as enrollment at a post-secondary institution sometime
between August 15 of the graduation year and August 14 two years later using National
Clearinghouse data.

Numerator: Number of on-time graduates who have completed a dual credit course with a
B or above (A, B+, B, B-).

Denominator: Number of on-time graduates.

Notes: Dual credit courses are courses designated as AP (A), College in the High School (C),

Numerator: Number of ninth graders who have earned all credits for all courses

evaluated.
