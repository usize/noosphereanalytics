HEARING SESSION:
Fiona Jorgensen introduced herself to the Board. Fiona is the new
Ag/CTE Teacher for the High School. Background is she is from
Coulee City , Washington, went to WSU and graduated with an AG Ed
Degree and CTE certs. Grew up on a wheat farm and has been around
Ag culture all of her life. She also has her Cert in national approved
animal science curriculum. The Board welcomed her to the Zillah
School District.

I.

Sandra Adams read the following letter: Dear Members of the Zillah
Board of Directors, Mr. Burge, Mr. Irion, ZSD Staff, Students, and
Zillah Community, This letter is my formal notice of resignation from
the Zillah School District Board of Directors. It is effective September
26, 2019 at the conclusion of the board meeting. My time on the board
has been one of great appreciation for all employees of this district.
Our school district would not have such a strong reputation if the men
and women of Zillah School District, who devote their time and talents
in a myriad of ways, were not dedicated to the goal of providing the
strongest possible education for each and every student who has
attended school in Zillah. Serving on the Board for these 12 years has
been a wonderful experience and I would like to thank the staff and
community for their support during my tenure. With Warm Regards,
Sandra Adams.

Administrator’s Report
Mr. Phillips reported on New Teachers in his building and have a great mentor program
to help guide them in their new positions at Hilton Elementary. Hilton also has some
new staff assistants in the building and looking forward to a smooth transition with
students. This year Hilton was able to use ESGI kinder screening, 74 out of the 81 new
kinder students were screened. This is a great starting point for teachers to see where
their students are academically. We are still waiting to see if we have received the Blue
Ribbon Award, we should hear back sometime in September. Looking Forward to
another Great Year.
Mrs. Dasso shared they have already started their training with their experts from ESD
105, Lyn Seamon and Sasha Hammond. PLC is still a learning process and the teachers
are eager to learn. Open House is on Monday and we have all but 4 kids left that have
preregistered for the new school year. We have lots planned for the first day of school
with our parent support doing a warm welcome.

Mr. Charbonneau has been gearing up with brand new Art, Social Studies, and ELA
curriculum. Very excited with the brand new digital library that comes with curriculum,
access to more than 2500 books. PLC work called Guaranteed viable curriculum that
middle schoolteachers with be applying. The middle school will be using a different
system when it comes to core classes that are completed during the year. Failures are
tracked and remediation processes are in place. On a lighter note, the ZMS will have
Frisbee golf installed on the campus, huge kudos to Mr. Reed for applying for grants to
make this happen. We also have received a grant of $25000 from the Yakima community
Foundation, $1000 from Parent Association to Build a Gazebo/seating area on our
campus for different school functions. We are still $4000 away from the project being
fully funded. The structure will be built by Steel Structures of America and will need to
be completed by next year or we will lose the funding from the Yakima Community
Foundation.
Mr. Torres talked about the PLC work that the staff at the High School is working on this
Year. He is getting input from staff and the last few days collaboration so far has been
taking place. Last year we did a lot of data and this year we want to work on the
collaboration and hammering down on classroom success and on track to graduation.
Our staff is close knit and looking forward to the learning process as a school. Activities
are starting and it has and will be a process with the construction project, but our
students and staff are adjusting and making it a smooth process. ZAP is a very successful
program and very viable for some of our students. We currently have 21 students
enrolled in the program. Ms. Lewis did summer school this year and it was very
successful.
Mrs. Savage talked about CTE and the Teals program is starting again and this year we
have an instructor from Disney, SnapChat, and 2 from Microsoft. They are very excited
for a new year to start. We have 17 students enrolled right now. Fiona Jorgensen is a
great fit for our district and she has been proactive in reaching out to other district Ag
teachers. Off to a great start so far this year.
Mr. Winters said every year is a great year and this year is gearing up the same.
Numbers in football are down a bit this year, we have 40 kids out for practice, we had
Jacob Tilley resign so if we get over 50 we will replace since it is a BON position. Other
fall sports will start on Monday the 26th and the following week Middle School sports will
begin. We have 6 home football games scheduled this year which could increase to 8
depending on playoff games. Mr. Winters shared the process on students transferring
and being eligible to participate in sports.
