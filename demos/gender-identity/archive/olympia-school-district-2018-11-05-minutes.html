  3.1 Mobility Data 
Executive Director Lauri Klancke spoke about mobility, defined as "students not enrolled for the entire school year." She said there is lots of information from many sources, but not much available to guide the use of the data. In K-8, transitions and poverty are big factors, and we are gathering additional information as we go. Other factors we are looking at is whether transferring students are coming in to the district or transferring out, as well as those students who are moving within the district.

  3.3 Dress Code Update
Superintendent Murphy said the subject of dress code came up in several ways during the Educational Summit. As there was no existing policy, the committee prepared a recommendation to create a non-biased policy.  The committee was predominantly female and representative of our smaller schools, and reflected those who were interested in this topic. Principals have now shared their feedback on the recommendations, as they will ultimately enforce the dress codes in their buildings. Others have shared through email. Questions were raised about both process and content, and when it would be implemented. He suggested a schoolwide, community-wide, survey to gather additional input.
  Charles LeBlanc, Robin Bailey, Julie Woods, Linda Brown, Caleb Backholm and Corrie Martin addressed the Board regarding the dress code recommendations. Mr. LeBlanc questioned whether there is currently a problem, or are we seeking to discover if there is a problem. He added that 45 instances of dress code challenges in five years seemed minimal. Other comments included concerns that there weren't many people involved in the process leading up to the recommendations, that it seemed to be moving very quickly, what it would look like in the classroom, the ability of middle school students to deal with clothing distractions in addition to other districations from learning, training staff to interact appropriately with students in terms of dress code violations, and seeking more feedback from students, teachers and parents, with representation from all schools. 
  The Board members expressed their own concerns and agreed to review a draft policy at their regular meeting on November 19th. Superintendent Murphy will draft the policy.
