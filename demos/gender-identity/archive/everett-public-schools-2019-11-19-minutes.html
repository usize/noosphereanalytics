  9.10 Approval of 2019 Portables Electrical Package Change Order No. 2
  Change order No. 2 is the final change order for the 2019 Portables Electrical Package and covers the extra costs incurred by the contractor to relocate existing data equipment, pay for Snohomish County PUD electrical connection fees, and conduct emergency responder radio system testing at Gateway Middle School, Silver Firs Elementary School, and Jefferson Elementary School. The emergency responder radio system testing was required by Snohomish County Fire Marshall as part of their requirement that the Distributed Antenna Systems (DAS) be added to these three schools because of the addition of portable classrooms at these sites. DAS systems help facilitate the operations and use of first responder radios inside buildings that would otherwise be impenetrable by radios and the required DAS systems will be added to these schools under a separate project in the near future. The contractor’s costs for this work has been reviewed and confirmed by the project architect and district staff.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves 2019 Portables Electrical Package Change Order No. 2 in the amount of $14,975 plus WSST to the contract between Everett Public Schools and Steele Electric, Inc.

  9.14 Adoption of Policy 2100, Educational Opportunities for Military Children, Proposed Revision (second reading)
  Proposed revisions to Policy 2100, Educational Opportunities for Military Children, were provided for second reading and adoption. Districts are now required to enroll a student on a conditional basis when an active-duty military parent is transferred or when transfer is pending. After conditional enrollment, the parent must provide proof of residence in the district within fourteen days of the arrival date. HB 1210 further allows this conditional enrollment by electronic means. Districts can meet this requirement by enrolling these students based on a telephone call or email. Districts may also choose but are not required to use online registration or enrollment systems. In addition to enrolling the student in the district, HB 1210 requires that districts enroll students in a specific school, program, or course, as needed to minimize any disadvantage from having an active duty parent in the military. The revisions also include changing the title from "Educational Opportunities for Military Children," to "Educational Opportunities for Students with a Parent/Legal Guardian in the Military."
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves for adoption upon second reading proposed revisions to Policy 2100, Educational Opportunities for Military Children.
  9.15 Adoption of Policy 2410, High School Graduation Requirements, Proposed Revision (second reading)
  Proposed revisions to Policy 2410, High School Graduation Requirements, were provided for second reading and adoption. Revisions are to comply with HB 1599 which changes the graduation framework by the delinking of statewide assessment from graduation. Other changes include establishing options for graduation pathways, modifying the requirements of credits and credit waivers, and retooling high school and beyond plans.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves for adoption upon second reading proposed revisions to Policy 2410, High School Graduation Requirements.

Regional Superintendent Sally Lancaster shared the structure and timing of Instructional Reviews. Regional Superintendent Jeanne Willard reviewed the Instructional Review debrief process and action planning.   11.03 Fiscal Outlook 2019-23
  Chief Financial Officer Jeff Moore shared an updated multi-year fiscal outlook presentation that integrated the favorable outcomes mitigating major cuts for the 2020-21 school year. Annual updates to long-range projections typically depend heavily upon the predictability of state, federal, and local revenues. For the past several years the four-year fiscal outlook defined two projections. The best case was incumbent upon a favorable legislative outcome, while the worst case modeled legislative inaction. This volatility left school districts across the state on the edge of significant cuts. The 2019 legislature approved the 2017-19 budget within the 105-day regular session making several favorable decisions. Discussion followed regarding salaries and benefits.

  13.11 Policy 5320.9, Family and Medical Leave, Proposed Revision (first reading)
  Regional Superintendent Larry Fleckenstein shared proposed revisions to Policy 5320.9, Family and Medical Leave, provided for first reading. Proposed revisions reflect additional paid family and medical benefits for workers. Starting in 2020, Washington will be the fifth state in the nation to offer paid family and medical leave benefits to workers. The program will be funded by premiums paid by both employees and many employers and will allow workers to take necessary time off when they welcome a new child into their family, are struck by a serious illness or injury, or need to take care of an ill or ailing relative. As directed by the Legislature, premium payments began on January 1, 2019 and benefits can be taken starting January 1, 2020. Importantly, the program is administered by the Employment Security Department, not the district. Revisions also include changing the title from "Family and Medical Leave", to "Family, Medical, and Maternity Leave". After brief discussion regarding the new title of the Policy, President Andrews asked that the item be moved to a second reading for adoption at the next regular board meeting.
