      1. OPENING SPECIAL MEETING- BUDGET HEARING @ 5:30 PM
  A. Opening Time at 5:30 pm by President Robert Butler.
  B. 22-23 Budget
Marshall Kruse gave an overview of the 22-23 Budget. Mr. Kruse explained the requested fund balance of the board is at 5%. Revenue has increased, expenditures have decreased, and understanding going forward of a 5% fund balance being non-negotiable, increases to salaries beyond the budgeted amount will require proportionate reduction and a portion of the upcoming administrator retreat will be dedicated to right-sizing staffing and reinforced spending controls.
Mr. Kruse covered assumptions and changes that reflect enrollment at a flat projection, staffing staying flat, budgeted salary increases, revenue projected to stay flat with the exception of CARES (ESSER) money, 22-23 beginning fund balance - 1.1 (Decline of 200,000). Summary of staff contracted through agencies covered what's in the budget vs positions we cannot fill may have to be filled with contracted services; the big picture breakdown.
Enrollment breakdown comparison from 2019-2020 - 2022-2023 with a projected enrollment of 2192 for the 2022-23 school year; 22-23 Budgeted Expenditures explained; Budgeted risks covered included salary increases, Kindergarten enrollment, hard to hire staffing, budget creep (spending), COVID-19 unexpected surges; Fund balance comparison shared, regionalization comparison shared, and salaries comparison by district compared.

Director Butler confirmed the protected $500,000 for curriculum from levy dollars.
Mr. Kruse clarified this is correct.

Wendy Beach- Librarian Technician (was at Middle School, now at High School) and PSE member spoke to the board about who their members are and what they do daily. Beach stated these are the employees who elected the board members many of whom live in the community. They are parents and members of the community. The fund balance is important but we live here and our wage is just as important. We would like to keep a stable and educated staff here in Granite Falls.

Director Cary shared appreciation to Mr. Kruse for adding enrollment in the presentation, is weary about enrollment numbers because it's a best guess and inquired about the Kindergarten Round-up numbers this year.
Mr. Kruse noted tracking trends started three years ago, last year we had 170 prior to the first day of school, about this time last year we had roughly 130 enrolled and only 94 are enrolled as of today.
Director Cary shared his concern regarding the projected number in the budget.
Director Veatch inquired if this is one of the grades we have to hire based on a number count per classroom.
Mr. Kruse explained the movement of teachers based on enrollment.

Director LeDoux expressed love for the parent patrol idea and shared his experience in WatchDogs at Lake Stevens and noted it's a great program. LeDoux thanked Director Butler for everything including helping him get started on the board and shared appreciation for Butler's support. LeDoux reminded parents and families to help their kids to retain all they learned last school year and to support a strong start to school this next year. Director Veatch recognized all of those that came tonight and chose to endure the heat that is here in this room. Veatch thanked Director Butler for his work and appreciates the leadership from her peers. Veatch expressed the benefit of the Watchdogs program, especially in these times. Veatch shared that we know how our community feels and reflected on what this looks like to our community.
  6. DISTRICT REPORTS
  A. Business Report - Prepared by Marshall Kruse
Mr. Kruse gave an update on the fund balance and reported we are negative 1 million dollars. Kruse stated that May and June are always the worst because of how the district receives funding and noted this puts the district on everyone's (the region and state) radar because we go into the negative and shared this is the importance of having a fund balance at 5%. Kruse shared the added slide which shows a snapshot of how the district is doing financially and intends to add a single slide each month next year on how our levy dollars are being spent.
  B. Superintendent Report- Presented by Dr. Middleton
Dr. Middleton thanked Director Butler for everything through the years. Dr. M noted he is starting his 4th year and his duty as an instructional leader and shared plans to push on that more. Dr. M expressed appreciation in being able to partner with the board chair during COVID the last couple of years, first Director Cary during the first year, then Director Butler and noted they were always a phone call away. Dr. M is looking forward to carrying this on seamlessly with Director Veatch as board chair.
Dr. Middleton thanked Wendy Beach and members of PSE for coming tonight and noted all Wendy's comments during "Items from the Floor" were correct in what the PSE members do and how they support the district and shared we will resume at the bargaining table and we will continue to build on the positive relationship we have.
Dr. M shared two themes going forward into this next school year working with staff and that is safety and communications. First, with safety, schools are still a very safe place for students. Dr. M expressed that we do not want to assume or take a gamble that the schools are safe. Distractions in the last couple of years have caused us to do things in a different way per COVID guidance. Dr. M reflected on the bond we passed a few years back, noted the safety measures put in place and we will work to improve our emergency drills and other safety measures and noted it will be a focus on safety first. Dr. M noted a focus on bringing more parents into the buildings, stated they want to be in our schools and shared with a program like Watchdogs this would be a great opportunity. Dr. M expressed excitement about this work. Second focus is on communication. The plan to increase two-way communication with our stakeholders. Families want their children to grow up and be successful and have a happy life. Dr. M shared we need to go risk-free and expose students more and more to careers and career exploration. Dr. M shared that career exploration provides clarity for students and reported the State is pushing forward the career exploration curriculum that we will be looking at.

    C. Adopt Transition Plan - New Comprehensive School Counseling Plan
Discussion: Director Butler asked about the requirement based on a state funding bill and if there are costs attached to this.
Dr. M explained this is a requirement and noted there is no funding for this.
Director Cary clarified there was a slight increase in the prototypical funding model for counselors but not enough to pay for the additional requirements in this plan. They did not give us nearly enough for this. The considerations are suggestions but not mandates.
Beth Mills shared the research and work done that went in to the creation of this plan and noted this aligns with the national model. Mills shared we are asking for adoption of the plan this year, implementation will happen next year and then the following year it will be fully implemented.
Director Butler noted we are talking about reaching a 5% fund balance and shared concern stating now we are talking about additional requirements that are not funded. If they are not going to fund us, this goes against everything we just said about building our fund balance.
Beth Mills shared this plan clarifies the work of the counselors.
Director Veatch noted knowledge that the state is giving us nothing and inquired if there is grant opportunities that may be available to help support something like this.
Dr. M and Beth Mills reported no funding available through the usual channels.
Director LeDoux asked for clarification on the approval of the plan or the implementation of the plan.
Dr. M shared the plan is set but this is for implementation.
Director Veatch requested we check in with OSPI and the ESD to see if there is funding in the works to support the implementation.
Director LeDoux asked what the cost of this transition plan as a whole is vs. the component cost and if this is going to improve our students lives.
Director Cary noted this is the plan and asked if this is coming before the board again or are we approving this now and we will not see this again.
Dr. M clarified that this is just approval for the plan. It will come back to the board for approval with costs attached.
Director Butler clarified there is no way this is not going to cost us more money because there are additional duties involved.
Director Cary clarified that the board will be looking at this plan again in 2023-24 and vote on whether to approve it or not.
Director Butler asked Dr. M how would you proceed with this.
Dr. M supports the content but wouldn't be surprised if we were unable to achieve the things in this plan due to the funding model.
  Motion to adopt the Transition Plan - New Comprehensive School Counseling Plan.
  Motion by Carl Cary, second by Karley Kincaid.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Robert Butler, Tina Veatch, Carl Cary, Karley Kincaid, Peter LeDoux
