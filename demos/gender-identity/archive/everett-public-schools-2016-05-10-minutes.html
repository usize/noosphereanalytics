  7.02 Recognition of 2015 Washington State Achievement Award Winners
  Superintendent Cohn shared that five of the district’s 26 schools are among the state’s top performing schools, based upon statewide assessment data for the past three consecutive years: 2015, 2014, and 2013. Each of these has earned the title Washington State Achievement School for 2015. Most of this year’s winners have earned an achievement award before. Because the award is based upon continuous student learning measured over consecutive years, earning successive honors becomes more challenging over time.

Associate Superintendent Teaching & Learning Joyce Stewart introduced Whittier Elementary Principal Tony Wentworth and his representative team for the High Progress award; Assistant Superintendent Teaching & Learning (central region) Molly Ringo introduced Monroe Elementary Principal Gerard Holzman and his representative team for the High Progress award; Assistant Superintendent Teaching & Learning (south region) Peter Scott introduced Cedar Wood Elementary Principal David Jones and his representative team for the Overall Excellence and English Language Arts Growth awards; Mill Creek Elementary Principal Brenda Fuglevand and her representative team for the Language Arts Growth and English Language Acquisition awards; Gateway Middle School Principal Shelley Petillo and her representative team for the English Language Arts Growth award.

President Wenta presented each school with a certificate of recognition followed by group photos with the board of directors.

    8. Information/Discussion
8.01 Associated Student Body Budget 2016-17
  The Associated Student Body (ASB) budget is the consolidation of the individual school budgets from five middle schools and four high schools. The budgets are developed by student leaders at each school under the guidance of their respective ASB advisors. The total proposed ASB appropriation for 2016-17 is $2.9 million to support student activities, athletics, community service projects, and philanthropic contributions. The high school ASB budgets comprise the majority of the total budget ($2.4 million) and, accordingly, the ASB officers from each high school provided the board with a presentation of their preliminary plans. Formal adoption of the ASB fund is scheduled for August 23, 2016, along with other district funds.

Student leaders Love Richardson and Emmerson Hunter presented the Sequoia High School 2016-17 ASB budget proposal; ASB Officers Martha Jeanice, Rayna LaFave-Clark, Anh Nguyen, Zack Demars presented the Cascade High School ASB 2016-17 budget proposal; ASB officers Ian Davis-Leonard, Michael Larson, Hank Tian, and Trayana Rogers presented the Everett High School 2016-17 ASB budget proposal; ASB officers Angelo Timbol, Dartagnan Phan, Julie Lee, and Jack Ellis presented the Jackson High School 2016-17 ASB budget proposal. Following each presentation, board members asked clarifying questions and then posed for photos with the ASB groups.

  10. Strategic Progress Monitoring
10.01 High School Readiness
  At 6:56 p.m., President Wenta reconvened the regular meeting.

Assistant Superintendent Teaching & Learning (central region) Molly Ringo, and Evergreen Middle School Principal Larry Fleckenstein used a PowerPoint presentation to provide directors with the annual update on the implementation of the high school readiness indicators at the middle level. It also highlighted multiple strategies and support for middle school students transitioning to high school. The indicators serve as an early warning system in the preparation of middle school students for high school. Emphasis on the indicators continues to raise awareness and create a focus on the importance of consistent student attendance, rigorous course work, and the importance of school behaviors, in order to have middle school students prepared for a successful high school experience. This is the fifth year of implementation and student data on the indicators is now collected from the 2011-12 school through the first semester 2016. The assistant superintendents responded to questions throughout with topics focused on socio-economic status of students taking high school credits, student attendance concerns and strategies, Spanish enrollment and course availability.
  10.02 24 Credit Plan Progress Update
  Since August 2014, a district steering committee has been leading the state-mandated 24 credit graduation requirement process, including extensive planning, research and staff input. Associate Superintendent of Curriculum, Assessment and Special Programs Tony Byrd, and On-Time Graduation Director Jeanne Willard provided the board with an update on progress to implement a 24 credit diploma for the class of 2021 and beyond; with particular focus on scheduling options for the high school. Dr. Byrd and Dr. Willard responded to questions throughout. Topics included how postponing 24 credit requirements affects eighth graders, competency-based credits, legislative actions, and scheduling options.
    11. Information/Discussion
11.01 Budget Development II
  The 2016 regular legislative session convened on January 11, 2016; however, the legislature fell short of completing the 60-day "short" session by the March 10, 2016 deadline. A special session began immediately and required 20 days additional to conclude the budget work and major policy and budget-related items. Much to the disappointment of school districts across the state, the legislature failed to address the 2018 “levy cliff”. On March 15, 2016, the board reviewed the updated three-year fiscal outlook as well as a set of 2016-17 budget recommendations processed through the superintendent’s cabinet and the Fiscal Advisory Council (FAC). Feedback was provided on the proposed recommendations with the understanding that final review would occur following the April 26 special election for the 2016 capital bond and levy. Passage of both measures reduces further fiscal impacts to the general fund and supports final budget decisions.

Executive Director of Finance and Business Services Jeff Moore provided an update on a more favorable the three-year fiscal outlook, which now builds sufficient general fund balance to create a one-year buffer against the 2018 levy cliff. In addition, the board reviewed the remaining 2016-17 budget recommendations. Feedback will facilitate finalization of the formal budget document required under state form F-195 scheduled for first reading on July 5, 2016. Topics of discussion included the fund balance and the operational impact of staffing models. Director LeSesne requested to see different budget and hiring scenarios at various fund balance levels.
