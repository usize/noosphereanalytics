Audience Comments Procedure
We welcome comments about School Board and district decisions, processes and concerns.
Forms are available in the back of the room to sign up to speak and outlines the School Boards
process to do so.
While the form outlines our procedure to Audience Comments I will take a few minutes to remind
everyone how this works.
Each speaker has 3 minutes; however if several are here on the same topic a representative
may be selected for the group and speak for 10 minutes.In doing so, the individuals in that group
yield their time to their representative.
It is important to note that a separate process exists for concerns about staff.
Audience Comments during the School Board meeting is not the forum for staff complaints.
If you are here for that reason, please see any staff member here in the room and they will
help you learn more about the staff complaint process.
Be aware that if at any time during a speakers comments, their words begin to approach a
personnel concern; the Board President will remind the speakeronceto keep comments
centered on district or School Board decisions, processes and concerns.
If that speaker continues with staff-related comments, the Board President will give 30
seconds for the presenter to immediately sum up their thoughts and return to the audience.
Again, any staff member in the room can help with how to go forward with a complaint
about personnel.

Attached for your review are:
Policy and Procedure 3207 - Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying
Policy and Procedure 3211 - Gender Inclusive Schools
Per OSPI Bulletin No. 089-19 Legal Affairs - December 12, 2019
During the 2019 regular session, the Legislature passed Senate Bill (SB) 5689 concerning
harassment, intimidation and bullying (HIB), and discrimination in public schools. Sections of this
bill include new requirements for school districts regarding nondiscrimination policies and
procedures, notifications, and designated coordinators. This bulletin contains a summary of the
new requirements related to nondiscrimination, which are codified in Revised Code of
Washington (RCW) 28A.642.080
1. Adopt Gender-Inclusive Schools Policy and Procedure

By January 31, 2020, each school district must adopt or amend, if necessary, policies and
procedures that, at a minimum, incorporate all the elements of the Washington State
School Directors Association (WSSDA) mode Gender-Inclusive Schools policy and
procedure, 3211/3211P. This policy and procedure are updated versions of WSSDAs
formerly titled Transgender Students. The district had already adopted WSSDA policy and
procedure 3211/3211P, this is the required updates only.
2. Harassment, Intimidation, and Bullying

SB 5689 also included revisions to the Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying
statute, which is now codified at RCW 28A.600.477. By January 32, 2020, each school
district must adopt or amend, if necessary, a policy and procedure that, at a minimum,
incorporates the WSSDA model Harassment, Intimidation, and Bullying policy and
procedure 3207/3207P. The district had already adopted WSSDA policy and procedure
3207/3207P, this the required updated only.
A first reading was held at the January 9, 2020 Special Board Meeting.
