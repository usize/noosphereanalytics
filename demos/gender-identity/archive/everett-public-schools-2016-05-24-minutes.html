    7.08 Acceptance of the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report 2014-15
  The Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) for the fiscal year ending August 31, 2015, was provided to the board as a resource. This document provided detailed financial and statistical information for the prior fiscal year. The CAFR is compiled according to national standards of Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and the format is compliant with the Governmental Accounting Standards Board’s Statement Number 34 (GASB 34). GASB 34 not only aligns our financial reporting with other municipalities and governmental agencies, but also aligns with private sector corporations. This report was reviewed by the Washington State Auditor’s Office (SAO). Last year, Assistant State Auditor Erika Davies wrote in an email to the district, "Congratulations on another clean financial statement audit! I appreciate all the time and effort the District clearly dedicated to preparing and reviewing the statements. Thank you for your continuing commitment to accurate financial reporting." In an email to the district following this year's annual audit, Assistant Audit Manager Kirk Gadbois wrote, "Once again you and your staff were able to produce statements that were clean of material issues." In our annual audit exit conference, it was highlighted that "For years, on-site audit staff commented on how well prepared the District's financial statements have been. This year, our reviewers in Olympia mentioned how clean the financial statements are for a GAAP entity."
  Recommendation: That the board of directors, acting as a committee of the whole, accepts the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report 2014-15.

  9. Strategic Progress Monitoring
9.01 Class of 2016 Progress to Graduation
  The district's high schools' classes of 2016 are approaching their June commencement dates. Each high school has engaged students in preparation for graduation and is working to ensure each senior is making necessary progress toward on-time graduation in June or August. Director of On-Time Graduation Jeanne Willard provided the board with a status report on the class of 2016 with specific information about the strategies and interventions taking place to help students fulfill their graduation requirements. The board also received a complete report of recently published graduation rates, including four year adjusted cohort graduation rates for the class of 2015, and five year adjusted cohort graduation rates for the class of 2014. Dr. Willard responded to questions throughout the presentation. Topics included summer school programs, clarification of the state's Student Retrieval Act (ESSHB 1418), STEM academy, and contract learning.
  9.02 Postsecondary Enrollment Patterns
  Everett Public Schools continues to implement its strategic priority that each student is prepared for transition to postsecondary education and career choices. Data for the classes of 2005 through 2013 demonstrate steady patterns of postsecondary enrollment. Director of On-Time Graduation Jeanne Willard shared newly available data, sourced from seniors in the class of 2016 who have completed the High School and Beyond Plan Survey in Naviance, which indicates student perceptions of preparing for college and career, as well as anticipated postsecondary enrollment. Dr. Willard responded to questions throughout the presentation. Topics included an explanation of Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA), factors for college graduation rates, Naviance, addressing the low rates of students enrolling in college, and how enrollment data is gathered.

13.01 Approval of Student Representative to the School Board
  Policy 1140, Student Representation to the School Board, provides for two student representatives to be appointed by the board of directors to a non-voting advisory role. The purpose of the policy is to foster an environment whereby student views may be voiced, and students may listen to and evaluate the opinions of others. The student representatives are expected to increase directors’ awareness of students’ views, as well as to communicate the perspectives of the board and understanding of district policy to the students. The 2011-12 board work plan provides that two student representatives will be integrated into the school board during the year. Procedure 1140P describes the process for nominating two candidates to serve as representatives to the school board. A committee established by the superintendent to select a student for board appointment has completed its work and nominated Dhruvik Parikh, a sophomore at Jackson High School for appointment as a student representative to the Everett Public Schools Board of Directors through June 2018, filling the slot vacated by Scott Pease, a graduating senior from Cascade High School. The board of directors was presented the nomination for approval.

Director of Athletics, Health/Fitness and Activities Robert Polk introduced Dhruvik.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves the selection committee's nominee, Dhruvik Parikh of Jackson High School, for appointment as student representative to the Everett Public Schools Board of Directors beginning with the September 6, 2016 regular board meeting, to serve in accordance with Policy 1140 until June 2018.

  13.02 Policy 3213, Transgender Students, Proposed New Policy (first reading)
  Proposed new Policy 3213, Transgender Students, was provided for first reading with second reading and adoption to be recommended at a future board meeting. Assistant Superintendent Molly Ringo used a PowerPoint presentation to share the recent passage of laws and regulations that address discriminatory practices regarding sexual orientation, gender identity, or gender expression, clear expectations and guidelines are needed for the district's students and staff to create an inclusive approach for transgender students in order that they may be provided equal educational opportunities.

Following the presentation, Cascade High School Principal Cathy Woods described how Cascade is accommodating current transgender students. Aiden Key, who has facilitated transgender community meetings in several school districts, and therapist Brenda Newell shared their experiences and perspectives in supporting transgender youth. Director questions and comments included the need for policy communications to staff, students, and the community, and addressing parent concerns. Student Representative Pease said this is a big step in addressing the transgender community and expressed his delight with the district moving forward on this policy. Student Representative Carrillo added that this policy will help many students; this has been going on for years, but with the increased media exposure it has made it more difficult for these students.

President Wenta announced that the policy will be moved for second reading at the next regular board meeting.
