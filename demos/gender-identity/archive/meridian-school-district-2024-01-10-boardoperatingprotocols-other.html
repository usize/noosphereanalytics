SCHOOL BOARD OPERATING PROTOCOL

For the purpose of enhancing teamwork among members of the board and between the board and
administration, we, the members of the Meridian School District Board of Directors, do hereby publicly
commit ourselves collectively and individually to the following operating protocol:

1. The board will regularly review and recognize its governance responsibilities in the five core
principles: 1. Responsible school district governance, 2. Commitment of and commitment to high
expectations for student learning, 3. Creating conditions district-wide for student and staff success,
4. Holding the district accountable for student learning, and 5. Engagement of the community in
education as outlined by WSSDA School Board Standards.

2. The board will represent the needs and interests of all the children in our district.

3. The board will lead by example. We agree to avoid words and actions that create a negative impression
on an individual, the board, or district. While we encourage debate and differing points of view, we will
do it with care and respect.

4. Surprises to the board or the superintendent will be the exception, not the rule. We agree to ask the
board chair or the superintendent to place an item on the agenda instead of bringing it up unexpectedly
during the meeting.

5. Communications between staff and the board are encouraged. However, board requests which will
likely require considerable time or have political implications are to be directed to the superintendent.

6. All personnel complaints and criticisms received by the board or its individual members will be
directed to the superintendent.

7. The board will encourage others to present their own issues, problems, or proposals to the person who
can properly and expeditiously address their issue.

8. To be efficient and effective, long board meetings must be avoided. If a board member needs more
information, either the superintendent or board chair is to be called before the meeting. Audience

participation protocol will be consistently practiced.

9. The board will consider research, best practice, fiscal responsibility, legal guidelines, and their role as
representatives of the entire community in making decisions.

10. The superintendent is the chief executive officer and should recommend, propose, or suggest on most
matters before the board.

11. The board will speak to the issues on the agenda, not engage in inappropriate debate. Facts and
information needed from the administration will be referred to the superintendent.

12. Study sessions will be for in-depth discussion on one or two agreed upon topics.


13. Executive sessions will be held only when specific needs arise. Board members must be sensitive to
the legal guidelines which govern such sessions. All discussions that occur during executive sessions are
privileged and confidential and shall not be shared with anyone unless it is the express decision of the
board to do so.

14. The board will conduct a yearly self-evaluation and promptly address individual problems that hinder
board effectiveness such as poor meeting attendance, tardiness, or leaks of confidential information.

15. The board and superintendent will review the goals established in the District’s Strategic Plan
annually. Input from staff, parents, and community members will be considered in this process.

16. Board members will do their “homework” by being prepared for meetings and will attend board
development workshops as appropriate.

17. Individual board members do not have authority. Only the board as a whole has authority. We agree
that individual board members will not take unilateral action.

18. The board chair or designee will be the spokesperson for the board. When warranted and possible,
consultation will occur with other board members.

19. The board will establish the vision, create policies, and assure accountability. The superintendent will
manage the schools.

20. Debate over issues should be open and honest. Once a final board decision is reached, however, each
member has the responsibility to support the decision and aid in its implementation.

21. In any instance of possible conflict of interest, board members will notify the board and the
superintendent and will excuse themselves from deliberation and/or action.

22. Attendance to board meetings should be prioritized. Board members will confirm
attendance with the Board Chair 72 hours prior to the upcoming scheduled board meeting.

23. This Board Protocol document shall be reviewed annually and may be amended as needed by a
majority vote of the Board. It shall also be reviewed whenever a new director joins the board.

Approved: January 17, 2007
