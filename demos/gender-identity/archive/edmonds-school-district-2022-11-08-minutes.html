  Information: 8.1 Audience Comments
  Bob Fretz, community member, spoke in regards to the book, "The 1619 project" and how he does not believe it's an accurate history text.

Deborah Lobe, community member, spoke about the importance of safety in our schools. She encouraged the board to reconsider putting police officers in schools.

Karen Mosman, community member, spoke about school safety in the wake of the shooting at Ingraham High School. She encouraged the board to reconsider putting police officers in schools.

Bill Klatt, community member, spoke about broad societal concerns.

Loni Rogers, ESD parent, encouraged a welcoming environment for students so they all feel supported and are able to thrive as their authentic selves.

Jennifer Taylor, ESD teacher, spoke about the dual language program implementation at Mountlake Terrace Elementary School. She encouraged central office staff to hear input from the teachers and community before beginning a new program.

Ryan Martin, ESD parent, spoke about safety concerns at schools. As a federal investigator he encouraged the board to do everything they can to make our schools safer and be aware of gang actvity in our area.

Tamara N, community member, shared that she is concerned about school safety and asked the school board to reconsider putting police officers in schools. She also shared concerns about the sex ed curriculum and urged parents to review the material.

Elizabeth Fleming, ESD parent, shared safety concerns about a recent incident at Edmonds Woodway High School when a weapon was found on a student.

Stephanie Schindele, ESD teacher, urged the board to delay the dual language expansion to allow for time to hear the voices of stakeholders at Spruce Elementary.

Kairi Kuritani, ESD Employee, encouraged the board to ensure that student voice be considered in district decision making.

Sarah Dilling, ESD parent, encouraged the community to work together to address school safety concerns and noted that the answer is not always police.

Nicole Harreld, ESD employee, spoke about her confusion when the dual language expansion was announced at her school. She requested additional information about the budgetary needs of the program as well as additional time to implement.

Atlis Morrison, ESD student, spoke about their involvement with the recent effort to bring gender neutral bathrooms to Mountlake Terrace High. They noted that there have been no issues so far with the project. They also noted that lockdowns at school are very scary from the perspective of students.

Victoria Castaneda, ESD employee and parent, spoke in favor of gender neutral bathrooms in schools and other inclusive policies that show students of all kinds love and respect.

Melanie Norris, ESD teacher, requested that the board delay the expansion of the dual language program at Spruce Elementary for another year so they can properly implement the program.

  12. LEGISLATIVE UPDATES
  Director Chase provided a legislative update. She reviewed WSSDA's 2023 Legislative Priorities, which have been grouped into five categories: Meet the Requirements for Special Education, Provide the Needed Resources for Ample, Equitable and Stable Education, Feed Students, Construct Safe and Healthy Schools and Transport Each and Every Student. WSSDA will use this list to create a briefing for each category. The board spoke in favor of supporting the "Feed Students" policies as well as the Special Education and Providing Ample resources categories. Director Chase also shared the possibility of forming a legislative committee made up of district staff, students and teachers that could go to Olympia to lobby. 
    13. BOARD MEMBER COMMENTS
Information: 13.1 Board Member Comments
Director Smith noted that many people are struggling right now and its important to direct community members to resources whenever possible. He also thanked the community for their interest in safety in our schools.

Director Noble sent condolences to the family, students and staff at Ingraham High School. He noted that these events are traumatic for all students in our region and that students should be given extra support and grace in the wake of this tragedy. He noted that he recently went on a field trip with Westgate Elementary and on a school visit to Oak Heights Elementary.

Director Chase thanked the community for coming to speak about school safety. She spoke about the WSSDA Equity training that the board recently attended and how she believes this work makes them better leaders.

Director Kilgore acknowledged that the board is listening to all the public comments about safety. She described how our comprehensive safety plans take into consideration all elements of safety in our schools. She spoke about the board's decision to remove SROs from schools and how a large component in this decision was students who said police on campus made them uncomfortable. They also reviewed research regarding police on campus when making their decision. She emphasized the importance of social emotional learning in keeping our kids safe. She also mentioned that she is in favor of the dual language programs in our schools and that research shows these programs are working.

Director Katims encouraged everyone to take the superintendent survey about what qualities we need in our next leader. She spoke about the WSSDA equity training the board recently attended and how it mirrored many of our district policies. She talked about school visits and school presentations she gave recently. Finally, she thanked Dr. Miner for all of the hard work she has been contributing as our Interim Superintendent.

Dr. Miner shared how she recently attended the Dias de los Muertos celebration in Edmonds. She also thanked Veterans in our community for their service.
