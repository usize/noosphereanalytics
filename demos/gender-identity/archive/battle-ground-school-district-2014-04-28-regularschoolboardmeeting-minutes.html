Audit Committee Report
Audit Committee members are Monty Anderson and Stephanie McClintock. The audit committee is a
board sub-committee that was formed on January 13, 2014 by the Board of Directors.
Per Board Policy No. 1240, the audit committee consists of two members of the Board of Directors. The
committee is charged to
1. Provide oversight with the external auditor,
2. Review and approve the superintendent contract, timesheets, reimbursements, cell phone bills,
leave buybacks, and other expenditures,
3. Provide communication to other board members if needed,
4. Provide management a copy of the superintendent's contract for legal and compliance review prior
to finalization,
5. Provide management a copy of the superintendent's contract directly upon finalization,
6. Meet monthly, at least.
The audit committee meets the first Friday of every month.

D. Procedure 2409 - Credit for Competency

Meeting :

Apr 28, 2014 - MINUTES Regular School Board
Meeting

Category :

6. Communications

Type :

Information

Public Content
Presenters:
Marcia Christian, Assistant Superintendent of Teaching and Learning
Sheryl Piper, Head Counselor, Battle Ground High School
It has come to our attention that school districts throughout the county are awarding credit to students who pass the Washington
State High School Proficiency Exam (HSPE) in either Reading or Writing. Students in those districts (Vancouver, as an example)
who pass a test receive .25 credit towards graduation. If students pass both tests, they could receive .5 credit.
Sheryl Piper, head counselor at Battle Ground High School, has done some investigation and found that there are 77 students at
BGHS who would benefit and possibly graduate on time if they could receive credit for passing the HSPE exams.

Board Policy 2409 gives the administration the flexibility to award that credit. The change to allow credit would be made in the
procedure, rather than the Board Policy. Therefore, this item is communication only and does not require board approval.
Attached are Board Policy 2409 and Procedure 2409P, with the change in language noted in bold.

Recommended Action :

The Board of Directors approve the student travel
requests submitted for the April 28,2014 board meeting.

Public Content
Student travel requests are to be submitted well in advance of travel to Linda Gellings, Director of Business Services and Risk
Management. Overnight travel requires board approval per Policy 2320 and several types of backup information are required per
Procedure 2320P. Advisors must clear the trip with their building principal prior to submitting it to Risk Management. Trip
itineraries, budget information, transportation and often learning objectives are among items required to accompany the request.
The request form lists the dates of travel, group, location, transportation, purpose, and requires approval signatures.
Exceptions to the rule are travel requests for state and national tournaments and competitions, also outlined in Procedure
2320P. Many times the time frame is limited when a group qualifies to attend a state/national competition. In this case, the
building is required to submit the Student Travel Leave Request, along with a full itinerary, to the Risk Management Office prior
to the trip. Documents are reviewed and the trip is approved accordingly. The trip is ratified by the Board at their next board
meeting. All documentation for each trip is processed and filed with the Director of Risk Management and is available for
inspection upon request.

A. Approval of Pupil Transportation Services Contract
Extension and Amendment

Meeting :

Apr 28, 2014 - MINUTES Regular School Board
Meeting

Category :

Type :

Action

Recommended Action :

The Board of Directors approve the Pupil Transportation
Services Contract Extension and amendment with
Cascade Student Transportation for a rate increase of
0.8%.

Public Content
Presenter:
MaryBeth Lynn, Assistant Superintendent of Finance and School Operations
On February 26, 2011 the Board of Directors approved award of the Pupil Transportation Services bid to Petermann
Transportation. Since that date, Petermann was purchased by Cascade Student Transportation and as part of that purchase,
Cascade Student Transportation assumed the Petermann contract. The original bid award was for a three-year period from July
1, 2011 through June 30, 2014, with the option to extend two one-year terms.
The contract included an escalation clause that the district would assume any increases greater than 2% of the January
percentage change in the U.S. City Average, Consumer Price Index for All Consumers Series CUUR0000SA0. The increase for
this most current time period was 1.6%, which would not allow for a price increase since it is less than 2%. The contract also
stipulates that in the event of any unusual circumstances that the Contractor and District will work to establish a mutual
resolution.
Cascade Student Transportation provided the District with information on the financial impact the waiver days have had on the
fixed costs of their operations. For the most recent year, the fixed costs that were not covered are approximately $40,800 (60%
of $68,000, information provided by Cascade). After discussion between Cascade and the District, it is recommended that
Cascade be granted a .8% price increase in recognition of the unanticipated waiver days that were not recognized in the original
bid. This would result in an estimated increase in the contract of approximately $48,000. The total cost of the contract for 201314 is estimated to be $5,988,500.

The Washington State Healthy Youth Survey is administered every other year to measure the following
adolescent health behaviors and related risk and protective factors.
Unintentional and intentional injury behavior
Physical activity and dietary behaviors
Alcohol, tobacco and other drug use
Risk and protective factors
Access to school-based services
The Board of Directors has previously approved the administration of this survey. For the 2014-2015
school year, the survey will be administered during the week of October 13-17, 2014. Results will be
available in March 2015. The district will not be administering questions related to sexual behavior; those
questions are optional. Student participation is voluntary and anonymous.
