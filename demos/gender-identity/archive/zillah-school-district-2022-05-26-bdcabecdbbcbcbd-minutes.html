HEARING SESSION:
Mr. Brady announced we would be going into the hearing session of the meeting.
Mike Collins made the following statement: Twice I have come before you and asked for an open
discussion on the transgenderism and the teaching of this so-called gender identity in the sex ed
curriculum. I have seen no open discussion on what your thoughts are on that and no response.
Maybe this will make it a little easier for you. Under the Zillah motto under what’s best for kids.
Do you believe teaching ideology of transgenderism, multiple gender identities in our sex ed
class curriculum is best for kids? And along with that policy 3211 allowing a biological boy into

girls bathroom, locker, and vice versa and keeping or hiding a kids sexual identity from their
parents. Is this what’s best for kids. Mr. Fender? No response. Mr. Belton? Response from
Mr. Belton: Yes, I would be happy to engage you offline, I can give you my cell phone number
and you can hear how I feel about it and the policy specific any time. Mr. Collins asks: Why
can’t we hear it now? Mr. Belton’s response: you heard me discuss it, I asked the questions
about the policy earlier. It is a state restriction that we adopted so we don’t….Mr. Collins
interrupts: There are a lot of ramifications with that 3211 and we don’t know what those
ramifications are? How are you going to address them? Thank you I appreciate it. Mr.
Jones…no comment….Mr. Brady? Mr. Brady responds: I believe that I will exclude nobody.
Mr Collins replies with thank You. In Loudoun County in October 2021, a boy wearing a skirt
sexually assaulted a female student in the bathroom. Can you absolutely 100% guarantee it that
this will not happen in the Zillah School District under this policy? He asked Mr. Fender? Mr.
Fender replies: There are very little guarantees in life. Mr. Collins replies: yes, I agree with
you. He then askes Mr. Belton? Mr. Belton replies: yes specifically I will repeat the same
thing, if you would like to meet with Mr. Burge, I will tell you how we are going to employ this
policy if it comes up period. We are going to protect kids no matter what. No matter what. Mr.
Collins interrupts with the following: But we are not allowed to hear how you are going to
protect them right now. How are you going to keep that boy out of that restroom and prevent
that from happening? Mr. Brady interrupts with: We have already talked about making
accommodations; we have already discussed all this. Why do you keep going over and over
this? Mr. Collins replies: we have not heard from you, how you are going to address these
ramifications. All you tell me is you are going to protect kids. You are going to allow everyone
to do what they need to do. I am not trying to disrespect anyone but there are a lot of parents
who might be concerned about this. Having their child go into a bathroom and knowing that
someone of the opposite gender might come into that bathroom, or that locker room. Mr. Fender
interrupts with the following: that is the purpose of the policy to keep all kids safe and parents
desires to be fulfilled. Mr. Collins time is up. Mr. Burge made the following statement: Policy
and Procedures are approved by the board of directors, application of policy and procedures
are applied through us at the district level. Mr. Collins interrupts with: and we are not privy to
those applications? Mr. Burge and Mr. Irion said they would sit down and talk with Mr. Collins
one on one and Mr. Irion said we had a parent meeting last week and with a great discussion.
Jake Friedrich made the following statement: Obviously, a big thing that is on the minds of
parents is what happened this week in Texas. I know you guys spoke a little bit about safety and
procedures. But one thing I am concerned about is the easy access to our campuses in
particular this one here (ZMS) there is no gates of any kind for controlled access. The
elementary school as well, you are able to drive right up to the preschool classroom. That is
something that I wanted to just bring to light and maybe just consider just how easy our schools
are accessible and they don’t feel and as time have changed they don’t seem as safe as they use
to be. Not that schools themselves aren’t just that the way these events keep occurring I feel like
there are further steps we can take to increase the security for our students and help put parents
minds at ease. I know the odds of something like that happening are not very high in our
community. The odds of this happening in Texas this week were not very high either. It can
happen anywhere, anytime. I feel like every classroom while students are inside those
classrooms should be locked so that if someone does gain access they are not able to get into the
classroom the way they do. I am sure you guys take that very seriously and maybe discuss it.
These are just some things that I’ve noticed personally being a past student here for my whole

life and raising a family now. The main thing for me is just how easy it is to access some of our
campuses because it is unabated and I wish there was more restrictions to getting access to our
campuses. That is all I had to say. Mr. Brady made the following comment: We had a safety
committee meeting and I would really encourage you to be a part of that. Mr Burge commented
he would love for him to be a member of his safety committee team.
There was no more comments for the Board tonight so Mr. Brady concluded the hearing session.
