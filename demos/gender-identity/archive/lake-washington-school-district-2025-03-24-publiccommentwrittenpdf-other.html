LWSD Written Public Comments for 3/24/25

Role
LWSD Parent, Guardian or Family Member Anti DEI efforts

"Re: Concerns Regarding the U.S. Department of Education's ""End DEI"" Portal

Dear Members of the Lake Washington School Board,

| am writing to express my concerns regarding the U.S. Department of Education's ""End DEI"" Portal and its potential implications for our
school district. | respectfully submit these comments for your consideration at the upcoming board meeting.

| am deeply concerned about how this federal initiative might impact our educational community in several ways:

Teacher Protection: What steps will our district take to protect teachers who may be targeted by complaints through this portal? How will
we ensure that our educators can continue to teach inclusive curriculum without fear of reprisal?

Diverse Literature: | am concerned about whether this initiative will result in the removal or restriction of books by authors from
marginalized backgrounds. Will our students still have access to literature written by diverse authors that reflects a wide range of
experiences and perspectives?

Curriculum Impact: How might this development affect our overall curriculum development and implementation? Will teachers face new
limitations in presenting diverse historical perspectives and cultural contexts that are essential for a well-rounded education?

| would appreciate the board addressing the following questions:

¢ Has the district begun developing a response strategy to these federal changes?

¢ What assurances can you provide that our district will maintain its commitment to inclusive education?

¢ How can parents and community members support our teachers during this potentially challenging period?

e Will there be formal opportunities for community input regarding any potential changes to curriculum or library materials?

| firmly believe that a quality education requires exposure to diverse perspectives, histories, and experiences. This approach not only
enriches our students’ learning but also prepares them to succeed in our increasingly diverse society.

| look forward to hearing the board's plan for navigating these changes while maintaining our district's commitment to educational
excellence for all students.

Thank you for your consideration of these concerns.
Respectfully submitted,
Michelle



LWSD Written Public Comments for 3/24/25

Role
LWSD Parent, Guardian or Family Member;LWSD Teacher Absences & Lack of Counselor
Resident;Business or Organization Availability

"Dear Members of the School Board,

| am writing to express serious concerns regarding the well-being and academic success of students in our district. As a parent of two high
school students—one at Redmond and one at Tesla—| have seen firsthand the barriers preventing students from accessing the support
they need.

One of the most pressing issues is the high rate of teacher absences, which is severely disrupting learning. Students need consistency in
the classroom to succeed, yet they are frequently left without their teachers, leading to instructional gaps and a lack of academic stability.
What measures are being taken to address this ongoing problem? How is the district holding schools accountable for ensuring students
receive the education they are promised?

Additionally, the lack of availability of school counselors is leaving students without academic guidance, social-emotional support, or a
trusted adult to turn to. There are days when certain counselors have no open meeting times at all, making it nearly impossible for
students to access critical support when they need it most. How is it that counselors are so unavailable before school, after school, and
even during lunch? What is preventing them from prioritizing direct student support?

| urge the district to take immediate action to ensure that students have reliable access to their teachers, counselors, and a stable support
system. If these issues persist, | will escalate these concerns to OSPI to ensure oversight and accountability.
