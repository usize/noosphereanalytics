  8. NEW BUSINESS
  8.01 Beginning Educator Support Team (BEST) Update
  Dr. Bob Ettinger, Director of Digital Learning and Emily Ganyo, Executive Director of Human Resources presented an update on the BEST grant (Beginning Educator Support Team). The program is built around standards for beginning educator induction. The grant funding is specifically provided for first and second year certificated teachers and this year will also support counselors. The components of the program include hiring, orientation, mentoring, professional learning, formative feedback and program impact data.
  Highlights from 2019/2020 were shared, to name a few, providing virtual support for mentors and new teachers during in-home learning and beginning a New Teachers of Color PLC. Survey data was reviewed from feedback gathered from first year teachers around the program impact from 2018, 2019, and 2020.
  8.02 2020 Capital Planning & Construction Status Update
  Matt Feldmeyer, Executive Director of Capital Planning and Construction and Traci Rogstad, Senior Project Manager presented an update and review of the 2016 Capital Levy and 2019 Capital Bond programs. In 2020 there were 84 initial projects for the 2016 “Building for a Lifetime of Learning” Capital Levy of which 33 were completed with 51 projects remaining and $37,091,846 in funding remaining. In 2020 the 2019 “Building for Excellence” Bond projects had 156 projects of which 30 projects were completed and 126 projects remain with $228,745,855 in funding remaining.
  The department approved and completed four candidate projects out of 44 candidate requests.
The financial summary for 2020 was shared and approximately $2.6 million will be returned to the Capital Projects fund because the full funding was not needed for the Lindbergh High School Auxiliary Gym Floor project and the Renton Memorial Stadium turf and track did not need replacing as it was determined that it had another 10 years of life.
  An update was provided for elementary school #16. The department continues to move forward with challenges surrounding site development including complicated site wetlands, steep slope, utilities, the surrounding neighborhoods and Kingdom Hall, the principal arteria (Duvall Ave NE) and Vehicular and pedestrian access.
  Planning for upgrading high school science classrooms is beginning and construction is scheduled to begin in 2022 and 2023. Upcoming projects for 2021 were reviewed as well.
  The department has also reviewed the HVAC systems throughout the district and identified modifications in order to minimize the risk of viral transmission of COVID-19.
