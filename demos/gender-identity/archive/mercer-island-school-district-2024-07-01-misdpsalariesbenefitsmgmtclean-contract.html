The salary schedule consists of two levels: "entry" and "continuing." The entry level applies to
individuals newly hired to a role they have not previously held. After one year of satisfactory
performance, employees may advance to Step 2 on the salary schedule, "continuing." Typically, the
anniversary date for consideration of this advancement is July 1. Employees hired and placed into
entry-level salary position during the contract year will transition to Step 2, continuing, after twelve
(12) months, even if this requires a mid-contract adjustment. The adjustment will occur on the first of
the month following the employee’s anniversary date.

An employee may also advance from entry to continuing status at any time after the first ninety (90)
days of employment, contingent on the Superintendent's approval following the recommendation of
the employee’s supervisor. Placement on the salary schedule is determined by the Superintendent,
with consideration given to the employee's training, experience, responsibility, and performance.
Retention Incentive

An employee shall be paid an additional 1% of the ongoing base salary beginning in the 4th year of
continuous administrative employment with the District; 2% of the ongoing base salary beginning in
the 7th year of continuous administrative employment with the District; 3% beginning in the

11th year of continuous administrative employment; and 5% of the ongoing base salary beginning in
the 16th year of continuous administrative employment.

Benefits

The School Employees Benefits Board (SEBB) Program will administer health insurance and other
benefits to all school district employees. Benefit funding and allocation will be determined at the state
level. Each non-represented Management Team employee shall receive the fiscal allocation received
by the District under SEBB for a full-time equivalent (FTE) employee per month for benefits
administered by SEBB.

The District may also provide optional benefits for employees to self-pay through payroll deduction.

Vacation

All non-represented Management Team employees shall be granted vacation at the rate of 28 days
annually (earned on a monthly basis of 2.33 days per month). Management Team employees will be
able to cash out up to ten (10) vacation days each year, of which the per diem value of each vacation
day shall be computed by dividing the employee’s annual salary as identified in BP 5415.

Personal Leave
Five (5) days of personal leave may be used each year for personal business.

Professional Growth Fund

For each Management Team employee, the District will establish a fund for the individual’s
professional growth and academic pursuits (the “Fund”). Annually, the District will allocate two
thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500) to each Management Team employee’s Fund. To access their
respective Fund, each Management Team employee must discuss their professional development plans
and submit a written application to their supervisor. Examples of approved Fund expenditures include
but are not limited to:

Registration for a professional conference in an area relevant to one’s professional role;
Completion of an academic class or seminar to enhance skills for current or future role in the
field of education;

Observation/visitation in another district to enhance skills and knowledge for current role;
Membership to professional societies;

Professional journal or similar subscriptions;

Engage in activities or practices that enhance personal wellbeing to improve workplace
performance or retention;

Participation in a professional conference as a presenter;

Participation in a professional association as an officer; and

Successful completion of a professional growth plan (PESB form) as deemed by the evaluator,
a management member may be compensated $1,500 out of the professional growth fund.


Disbursements from an employee’s Fund will be made only after the professional learning activity is
approved by the appropriate supervisor and itemized receipts or invoices are provided to the District
by the employee.

The entire amount of an employee’s Fund does not have to be requested all at once. Employees can
request smaller amounts multiple times until the Fund is exhausted. Any funds not utilized in one
contract year will be automatically carried over to the ensuing year. The cumulative total carried over
for multiple years cannot exceed a balance of four thousand dollars ($4,000) in any given year.
Cumulative amounts from individual Management Team employee Funds that exceed $4,000 in any
given year will automatically revert to the District.

Professional Dues

The District shall pay the employee’s dues for either the Washington Association of School
Administrators or the Association of Washington School Principals, or an equivalent association of the
employee's choice.

Doctorial Stipend
The District shall pay a $2,000 Doctorial Stipend to recognize the professional knowledge achieved in
obtaining the advanced educational degree.

Technology Stipend

The District shall pay a three thousand ($3,000) Technology Stipend to recognize additional work
outside the current contract. This is to provide time for instructional technology planning for
integrating technology into the curriculum and teaching practices within each school. For non-
instructional staff, the stipend will pay for work to convert business processes from manual systems to
new software solutions.

Supplemental Contracts

Supplemental contracts for additional time or responsibility shall be submitted to the Board for
approval. When the opportunity arises for a supplemental contract that requires administrative
expertise or certification, before posting the position, the Superintendent will present a selection
process to the Management Team representatives.

Employment of Retirees

Pursuant to Board Policy 5001, the District may hire retired employees as principals or assistant
principals for a one-year term. Different terms and conditions of such contracts may be set by the
Board based upon the experience of the candidate and the needs of the District.
