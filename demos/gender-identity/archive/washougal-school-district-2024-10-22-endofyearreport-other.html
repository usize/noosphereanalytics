Washington Association of School Administrators (WASA) 2025 Legislative Priorities
Contact your legislators

WASA will advocate for three key areas in the 2025 legislative session:

iB

Special Education: WASA calls for full funding of special education services, as
current funding falls short, forcing districts to rely on local levies. The Legislature
must increase tiered multipliers and remove the enrollment cap to cover actual
service costs.

. Pupil Transportation: The current transportation funding formula is inadequate and
unpredictable. WASA urges the Legislature to fix the formula to align with actual
costs, making it more transparent and reliable for district budgeting.

. Materials, Supplies, and Operating Costs (MSOC): MSOC funding has not kept up
with rising costs like insurance, utilities, and fuel. WASA seeks increased MSOC
funding to reflect actual district expenses, reducing the need to rely on local levies.

Talking Points for Legislative Representatives

1) Thank you for the help we have received for Special Education funding and
Materials, Supplies and Operating Costs (MSOCs)

2) We are hopeful and doing focused work in our schools and here are a few
examples (Professional Learning Communities, state test scores improving, student
voice, strong sense of belonging, Career and Job ready, etc...)

3) We need your help with the growing "gap" in what we get from the state and
what it costs to do business in today's economy
Fully fund special education
Provide consistent and equitable educational resources (e.g. materials, supplies
and office supplies)
Fix pupil transportation
Update staffing allocation
