Board Meeting
MEETING MINUTES
Thursday, November 14, 2024
Knox and via Zoom
https://osd111.zoom.us/j/88207843182
6:30–8:13 pm
  1. OPENING
1.1 Call to Order and Pledge of Allegiance
President Hilary Seidel called the OSD Board Meeting to order at 6:30 pm.  She led the Pledge of Allegiance. 
  1.2 Board Recognition of Tribal Land
President Seidel read the following statement:
The Olympia School District rests on the ancestral territory of the Squaxin who have stewarded this land throughout the generations. The Board is committed to an ongoing dialog and partnership with Tribal neighbors and we pay our respects to elders both past and present.
  1.3 Roll Call
The following members were in-person and confirmed their attendance:  President Hilary Seidel; Director Jess Tourtellotte-Palumbo; Director Darcy Huffman; Superintendent Patrick Murphy; and Student Representatives Declan Borden, Alijah Manzanares, Vy Le, and Sovana Roberts.
The following members attended via Zoom:  Vice President Scott Clifthorne and Director Maria Flores.
  1.4 Approval of the November 14, 2024 Board Meeting Agenda
The board reviewed and approved as presented.
1.5 Approval of the October 24, 2024 Board Meeting Minutes 
The board reviewed and approved as presented.
1.6 Approval of the November 7, 2024 Work Session Minutes 
The board reviewed and approved as presented.
1.7 Public Comment:  Agenda Items
Each speaker on the public comment list had up to three minutes to speak.
The following person spoke on an item related to the meeting agenda:
  1.8 Superintendent’s Report
Superintendent Murphy thanked Principal Jen Brotherton, Secondary Education Executive Director James Whitehead, and Multilingual & Categorical Programs Director Heather Randolph for trainings attended by principals, teachers, and counselors.  He also thanked Representative Joel McEntire, Representative Travis Couture, Representative Andrew Barkis and Senator Jessica Bateman for meeting with Thurston County superintendents to discuss the needs of school districts before legislative session.  Superintendent Murphy reminded attendees that the district’s legislative priorities can be found on OSD’s website.  He thanked parent group leaders who attended last night’s meeting; he thanked Communications & Community Relations Director Conor Schober for arranging the training which was led by Greg Barker from AWSP.  He visited Boston Harbor & Madison Elementary Schools, Capital & Olympia High Schools, and Washington Middle School.  There were wonderful Veterans Day assemblies and he thanked the music teachers for incredible performances at Hansen Elementary School and Thurgood Marshall Middle School.     
Superintendent Murphy shared data submitted to OSPI including the 2024 graduation rate was 94.2%, the increase of students that identify as gender X was up 21.7%, the increase of low-income students was up 5%, and Avanti High School’s on-time graduation rate increased by almost 10%.
1.9 Student Representatives’ Reports
Student Representative Alijah Manzanares shared that the Student-Led Advisory Council met for the first time on Monday, November 4th; all Student Representatives and Director Darcy Huffman attended.  Agenda items included explaining the functions of the school board, explaining the responsibilities of council members, reviewing the schedule for the school year, and discussing mental health, career readiness, extracurricular activities, communication, student voice, and other policies.  The next meeting is on Monday, December 2nd.    
Student Representative Vy Le shared there was a huge turnout at Olympia High School’s Native Heritage Assembly Homeroom.  The handle for the Student Representatives’ Instagram account is “osd.student.board.reps”. 
Student Representative Sovana Roberts shared that Avanti High School started its second session of classes. 
Student Representative Declan Borden had no comments at this time. 
2. CONSENT AGENDA
The basic purpose of the consent agenda is to act upon routine matters expeditiously. Items placed on the consent agenda are determined by the Board President in cooperation with the Superintendent, and are those which are considered common to the operation of the District and normally require no special Board discussion or debate. A Board member, however, may request that any item on the consent agenda be removed and inserted at an appropriate place on the regular agenda.

3. REGULAR AGENDA
3.1 Special Project Budget Direction
President Seidel proposed that the board release $5000, from its Special Projects budget, for the OlyReads program led by the district’s teacher-librarians.  There was no objection to this.  President Seidel explained that the remainder of the funds would go towards the Student-Led Advisory Council. 
4. REGULAR AGENDA
4.1 Board Liaison Partner Report
President Seidel introduced Marc Daily, Executive Director of the Thurston Regional Planning Council, who presented. 
5. CLOSING
5.1 Board Member Comments
Student Representative Sovana Roberts shared that the Student Representatives are excited to attend & learn from the WSSDA Annual Conference next week.
Director Maria Flores encouraged people to get involved during the legislative session as there may be changes at the federal level that will impact school funding.
Vice President Scott Clifthorne thanked NSPRA and Communications & Community Relations Director Conor Schober for the focus groups that engaged with the community.  He thanked the students & staff of Avanti High School for Exhibition Night.  He thanked Superintendent Murphy and Director Tourtellotte-Palumbo for their work on the Safety Citizens Advisory Committee applications.  He shared that he spoke with former Student Representative Christine Zhang who continues to work on transparency & interactive communication between local governments & constituencies.  He encouraged people to house student pages for the week during the legislative session as some students cannot afford housing options.  He spoke about the impact of the national election.      
Director Jess Tourtellotte-Palumbo shared that she attended the Olympia Education Foundation meeting; she thanked Director Schober for also attending and Principal Domenico Spatola-Knoll for hosting the meeting at Madison Elementary School.  She reminded people that the OEF is hosting a Well-Being Series with the first meeting being held virtually on November 19th, 6-7:30 pm.  For those that purchased a wreath from the OEF, pick-up is Sunday, November 24th, 10 am-1 pm.  She encouraged people to work with the Yelm School District and community as they face obstacles in the upcoming legislative session.  She thanked the Facilities department for new signs being posted that encourage owners to keep their dogs on leashes.  She is excited to hear about the focus on safe routes for students as there are crosswalks that need lighting particularly near ORLA.   She acknowledged that legislative priorities are also taking place in higher education.     
Director Darcy Huffman thanked public commenters.  She attended a retirement event for Senator Sam Hunt and thanked him for his advocacy & service.  She and President Seidel have re-started the Legislative Action Network.  Please reach out to them if you want to sign-up to participate.  She thanked the Student-Led Advisory Council for allowing her to participate in their meeting.  She thanked Thurgood Marshall Middle School for a great Veterans Day program; she thanked veterans & their families.          
President Hilary Seidel informed the Board that she & Director Tourtellotte-Palumbo noticed there was an error in a policy that was approved.  They will correct it and bring it back to the Board for First & Second Readings.  She encouraged people who want to discuss the housing needs assessment to contact her.  She thanked Director Schober and his team for the wonderful content they create.  She looks forward to the WSSDA Annual Conference.  She encouraged people to attend a student play and/or performance.  Post-election, she reminded people that the district’s outcomes are for everyone and the focus should be on them and on doing right by each other.        
5.2 Five Minute Recess
