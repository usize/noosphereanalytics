  4.10 Approval of First Amendment to Agreement with The McNaughton Group Regarding Dedication of Land as School Impact Mitigation
  In a 2008 agreement between the district and The McNaughton Group, LLC (TMG), the district purchased approximately 30 acres of land for future schools on the north side of 180th Street S.E., east of Sunset Road.  Payment by the district consisted of $18.1 million in cash and $4.66 million in credits for future impact and mitigation fees that could be used by the developer to offset fees they would otherwise have to pay to the district for the impacts of future housing projects. This agreement included reference to certain properties owned at that time by TMG, properties that TMG-affiliated entities held an interest in, and properties that TMG was interested in possibly acquiring in the future.  TMG has proposed an amendment to the agreement which would provide that the credits are personal property and do not run with lands referenced in the original agreement, clearing title to avoid confusion by future property owners regarding control of these credits. This amendment has been reviewed by district staff and legal counsel and was presented to the board for approval.
  Recommendation:  That the board of directors approve the first amendment to the agreement with The McNaughton Group regarding dedication of land as school impact mitigation as presented. (E:308/11)

7.01 Superintendent's Report
Superintendent Cohn reported that the district's four high school commencements saw approximately 1,100 graduates receive diplomas.  By August, the goal is to reach approximately 1,150.  At a previous board meeting, a board member expressed concern about whether the district is supporting disabled students to participate in graduation ceremonies.  Information was provided to the board recently that in 2009, 49 disabled students participated, 61 in 2010, and several visibly participated this year.  District programs are doing wonderful work in getting students ready, getting them to commencement and supporting everyone to participate in commencement exercises.
  Dr. Cohn discussed just-released high school proficiency exam results.  He reported on the results of the early college math placement exam for which the district has partnered with Everett Community College.  An early college symposium will be held in August.  Seven district schools (Evergreen Middle, Gateway Middle, Silver Firs Elementary, Silver Lake Elementary, Woodside Elementary, Cedar Wood Elementary and Garfield Elementary) received Washington Achievement Awards.  Everett was ranked by the Center for American Progress in the highest category for what they describe as "return on investment" in a comparison of our student achievement to what we spend to help students achieve.  Two modernization projects, Whittier and Jefferson Elementary Schools, were completed this year, and the Monroe and View Ridge Elementary Schools modernizations began.  The district recently received $357,000 in safety net award, the maximum possible for Everett based on our needs capacity, for a total of $817,000 for the year for our safety net grants.  This is a significant accomplishment involving a huge amount of work which requires the submission of legally compliant IEP files.

There was a good turnout for the meetings with the Cascade High School and Jackson High School booster groups.  A variety of ideas, opinions and responses were received.  There was a solid understanding of the change in long-time policy on the part of the district that doing this work will signal.

Within the next couple of board meetings, BoardDocs will have been in use in the district for one year.  Its presence offers lots of access for lots of folks to information they did not have before.  Research continues in looking at options for broadcasting meetings, but that can be fairly expensive and may involve increased staff time.

In noting the passing of former Everett school board member (1973-91), businessman and community leader Earl Dutton, Dr. Cohn lauded Mr. Dutton for his work for kids and as a moving force behind several projects in Everett that have contributed to the growth in the area.  His daughter-in-law is current school board member Kristie Dutton.  Long-time Everett Public Schools teacher Val Dede passed away recently. 9.01 Adoption of the Fiscal Year Budget for 2011-12 (first reading)
In compliance with RCW 28A.505.040, which requires school districts to make their operating budget available for review by July 10 of each year, and RCW 28A.505.050, which requires board adoption of the fiscal year budget for the ensuing year no later than August 31 of each year, this agenda item provided the directors with a presentation, as a first reading, of the fiscal year budget for 2011-12. The presentation provided an overview of the budgets for our five major funds including General, Transportation Vehicle, Capital Projects, Debt Service, and Associated Student Body. (E:310/11)

Attached is a copy of the state required budget report F-195, an Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) web-based software structure, in which districts input their unique budget data into a common format. OSPI has identified a few corrections that need to occur in the next several days which do not alter the integrity of the information presented, but will add some supporting detail. Examples include page GF 1, in which the 2009-10 enrollment does not show in the report, and later in the document where new activity 31, Instructional Professional Development, and activity 32, Instructional Technology, are not listed while their budgeted amounts are included in the total. Also attached is the state revenue report F-203 which defines the state's projection for the district's 2011-12 revenues to be incorporated into, and a companion of, the F-195.

As indicated by the extensive list of prior actions, the general fund budget development has thoroughly engaged the Fiscal Advisory Council (FAC), community, parents, students, and staff to support final development of the Reduced Educational Program (REP), as adopted by the board on April 19, 2011. The REP is the formal action that provides the direction necessary to make adjustments to district instructional and operational programs, as a basis for final development of the general fund budget. The attached F-195 budget incorporates all identified REP reductions approved by the board. It further relies upon utilization of approximately $2 million of general fund reserves (fund balance) carried forward from the 2010-11 fiscal year. Finally, the F-195 includes resources necessary to fulfill all current local commitments to our labor associations. The final F-195 will be available for the formal budget hearing and adoption, scheduled to occur at the July 5, 2011, regular meeting.

Executive Director of Finance and Operations Jeff Moore complimented Budget Analysts Shirley Rochon and Karen Buchmann for their superb work on the budget.  The presentation included information about the budget development process, the Fiscal Advisory Council outcome, the Reduced Educational Program (REP), the 2011-12 General Fund budget and summary, and budgets from other funds. There will not be a reduction-in-force this year.  Discussion followed about funding of classified staff, employee benefits, rental income, EduJobs, and IDEA funding.
  Mr. Moore will gather information for the board defining categories for the Capital Projects Fund (reserve for bond fund proceeds, other reserves, committed to other purposes, assigned to fund purposes, and unreserved).  Mr. Moore will gather information clarifying rental income, and he will provide more detail on capital projects funding.

Vice President Russell stated that it is good to see the wisdom and skill that the district has used in dealing with a REP.  Director Dutton said she has confidence in the budget numbers and looks forward to receiving the supplemental information.  Director Andrews commented that many people are involved in building the budget; it is a meaningful process.  Director Olson asked about notification of the public hearing, and collective bargaining as it relates to adoption of the budget. She said the fact that the budget is being adopted now rather than on August 31 makes the process seem rushed.  Director Dutton said this is a process that has happened this way for many years. Staff know what budgets they will have to deal with in the coming year. It is a solid way of functioning.  Mr. Moore said this board is very engaged in the process. 11.01 Policy 3204, Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying, Proposed Revision (second reading)
The attached proposed revised Policy 3204, Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying, was presented for second reading and adoption.  Proposed revisions to this policy made during the 2010 legislative session are the result of amendments and additions to RCW 28A.300.285, Washington State’s anti-bullying law. The new law is focused on a greater awareness and reporting of harassment, intimidation and bullying (HIB) in the school environment. The focus of the legislation was a directive to the Office of the Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) to work with the Washington State School Directors’ Association (WSSDA) and the Office of the Education Ombudsman (OEO) to enhance existing school district policies and procedures to ensure that greater protections are provided to students. The legislation states that by August 1, 2011, each school district in the state is required to adopt or amend its existing policy and procedure to incorporate the changes to the law.

Vice President Russell moved to adopt Items 11.01 through 11.08.  Director Olson raised a point of order, saying a consent agenda is being created.  Discussion followed.  Director Dutton said she would prefer to go through the policies individually.  The motion failed for lack of a second.

  13. Board-Superintendent Team Comments
13.01 Board-Superintendent Team Comments
In accordance with the regular order of business approved by the board of directors pursuant to Policy 1440, Meeting Conduct and Order of Business, the regular meeting agenda includes time for directors and the superintendent to share information with one another. The agenda item is established for information and discussion only.

Director Andrews spoke of this year's graduation ceremonies.  Each was exciting and unique.
  Director Olson wondered why a resolution was required for the tracks.  She was disappointed that there was no second on her motion to separate the two items in Resolution 1022.  It says that if you want to vote for the tracks, you must vote for the administration building. She stated that it is not proper for Director Russell to sit in on closed sessions about negotiations when his wife is a teacher in the district. Although it is legal for him to assert his right to come into that meeting, it is improper.  Someone's presence can make others uncomfortable and reading facial expressions can make others uncomfortable.  She asked him to voluntarily recuse himself from closed sessions regarding negotiations with the rest of the board.  President Petersen noted that facial expressions made by Director Olson from time to time are a little exasperating.  Discussion followed about whether to segregate the two items as Director Olson prefers.

Director Russell said he received legal advice from WEA and EEA attorneys about his presence during closed sessions regarding negotiations.  They argued for greater latitude but he was happy to abide by the district's legal advice that allows him to be present but not comment and not vote on the contract when it comes before the board. His presence is essential and provides him perspective on key issues of the leadership.  He sat through all the bargaining talks that took place last year.  The graduations were a delight to attend.  His daughter graduated from Everett High School as did several of her friends.  Placing the diploma in the hands of a young man in a motorized wheelchair was an emotional experience.

Director Dutton enjoyed the graduations.  The Sequoia High School graduation has grown each year from about ten students to a stage full of graduates. Each teacher talks about each child which is a longstanding tradition and is so special.  Earl Dutton was a board member who worked many years on behalf of the students in this district.  He regretted not finishing college because he had to work. He had seven children and he was intent on them going to college.  He wanted to give back to community.  His approach was long-term and pragmatic.
  Superintendent Cohn announced that there would be no Friday Report this week. Discussion of the board summer workshop will be on July 5.  He thanked staff for the significant work they did on two of the major items tonight. He reiterated that the community resource center is not a bond project.
