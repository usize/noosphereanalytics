Pomeroy School Board Meeting Review
January 27th, 2022
.
1. Board President Ashley Koller called the board meeting to order at 5:00 pm. All board members were
present. Superintendent Gwinn and High School Principal Miller were also present, along with Naomi
Scoggin, representing the East Washingtonian.
2. Minutes for the December 16th, meeting were approved, as well as the bills and vouchers.
3. ASB officer Chase Caruso reported that they are working on planning some activities during Valentine’s
Day week—dodgeball, locker grams and some other ideas. Winter sports are getting close to post
season.
4. Sherry Johnson presented to the board medical information from the DSM-5 in regards to the medical
definition for gender dysphoria—this information is associated with the Comprehensive Sexual Health
requirements and the Making Healthy Curriculum being reviewed by the school board for adoption.
5. Enrollment for the year is currently at 345, this is a drop in numbers, but still holding strong.
6. Superintendent Gwinn gave an update on the recent COVID guideline changes and challenges. Testing
for student-athletes has been reduced from three times a week to competition days only. PSD continues
to promote the test to stay program for students and staff. The district has experienced some staffing
challenges, but have been able to maintain in-person learning. Mrs. Gwinn also shared with the board
the requirements of distance learning if the district had to switch to that modality. The state has a very
high expectation for DL days, which includes 70% of the day being synchronous teaching (which means
live with students). Gwinn identified this as being a challenge for many of our families. Gwinn’s goal is
to continue to offer in-person learning as long as we can staff classrooms. Currently Dayton and
Waitsburg on offering distance learning.
7. After having great discussion, doing extensive research and conducting a board work session, the board
of directors unanimously voted to adopt the Making Healthy Decisions Curriculum that was created and
will be presented by the CSAP program from QBH.
8. The board approved the surplus of a 2004 ITC 47 passenger state bus.
9. Fred Knebel was approved as the junior high wrestling coach for the 2022 season.
10. The next board meeting is scheduled for Tuesday, February 22nd at 5:00 PM in the high school library.
There will be a staff meet and greet with the school board that same day from 4:00-5:00. Please stop in,
introduce yourself, and chat with the board of directors!
11. Rachel Gwinn gave a brief business manager report. Amy Miller provided a HS principal report stating
that they completed finals and the first trimester, challenges with COVID are ongoing. Principal Rachel
Gwinn reported that she is completing the second round of observations, staff continue to engage in PD
for CCDEI and she is very thankful for our para pros for stepping up and helping cover in all areas, as
we have not yet been able to hire replacements for open positions. Superintendent Gwinn shared fall test
scores for grades 3-12; some were lower than previous years, so we do have some work to do. Gwinn
will be applying for a $75,000 grant that will support staff discussing what a balanced calendar would
look like—the district does not have to implement a balanced calendar, it is just expected to research
discuss and see if it would be a fit for the district. Many schools are beginning to implement this type of
schedule. A balanced calendar is still 180 school days and an average of 1027 hours; however, the days
are spread throughout the 12 months, often reducing summer break from 13 weeks to 6, and
implementing weeklong breaks in various months. The thought is these types of calendars reduce

summer learning loss and allow districts to target the students in most need of academic support. Rachel
will be attending the superintendent’s conference in Chelan in May.
12. An executive session was called for 30 minutes, and then extended for 10 more minutes to discuss junior
high coaching evaluations and staffing concerns. The board approved the evaluations after executive
session. The meeting was adjourned at 6:52 PM.
13. Any forms or handouts at the board meeting may be requested from the district office electronically or
in person. Call 843-1651 or email rgwinn@psd.wednet.edu
