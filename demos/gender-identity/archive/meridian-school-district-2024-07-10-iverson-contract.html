CONTRACT

THIS AGREEMENT (the “Agreement”) is made and entered into this__ 10th day of _July__, 2024,
by and between the Meridian School District No. 505, a Washington quasi-municipal corporation
(the “District’), and Iverson Earthworks LLC., a General Contractor licensed in Washington
(the “Contractor’).

WHEREAS, the District desires to have certain work, services, and/or tasks performed as
set forth below requiring specialized skills and other supportive capabilities; and

WHEREAS, on 21 May, 2024, the District’s Board of Directors adopted a resolution
declaring the existence of an emergency and authorizing the waiver of competitive bid

requirements for this contract; and

WHEREAS, the Contractor represents that the Contractor is qualified and possesses
sufficient skills and the necessary capabilities, including technical and professional expertise,
where required, to perform the work, services, and/or tasks set forth in this Agreement.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the terms, conditions, covenants, and
performance contained herein, the parties hereto agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1. WORK.

The Contractor will perform such work and accomplish such tasks, including obtaining permits and
furnishing all labor, materials, equipment, supplies, and services to construct the MERIIDAN HIGH
SCHOOL LOSS PRESSURE DRAINFIELD REPLACEMENT. The Project includes but is not
limited to a drain field replacement and other miscellaneous items as further shown, described and
indicated in the Contract Documents. All construction survey services will be provided by the
District.

1. INTENT OF PLANS AND SPECIFICATIONS
The plans and specifications shall be considered and used together and anything

appearing as a requirement of either shall be accepted as applicable to both even though
not so stated therein or shown. The Engineer may furnish supplemental plans and
specifications to define more clearly any requirement of the original documents. In case
of any conflict between the listed and supplemental plans and specifications, the latter


shall govern. The Contractor shall not be entitled to extra compensation because of their
compliance with the requirements of such supplemental plans and specifications unless
they contain new requirements involving cost which clearly could not have been
anticipated by an experienced contractor in their examination of the original listed Value
Schedule or could not reasonably be inferred therefrom as requirements of the contract
and then only as provided in the Contract Plans and Specifications.

All specifications and notes appearing on the plans shall have the same force and effect
as though they were repeated herein.

2. EXAMINATION OF CONTRACT DOCUMENTS
The Contractor shall thoroughly examine and be familiar with the contract. The Contractor

has thoroughly examined and is familiar with the Contract Documents. The failure or
neglect of the Contractor to receive or examine any of the Contract Documents shall in no
way relieve them from any obligations with respect to their proposal or to the contract. No
claim for extra or additional compensation will be allowed which is based upon a lack of
knowledge of any contract document, and the District will in no case be responsible for
any loss or for unanticipated cost that may be suffered by the Contractor as a result of
conditions pertaining to the work.

3. EXAMINATION OF SITE AND CONDITIONS
The Contractor has examined the site of the work and ascertained for themself all the

physical conditions in relation thereto. Failure to take this precaution shall not release
them from their obligation as implied by the proposal they submitted nor excuse them from
performing the work in strict accordance with the requirements of the Contract Documents.

9. TIME OF COMPLETION
The Work shall commence within seven days after the District issues the “Notice to

Proceed” provided that the district issues Notice to Proceed no later than July 10, 2024.

Work shall be completed by August 21, 2024.

If said work is not completed within the time specified, the CONTRACTOR agrees to pay

Construction Safety Standards.

Contract Documents shall be deemed advisory only; and the feasibility of such methods,

Contractor under this contract.

18. STANDARD SPECIFICATIONS
Divisions 2 through 9 of the Standard Specifications for Road, Bridge and Municipal

Construction, current edition, as prepared by the Washington State Department of
Transportation and the Washington State Chapter of the American Public Works
Association, referred to hereinafter as "WSDOT Standard Specifications"; the Standard
Plans for Road, Bridge and Municipal Construction, latest edition, as prepared by the
Washington State Department of Transportation and the Washington State Chapter of the
American Public Works Association, referred to hereinafter as "Standard Plans" are


hereby made a part of this Contract. The Contract Plans and Specifications supplement
the Standard Specifications and are hereby made a part of this Contract. The Contract
Plans and Specifications supersede any conflicting provisions of the Standard
Specifications.

Plans and Specifications as part of the functioning systems as shown on the drawings and
as described in these specifications and in accordance with the Standard Specifications

that may have been disturbed by the Work but not specifically identified as to be removed
or demolished, is considered incidental to the Work described in the Special Provisions.

Guidance for Performance, Application, Design, and Operation & Maintenance for
Pressure Distribution Systems, Current Edition (DOH Publication 337-009).

chooses to request the digital file, the Contractor shall be solely responsible for verification
of the accuracy of the data and shall be solely responsible for all use of the survey


information provided by the Engineer. The Engineer makes no warranty regarding the
suitability of the digital information for the Contractor's purposes.

21. INSURANCE

the Contract and has verified the availability of and the undersigned’s eligibility for all required
insurance. The undersigned verifies that the cost for all required insurance has been included
in this proposal.

ARTICLE 2. CONTRACT TIME.
The Contractor shall substantially complete the Work required by August 21, 2024.

ARTICLE 3. LIQUIDATED DAMAGES.

The District and the Contractor recognize that time is of the essence in the Agreement and the
District will suffer financial loss if the Work is not completed within the time, plus any extensions
thereof, allowed in accordance with the Contract Documents. They also recognize the
inconvenience, expense, and difficulties involved in a legal proceeding to prove that the actual loss
is suffered by the District if the work is not completed within the time allowed in the Contract.
Accordingly, the Contractor shall pay the District $100.00 (US) per day for each working day beyond
the Substantial Completion Date that the Contractor achieves substantial completion of the Work
and $100.00 (US) for each working day beyond the Physical Completion Date that the Contractor
achieves physical completion of Work.

ARTICLE 4. CONTRACT PRICE.
The District shall pay the Contractor the amount(s) set forth in the Schedule of Values proposal (in
United States Dollars) for the completion of the work in accordance with the Contract.

ordinances may not have been quoted or referred to in these specifications.

The extensions in the column headed “Total Cost” were made for the sole purpose of
facilitating comparison of the proposals. If there are any discrepancies between the
unit or lump sum price and the extensions, the unity or lump sum price shall govern.
If there are any discrepancies between the written unit or lump sum price and the unit
or lump sum price expressed as a figure, the written unit or lump sum price shall
govern. The Contractor acknowledges that they have filled out all required forms in
their entirety and accepts the sole responsibility for their accuracy and completion.

The foregoing prices shall include all labor, materials, equipment, overhead, profit,
insurance and all other incidental expenses to cover the finished work of the several
kinds called for. If any material item or service required by the plans and specifications
has not been specifically listed in the Schedule of Values above, the same shall be
furnished and placed with the understanding that the full cost to the Owner has been
marked with the prices named above.
