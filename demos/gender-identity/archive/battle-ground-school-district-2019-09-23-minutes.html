  5. Superintendent and Staff Updates
A. Superintendent Report 
Mark Ross reported that Battle Ground Education Foundation received a $10,000 grant donation from the Honorable Frank and Arlene Price Foundation. The money will go to the Family and Community Resource Center for the district's Earn and Learn Program. The Earn and Learn Program is designed to help high school and beyond students that are homeless. In addition, there are eight businesses within the community that will offer the students job opportunities while they still attend school.
  Mr. Ross provided highlights from his visit at Maple Grove Primary School.  Mr. Ross was invited to the school to speak to fourth grade students on his roles and responsibilities as the superintendent.
  B. Human Resources Report 
Shelly Whitten explained that with the addition of the new kindergarten and sixth grade classrooms, there were changes to the allocation of classified staff. In addition, Ms. Whitten announced the district is well under the K-3 class size requirement. The district's K-3 class size ratio is 16.3:1, and the state compliance ratio is 17:1.
  C. Teaching and Learning Update 
Denny Waters reported the directors of teaching and learning have been busy preparing for the Oct. 11 professional development day. Mr. Waters also announced that a guest speaker would be presenting on Professional Learning Communities (PLCs) on that same day.

Mr. Waters introduced Angela Allen, the director of instructional leadership-secondary level. Mr. Waters reported that Ms. Allen has accomplished the task of creating a Common Course Planning Handbook. Ms. Allen announced that traditionally, all five high schools were operating with different course catalogs and course descriptions. She reported courses taught with the same title were using different course descriptions and codes. Ms. Allen stated if the course is not given the correct code, the teacher may not be qualified in that area to teach. The course guide will provide equity for all students in that no matter which school they go to, the courses will transfer.

Denny Waters said the Common Course Planning Handbook was a collaborative effort with the principals, counselors, and teachers.
  D. Finance Report
Chief Financial Officer Meagan Hayden introduced Director of Nutrition Services James Capen of Sodexo, who was going to provide an update on general nutrition and some of Sodexo's summer projects.

Mr. Capen reported that through the summer meal program, over 3,400 meals were served to children. Mr. Capen said that Sodexo partnered with the library and Battle Ground Parks and Recreation to offer reading and craft actives.
  Mr. Capen announced that Sodexo secured a $15,000 grant that was used to purchase new appliances for the kitchen at Laurin Middle School. In addition, a sneeze guard was added to the buffet table at Prairie High School. Decorations and signage at Maple Grove Primary School were also updated to look like the other district's primary schools.

Meagan Hayden reported that the state auditor's office conducted a statewide audit on school lunch times and determined that students should be provided 20 minutes of seated lunch time. She said the federal description of this 20 minutes begins when the last person through the hot lunch line is seated. It is also recommended that schools offer recess time before lunch because students seem to make healthier lunch choices.

  10. New Business
A. Approval of Revision to Policy 2100, Educational Opportunities for Military Children 
Angela Allen presented the revision to Policy 2100, Educational Opportunities for Military Children. Ms. Allen reported the revision permits families of the military to pre-enroll students before they arrive at their new district. In addition, the parents can start the enrollment process through a phone call or email instead of completing the online registration. WSSDA recommended the policy be retitled as Educational Opportunities for Students with a Parent in the Military.
  Motion by Tina Lambert, The Board of Directors approve the revision to Policy 2100, Educational Opportunities for Military Children.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 4-0.
Aye: Monty Anderson, Rob Henrikson, Mark Watrin, Tina Lambert
  B. Approval of Revision to Policy 2418, Waiver of High School Graduation Credits 
Angela Allen presented the revision to Policy 2418, Waiver of High School Graduation Credits. Ms. Allen explained the revision reflects the word "unusual" being removed, and changed to "circumstances may arise that prevent a student from earning all twenty-four credits."  WSSDA also reclassified the policy from discretionary to essential. 

Monty Anderson asked a clarifying question.
  Motion by Rob Henrikson, The Board of Directors approve the revision to Policy 2418, Waiver of High School Graduation Credits.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 4-0.
Aye: Monty Anderson, Rob Henrikson, Mark Watrin, Tina Lambert
  C. Approval of Revision to Policy 3141, Nonresident Students 
Director of Student Services and Security Tom Adams presented the revision to Policy 3141, Nonresident Students. Mr. Adams reported the policy revision includes: language that school districts use the Standard Choice Transfer System in the Education Data System; boundary exceptions can be denied or revoked if it creates a financial hardship on the district; and that boundary exceptions can be denied or revoked if a student repeatedly failed to comply with requirements for participation in an online school. 

Mark Watrin asked a clarifying question.
  Motion by Rob Henrikson, The Board of Directors approve the revision to Policy 3141, Nonresident Students.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 4-0.
Aye: Monty Anderson, Rob Henrikson, Mark Watrin, Tina Lambert
  D. Approval of Revision to Policy 3143, District Notification of Juvenile Offenders
Tom Adams presented the revision to Policy 3143, Notification of Juvenile Offenders. Mr. Adams announced the revision includes language that states, if the district receives the notification of juvenile offenders, they must forward the notification to the building principal. Another minor revision was changing the word "shall" to "will." 
  Motion by Mark Watrin, The Board of Directors approve the revision to Policy 3143, District Notification of Juvenile Offenders.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 4-0.
Aye: Monty Anderson, Rob Henrikson, Mark Watrin, Tina Lambert
  E. Approval of Revision to Policy 3226, Interviews and Interrogations of Students on School Premises 
Tom Adams presented the revision to Policy 3226, Interviews and Interrogations of Students on School Premises. Mr. Adams explained the revision includes language about interviews and interrogations by immigration agents.
  Motion by Tina Lambert, The Board of Directors approve the revision to Policy 3226, Interviews and Interrogations of Students on School Premises.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 4-0.
Aye: Monty Anderson, Rob Henrikson, Mark Watrin, Tina Lambert
