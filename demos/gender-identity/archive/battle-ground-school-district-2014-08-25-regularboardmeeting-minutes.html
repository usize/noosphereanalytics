A. Overview of High School Mathematics Pilots - 2014-

2015 - Paula Koehler-Martin
Meeting :

Aug 25, 2014 - MINUTES - Regular Board Meeting

Category :

6. Communications

Type :

Information

Public Content
Presenter:
Paula Koehler-Martin
Executive Director of Curriculum, Instructional Programs and Professional Development
This fall, Battle Ground high schools will be piloting two new math courses. Each has a different purpose.
The first pilot, Core to College, is designed to prepare students to transition into credit-bearing courses in mathematics without remediation
or additional placement testing when they enter colleges in Washington State. It is collaboration between state higher education and K-12
entities that will improve student college readiness and increase rates of enrollment and graduation using the Common Core State
Standards and assessments that will establish a statewide common definition of college readiness. Washington is one of eleven states
participating in the Core to College project. Core to College is being piloted at both Battle Ground and Prairie high schools.
The second pilot, Modeling With Mathematics, is designed to create a bridge between Geometry and Algebra 2 to support students who
might struggle with that transition. Algebra 2 is a requirement for graduation for Washington high schools. Modeling With Mathematics will
identify gaps in student mathematical understanding which are important for success in Algebra 2, and it will address those gaps. Upon
completion of this course, students should be thoroughly prepared for success in Algebra 2. Modeling With Mathematics is being piloted at
Battle Ground High School.

Legislative Committee
Mavis Nickels serves as the Board's Legislative Representative for the 2014-15 and 2015-16 school
years. Per Policy No. 1210 the Board elects a legislative representative in June of even-numbered years
from board members. The legislative representative serves for two years.
Updates to the Board may be periodic depending on news from the legislature and whether they are in
session.
Audit Committee Report
Audit Committee members are Monty Anderson and Stephanie McClintock. The audit committee is a
board sub-committee that was formed on January 13, 2014 by the Board of Directors.
Per Board Policy No. 1240, the audit committee consists of two members of the Board of Directors. The
committee is charged to:
1. Provide oversight with the external auditor,
2. Review and approve the superintendent contract, timesheets, reimbursements, cell phone bills,
leave buybacks, and other expenditures,
3. Provide communication to other board members if needed,
4. Provide management a copy of the superintendent's contract for legal and compliance review prior
to finalization,
5. Provide management a copy of the superintendent's contract directly upon finalization,
6. Meet monthly, at least.
The audit committee meets the first Friday of every month.

Public Content
Per Policy 2320, student travel requests require board approval if they are overnight trips. Advisors are
required to submit all required paperwork at least two weeks in advance to Linda Gellings, Director of
Business Services and Risk Management. Advisors must clear the trip with their building principal prior to
submitting the request to Risk Management. Advisors are required to submit itineraries, budget
information, transportation information and learning objectives along with the dates of travel and
information on the group of students traveling with chaperones.
Exceptions to the rule are travel requests for state and national tournaments and competitions. The
procedures for these types of trips is outlined in Procedure 2320P. Often the tournaments come up
quickly and there is not enough time to present the trip to the board prior to the trip. In this case, advisors
must submit all paperwork to the Risk Management Officer prior to the trip for approval. The trip is then
ratified by board members at their next regularly scheduled board meeting.
Student travel requests are attached.

Districts are now required by law as of June 12, 2014 to grant two unpaid holidays per calendar year to employees for reason of
faith or conscience or for an organized activity conducted under the auspices of a religious denomination, church or religious
organization, unless the employees absence would cause the district an undue hardship.

back to the Board for adoption at the August 25, 2014 meeting.

The Board of Directors approve Resolution J-14 Surplus
Property and authorizes the Assistant Superintendent of
Finance and School Operations to advertise a public
hearing regarding the proposal to dispose of surplus school
district property.

Public Content
Presenter:
MaryBeth Lynn, Assistant Superintendent of Finance and School Operations
RCW 28A.335.120 provides the authority to declare real property owned by the school district as surplus
if the property is no longer needed for school purposes and will not be needed for school purposes in the
foreseeable purposes. The property as described in Resolution J-14 is no longer needed for school
purposes and will not be needed for school purposes in the foreseeable future.
RCW 28A.335.120 requires the District to advertise a special hearing in a local newspaper once each
week for two consecutive weeks. The notice shall describe the property to be sold and designate the
place where and the day and hour when a hearing will be held. Public is allowed to admit evidence
offered for and against the propriety and advisability of the proposed sale.
After the hearing, if the Board determines that it is appropriate and advisable to sell the surplus real
property, the District will publish a Notice of Sale in a newspaper of general circulation within the district
that the surplus property is for sale. The District shall not sell the property for at least forty-five days
following the publication of the newspaper notice. Once 45 days have passed from the date of publication
of the Notice of Sale, the District may enter into a binding purchase and sale agreement with a buyer.
The District property cannot be sold for less than 90% of its appraised fair market value.
