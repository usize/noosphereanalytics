MASTER PROFESSIONAL SERVICES AGREEMENT
This Master Professional Services Agreement (“Agreement”) effective May 1, 2024, is by and between
OAC SERVICES, INC., a Washington corporation (hereinafter referred to as “OAC”) and BREMERTON
SCHOOL DISTRICT, a Washington School District (hereinafter referred to as “Client’) whose address is
134 Marion Ave N, Bremerton, WA 98312 for the Project identified below. OAC and Client are each a
“Party,” and together they constitute the “Parties.”

This Agreement is incomplete without the following attachments:
¥ Exhibit A (GENERAL CONDITIONS)
¥ Exhibit B (SAMPLE SERVICE DIRECTIVE)
¥ Exhibit C (REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES)

By signing below, Client acknowledges receipt of the foregoing attachments and their incorporation herein
as if fully set forth. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties respecting its
subject matter and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous commitments, negotiations,
representations, or agreements, whether written or oral, concerning its subject matter.

A. PROJECT:

Project Title: Bremerton SD Bond Program

Project No. R14-240082.00

Project Address: 134 Marion Ave N, Bremerton, WA 98312

Project Description: Support the initial Bond Program startup, prepare for the various projects
identified in the Bond Program, and manage projects as more specifically described in each Service
Directive.

B. SCOPE: On the terms set forth in the attached Exhibit A, OAC shall perform the services set forth
in each Service Directive mutually executed by the Parties describing the scope (the “Services”).
Each Service Directive shall be in substantially the same form as attached hereto as Exhibit B. The
Parties will work collaboratively to establish a schedule for performance following execution of each
Service Directive.

C. COMPENSATION: Client will pay for such Services at OAC’s then-current hourly rates, except as
otherwise specifically set forth in the Service Directive. If the Service Directive indicates that
compensation is on a Fixed Fee basis, the amount shall be paid in equal installments over the course
of the Term, except as otherwise defined in the Service Directive.

D. TERM: This term of this Agreement shall extend not more than Thirty-Six (36) months after the
Effective Date (the “Term’), unless terminated sooner as provided herein. Notwithstanding the
expiration of the Term, any Service Directive effective prior to the end of the Term shall remain in
effect until the completion of Services described therein, unless earlier terminated by the written
agreement of the Parties. The Parties may mutually agree to extend the Term by executing an
amendment to this Agreement.

Each individual executing this Agreement on behalf of a Party warrants that such person is authorized to
bind such Party to this Agreement and its terms.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Parties have made and executed this Agreement effective as set forth
above.

1. AGREEMENT
These GENERAL CONDITIONS are incorporated into and made part of the Agreement between OAC and
Client for the projects identified in the Service Directive and, together with the exhibits incorporated on the
first page hereof and any subsequently executed Service Directive, are collectively referred to herein as
“this Agreement.” The titles and headings of this Agreement are nonbinding.

2. EXECUTION
The Parties agree that a photocopy or other signed copy of this Agreement is as effective as the original.
The Parties also agree that this Agreement may be signed in counterparts. The Parties further agree that,
for the purposes of executing this Agreement and any amendments thereto, either Party, or both Parties,
may execute using an electronic signature authenticated using OAC’s then current electronic signature
systems and procedures. Both Parties intend that such electronic signatures shall be considered original
signatures and binding for all purposes.

3. PAYMENT

OAC will bill for performance of the Services monthly. Monthly invoices will be itemized to reflect the work
performed, including the personnel who performed the Services, when the Services were performed, the
hours worked, a summary of the tasks performed, and the respective hourly rates of the personnel who
performed the Services, as well as the nature and amount of any reimbursable expenses and the dates the
expenses were incurred. Client will pay all properly invoiced charges net 30 days. OAC will charge Client
interest on all past due balances at a rate of 1.0% per month, or the maximum rate allowed by law
(whichever is less), except that interest shall not accrue on amounts which the Client has promptly notified
OAC are subject to a reasonable dispute. To the extent OAC is required to respond to a subpoena or other
legal proceedings to which OAC is not a named party, and which directly relates to the Project for which
Client retained OAC’s services, OAC will invoice Client at its typical hourly rates. Client agrees to raise any
concerns or disputes about invoiced amounts or services to OAC in writing before the payment due date,
and OAC agrees to work with Client to address any such concerns.

OAC may coniract with subconsultants in performing the Services with Client’s written consent to such
subconsultants, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed. Subconsultant invoices
will be marked up at cost plus 10%. Any subcontracting of any of the Services shall not relieve OAC from
its responsibility for the performance of the Services in accordance with the terms of this Agreement nor
from its responsibility for the performance of any of its other obligations under this Agreement.

OAC will bill all reimbursable expenses with a markup of 12% and as further provided in Exhibit C, which
is made part of these General Conditions by this reference.

4. CHANGED CONDITIONS

This Agreement may be supplemented, amended, or revised only by way of an instrument signed by both
Parties. Should conditions affect OAC’s level of effort and/or time required change, OAC will notify Client
and will, as appropriate, propose an equitable adjustment to fee and/or schedule; however, no adjustment
to the scope of Services, time for completion of the Services, nor costs to the Client under this Agreement
will be modified absent written consent of the authorized representatives of both Parties. Should either
Party become aware of facts or circumstances which would impact OAC’s performance, such Party will
promptly notify the other in writing.

5. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR
OAC is and will be at all times during the term of this Agreement an independent contractor and not an
employee, joint venture partner, fiduciary, or associate of Client. In performing its Services, OAC is entitled
to rely on the accuracy and completeness of information supplied by or through Client, and the Client is
entitled to rely on the accuracy and completeness of information supplied by or through OAC.

OAC endeavors to provide continuity of services. Therefore, to the fullest extent permitted by law, Client
agrees not to invite, encourage, or otherwise solicit OAC employees to leave OAC’s employ during the term
of this Agreement and for a period of one-year thereafter. If an OAC employee voluntarily seeks
employment with Client, such employment shall not be a breach of this provision.

7. TERMINATION

a) Termination without cause - Client may terminate this Agreement without cause and without
liability upon seven (7) days’ written notice to OAC of its intent to terminate without cause. OAC may also
terminate this Agreement without cause, but OAC must give Client sixty (60) days’ written notice so that
Client has time to procure replacement services. The Parties will deal with each other in good faith during
the applicable time period following notice of termination. The Client will compensate OAC for services
properly performed and reimbursable expenses properly incurred through the date of termination upon
receipt of OAC’s invoice.

b) Termination with cause - Either Party may terminate this agreement for cause effective upon
giving fourteen (14) days’ advance written notice of the basis for termination for cause and opportunity to
cure. Substantial breach of a material term of this Agreement, including failure to pay a progress payment
when due that is not subject to a reasonable dispute, constitutes cause.

c) Suspension of project — To the extent the Client suspends OAC’s services under this Agreement
for a period of more than sixty (60) days, Client shall compensate OAC for the services rendered prior to
the suspension and, upon resumption or termination of OAC’s services, Client shall compensate OAC for
the costs incurred associated with the suspension and resumption. OAC endeavors to minimize such costs
and therefore may, without liability and at its sole discretion, reallocate its resources including employees
assigned to the Project to minimize the losses associated with the suspension. Client shall not be liable for
any costs incurred by OAC during such suspension unless Client directs OAC to perform Services during
such suspension.

10. SITE VISITS/OBSERVATIONS/SAFETY

If the Services include construction observation, OAC will visit the project site at appropriate intervals to
become generally familiar with the progress and quality of the construction work being performed by the
Client’s general contractor (the “Contractor’) and to determine if the construction work is proceeding in
general accordance with the Contract Documents. OAC is not responsible for construction means,
methods, techniques, sequences, or procedures or for safety programs or precautions in connection with
the Contractor’s construction work. OAC will endeavor to keep Client informed. OAC is not responsible for
the Contractor’s or any third party’s acts, errors, or omissions or failure to perform the construction work in
accordance with the Contract Documents.

If the Services include construction management, Client acknowledges that OAC is not the controlling
contractor and that overall control of the work site and site safety practices rests with the Contractor. If
expressly included as part of its Services, OAC will review safety programs developed by the Contractor
for purposes of coordinating its safety program with those of the others performing work on the Project.
OAC’s review and coordination shall not create liability for nor extend to OAC control over or charge of the
acts or omissions of the Contractor, subcontractors, or agents or employees of them, or of any other
persons performing portions of work. OAC will remain responsible for its employees on the jobsite.

If the Services include evaluations or constructability reviews of design documents prepared by others,
OAC’s review is in its capacity as Client's adviser and not as a design professional. Such reviews shall not
include services which constitute the practice of architecture or engineering and do not relieve the authoring
design professional of its responsibilities for delivering a complete and accurate design.

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Any claim or dispute between the Client and OAC arising under or related to this Agreement (including,
without limitation, the enforceability of this Agreement) that is not resolved by direct discussions is subject
to mediation as a condition precedent to litigation or arbitration. Unless the Parties subsequently agree
otherwise in writing, the mediation will be conducted by the American Arbitration Association acting under
its Construction Industry Mediation Rules and occur in the City of Bremerton, Washington. In the event that
the parties do not resolve a dispute by mediation, either party may bring an action to interpret or enforce
this Agreement in Kitsap County Superior Court, which will be the venue for and have jurisdiction over such
claims. Any claim or cause of action between the Parties arising under or related to this Agreement shall
be forever barred if it has not been filed with the court within the applicable statutory period or six (6) years
of the date on which OAC last performs Services under this Agreement, whichever is shorter.

Neither Party shall not be liable to the other Party for any delays resulting from circumstances or causes

If any provision stated in this Agreement is held unenforceable, all remaining provisions will continue in full

performance or the earlier termination of this Agreement.

17. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

The Parties agree to limit the aggregate amount of any damages and/or costs (including attorney fees and
expert witness fees) that either Party may recover against the other Party (together with its owners, officers,

available, at the time the damages and/or costs are paid, under the liable Party’s insurance policy or policies
applicable to the claim being made by the damaged Party. The types of claims to which this limitation
applies include, but are not limited to, claims based on negligence, professional errors or omissions,
professional malpractice, indemnity or contribution, breach of contract, breach of expressed or implied
warranty and strict liability.
