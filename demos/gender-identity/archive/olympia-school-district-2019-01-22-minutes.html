  Regular Board of Directors Meeting
Tuesday, January 22, 2019
  OPENING
  1.1 Call to Order & Pledge of Allegiance
President Wilhelm called the meeting to order at 6:30 PM. The Pledge of Allegiance was led by the LP Brown student council.
  1.2 Roll Call
President Wilhelm asked for a call of the roll. President Joellen Wilhelm, Vice President Scott Clifthorne, Directors Hilary Seidel and Frank Wilson, Student Representative Grant Erickson and Superintendent Patrick Murphy responded as present. Director Leslie Huff joined the meeting in progress. Student Representative Anna McClatchey was absent with notice.
  1.3 Approval of Agenda
The agenda was unanimously approved.
  1.4 School Board Appreciation
Superintendent Murphy read Governor Inslee's Proclamation declaring January as School Board Appreciation month. Communications and Community Relations Director Susan Gifford presented the Board members with a token of appreciation, a set of note cards created using art work from students.
  1.5 Focus on LP Brown Elementary School
Principal Charleen Hayes welcomed the Board and introduced LP Brown staff. She provided a brief history of the school, reviewed student demographics, highlighted student performance data, and talked about school challenges, and the structures they've put in place to address those challenges.
  1.6 Approval of Minutes - November 19, 2018
  1.7 Approval of Minutes - January 7, 2019
  1.8 Approval of Minutes - January 14, 2019
  Director Seidel moved to approve the minutes listed above; Director Wilson seconded. Board, five ayes; Student Representative Erickson, aye; motion carried. 
  1.9 Community Comment
Megan Green, teacher and LP Brown parent, in spite of our challenges we are a really great school with really good kids.
Chelsea Buchanan, Lincoln Elementary School and Washington Middle School parent, commented on the dress code, and said she looks forward to the work of the calendar/start times committee; she said she appreciates the legislative platform, noting that Lincoln parents are more concerned about the potential for budget cuts and how parent groups can help.
  1.10 Student Representatives' Reports
SR Erickson said school is going well, the CHS basketball team is doing well, and he reported that prom is being planned.
  1.11 Superintendent's Report
Superintendent Murphy acknowledged that we at a midpoint in the school year and looking forward to moving into the second half. He commented that there is work going on to point out that the legislative calendar doesn't match well with the school calendar.
    CONSENT AGENDA (see the regular agenda for attached reports)
The basic purpose of the consent agenda is to act upon routine matters in an expeditious manner. Items placed on the consent agenda are determined by the Board President in cooperation with the Superintendent, and are those which are considered common to the operation of the District and normally require no special Board discussion or debate. A Board member, however, may request that any item on the consent agenda be removed and inserted at an appropriate place on the regular agenda.
  Director Seidel moved to approve the Consent Agenda as presented; Director Wilson seconded. Board, five ayes; Student Representative, aye; motion carried
  2.1 2018-2019 Regular & Special Board Meetings
  2.2 Personnel Reports
The Board approved the Certificated and Classified Personnel reports of January 22, 2019.
  2.3 Instructional Materials Committee
The Board approved the IMC request as presented.
  2.4 Field Trip Requests
The administration recommends approval of the following field trip:
CHS - Vancouver, WA; WIAA Cheerleading State Championships; 14 students, two coaches; February 1-3, 2019.
OHS - Yakima, WA; WIAA Drill Team State Championship; 17 students, two staff coaches, one volunteer coach; March 22-24, 2019 (qualification pending).
2.5 Change Order - Roosevelt ES Modernization
The Board accepted Schwiesow Construction Change Order No. 14 to Roosevelt Elementary School Modernization Project (Bid No. 2018-CP2) in order to construct a preschool playground at the northwest corner of the school. The playground will be built with age-appropriate play equipment and will include rubberized safety tile, and will be surrounded by a hard surface striped pathway to encourage tricycle movement. The playground will be protected by a four foot chain link fence. The cost of the change order is $142,295.00, excluding tax.
  2.6 Budget Status Reports - December 2018
The Board approved the budget status reports for the General Fund, Capital Projects, Debt Service Fund, Associated Student Body Fund and the Transportation Vehicle Fund for the month of December 2018.
    REGULAR AGENDA
3.1 Policy 2418, Waiver of High School Graduation Credits - 2nd reading
Superintendent Murphy clarified that this policy does not lower the bar with regard to core academics, but will allow a waiver of elective credits.
  Director Wilson moved to approve Policy 2418 as presented; Director Huff seconded. Board, five ayes; Student Representative Erickson, aye; motion carried.
  3.2 Policy 3224 - Student Dress Code - 2nd reading
Superintendent Murphy noted appreciation of the work of Director Huff and the committee she facilitated. Through the survey process used, some commented it was too permissive, while others felt it was too restrictive.

Director Wilson moved to approve Policy 3224 as presented. Director Seidel seconded.

Discussion ensued about hats and hoodies from the standpoint of students and of administrators, about the gender bias aspects of dress codes, and appreciation for the work of the committee. And it was noted that bottom line is connecting with our students.

To the motion: Board, five ayes; Student Representative Erickson, aye; motion carried.
  3.3 Policy 5251, Conflicts of Interest
Assistant Superintendent Jennifer Priddy presented a policy change consistent with federal guidelines for organizations that expend federal funds. Director Wilson suggested that this be placed on the Consent Agenda for approval at the next Board meeting.
  3.4 Resolution 591, Legislative Platform
Superintendent Murphy reiterated how critical the levy authority is for the Olympia School District. He reviewed the various proposals currently being offered and how they will impact the district.

Director Wilson moved to adopt Resolution 591 as presented. Director Clifthorne seconded. Board, five ayes; Student Representative Erickson, aye; motion carried.
  3.5 Create Board Advisory Committee to Review the School Year Calendar
Director Clifthorne reviewed some of the concerns about student needs in terms of start times and how challenging it would be to change the school year calendar in a meaningful way. Many factors come into play with regard to long summer breaks and potential summer learning loss. The discussion involved the need for inclusion of all stakeholders in the process, and noted that this process would take a long time in order to be as inclusive as possible, and adding enrichment advisors to the committee.

Director Seidel moved to approve the creation of an Advisory Committee to review start times and the school year calendar. Director Wilson seconded. Board, five ayes; Student Representative Erickson, aye; motion carried.

  CLOSING
4.1 Board Member Comments
Director Wilson thanked Ernie Rascon and his staff at Hansen Elementary School, and Marcela Abadi and her staff at Lincoln Elementary School, for recent visits with them. He met with OHS's Community Council and attended the Pack the Gym right after. He said he was surprised by how much money the district loses as a result of Running Start students. 
Director Seidel said she enjoyed attending STEAM night at Boston Harbor Elementary, and the design meeting for the district office, and offered thanks to the LP Brown families in attendance.
Director Huff said her conversations with LP Brown families have been very positive with regard to academic experience. She thanked Jill Johnson at Capital High School and her English Learners class and was so moved by the students and their incredible life experiences and spirit. She said she is getting lots of inquiries from incoming kindergarten parents as to where to find the information they need for registration.
President Wilhelm thanked the high schools and CTE for the DECA competition they sponsored. She commented on the meeting with the Olympia City Council and talking with them further about housing for our homeless students and on benefits that may be available from the Cultural Access Tax She thanked the Board for all the time and energy they put into their work on the Board, and put in a plug for people to file for the vacant Board positions coming up.
  4.2 Executive Session
At approximately 8:25 p.m. the Board recessed into Executive Session for a period of 40 minutes in order to conduct the Superintendent's mid-year evaluation. The Board returned from Executive Session at 9:00 p.m. with no action taken.
  4.3 Adjourn
The meeting was adjourned at 9:00 p.m.
