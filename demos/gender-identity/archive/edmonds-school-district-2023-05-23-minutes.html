  12. PUBLIC COMMENTS-7:30 pm
  Information: 12.1 Audience Comments
  Kolleen Seals, ESD employee and parent, shared concerns about the reduction of supervision and security positions at Meadowdale High School next year resulting from budget cuts.

Janis Hewitt, ESD employee, shared concerns about safety at Meadowdale High School. She requested additional support from the district.

Corinne Daycross, ESD employee, spoke about the important roll of the security and supervision staff at Meadowdale High School and shared the work that they do.

Aaron Claar, ESD employee, encouraged the board to fund and hire supervision and security staff for Meadowdale High School at the start of the year instead of waiting to do so reactively.

Amy Stevenson, ESD employee, spoke about the importance of music education and encouraged the board to reinstate the positions reduced due to budget cuts.

Cassie Garrett, ESD student, spoke about how much she enjoys participating in music programs and encouraged the board not to reduce the number of music classes offered.

Timothy Castle, ESD student, spoke about the importance of the music classes he has taken and encouraged the board not to reduce the number of music classes offered next year.

Justine Locke, ESD employee, spoke about equity and how the budget cuts will impact the students at Lynnwood High School.

Joel Steinke, ESD alumni, spoke against budget cuts that will affect music programs in our schools.

Tony Shapiro, community member, spoke against gender neutral bathrooms in schools.

Christy Robertson, community member, spoke about respect for traditional family values.

Jenny Mao, ESD parent, spoke about how cutting the number of music classes will negatively impact students.

Scarlett Luo, ESD student, spoke about racism faced by Asian-American students.

Jake Bergevin, ESD employee, spoke about the benefits of the music programs in our schools and how music education has benefitted students in college and in their careers.

Sarah Dilling, ESD parent, encouraged the board not to allow comments that are discriminatory and bigoted.

Larissa Pigott, ESD parent, requested more feedback from the board about the budget cuts affecting the music course offerings.

Rochelle Hume, ESD parent, spoke against budget cuts that impact students. She noted that music classes are beneficial for student mental health.

  18. BOARD MEMBER COMMENTS
  Information: 18.1 Board Member Comments
  Roshni Gill, student advisor to the board, spoke about her experiences in music classes at Edmonds-Woodway High School and agreed that student safety should be a top priority for the board.

Ohanna Jimenez, student advisor to the board, advocated for the rights of transgender students in our schools and the rights of these students to use the bathroom of their choice.

Elizabeth Lopez, student advisor to the board, shared a few words from Peter Garcia (student advisor from Lynnwood High who had to leave the meeting early) and how he was grateful for participating on the board. She shared some information about the important work being done by the security team at Meadowdale High School and how they made a difference for students. She thanked the board for allowing her to share her voice as a board advisor.

Director Smith noted that he walked out earlier because he felt some of the public comments were hateful towards transgender students. He encouraged those commenting to remain civil and not target any specific group.

Director Noble thanked student advisor Ohanna for their comments and for speaking up. He thanked Edmonds Heights for their presentation.

Director Chase thanked Ohanna for their comments. She thanked all the students who were highlighted during the celebrations and presentations. She suggested some changes to the public comment script that we read as a board, as suggested by WSSDA that encourage civility.

Director Kilgore thanked Ohanna for their bravery in speaking up. She spoke about the diffculty of making any budget cuts and encouraged those who make public comments not to cross lines and pick on individual groups. She encouraged everyone to remember that we are all here working together.

Director Katims thanked everyone for their comments. She thanked the mayor's challege students and talked about the benefits of programs like this that provide students with real life experiences. She shared that she is personally meeting with state legislators and learning more about the budget and what we can do to help reduce our budget deficit. She thanked the student advisors for their contributions to the board this year.

Dr. Miner thanked our student advisors for sharing their perspectives. She shared that she has been meeting with teachers whose positions have been reduced this week and that they all demonstrate great passion for teaching and for students. She has been honored to meet with all of them.
