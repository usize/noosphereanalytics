  Board Linkages:

  LINKAGE
Highlights from the Superintendent: 
She is working with staff to finalize budget decisions this week;
Schools and departments have submitted requests for one-time only expenditures; i.e. technology equipment, fencing for Manchester playground, carpeting, etc.;
All administrative hiring is now complete! New staff will be introduced at the August 6 board meeting;
The CTE West Sound Tech Summer School is up and running with approximately 80 students attending;
Tom O’Brien updated the Board on summer facility projects.
  Recommendations of the District Assessment Committee:
Marcus Whitman Principal, Brian Carlson, presented the Assessment Committee’s Final Report.
The Report can be found on the July 15, 2015 Board Agenda.
      Superintendent’s Recommendation Regarding Grade Re-Configuration:
Dr. Reid presented her formal recommendation, rationale, and timeline for grade re-configuration.
The Recommendation can be found on the July 15, 2015 Board Agenda.
  The Board raised the following questions, concerns, and comments:
Why include Hidden Creek rather than Burley Glenwood in the Cedar Heights’ feeder group?
The recommendation allows choice for families; they can choose to stay K-6 and 7-9, or they can open enroll to another school;
Where would Explorer go?  Looking at Madrona Heights and  exploring the option of leasing classroom space from local churches;
If Explorer becomes SKHS classroom space, it would be quite a distance to walk;
What consideration has been given to the social and emotional needs of 9th grade students?  We are looking at different options and the committees will address these needs in their work next year;
Consider adding a portable at Hidden Creek to avoid switching Cedar Heights’ feeder groups.
  The audience raised the following questions, concerns, and comments:
Concern about the prospect of asking voters to approve a bond for a new high school shortly after sharing the grade re-configuration plan; will it look like the district is purposely over-crowding the high school in order to pass a bond?
Concern about moving the Burley Glenwood feeder group from Cedar Heights.

  INFORMATION/DISCUSSION
Legislative Report: Rebecca shared that the legislature approved a two-year state budget which included an expenditure of $1.3 billion in the K-12 education system; Dr. Reid will provide the pivot table for South Kitsap; Rebecca also shared that reauthorization of No Child Left Behind is currently being addressed in D.C.
  Board Discussion Topics:
Rebecca shared that the Elton Goodwin Foundation is sponsoring the first annual “Grand Slam Community Picnic” on Saturday, July 25th at the high school; she will be with her son next week at the high school when he is interviewed by Boeing;
Greg commented on the new logo design options;
Chris shared that the District will consider how to use increased funding from the state, perhaps to fund technology and/or decrease levy requests; Dr. Reid shared that even though the state may increase funding, it is often combined with additional directives on how it can be spent.  In addition, the District is responsible for approximately $1.4 million of the COLA increases and additional retirement contribution obligations.
