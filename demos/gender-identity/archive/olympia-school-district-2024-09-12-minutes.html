Board Meeting
MEETING MINUTES
Thursday, September 12, 2024
Knox and via Zoom
https://osd111.zoom.us/j/81188493995
6:32–8:17 pm
  1. OPENING
1.1 Call to Order and Pledge of Allegiance
President Hilary Seidel called the OSD Board Meeting to order at 6:32 pm.  She led the Pledge of Allegiance. 
  1.2 Board Recognition of Tribal Land
President Seidel read the following statement:
The Olympia School District rests on the ancestral territory of the Squaxin who have stewarded this land throughout the generations. The Board is committed to an ongoing dialog and partnership with Tribal neighbors and we pay our respects to elders both past and present.
  1.3 Roll Call
The following members were in-person and confirmed their attendance:  President Hilary Seidel, Vice President Scott Clifthorne, Director Maria Flores, Director Jess Tourtellotte-Palumbo, Superintendent Patrick Murphy, and Student Representatives Declan Borden, Vy Le, and Sovana Roberts.
The following members were in attendance via Zoom:  Director Darcy Huffman.
The following member was absent:  Student Representative Kellan Eggleston.    
  1.4 Approval of the September 12, 2024 Board Meeting Agenda
The board reviewed and approved as presented.
1.5 Approval of the July 11, 2024 Board Meeting Minutes 
The board reviewed and approved as presented.
1.6 Approval of the August 10, 2024 Board Summer Retreat Minutes 
The board reviewed and approved, with one nay, as presented.
1.7 Approval of the August 22, 2024 Board Meeting Minutes 
The board reviewed and approved, with one abstention, as presented.
1.8 Approval of the September 5, 2024 Work Session Minutes 
The board reviewed and approved, with one abstention, as presented.
1.9 Proclamation:  Walk to School Month
Director Tourtellotte-Palumbo read the Walk to School Month Proclamation.  Superintendent Murphy encouraged all to participate in Walk to School Month events.  President Seidel encouraged all to participate in Week Without Driving which is September 29th to October 6th.    
1.10 Public Comment:  Agenda Items
Each speaker on the public comment list had up to three minutes to speak.
The following people spoke on an item related to the meeting agenda:
1.11 Superintendent’s Report
Superintendent Murphy thanked those that gave public comment.  He shared that he & President Seidel rode Bus #31 on the first day of school.  Superintendent Murphy thanked the organizers, supporters, and attendees of the Spaghetti Bowl.  He thanked Amelia Layton of the City of Olympia for working with MaryGrace Goddu on the Inspire Olympia program; he & Director Tourtellotte-Palumbo attended a meeting with them.     
1.12 Student Representatives’ Reports
Student Representative Vy Le shared that Olympia High School community service students assisted with preparing the installation of washers & dryers at Garfield Elementary School.  She shared that there will be an Equity Retreat, hosted at Evergreen State College and open to all students, where culturally responsive teaching & student identities will be discussed.  Student Representative Le shared her experience at the Spaghetti Bowl.      
Student Representative Sovana Roberts did not share comments at this time.    
Student Representative Declan Borden did not share comments at this time. 
2. CONSENT AGENDA
The basic purpose of the consent agenda is to act upon routine matters expeditiously. Items placed on the consent agenda are determined by the Board President in cooperation with the Superintendent, and are those which are considered common to the operation of the District and normally require no special Board discussion or debate. A Board member, however, may request that any item on the consent agenda be removed and inserted at an appropriate place on the regular agenda.

3.1 2024-2025 Enrollment Update/First Day of School

CLOSING
3.1 Board Member Comments
Student Representative Declan Borden spoke about the return of parents on-campus at ORLA and seeing new students.
Student Representative Vy Le provided details about the Equity Retreat she mentioned earlier. 
Student Representative Sovana Roberts spoke about the first day of school.  She shared that there is a large graduating class.     
Director Darcy Huffman apologized for not attending in-person.  She apologized to former director Talauna Reed for her previous use of the n-word.
Director Maria Flores has contacted the Thurston County Dispute Resolution Center about a mediation session for the board; she has also contacted WSSDA for more information about a facilitator conducting a self-assessment.  She also spoke about PTSD.        
Director Jess Tourtellotte-Palumbo attended the Garfield Elementary School’s Family Dinner Night and a meeting regarding Cultural Access in Public Schools.  She reminded all about the Olympia Education Foundation breakfast at 7:30 am on Friday, October 4th.
President Hilary Seidel enjoyed the bus ride on the first day of school.  She thanked Garfield Elementary School for invited her to its Preschool Welcome Back Event.  The Thurston Regional Planning Council discussed the state facilities plan and transportation improvement.  President Seidel thanked Director Flores for sharing her intentions regarding mediation; she shared that she has spoken with each board member about their intentions & preparation for this.
