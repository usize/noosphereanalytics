I.

CALL TO ORDER:
Directors present: Chair Price-Morris, Vice-chair Carrier, Director Murphy, Director O’Connell. Staff
present: Superintendent Smith, Business Manager K.Moore, J.Hammond, S.Jones
Chair Price-Morris called the meeting to order at 6:02 pm.
A quorum was present. Director Taylor has asked for a leave of absence and will be in touch with the
Chair with notice of his return.

II.

PLEDGE OF ALLEGIANCE AND LAND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT:
Vice-Chair Carrier led the flag salute. The chair gave the land acknowledgement.

III.

APPROVAL OF AGENDA:
Vice-Chair Carrier moved to approve the agenda. Change the order of new business items A and B.
Also, Policy “6770” Wellness should be Policy “6701”. The board approved the agenda as adjusted
unanimously.

APPROVAL OF MINUTES:
A. Regular Board Meeting of April 24, 2024
B. Board Work Session of May 8, 2024

minutes unanimously.

VI.

No public comment.
VII.

RECOGNITION AND GRATITUDE:
The board chair recognized the staff and teachers who are retiring and all of their years of experience:
Coach Larry Berg, Transportation Director Teri Linneman and Secondary Math Teacher Kathryn Kester..
Director Murphy recognized all staff who have not received their renewal for contracts and who have
received reduction in force letters. She recognized how this is a hard transition for individuals and the
community and the board thanked them for all of their hard work.

VIII.

FINANCIAL REPORT: Kara Moore, NWESD Business Services Manager
K.Moore presented the enrollment report and monthly financial report slides. She also reviewed
purchasing controls and procedures in place for the district.

IX.

ADMINISTRATIVE REPORT:
A. Principal Report
B. Superintendent Report
Superintendent Smith shared slides for Dr. Martin who was with the 8th graders at Camp Moran
for the LIFRC Since In Time Memorial, Coast Salish Land, Sea and Place trip. An electronic copy
of the slides is in the board files.
Superintendent Smith shared a slide presentation. An electronic copy is in the board files.

X.

UPDATES:
A. Associated Student Body (ASB)
Outgoing ASB Board Representative Ruby Ervin-Mclean introduced the 2024-2025 rep,
Ana-Sophia Rempel. Staff appreciation cards were written. MS dance this Friday. Friday, May
31st will be Track and Golf season end celebration and outdoor activities. Director Murphy
requested that the ASB collaborate with the board in information gathering in setting big level
goals about school. The chair complimented Ruby’s great work and expressed her thanks for her
time with the board.
B. Lopez Education Association (LEA)
President R.Ellis reported that LEA enjoys good collaboration and communication with Brady for
discussions . Teachers met with Martha to collaborate and discuss strategies to improve behavior
issues. They are excited for the graduating seniors. They look forward to hosting the staff end of
the year party with PSE on June 14th,
C. Public School Employees (PSE)
President D.Steinbrueck reported the PSE appreciates Brady for working with them in
negotiations. They are also appreciative of the PSE representative. Welcome Tammy Schibble and
Katie Haines, new PSE staff. They thanked Teri Linneman for over 40 years of work in schools.
She was PSE president for two terms for a total of 11 years.
D. Lobo Pack

Approved 6/20/24
K.Warner, vice-president of the Pack addressed the board. They celebrated Coach Berg last
weekend at the Community Center. They thanked him for all of his support of Pack. The 2nd
Annual Sports Banquet is in planning. They are looking for set up and clean up support for June
5th. The banquet is not just to recognize the student athletes, but also the parents. Pack will be
hosting youth camps this summer as part of their programming and fundraising. They will be
hosting a Varsity Volleyball camp on campus this summer. The Pack raised funds to resurface the
gym floor which hasn’t been resurfaced in the last 8 years. Funds were raised by offering business
advertising space on a banner. The banner will be up for1 year and renewed next year. The upper
baseball field has been getting renovated to be used for a potential girls fast-pitch team. Much of
the renovation is being done by volunteer hours and machine time. They expect to have surplus
funds that can be used for other athletic activities.
XI.

OLD BUSINESS:
A. Water Project
Documents were recorded with San Juan County. DOH comments are being addressed.
Governance is required. Bylaws need to be created, insurance exceptions have been received,
specific coverage for the utility is being explored.
B. Tech and Safety Levy
The explanatory and statement resolution have been submitted to the county. The auditor
addressed calculations based on $0.24 per thousand, but the assessed values of properties have
changed before the ballot. The new rate is $0.21 per thousand. A new explanatory statement will
need to be submitted. The board asked what the timeline is. The timeline was selected because
other levies are not up for approval at the same time. J.Hammond outlined the outreach timeline
and community partners assisting with this.

XII.

NEW BUSINESS:
A. School Calendar Change
A. Resolution 23/24-24 180 Day Waiver
The state requires a resolution to approve the reduction in student contact days from 180 to 176
days. Director oO'connell The board discussed the activities that would take place on those days.
The board asked how this impacts staff work days. Vice-Chair led a roll call vote: RO-yes,
KC-yes, MPM-Yes, SM-Yes. The Resolution was approved unanimously (4/4).
B. Resolution 23/24 - 24 180 Day Waiver
B. School Calendar Change
To accommodate a balance of A & B days, the leadership team requested moving a day off from a
Friday to a Monday. The new calendar will be shared once updated.
C. Policy 1733 - School Board Compensation - First reading
The board discussed maintaining a log of board meetings attended and receiving compensation if
a board director wishes to receive compensation. Director Murphy requested that ‘excess funds’
be removed from the policy. The waiver will be distributed at the next meeting.
D. Policy 6770 6701 Wellness -First reading
The board would like to discuss this and its history at the next work session.
E. Water Utility Board Representative
Director Murphy moved to establish a Water Utility Board Representative and nominated
Chair Price-Morris for the position. No discussion. Vice-Chair led a roll call vote: RO-Yes,

Approved 6/20/24
SM-Yes, KC-Yes, MPM-Yes. Price-Morris was voted as representative unanimously and
accepted the position.
F. Bus Purchase
A quote to purchase a new bus was presented. Discussion: depreciation period for a bus depends
on the model, but typically is 13 years. Director Murphy moved to approve the bus purchase. All
voted in favor.
XIII.

ADJOURNMENT:
Director O’Connell moved to adjourn. The board approved unanimously. Chair Price-Morris
adjourned the meeting at 7:56 pm.
