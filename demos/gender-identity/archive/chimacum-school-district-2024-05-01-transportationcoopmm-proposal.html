PTSD proposal of 25-26 new bell schedule

Reasons for new bell schedule:

- Loading PTHS first in the afternoon allows everyone to sit where they are supposed to sit, filling in will
be natural. As High school students load into the back portion of the bus, BHMS would be next loading
into the middle of the bus giving Salish Coast the front portion, keeping them closer to the driver. An
added benefit will be that Salish Coast is on the bus the shortest amount of time.

When arriving at BHMS, we will have PTHS students exit at BHMS if their stop is within 1.0 mile to
walk, along with having BHMS students who also live within the 1.0 miles walk.

Once we arrive at Salish Coast, we will have even more PTHS & BHMS students unload to walk within
the 1.0 mile from that school along with walkers of Salish Coast.

By having PTHS and BHMS picked up first, it gives our Salish students their older siblings or family

members to walk home with vs Salish students riding the bus around waiting for their older family
members to get out of school and walk home with them from the Salish coast bus stop.


City of Port Townsend has created many walking paths and new bike lanes giving our students a safe
and environmentally friendly way to and from school. Families can take advantage of this opportunity
with ease knowing their younger students have older students to walk with.

With these changes made to the schedule, this will reduce the number of students riding the bus at
the same time. This will decrease the number of bus referrals and behavioral issues that come from
crowded buses.

Salish students will not have to ride the bus for an extra 25 minutes while waiting as we pick up at the
other schools before going on the route to take home.

Behavioral issues can increase when students are on the bus longer than needed.

By giving a direct take home from Salish Coast and getting older students off sooner, this may
increase ridership.

The sports bus can go directly to PTHS and BHMS without being late to Salish every day.
