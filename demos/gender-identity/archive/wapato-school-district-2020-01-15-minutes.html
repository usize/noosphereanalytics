2. Recognition
January is School Board Recognition Month.  Superintendent Kelly Garza recognized the Board for their dedication to students, staff, and parents of Wapato Public Schools and commitment to increase student achievement.  He thanked the Board for all of their efforts to increase student achievement by attending and participating in numerous meetings such as WSSDA Regional meetings, WSSDA annual conference, Legislative visits, and the many school events.  All of this time adds up to 100’s of hours of volunteer time.  Mr. Garza praised the members for keeping the best interest of student staff, parents and community at the forefront of their decisions.
Javier Vela was also celebrated for 10 years of service on the Board.
3. Public Comments
Lorenzo Alvarado: commented he recently attended a parent/community meeting to address a racial comment made by a volunteer. He shared some of the concerns and comments he heard at the meeting.  He said the group was concerned about how impact aid funds are spent in the district and what plan is in place to address cultural diversity issues in schools.  He said parents want to see action that addresses race and insensitivity.
4. PUBLIC HEARING
Overview of Impact Aid: Kelly Garza, Superintendent, presented a review of the 2021 Impact Aid application. He explained the purpose of the application is to reimburse schools for loss of property tax from tribal lands or military lands.  He explained the Impact Aid Program, eligibility determinations, and funding components.  He presented historical data on funds received from Impact Aid and data of students living on trust land by school year.  Mr. Garza said the District will report 139 students with disabilities and 735 other students who reside on Indian land in the 2020 Impact Aid application.
Overview of Indian Policies and Procedures: Superintendent Garza presented the Indian Policies and Procedures for the 2021 Application.  This report is an annual requirement of the Impact Aid application.  Mr. Garza said there were no changes to the presented policies and procedure and they will be reviewed at the upcoming JOM parent meeting this week.  After public review and input the policies and procedure will be presented for action at the next regular board meeting.
Public Comment: Impact Aid and Indian Policies and Procedures
Sharon Goudy had the following comments/concerns:
How are funds collaborated and utilized?
How families are made award to access those programs.
Concern of equal treatment and needs of all Native American students.
Athletics are important to Native American families and are there programs in place to help those student who are not eligible to play access support or help to improve grades.
What is the district’s plan to recruit and retain Native American teachers who can better identify with Native American students?
Superintendent Garza encouraged Mrs. Goudy to attend tomorrow evening’s Indian Education/JOM parent meeting where she would receive responses to her questions.
5. Reports
Superintendent's Report: Kelly Garza, Superintendent, reported:
He appreciated the Board of Directors for their visibility at and support of many PK-12th grade student programs including academic, extra-curricular, and social events.
Classes have resumed after the Winter Break and staff and students are back on track to learning and school activities.  Mr. Garza invited the Board to visit classrooms and PLC’s. This month there will be many school programs to observe Martin Luther King Day and Temperance and Good Citizenship Day.  Mr. Garza invited everyone to attend those events throughout the schools.
WHS has posted the open position for Student Representative to the Board of Directors for a two-year term 2020-2021.  Principal David Blakney will be accepting application until Friday of this week.  The plan is to interview the applicants the following week.  Mr. Garza asked for two volunteers from the Board to participate in the interview panel.  Board members Maria Erickson and John Francisco will join Cameron Mesplie, current student representative, Principal David Blakney and Superintendent Garza for the interviews.
Mike Balmelli, reported the Legislative session is underway.  This is a short session and some items to watch are Homelessness, Gun initiatives, Safety and Transportation and it appears Education is not a priority this session.  Mr. Balmelli commented the session started 3 days ago and the legislators have already dropped 740 bills.  He noted there remain a few bills to watch that address SEBB issues.  He said it may be that the legislators feel that with the passing of the McCleary decision, education funding has been resolved.  He reported the District is planning to visit with Legislators on February 4th in Olympia to advocate for education issues, such as SEBB.
Financial Report: Sylvia Bazan, Director of Fiscal Services, presented the Cash Flow Analysis report for November 2019.  She explained in detail the expenditures and revenues in each fund category: General, ASB, Private Purpose, Capital Projects, Transportation, and Debt Service funds.  She reported the District has had to access the fund balance to meet its monthly financial responsibilities.  She is working with Kevin Hill on a Capital Projects priority list that will come forward for board discussion at a later meeting.  The Board engaged in discussion regarding the fund balance.
WIAA Reclassification of WHS Athletics: David Blakney, WHS Principal and Mark Lagerquist, WHS Athletic Director, reported CWAC and WIAA passed an amendment last year that changes the enrollment count to determine athletic classification for schools.  The amendment now takes into account free and reduced enrollment.  Mr. Lagerquist explained how WIAA has determined the division for WHS, and he presented options for the District in choosing which leagues to play in.  The amendment changes will also impact the State tournament format for sports.  The Board engaged in discussion regarding the distance in traveling to games on school nights and the different league options.
WHS ASB Report: Cameron Mesplie, Student Representatives of the Board, reported students have been working hard to finish the semester in good standing. 
Yakama Nation Tribal Council Liaison Report: Elese Washines, Yakama Nation Tribal Council Liaison, reported there will be an Attendance Summit on January 24, 2020, to review data reports and plan for next steps to improve school attendance for all Native American students.  She said there are a few bills that Tribal Council is keeping a close watch on.  HB2551 that encourages all schools to respect and allow students to wear Native American regalia to school.  SB6262 proposes Tribal History and Culture curriculum in social studies.  They have also presented a senate bill to better identify Native American students with multiple races, and a senate bill that requires schools to collaborate with local tribes in consultation.  The Board engaged in discussion regarding self-identity vs. enrolled in a tribe identity.
