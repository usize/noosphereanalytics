Combined Athletic Agreement
East Jefferson Athletics

Reviewed and Revised Spring 2024

Administration of the Programs

A.

H.

The combined high school athletic programs will be called East Jefferson Rivals hereafter referred to
as the Athletic Combine. The team colors will be red, white, blue, and black. Middle School
Athletics will be combined as needed based on the sport. Middle School sports that are combined
will use the colors of the host school.

The East Jefferson Athletic Director will oversee the high school and middle school programs at
both schools and report to the Principals.

The Athletic Director will communicate with the Superintendents regularly and will present to the
School Boards at their annual joint meeting.

. The Administrators of the Combine are responsible for reviewing and evaluating the Athletic

Director and will provide input on the Directors annual evaluation and each party to this agreement
will have a representative present when an annual evaluation is reviewed. Representatives from both
districts will have monthly meetings to discuss prioritization of the directors duties.

Port Townsend School District will be the fiscal manager of the Athletic Combine.

Terms of the agreement must be approved by the school boards of the schools participating in the
Athletic Combine.

. Members of the Athletic Combine include:

1. Port Townsend High School

2. Chimacum JR/SR High School

3. Blue Heron Middle School

4. Chimacum Elementary (6th graders only)

By April 15th each year the division of costs for the following year will be determined based on the

current year participation percentages for each district rounded to the nearest 5%

Budget Process: By May 15th, the ASBs will approve their respective budgets for the following
year. Throughout the year the principals will collaborate with their respective ASB’s.

Billing Process: Port Townsend will pay all expenses, serving as the vendor to the Chimacum
School District. Port Townsend will bill Chimacum based on the participation percentage from the
previous year. Port Townsend ASB will approve requisitions and invoices at the individual level.
Chimacum ASB will approve invoices at the summary level as presented by Port Townsend.
Passes: Admission prices and the Rivals Annual Pass will be the same at all schools in the
Athletic Combine. The annual pass will also be used to provide free passes to families eligible
for free or reduced priced lunch at the families’ home district. These passes will be sold by both
Districts and will allow entry into all East Jefferson home games. There will be no discount for
bulk purchase of passes. The Districts will honor lifetime passes sold by their respective Booster
Clubs. Each district will keep track of the number of lifetime passes used for entrance and bill the
booster clubs for reimbursement.

Gate Sales: Ticket sales will follow current law and districts will issue tickets accordingly. Each
school will receive all gates and season ticket sales from the games hosted at their site. Port
Townsend will receive all online gate sales and will remit to Chimacum the events hosted at
Chimacum at the end of each season. Port Townsend will receive all gate sales at events hosted at
Memorial Field.

Fees: Each school will keep the revenue from ASB card fees and individual sport fees.
Concessions: Each district will determine how their concession stand operates and how the
money raised from concessions is to be distributed for events held at their site. Port Townsend
will operate concessions for games hosted at Memorial Field unless Chimacum is the host.
Memorial Field: If Chimacum is the venue host at Memorial Field they will operate the gate,
concessions, supervision and all related staff and permits.

Coaches

A. All coaches will be under contract with the Port Townsend School District, except Middle School
coaches hired for sports that are not combined. Those coaches will be hired by the host district.

B. All coaching contracts are non-continuing contracts.

C. Hiring of Coaches:

1. When a coaching vacancy occurs, the opening will be advertised in both Districts. Employees of
each District, including current coaches, have the first opportunity to apply for an opening. It will be
posted internally and externally at the same time. Being a district employee does not, however,
guarantee hiring for coaching vacancies.

2. The position will be advertised outside the districts, ie. in the local paper, WIAA webpage and other
media as appropriate.

3. An interview committee that includes the Athletic Director and possibly the building Principals or
designees will interview candidates for the position of Varsity Head Coach. At the discretion of the
Athletic Director, other individuals may be invited to be part of the interview committee.

4. An interview committee that includes the Athletic Director and Varsity Head coach will interview
candidates for the position of varsity assistant coach. At the discretion of the Athletic Director, other
individuals may be invited to be part of the interview committee.

5. The interview committee will recommend candidates to be hired.

X. Parent/Guardian Complaints

A.
B.

C.

D.

When a parent/guardian has a complaint about a coach, he/she needs to contact the coach first.

If the parent/guardian feels the problem has not been resolved, he/she should contact the head coach
of that sport or the Athletic Director, if the coach in question is not a head coach.

If the parent/guardian still feels the problem has not been resolved, he/she should contact the
Principal of the school in which the athlete attends.
The final step would be to contact the superintendent of the school the athlete attends.

XI. New Programs

A.

If any of the districts involved in the Athletic Combine wish to start a new program or sport they are
to contact the Athletic Director who will inform the administrators of the other districts with the
details of the proposal.

Approval by the Port Townsend and Chimacum school boards is required for starting a new athletic
program. If the school boards of the other schools in the Athletic Combine do not agree with starting
the new program, they do not have to allow their athletes to participate.

XII. Insurance

A.

B.

Each District will maintain insurance coverage for liabilities arising from activities carried out under
this agreement.

Said policies will be maintained at levels no less than the levels of coverage currently maintained by
each District.

XIII. Terms of the Agreement

A.
B.

Cc.

The term of this agreement will begin September 1, 2024, and end August 31, 2028.

By February 1* of each year, both Districts will provide notice of its intent to continue or terminate
its participation in this agreement for the upcoming school year.

By March 1* of each year, a District must notify the other District/s of any proposed changes to this
agreement. These changes would not take effect until at least the beginning of the following school
year.

. This agreement will be reviewed in the spring of each year to determine if revisions are needed.

Those revisions will be completed by May Ist of each year.
Disclaimer: If there is mutual agreement among all schools in the Athletic Combine, terms and
conditions of this agreement can be altered at any time during the agreement year.
