Public Input (Agenda item 5 Written Reports: Staff Safety Reports)
Laura Rarig <lrarig@gmail.com> Thu, Aug 1, 2024 at 9:04 AM
To: bsd-board@bremertonschools.org

Cc: Marlaina Simmons <marlaina.simmons@bremertonschools.org>

Hello,

Please review the safety reports included in today's agenda, specifically the staff injury causes. A majority of the reported
staff injuries list "dysregulated student" or "escalated student" as the reported cause. Staff responding to a "dysregulated"
or "escalated" student need to maintain physical proximity to ensure student safety.These staff injuries span each
category of employment, including classified, certificated and administrative staff.

The current Right Response training is insufficient to protect staff (particularly classified staff) from injuries caused by
student escalation. Protocols need to be developed to address what happens for staff and students after a student
escalation results in injury to anyone. My experience as staff at West Hills STEM Academy last year leads me to believe
that staff injuries are underreported, specifically wnen student behavior is the cause of injury. The staff safety report
demonstrates a clear need for change, to protect the physical and psychological safety of staff responding to student
escalations.

As a parent of a West Hills Griffin, I'm counting on the Bremerton School District to create a protocol for response to staff
injuries caused by students. A culture of belonging includes safety for the adults that we entrust with our children.

Respectfully,

Laura Rarig Pugh

Former BSD staff

Parent of a 1st grader at West Hills STEM Academy
