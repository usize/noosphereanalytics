    C. Finance Report 
Meagan Hayden, Interim Director of Finance, gave the Board an update on district financials and discussed the district's financial health indicator. She presented a slide show and distributed a handout regarding financial indicators, comparing eight years of scores of fund balance revenues. Financial Indicators are:  Fund Balance to Revenue Ratio, Expenditure to Revenue Ratio, Days Cash on Hand, and Weighted Scores. She explained that the board policy 6022 requring a minimum fund balance has helped immensely in correcting the past low fund balance.
  Fund Balance to Revenue Ratio:  Battle Ground's score is 2, with a ratio of 4.86% of the $7,048,259.32 current fund balance. 
  Expenditure to Revenue Ratio:  Battle Ground's score is 4, with a ratio of 97.32%. 

Days Cash on Hand - the state average is 44.5 days, in 2008 Battle Ground averaged 4 days of cash on hand. Currently the district  has an average of 21 days of cash on hand.
  Weighted Scores - The total profile score for 2015-2016 is 2.50. During the financial warning periods the scores ranged from 1.25 to1.65.

Ms. Hayden compared Battle Ground's scores to Vancouver School District (51 days Cash on Hand, Fund Balance to Revenue ratio is over 9) and to Evergreen School District (39 days Cash on Hand, Revenues Fund Balance is 7%). This information can be found on OSPI's web site.

Mr. Hottowe asked to clarify that the 4.86% fund balance is unrestricted. Ms. Hayden explained that 2.75% is designated as minimum fund balance by board policy, the other 2% is for carryovers which are not assigned. The Fund Balance is currently $7 million ( 2.75% uncommitted but restricted). 

Per Policy 6022, the fund balance goal is 4% Mr. Anderson noted it could be revisited,

Ms. Nickels appreciated the hard work from many people to re-build the fund balance.
  Ms. Hayden also reported that her department is in the midst of updating Policy 6700: Nutrition and Physical Fitness and establishing a local wellness policy. She asked if a board member would like to participate on the committee reviewing the policy to send her an email.

      B. Educational Cooperative Agreement between BGPS and Daybreak Youth Services
Denny Waters, Executive Director of Special Services, presented the agreement between Battle Ground Public Schools and Daybreak Youth Services to the Board. He noted that the item was discussed at a board meeting a few months ago. The drug rehabilitation facility is  located in Brush Prairie and will house children aged 12-18. Since the facility is located in the district, we are required by law to provide services. State funding will flow through and be managed by the district  to Daybreak Youth Services. There is an administrative fee of 7% which the district  will retain. 

Students from all over the state will reside in the facility for a seven week period for intensive therapy. It will be the largest facility in the state. There is no preference for Battle Ground students. The facility is anticipated to open in June.
    C. Resolution C-17: Adoption of the Clark County Regional Natural Hazard Mitigation Plan
Scott McDaniel, Director of Technology Services, and Linda Gellings, Director of Business Services and Risk Management, presented the Clark County Regional Natural Hazard Mitigation Plan to the Board for approval. Mr. McDaniel noted the details were presented to board members at a board meeting two weeks ago. The plan is a 700 page long document. He emphasized that it is a separate document from the District Emergency Response Plan, and explained the difference between the two plans.

If the Board approves the plan, the district will become eligible for FEMA grants. There is no cost for the plan. 

Ms. Gellings noted that the Washington Mitigation Officer will be in the area soon to discuss grants with the rest of the county. She noted that it is a win to be part of the county-wide plan. She discussed the purpose of grants is for disaster preparedness in case of any number of disasters that could occur.
    Motion by Mavis Nickels, The Board of Directors Adopt the Clark County Regional Natural Hazard Mitigation Plan Volume 1 and the Introduction, Appendices and Battle Ground Public School’s portion of Volume 2 of the Plan.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 5-0.
Aye: Monty Anderson, Ken Root, Jim Pegoraro, Mavis Nickels, Stephanie McClintock
