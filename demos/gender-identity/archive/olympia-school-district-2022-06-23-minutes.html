  1.6 Board Announcement
At the June 9 Board Meeting, Vice-President McKaughan announced that he will be leaving his position as Director for District 2 and will be stepping down as Vice-President on June 22, 2022 but will continue as Director of District 2 through August 31st.
  The Board accepted Vice-President’s McKaughan’s resignation and his wish to continue as Director until the end of August.  The Board will follow district Policy and Procedure 1115 to fill the vacancy.  Per policy, if there is a board vacancy, the remaining Board members shall fill such vacancy by appointment. The Board will receive applications from any qualified persons seeking to fill the position after suitable public notice. The Board will appoint one of the candidates to serve until the next regularly-scheduled Board election, at which time a director shall be elected for the unexpired term, if any.  The board will have 90 days to fill the seat when vacated by Director McKaughan on August 31, 2022.  The board is currently in the planning phase of the recruitment process.  More information about the board director search and how to apply for the board director position for District 2 will be shared through district communication.
  President Flores entertained a motion for nominees for the OSD Board Vice-President vacancy.  The following board director(s) were nominated:
Darcy Huffman, District 3
  With no other names, President Flores closed the nominations and asked Director Huffman if she accepts the nomination of Board Vice-President.  Director Huffman accepted.
  President Flores entertained a motion to nominate Darcy Huffman, Director of District 2, as Vice-President of the Olympia School Board.  Director Seidel made a motion to nominate Director Huffman for the Vice-President position.  The motion was seconded and passed by 4 ayes. Director McKaughan abstained.
  Realizing that anytime the Board votes a member for President or Vice-President, it must be done by roll call vote.  
  President Flores entertained a motion to nominate Darcy Huffman, Director of District 2, as Vice-President of the Olympia School Board.  The following votes were recorded:
President Flores – aye
Director McKaughan – aye
Director Huffman – aye
Director Seidel – aye
Director Clifthorne – aye
  The roll call vote was complete. The board congratulated Vice-President Huffman in her new board position.  Vice President Huffman will assume the role until the board’s reorganization in December 2022.

    2. CONSENT AGENDA
The basic purpose of the consent agenda is to act upon routine matters expeditiously. Items placed on the consent agenda are determined by the Board President in cooperation with the Superintendent, and are those which are considered common to the operation of the District and normally require no special Board discussion or debate. A Board member, however, may request that any item on the consent agenda be removed and inserted at an appropriate place on the regular agenda.

  3.3 Family Partnership (Engagement), Family Partnerships; Student Survey Results
Director of Whole Child Success, Char Franz, and Family and Community Partnership Coordinator Marisha McDowell shared with the Board updates on the family partnership program highlighting where we are and where we are going.
  Where We Are:
Family Partnerships
1. We are creating roots and growing leaders through our Family Liaisons to partner with   educators across the district
2. Family Liaisons–dedicated roles to facilitate partnerships with families, communities, and   schools
  Panorama Well-Being Screener – Fall 2020-Spring 2022 compared.
  Mission, Vision, and Shared Leadership
What we believe - Vision
Family engagement is a full and equitable partnership among families, educators,
providers, and communities to support learners’ development from birth through
college and career. It is a collective responsibility that means doing with—not
doing for—families
.
Our OSD Family Liaison Focus - Mission
1. Move towards a meaningful partnership between the community and the schools
2. Create schools to be community hub to facilitate access to resources for families to thrive:
3. Co-lead practices and co-create systems towards anti-oppression and anti-racism in
    Olympia School District
  Site-Based Family Liaisons (FLs): Leadership Highlights
Hansen: Created and implemented inclusive PRIDE programming for the students to learn and participate in age appropriate conversations surrounding LGBTQIA+ folks
LP Brown: Collaborated with Family Support Center to have office space to meet families where they are at in the community and bridge the gap of accessibility for families
Boston Harbor: Partnered with the PTO and administration to implement Black Lives Matter programming and activities during Black History Month
Garfield: Implemented a “walk kids to school program” that focused on building upon community, and facilitating connections between local apartment buildings and homes
Roosevelt: Organized a resource fair for OSD families to access community partners to help families form natural resources
  Family Liaison Services: Leadership Highlights
Resource Leadership Group: Partnered with local grocery stores to provide fresh fruit to our elementary school families who receive Friday Food Bags. This opportunity provides direct support for families to receive these items, as well as providing fresh produce to those who have monetary, transportation and accessibility disadvantages that may relate to their abilities in utilizing other community resources.
Panorama: Family Liaisons developed materials and systems to increase family and student participation in the Panorama survey
CR-SEL: Family Liaisons were leaders participating in the CR-SEL training focusing on Healing, Empowerment and Leading through the lens of cultural responsiveness
Family Liaison Framework: Family Liaisons utilized shared leadership tactics to interpret the OSPI Family Engagement Framework to then create a specific Framework document for OSD
  A thirteen-minute video was shared highlighting live talk between Nancy Swanson and Char Franz about the family partnership program.

Create a handbook of resources for our FL team to reference
Have a suggested ideas list for cultural and social justice themed activities per month that FL’s can implement throughout the year
FLs can partner across schools to create ongoing cross collaboration

  3.4 Professional Growth and Systemwide Inventory
Chief Academic Officer, Dr. Hannah Gbenro, and Senior Director of Teaching and Learning, Dr. Cherlyn Pijanowski provided the Board an overview of the district’s professional growth and systemwide inventory.
  For professional growth, Dr. Pijanowski shared…
Looking back & planning ahead, professional growth includes annual teaching & learning professional survey 2021-22; professional development planning committee 2022-23; and dates to launch the 2022-23 school year.  In 2021 – 2022; Professional Growth (Trainings, Professional Development, and Professional Learning) offerings have taken place. Highlights are included in previous Board Reports.  The district is currently collecting data through an annual Professional Growth Survey for certificated staff, establishing a Professional Growth Committee and partnering across Cabinet Leaders for a systemwide inventory of professional growth.  Dates to launch for the upcoming months were shared.
  For systemwide inventory, Dr. Gbenro shared…
Prior to 2021-22SY Inventoried required and recommended professional growth across all departments, aside from Teaching & Learning. Each department established initial professional growth opportunities for staff based on required needs.  Funds were prioritized to support professional growth opportunities. An annual survey of certificated educator was needed inform Teaching & Learning professional growth efforts as well as department-specific surveys to inform professional growth.
  Where We Are
 In 2021-2022, the district is currently updating the inventory of required Professional Growth, including the addition of Teaching & Learning state requirements, establishing an initial, multi-layered vision for systemwide Professional Growth and have included professional growth data sources within a Data Stream Inventory that is taking place under the leadership of the Technology Department.
  In 2023 and beyond, the district will use its data to inform the establishment of systemwide supports for professional growth by role. The district has established a partnership from Teaching & Learning (T&L) Team across the system to align professional growth with the multi-layered vision.
