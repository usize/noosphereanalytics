Superintendent’s Announcements and Report: Summary of Dr. Baker’s comments:
• The annual Options High School gratitude lunch was held on Nov. 27, and Dr. Baker
shared photos from the lunch.
• Dr. Baker shared highlights from recent state sports competitions, where individual
students and teams from our district performed well. District teams were also
recognized with scholastic awards by the Washington Interscholastic Activities
Association (WIAA).
• Students from district music programs participated in the Port of Bellingham’s
holiday festival Dec. 7-8 at the ferry terminal.
• The district is in the process of creating a new website, which will be launched in the
summer of 2025.
• There has been recent media coverage regarding the state’s budget challenges,
demonstrating that budget concerns continue to be a challenge across the state. We
continue to invest significantly in special education support. District leadership has
been working closely with our labor groups and are looking for attrition opportunities
and creative ways to fund unplanned needs. The district is exploring the idea of
submitting a waiver to the state to decrease the number of student days from 180 to
177, while maintaining a consistent number of instructional hours. The board will
discuss this evening a resolution and letter to state legislators. The superintendents of
school districts in Whatcom County are drafting a letter to the community. The
governor’s state budget proposal is expected on Dec. 17. When the new governor
takes office in January, he will decide whether to adopt Governor Inslee’s budget or
make changes. The district is looking at ways to bring in revenue, including
advertising on digital signs and score boards.
• This evening’s consent agenda contains reports on EL-7 and EL-9, and Dr. Baker
gave a brief overview of what will be found in those reports.
• We expect the City of Bellingham to share an update with the community regarding
the future of the Civic Complex, which includes potentially relocating Carl Cozier
Elementary School.
• The Roosevelt Educational Specifications/Design Advisory Committee has been
working to design a new building, which may be added to a future bond proposal.
• The Roosevelt Elementary staff will present its annual performance on Dec. 20. This
year’s theme is “The Neverending Story.”
Dr. Baker responded to questions from board members.

School Board Directors’ Reports. Highlights of comments:
• Mr. Benjamin was pleased that all board members and Dr. Baker were able to attend
the annual WSSDA conference in Spokane in November. While in Spokane, he
attended a 6-hour WSSDA directors’ meeting. He learned a lot from the board’s
recent visit to Sehome High School.
• Mr. Ritchey shared that he is concluding his first year as a board member. He thanked
Mr. Benjamin for being an inspirational board member and president. Ms. Mason has
served as Mr. Ritchey’s mentor, and he can’t imagine learning from a savvier and
more experienced mentor. The board’s visit to Sehome High School was incredible.
He appreciated Mr. Benjamin’s presentation at WSSDA and heard a lot of
appreciation for the district’s policy governance model.
• Ms. Rose enjoyed the WSSDA conference. The session about community
engagement was interesting and informative. She has seen many examples of The
Bellingham Promise in action recently: a co-worker who attended the Port Festival
was blown away by the student performers, district participation in the Mad Hope
program, the partnership with the North Cascades Institute, and the district’s strong
robotics program.
• Ms. Diaz Hackler helped to serve dessert at the Options High School gratitude lunch
and very much enjoyed it. She thought the WSSDA conference was great and
especially liked the keynote speakers. The board’s visit to Sehome High School was
informative.
• Ms. Mason enjoyed the visit to Sehome High School and learned a lot about the
Multilingual Learners program. She recently participated as a speaker for the
Washington Statewide Family Engagement Center. There was a lot of interest about
how we serve Spanish speakers and many positive comments about our Family
Engagement team led by Executive Director of Family Engagement Isabel Meaker.
She recently visited Squalicum High School’s cosmetology program as a part of her
board education plan. It feels like a real salon! The teacher is a former salon owner
and brings a lot of experience to the program. There are 44 students in the program,
all working independently. Ms. Mason found the level of interest, autonomy and
engagement to be great. She’s glad that the program is available for students.
