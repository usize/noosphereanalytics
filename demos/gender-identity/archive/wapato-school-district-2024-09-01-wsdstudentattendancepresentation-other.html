School Aifendance

Every child who is at least 8 years old is required to attend school (public,
private, or home-based) until age 18. Parents in this state shall cause their

children to attend.

If a parent enrolls a child who is 6 or 7 years of age in a public school, the
child is required to attend, and that parent has the responsibility to ensure
the child attends for the full time that school is in session.

RCW 28A.225.010-- Attendance Mandatory

Chronic absenteeism is when a student misses at least 10% of their school

days in a year, for any reason (excused, unexcused and suspensions). This is
equivalent to missing around 18 days of school in a year.

Truancy Law

Washington State’s truancy law is also known as the “Becca Bill.”

¢ The law requires parents or legal guardians to make sure their young
people are in school regularly.

¢ School Districts are required by law to file a truancy petition once a
student has accrued 7 unexcused absences in a 30-day period or 15
unexcused absences in a school year.

¢ State law requires the schools and school districts take specific
actions when youths are truant.
