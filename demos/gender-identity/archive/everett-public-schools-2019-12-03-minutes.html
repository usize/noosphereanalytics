  11.22 Adoption of Policy 5320.9, Family and Medical Leave, Proposed Revision (second reading)
  Proposed revisions to Policy 5320.9, Family and Medical Leave, were provided for second reading and adoption. Proposed revisions reflect additional paid family and medical benefits for workers. Starting in 2020, Washington will be the fifth state in the nation to offer paid family and medical leave benefits to workers. The program will be funded by premiums paid by both employees and many employers and will allow workers to take necessary time off when they welcome a new child into their family, are struck by a serious illness or injury, or need to take care of an ill or ailing relative. As directed by the Legislature, premium payments began on January 1, 2019 and benefits can be taken starting January 1, 2020. Importantly, the program is administered by the Employment Security Department, not the district. Revisions also include changing the title from "Family and Medical Leave", to "Family, Medical, and Maternity Leave".
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves for adoption upon second reading proposed revisions to Policy 5320.9, Family and Medical Leave.

  15.01 Policy 3204, Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying, Proposed Revision (first reading)
  Regional Superintendent of the South Region Larry Fleckenstein presented the proposed revisions to Policy 3204, Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying, Proposed Revision, provided for first reading with second reading and adoption to be recommended at a future board meeting. The state legislature has implemented a number of changes to the required harassment, intimidation and bullying (HIB) policies and procedures, now codified under RCW 28A.600.477. One of these changes is the requirement to adopt the WSSDA model by January 31, 2020. In addition, each school district must provide to the office of the superintendent of public instruction (OSPI) a brief summary of its policies, procedures, programs, partnerships, vendors, and instructional and training materials prohibiting HIB. This information will be posted on OSPI's school safety center website. The district must also provide OSPI with a link to the district's website for further information on HIB. The district's primary contact for HIB issues must annually verify posted information and links by August 15 and notify the school safety center of any updates or changes. Revisions also include changing the title from "Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying", to "Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation or Bullying".
  Following a brief conversation regarding name calling, hazing, and targeted students, President Mason asked that the item be moved to second reading for adoption at the next regular board meeting.
  15.02 Policy 6100, Revenues from Local, State and Federal Sources, Proposed Revision (first reading)
  Regional Superintendent of the South Region Larry Fleckenstein presented the proposed revisions to Policy 6100, Revenues from Local, State and Federal Sources, provided for first reading with second reading and adoption to be recommended at a future board meeting. Proposed revisions are to align with WSSDA's model policy and integrate new requirements established by the legislature pertaining to local enrichment levies and local effort assistance. Such laws are enacted in a number of RCWs providing regulatory guidance on the allowable uses of enrichment levies, requirements for OSPI approval of enrichment levy plans prior to placement on a ballot, and additional audit requirements. Also included are WSSDA updates to the policy sections on state and federal revenues.
  Following a brief conversation regarding sub group naming, President Mason asked that the item be moved to second reading for adoption at the next regular board meeting with suggested revisions.
