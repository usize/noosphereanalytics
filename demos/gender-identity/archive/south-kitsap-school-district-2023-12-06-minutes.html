    REGULAR MEETING OF THE BOARD
Wednesday, December 6, 2023
Minutes
  The meeting was held in-person at the District Administration Building with a Zoom option. Notice was provided to community members wishing to access the meeting.
  Present:
Jeffrey Wilson                    President
Brian Pickard                     Vice President
Kate Espy via Zoom            Director
John Berg via Zoom           Outgoing Director
Megan Higgins                   Incoming Director
Jay Villars                         Incoming Director
                                                  Tim Winter                        Superintendent
Tom Edwards                     Assistant Superintendent of Student Achievement
Monica Hunsaker via Zoom Assistant Superintendent of Business and Operations
Lisa Fundane’t                   Executive Director of Teaching & Learning
Eric Nerison                       Executive Director of Special Services      
Will Sarett                         Executive Director of Human Resources
Amy Miller                         Communications and Public Information Officer
Anna Tenzler                      Executive Assistant/Recorder
  1. CONVENE
Call to Order: President Wilson called the meeting to order at 5:00 pm and provided information regarding the nature of the meeting.
  SKHS NJROTC Color Guard: A Color Guard ceremony was conducted by SKHS NJROTC students.
  Pledge of Allegiance: SKHS NJROTC led the flag salute.        
  2. ADOPT AGENDA
MOTION: WILSON moved to approve the agenda as presented. Motion carried.
  3. BOARD RECOGNITION & REORGANIZATION
Recognition of Outgoing Board Members: To honor outgoing Directors John Berg and Jeff Daily, Superintendent Winter thanked them for their service to the South Kitsap community and presented each of them a plaque of appreciation. Director Berg was in attendance via Zoom and a family member accepted his plaque on his behalf.
  Oath of Office to Newly Elected School Board Directors: Superintendent Winter administered the Oath of Office to newly elected Directors Kate Espy (via Zoom), Megan Higgins and Jay Villars.
  Board Reflection: President Wilson gave each director a chance to share their thoughts as they join as a new governing team. President Wilson thanked each board member for their commitment to the Board and South Kitsap.
  Board Reorganization: At the first meeting in December annually, the Board reorganizes positions. The positions for 2023 are President, Vice President, Washington State Interscholastic Activities Association (WIAA) Representative, and WSSDA Delegate.
  Position Nominations:
  President:
Kate Espy nominated Jeff Wilson for President. Jeff Wilson accepted the nomination.
Megan Higgins nominated Brian Pickard for President. Brian Pickard declined the nomination.
There were no further nominations.
Superintendent Winter took a roll call vote in favor of Jeff Wilson as President: Brian Pickard (yea), Megan Higgins (yea), Jay Villars (yea), Kate Espy (yea), Jeff Wilson (yea). Jeff Wilson was elected President by general consent.
  Vice President:
Megan Higgins nominated Brian Pickard for Vice President. Brian Pickard accepted the nomination.
There were no further nominations.  
Superintendent Winter took a roll call vote in favor of Brian Pickard as Vice President: Brian Pickard (yea), Megan Higgins (yea), Jay Villars (yea), Kate Espy (yea), Jeff Wilson (yea). Brian Pickard was elected Vice President by general consent.
  WIAA Representative:
Kate Espy nominated Brian Pickard for WIAA Representative. Brian Pickard accepted the nomination.
There were no further nominations.
Brian Pickard was elected WIAA Representative by general consent.
  WSSDA Delegate:
Brian Pickard nominated Kate Espy for WSSDA Delegate. Kate Espy accepted the nomination.
There were no further nominations.
Kate Espy was elected WSSDA Delegate by general consent.
  4. PUBLIC COMMENT: The following community members addressed the Board: Judy Abogast via Zoom welcoming new board members, Chrissy Wiley welcoming new board members and feedback on the Bond, Jeff Daily regarding formal complaints against district staff and board members, Gena Cole regarding the Pearl Harbor memorial and bus driver shortages, Marty Mioni regarding national news issues, Stefani Kerr regarding changes at SKSD, Dave Kimble via Zoom regarding the future of the new board and future bond/levy measures, Holly Cruz regarding district policies and health curriculum, Martha Hammann welcoming new board members and student support, Marcie Decker in support of the Board, John Richardson in support of the Board and student wellness, Dave May in support of students and the Board, Paula Jewett in support of the District and Bond, Normana Schaaf with appreciation of the Board and student civility, and Rhiannon McCarthy regarding gender policies, social emotional learning and parental rights.
  5. RECOGNITIONS AND REPORTS
Year End Financial Report for 2022-2023 and 23-24 Budget Status Report: Assistant Superintendent of Business and Operations, Monica Hunsaker, presented a year-end financial status review, student enrollment update, and current budget status data. Currently, the district is reflecting positive student enrollment at 172 FTE (estimated headcount of 200) over projected budget. In addition, Monica Hunsaker also reviewed the October and November budget status updates. The Board had an opportunity to ask questions and made comments in appreciation on the clarity of information presented.
  At 6:21pm the Board took a 9-minute break. The meeting resumed at 6:30pm.
  Staff and Student Recognition: Superintendent Winter introduced Amy Miller, Communications and Public Information Officer, who recognized two staff retirements and four staff members receiving SK Way awards: Tricia Kobayashi at the SKSD District Office, Emily Fogel at Marcus Whitman Middle School, Mike Allen at South Kitsap High School, and Mary Smiley-Tucker at South Colby Elementary.
  Superintendent Report: Superintendent Winter shared the following:
Donations to Orchard Heights Elementary and Olalla Elementary
Reflection on the future and impact of our new school board
SKSD Priority Plan
Superintendent / Board operating protocols, expectations, and objectives
Academic improvement, district goals, and timeline of future focus around student achievement
President Wilson asked for clarification on district transportation and recent route cancellations. Superintendent Winter shared 26 new drivers will begin bus driver training soon.
6. FOCUS ON ACADEMIC ACHIEVEMENT
SKSD Academic Update: Assistant Superintendent of Student Achievement, Dr. Tom Edwards, and Executive Director of Teaching and Learning, Lisa Fundane’t shared an overview of SKSD academic data. Historical Smarter Balanced Assessment trend data in ELA and Math was shared with the Board. Fall 2023 STAR Benchmark Assessment data at third and seventh grade in reading and math was reviewed. In addition, student behavior incident and exclusion data was shared. Student attendance data was reviewed along with associated risk levels for student achievement. The data reflected improvement of student attendance since last school year. The Board had an opportunity to ask clarifying questions on the information presented.
  7. BOARD DEVELOPMENT & DISCUSSION
Board Updates and Linkages:
Director Espy participated in legislative sessions during the WSSDA conference and is also serving on the WSSDA State Board.
Director Pickard attended the SKHS girls basketball game and attended the SKHS culinary room grand opening. Director Pickard recently visited Mullenix Ridge Elementary discussing topics with staff. The SK Helpline Food Bank recently flooded and has received a positive response from our community and schools to support the food bank.
Director Higgins attended the culinary room grand opening at SKHS and the WSSDA conference.
Director Villars shared his support of donations being raised to help the SK Helpline Food Bank. Director Villars also had conversations with community members around the future of SKSD.
President Wilson attended the SKHS culinary room grand opening and attended the SKHS Magical Feaste. President Wilson also has had conversations with community members regarding the future of SKSD.
  Community Bond Survey Feedback & Next Steps: Superintendent Winter shared feedback provided by the recent community bond survey and identified themes. Board members discussed ideas and next steps for a Bond measure in the future. Potential scenarios of continued bond failure impact was discussed among board members.
  MOTION: ESPY motioned for a bond to be placed on the ballot in 2024. Second by PICKARD.  After board discussion, Director Espy amended her original motion to identifying future bond plans in 2024. Amended motion carried.
  8. MATTERS RESERVED FOR BOARD ACTION
GC-12 Meetings: Superintendent Winter and the Board discussed policy changes to Governance Culture Policy GC-12: Meetings.
MOTION: VILLARS motioned to adopt changes to GC-12 as presented. Second by ESPY. Motion carried.
  Results: Mission: The Board discussed policy changes to the Coherent Governance Results: Mission.
MOTION: PICKARD motioned to adopt changes from Results: Mission to Goals: Mission as presented. Second by WILSON. Motion carried.
  2023-2024 Board Calendar: The Board discussed policy changes to the 2023-2024 Annual Board Agenda Calendar.
MOTION: PICKARD motioned to adopt changes to 2023-2024 Annual Board Calendar and adopt as GC-12-A as presented. Second by ESPY. Motion carried.
  9. SUPERINTENDENT CONSENT AGENDA
Consent Agenda
Human Resources Employment Reports
Affidavit of Vouchers and Payroll
Surplus Equipment from Technology
MOTION: WILSON moved to approve the Superintendent Consent Agenda as presented. Motion carried.
  10. BOARD CONSENT AGENDA
Minutes of the Regular Meeting of November 14, 2023
Minutes of the Special Meeting of November 16-18, 2023
MOTION: WILSON moved to approve the Board’s Consent Agenda as presented. Motion carried.
  11. ADJOURNMENT
Meeting adjourned at 8:26pm.
        Dr. Jeffrey Wilson, President
