    Board Meeting
MEETING MINUTES
Thursday, October 10, 2024
Knox and via Zoom
https://osd111.zoom.us/j/87421477017
6:30–8:21 pm
  1. OPENING
1.1 Call to Order and Pledge of Allegiance
President Hilary Seidel called the OSD Board Meeting to order at 6:30 pm.  She led the Pledge of Allegiance. 
  1.2 Board Recognition of Tribal Land
President Seidel read the following statement:
The Olympia School District rests on the ancestral territory of the Squaxin who have stewarded this land throughout the generations. The Board is committed to an ongoing dialog and partnership with Tribal neighbors and we pay our respects to elders both past and present.
  1.3 Roll Call
The following members were in-person and confirmed their attendance:  President Hilary Seidel; Superintendent Patrick Murphy; Director Maria Flores; and Student Representatives Declan Borden, Vy Le, and Sovana Roberts.
The following members attended via Zoom:  Vice President Scott Clifthorne and Director Tourtellotte-Palumbo.
The following member was absent with notice:  Director Darcy Huffman.  
  1.4 Approval of the October 10, 2024 Board Meeting Agenda
The board reviewed and approved as presented.
1.5 Approval of the September 26, 2024 Board Meeting Minutes 
The board reviewed and approved as presented.
1.6 Public Comment:  Agenda Items
Each speaker on the public comment list had up to three minutes to speak.
The following person spoke on an item related to the meeting agenda:
1.7 Superintendent’s Report
Superintendent Murphy thanked the public commenter.  He shared the district will have a Squaxin Island Tribe Flag-Raising Ceremony at 3 pm on Monday, October 21st at the district office; he thanked President Seidel, Executive Director of Operations Frank Wilson, Native Education & Tribal Relations Program Manager Sandra Gordon, Squaxin Island Tribe Chairman Kris Peters, and Intergovernmental Affairs/Council Liaison Ray Peters for bringing this to fruition.  A Communications survey has been shared; its purpose is to review the Communications processes.  Almost 400 people have responded; it will be open until October 13th.  The Panorama survey has also been shared; it measures the district’s progress toward its outcome goals.          
1.8 Student Representatives’ Reports
Student Representative Sovana Roberts reminded attendees to vote.  She also shared that October 11th is National Coming Out Day and congratulated those that have come out or are waiting to.
Student Representative Vy Le shared that the Equity Team Students created a board game to present culturally responsive teaching to staff.  The group is currently preparing to its street data, on student engagement, at a staff meeting.  She also shared that the Student-Led Advisory Council applications closed and the Student Representatives will review them. 
Student Representative Declan Borden did not have comments at this time.
2. CONSENT AGENDA
The basic purpose of the consent agenda is to act upon routine matters expeditiously. Items placed on the consent agenda are determined by the Board President in cooperation with the Superintendent, and are those which are considered common to the operation of the District and normally require no special Board discussion or debate. A Board member, however, may request that any item on the consent agenda be removed and inserted at an appropriate place on the regular agenda.
  Director Flores motioned to amend the Consent Agenda by removing the 2024-2025 Board Planning Calendar as it does not specify when mandatory reports will be presented to the board.  This motion was seconded and approved.
Superintendent Murphy shared that the board calendar will list which mandatory reports are presented within each Monitoring Report.  This information will be shared with the board this weekend and then with the public. 
  2.1 2024-2025 Board Planning Calendar
The board removed this from the Consent Agenda.

3. REPORT
3.1 Outcome 6 Monitoring Report
Superintendent Murphy, Executive Director of Operations Frank Wilson, Chief Information Officer Marc Elliott, Executive Director of Teaching & Learning Inger Owen, and K-12 Science Specialist Carmen Kardokus presented this report.
CLOSING
3.1 Board Member Comments
  Student Representative Declan Borden did not have comments at this time.
Student Representative Vy Le did not have additional comments.
Student Representative Sovana Roberts shared that the Student Representatives are working on an Instagram account with the assistance of Communications & Community Relations Director Conor Schober. 
Director Jess Tourtellotte-Palumbo thanked the Student Representatives for their work on the Student-Led Advisory Council.  She shared that Capital High School Student Representative Kellan Eggleston resigned his position; a new student representative will join at the next board meeting.  She thanked Executive Directors Autumn Lara & James Whitehead, and Superintendent Murphy for their work the past couple of weeks.  She thanked the volunteers of school events & festivals.  She met with Superintendent Murphy and Vice President Clifthorne to discuss the School Safety CAC.  She & Superintendent Murphy will be meeting with Dare to Lead facilitator Jolene Lockwood.  She shared the process of Policy Review.     
Vice President Scott Clifthorne thanked WSSDA for offering the “Discovering Educational Disparities” workshop which he attended.
Director Maria Flores thanked Superintendent Murphy for sharing information on the Squaxin Island Tribe Flag-Raising Ceremony.  She shared appreciation for the work Manager Gordon has done which includes the 100% graduation rate for Olympia School District Native students.  Director Flores will bring an amendment to Policy 6883 before the next board meeting; it will revert back to the WSSDA model policy. 
President Hilary Seidel expressed gratitude to the monitoring report presenters.  She appreciated the thoughtfulness around accessibility in field science.  She congratulated the Olympia Education Foundation for their Rise & Shine event.  She thanked club advisors & coaches for their work which provides students with rich opportunities.  She encouraged people to attend school events such as the Avanti Haunted School.  She expressed gratitude to the people who having been working on stewarding the district’s relationship with the Squaxin Island Tribe.  She thanked Manager Gordon and Capitol High School Counselor Nicole Sande for their incredible work with native students.
