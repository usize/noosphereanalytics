  WORK SESSION
The Board met at 5:00 pm to discuss revisions to the Board’s Ends policies. Directors Diehl and Daily presented a draft of proposed revisions. The Board discussed and will continue to review. They agreed to look at Board/Superintendent Relations policies and will review at a 5:00 pm work session on March 4.
  CALL TO ORDER
President Gattenby called the meeting to order at 6:00 pm.
  Pledge of Allegiance: EPO students, Elaina Hastings and Makayla Sanders, led the flag salute.
  Establish Agenda: Director Gattenby requested that Item 7.03 Affidavit of Vouchers and Payroll be removed from the agenda and Director Daily requested that Items 4.01 Executive Limitation 10: Asset Protection, 9.01 Review Policy Governance Executive Limitation 10: Asset Protection, 9.02 Review Policy Governance Board/Superintendent Relations Policy 4: Delegation to the Superintendent, 9.03 Review Policy Governance Board/Superintendent Relations Policy 5: Monitoring Superintendent Performance, and 12.01 Board Meeting Assessment be removed from the agenda.
MOTION: President Gattenby moved to remove Item 4.01; the Motion failed with one vote in favor and four against.
MOTION: President Gattenby moved to remove Item 7.03; the Motion carried.
MOTION: President Gattenby moved to remove Items 9.01, 9.02, and 9.03; the Motion failed with one vote in favor and four against.
MOTION: President Gattenby moved to remove Items 12.01; the Motion failed with one vote in favor and four against.
The agenda was approved as amended.
  Welcome from EPO Elementary Principal Paul Hulbert: Mr. Hulbert welcomed the Board and shared that student Wit and Wisdom work is on display in the library.
  Public Comment: Community member Carol L. Johnson addressed the Board regarding school taxes.
  PRESENTATIONS/REPORTS
Monitoring Student Data: Executive Director of Instructional Support Dr. Shannon Thompson provided an overview of District state assessment scores and reported how student data will be monitored going forward. Dr. Thompson’s presentation can be found on the February 19, 2020 Board Meeting Agenda.
  PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
Ends and Executive Limitation policies are monitored through the evaluation of the Superintendent’s evidence of compliance submitted by the Superintendent to the Board. Tonight, the Board evaluated the Superintendent on:
  Monitoring Report for Executive Limitation 10: Asset Protection.
Superintendent Winter reviewed his evidence of compliance including documents related to insurance coverage, risk management, district inventory, and the bid requirement process.
  MOTION: With respect to the provisions of its policy, EL 10, the South Kitsap Board of Directors concluded that the Superintendent’s performance has been in compliance. The Motion carried.
  The Superintendent’s evidence in support of EL 10, can be found on the February 19, 2020 Board Meeting Agenda.
  BOARD DISCUSSION/LINKAGE
Board Linkages:
Director Gattenby attended the WSSDA Legislative Conference and Day on the Hill; he met with 26th District Representatives Jesse Young, Michelle Caldier, and Senator Randall;
Director Diehl also attended the WSSDA Legislative Conference and Day on the Hill; it was an opportunity to work together and connect with the 26th District elected representatives;
Director Berg attended Day on the Hill and enjoyed meeting with legislators; he has also reported site visits to Hidden Creek, John Sedgwick, and SKHS;
Director Sebren spoke with the new District orchestra director who offered to have students perform at board meetings; she also recalled student teaching at EPO in 1985.
  Legislative Report: Director Berg reported that during the meeting with legislators, the discussions focused on the state funding model, expectations of teachers, and the proposed bill addressing sexual health education. Representative Caldier and Senator Randall provided perspective from serving on education committees and Representative Young was helpful in clarifying the legislative process.
  Board Discussion: The Board discussed the following items:
The Board requested that the Superintendent provide a detailed voucher report for review the Friday before each board meeting.
WSSDA is offering Board training on state, local and federal funding, and district budgets. The dates for our area have not yet been set.
Burley Glenwood water: for several years, a wing of the building has tested positive for lead in the water; consequently, the water has not been used and bottled water has been provided. The Board discussed replacing pipes as a possible capital levy project.
Director Berg requested that the Minutes of the February 5, 2020 board meeting be corrected, revising the Motion to Establish Agenda. Item 6.02 Minutes of the Regular Meeting of February 5, 2020 was removed from the agenda.
