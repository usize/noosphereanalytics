Hello LWSD Board Members,

Good evening, my name is Wenming Huang, and! am a concerned parent residing here in Kirkland. My daughter, Maggie Huang, is currently
a 4th-grade student at Cedar Crest Academy (CCA).

| am here tonight because Maggie, despite achieving exceptional test scores in the top 1-2 percentile —- scoring 99% on the NNATS3, 99% in
IOWA Math, and 98% in IOWA Reading - was denied the opportunity to enrollin LWSD Full Time Quest last year. | believe this was due toa
discriminatory policy.

This year, Maggie didn't give up and reapplied. However, she was rejected without even being offered an enrollment test.

Beyond Maggie's experience, | have significant concerns about the lack of transparency surrounding the Quest program. It is incredibly
difficult for parents to understand the specific admission criteria and factors considered, creating a sense of operating in a ‘black box.’

Furthermore, | believe there is an unfair bias against private school students in the Quest admissions process. This year, zero percent of
students were deemed eligible from CCA, as well as Bel-Red Bilingual Academy and Chestnut Hill Academy - all private schools with a large
number of Asian students. This raises serious questions about equitable opportunity.

| urge the School Board to address the lack of transparency in the Quest program and to ensure a fair and equitable opportunity for all
qualified students in LWSD, including those who attend private schools. Thank you for your time.

Best,
Wenming Huang
