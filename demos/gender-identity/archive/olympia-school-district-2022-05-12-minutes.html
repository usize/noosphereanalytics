President Flores read the following statement:
The Olympia School District rests on the ancestral territory of the Squaxin, Cowlitz, Coast Salish, and Nisqually People who have stewarded this land throughout the generations. The Board is committed to an ongoing dialog and partnership with Tribal neighbors and we pay our respects to elders both past and present.

    2. CONSENT AGENDA
The basic purpose of the consent agenda is to act upon routine matters expeditiously. Items placed on the consent agenda are determined by the Board President in cooperation with the Superintendent, and are those which are considered common to the operation of the District and normally require no special Board discussion or debate. A Board member, however, may request that any item on the consent agenda be removed and inserted at an appropriate place on the regular agenda.
  Director Clifthorne moved to amend the consent agenda to move Item 2.3 Collective Bargaining Agreement – Teamster Local Union No. 252 and OSD Food Services – September 1, 2021 – August 31, 2024 to the Regular Agenda as Item 3.1. The motion was seconded and passed unanimously.

  3.3 Policy 2020: Instructional Materials; 1st Reading - Preview
Dr. Hannah Gbenro and the Teaching & Learning (T&L) Team provided a preview of Agenda Item 3.1 from the March 24, 2022 Board Meeting (Policy 2020 Project Plan).  The first reading of the policy is scheduled for May 26 followed by second reading and board approval on June 9, 2022.  
  Contrasting Rules of Engagement in District-Community Relations information was shared as well as initial lessons learned:
  We have opportunities to improve stakeholder engagement and communication with stakeholders.
We have opportunities to improve the representation of diverse identities and accurate narratives within our existing curriculum and processes for identifying curriculum. 
We have opportunities to ensure alignment and calibration around prioritized content, inclusive instructional methods that support engagement of all students, and learning within a one-to-one system.
We need access to assessments and data that are useful.
We need access to pathways for exercising learning courage across roles - Educators, Families, and Students.
We need reasonable, up-to-date supports and related accountability measures from a servant leadership perspective.
    3.4 Teacher Professional Growth
On behalf of Dr. Cherlyn Pijanowski, Senior Director of Teaching and Learning, Dr. Hannah Gbenro, Chief Academic Officer, provided an update about where the district has been regarding Professional Growth to support Teachers with exercising learning courage during the 2021-2022 school year, as our updated Instructional Coaching Services Model responded to data from Educators as part of Recovery Plan efforts. Voices from the field were highlighted alongside traditional data sets, and the update had set the stage for the Systemwide Professional Growth Update in June, 2022.
  The following high-level overview was provided to the Board:
  Highlights from 2021-22
179 educators participated the Whole Child Institute (WCI) in August, 2021 
T&L Team partnered with Cabinet and fellow leaders to plan and carry out the District Leadership Team (DLT)/Admin Retreat in August, 2021
T&L Team created 67 professional development opportunities with a total of 914 attendees between 9/1/21 and 4/25/22
BEST Mentors offered weekly check in & 1:1 mentoring for 46 teachers in Year 1 and 19 teachers in Year 2
Instructional Coaches provided building level professional support: coaching cycles, co-planning & co-teaching, faculty meeting, grade level teams, PLCs
  Where We’re Going 2022-23
Survey Olympia Education Association Members Professional Growth Interests by June 1
Create a District Professional Development Steering Committee by June 1
New Teacher Onboarding Project to identify “core content” for all new to OSD by June 30
Develop & publish by August 15, 2022 a data-driven comprehensive professional development plan for 2022-23 ○ Linked to Student Outcomes, Strategic Plan, Recovery Plan, Equity, and Danielson 
Continue partnering with Center for Strengthening the Teaching Profession (CSTP) 
Continue partnering with the Teacher Development Group (TDG) to strengthen math instruction
  3.4 Teacher Professional Growth
On behalf of Joe Dyvig, K - 12 Visual Arts & Music Lead, Teaching & Learning Team and OHS Music Director, and Char Franz, Director of Whole Child Success, Dr. Hannah Gbenro, Chief Academic Officer, provided an update about the district’s three-year journey for prioritizing and implementing Arts, Music, and Physical Education (AMP) Specialist Program Services for students in our Elementary Schools. The presentation included implementation data alongside voices from our field.
  Where We’ve Been
Current Landscape – Growth; 35-minute AMP sessions instead of 30 minutes; Reduced itinerancies; Continued support for educator leaders to ensure that all schools move toward having students access 2x/week of Music and PE, and 1x/week of Art
Not all students are receiving 2x/week of music and 1x/week for art due to facility limitations
Voices from the Field
  Where We’re Going
Program Implementation versus Program Outcomes Data
Exploration of Potential Partnerships (Inspire Olympia, etc.)
