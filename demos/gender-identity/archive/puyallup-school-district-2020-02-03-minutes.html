  2. Communications
A. District Highlights
Dr. Brian Fox said between this meeting and the last board meeting, Kindergarten Academy began so we want to honor those kids by celebrating their first day so showed several pictures of the kids. He thanked Anne Martin and Nancy French for taking the photos. More than 100 four- and five-year-old students started their first day of school in the district's new program January 21. These students represent the first class of a transitional kindergarten program designed to support children who have not had an opportunity for preschool and show an ability and/or need for a developmentally appropriate kindergarten readiness experience.
  There are six transitional kindergarten classes strategically located among the three regions in the district. Classes are held at Dessie F. Evans, Firgrove, Northwood, Pope, Spinning, and Wildwood elementary schools. These locations provide options for local access for families.
  Next, Tracy Pitzer, director of instructional leadership, honored 33 junior high and high school students earning a varsity letter in volunteer services. To earn the Puyallup School District Varsity Letter in Volunteer Service, students between grades seven through 12 complete at least 150 hours of voluntary community service. Students document their community service through pictures and video and upload their work as well as their hours onto Schoology. To complete the portfolio, students write a 250 to 500 word reflection of their community service. The portfolio is then submitted and reviewed.

  5. Reports
A. Business Services - December 2019 Financial Statements; Laura Marcoe, Director of Business Services
Dr. Pecchia introduced Laura Marcoe to present our December 2019 Financial statements. Marcoe said the general fund is operating as we would expect. The fiscal year’s first principal and interest obligation of $28.6 million was paid out of the Debt Service Fund in December. There is a decline in our fund balance and it is at the lowest point in December as it has been in the last three years, which was also true for October and November.  Payments are twice a year, one in December and one in June. Property tax collection season is October and November, then in the spring in April and May. Those property tax collections are followed up by a payment from the debt service fund.The Transportation Vehicle, Capital Projects, Debt Service, Private Purpose Trust and ASB Funds are all operating as expected. 

Enrollment as of January, typically we see a decline in January. We saw an overall decrease of 32 students, most at the high school level. Headcount overall, excluding Running Start and alternative programs, we have actually increased 350 students overall since this time last year.

Marcoe reminded the audience that each year we are audited by the Washington State Auditor's Office, and they have been onsite for the last several weeks and these auditors review some of our federally funded programs, they look at our financial statements and then review some of the areas of accountability just to make sure that the district has protections in place to guard against the misuse or misappropriation of public funds. This year, they will be examining the federally-funded portions of our child nutrition program and our special education program. They'll also be reviewing our financial statements, and they'll review the district's financial sustainability, and look at accounts payable and payroll disbursements, ASB transactions, cash receiving and deposits and a variety of other areas. We expect them to be here through the end of March and work with a lot of different people throughout the district. She will return to share the auditor's final report with the board.

Director Kayaoglu said he is impressed by the increased enrollment, 350 is a large number even though we have a big district. He asked what was our projection in terms of the increase in enrollment?  Marcoe said our projection was an increase of 177 for this year.  He asked what are the financial implications for the additional 180 students? Marcoe responded they generate additional funding for us but also in the fall when we saw those kids come in we made some staffing additions at the elementary and junior high levels, and in fact we've made additional staffing at our secondary schools.  Director Kayaoglu asked why the difference between the actual enrollment and projection?  She explained that we always use a conservative estimate, because while our operations department has to worry about where we will house all those students, if the students don't come in that we project, we have contracts in place for staff so if the students don't come in, it is a double whammy, so we are always a little conservative. We want to be very careful.

A. Executive Administration - Resolution 139 2019-20 - Staffing and Program Reductions; Kathy Yang, Board President
President Yang announced that Director Keaton had planned to call in but his flight got changed, so he is airborne and unable to reach us.  She explained the next item is a resolution that is proposed. We have talked again and again about the need to reevaluate our programs and our staffing in light of the funding or the lack of funding and the expenditures that we have. President Yang read Resolution 139 2019-20, which states:
  WHEREAS, the Puyallup School District is restructuring and reorganizing in a manner that makes efficient use of public funds and resources, while also preserving and improving the student classroom experience; and
  WHEREAS, reductions in the District's administrative, instructional and/or classified staffing, as well as educational programs, may be necessary to achieve such reductions and reorganization in pursuit of the efficient use of public funds and resources, in support of the student classroom experience.
  THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Board of Directors of the Puyallup School District that:
  1. The Board hereby authorizes the Administrative Designee to take such action as is necessary to restructure, reorganize and/or reduce the educational program of the District as follows: non-represented administrative staffing (a minimum of eight (8) FTE), classified staffing, certificated staffing, and educational programs or offerings (as is necessary within the Designee’s discretion to make the most efficient use of public funds and resources); and
  2. The Administrative Designee is directed to take such action as may be required by law, collective bargaining agreements, or Board policy to implement such action, including timely notification of certificated and/or classified employees who will be affected by the implementation of the restructured staffing and/or educational programs.
  3. The Administrative Designee is to return all employment recommendations to the Board for final action

Director Kayaoglu said education is a two-phase process: it makes the unfamiliar, familiar and that is usually the easiest part. But education also makes the familiar, unfamiliar. When we talk about gender inclusiveness in the schools, it makes him think about one of the ways that education makes the familiar unfamiliar is when there is a challenge to the conception about who we are and what we can be, and how we can flourish in society. One of the ways, of course, when the challenge comes it is painful, because it requires a close questioning about some of our privilege. As he thinks about how we can move from transgender to gender inclusive, he asks himself how he can be privileged as a cisgender person. How can society value one type of identity over another? How is it as a male that I may have that kind of privilege, or people who are born here as opposed to immigrants, that the white skin can be privileged over others, how male and not female have been privileged, how our institutions privilege able bodies as opposed to those who may have some kind of difficulties or challenges. It's become the core of education, and he's looking forward to see how we are going to make sure that our schools are not just gender inclusive, but inclusive of all races, all religions, all faiths, all able bodies. These are definitely exciting times but also challenging times because it's going to challenge some of our conceptions about who we are, what society is about, and how we relate to the society at large. He is looking forward to how we are going to make sure our schools are gender inclusive.
