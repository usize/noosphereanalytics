Board Comments

Director Mullen Doyle provided the board with a reminder to submit their vote for the
Washington State School Directors’ Association district representatives. In addition, she is
requesting that Public Comment be placed at the beginning of the agenda rather than placing it
before Old and New Business.

Superintendent's Report:

Dr. Miriam Mickelson reported that Sedro-Woolley High School has implemented an attendance
incentive. This incentive is a friendly completion between the grade levels. The junior class won
the September completion with a 93.82% attendance rate.

School Board Representatives, Adriana Rodriguez and Spencer White, provided the donation
and activity report.

Dr. Mickelson provided the October enrollment report. The FTE is 4,314.32, which is currently
267.32 higher than originally projected. ,

Old Business

Danielle Russell moved and Lindy Mullen Doyle seconded to approve Policy #3246 — Restraint
Isolation and Other Uses of Reasonable Force. Executive Director Tony Smith is presented to the
board for second reading, Policy #3246 — Restraint Isolation and Other Uses of Reasonable Force.
The policy was presented September 23 for first reading. The revision includes technical revisions,
which reflect the repeal of RCW 70.96B.010 and WAC 392-400-235. Motion passed 3/0.


Executive Session — RCW 42.30.110 — to evaluate the qualification of a

candidate for appointment to elective office.

President Johnson called for an Executive Session of the Board at 7:08 p.m. to evaluate the
qualifications of a candidate for appointment to elective office pursuant to RCW 42.30.110.
President Johnson will recuse himself from all discussion and the Executive Session. Vice
President Danielle Russell will preside during the Executive Session. Superintendent Mickelson
was invited to attend the executive session. The board will resume regular session at 7:38 p.m.
At 7:39 p.m., Vice President Russell resumed the regular session of the meeting.

New Business:

First Reading 2024-2025 School Improvement Plans — Assistant Superintendent Brian Isakson
presented for first reading the required improvement plans. The board will be asked to take action
on the plans during the October 28 meeting.

Lindy Mullen Doyle moved and Danielle Russell seconded to direct Dr. Mickelson to work with
the city of Sedro-Woolley for a possible joint letter and/or to draft a school district letter to the
Energy Facility Site Evaluation Committee for board discussion and signature at the next board
meeting on October 28, 2024. Motion passed 4/0.

President Johnson reported that the school district has been contacted by Representative Carolyn
Eslick who is requesting feedback on issues that relate to the school district. President Johnson
would like the board to consider legislative policy changes and direct district administration to
respond to the request. Discussion ensued on the time constraints and best options to solicit
information. Dr. Mickelson will collect information and will report to the board.

Lindy Mullen Doyle moved and Brent Schiefelbein seconded to approve the following five
candidates to move forward in the interview process for District Director #5 position.
Anthony Alvarez

Brad Worley

Allan Henderson

Becky Taft

Debra French-Hanson

President Johnson recused himself from the discussion and vote. Motion passed 3/0.

Agenda Additions:

None.


Adjournment:
There being no further business, the meeting adjourned at 8:06 p.m., Brent Schiefelbein moved
and Danielle Russell seconded to adjourn the meeting. The motion carried 4/0.
