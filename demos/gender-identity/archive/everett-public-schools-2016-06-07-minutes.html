(E:44/16)
  7.12 Approval of Letter of Support for Healthy Kids–Healthy Schools Grant
  The district has received a preliminary award of the Healthy Kids–Healthy Schools grant from OSPI for $99,284. The grant will provide funding for new water bottle filling stations at all four high schools, and a new dishwasher at Sequoia High School. As a final condition of the Healthy Kids–Healthy Schools grant award, the board is required to provide OSPI a letter of support. The letter of support commits the district to completing the above mentioned projects by June 2017, and requires the district to provide district resources in the form of operating funds and maintenance of the new facilities brought about by these grant-funded improvements.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves a letter of support as a final condition of the Healthy Kids-Healthy Schools grant award. (E:45/16)
  7.13 Approval of Snohomish County School Impact Fee Agreement Addendum No. 1
  The Snohomish County School Impact Fee Agreement governs the collection, distribution, and expenditure of school impact fees collected by Snohomish County on behalf of the Everett Public Schools. In response to changes to state law, Snohomish County is in the process of amending its ordinance governing the collection of school impact fees. This addendum recognizes that the collection process would conform to the provisions of the county’s amendment. It further directs the county to enhance its report to the district and to provide additional information regarding the deferred payments.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves the agreement related to Snohomish County School Impact Fee Program Addendum No. 1. (E:46/16)
  7.14 Approval of Memorandum of Understanding with Sno-Isle Technical Skills Center for 2016 Summer School Classes
  The agreement between Everett Public Schools and Sno-Isle Technical Skills Center (Sno-Isle) provides for the teaching of Biotechnology and Research and Engineering classes at Cascade High School during the 2016 summer school session. The memorandum of understanding (MOU) outlines the responsibilities for both the district and Sno-Isle. The district will provide staff, instruction, and specified support services and Sno-Isle will reimburse the district for these expenses.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves the memorandum of understanding between the Sno-Isle Technical Skills Center and Everett Public Schools to provide teaching for the 2016 summer school classes. (E:47/16)
  7.15 Approval of Assistance League of Everett Operation School Bell and Graduation Awards Agreements
  Board approval was requested for two agreements between Everett Public Schools and the Assistance League of Everett. The Operation School Bell Agreement provides school-appropriate clothing to selected K-12 students within Everett Public Schools who demonstrate financial need. The Graduation Awards Agreement assists graduating high school seniors who are in financial need and find it difficult to finance their senior year/graduation expenses. The Assistance League maintains liability insurance coverage for both programs and holds Everett Public Schools harmless against all claims and damages that are the fault of the Assistance League. The school district maintains liability insurance for both programs and holds the Assistance League harmless against all claims and damages that are the fault of the school district. The agreements are for three years.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves the two agreements between Everett Public Schools and the Assistance League of Everett for Operation School Bell and Graduation Awards. (E:48/16)
  7.16 Adoption of Policy 3213, Transgender Students, Proposed New Policy (second reading)
  Proposed new Policy 3213, Transgender Students, was provided for second reading and adoption. With the recent passage of laws and regulations that address discriminatory practices regarding sexual orientation, gender identity, or gender expression, clear expectations and guidelines were needed for the district's students and staff to create an inclusive approach for transgender students in order that they may be provided equal educational opportunities.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves for adoption upon second reading proposed new Policy 3213, Transgender Students.
  7.17 Approval of Rescission of Revisions to Policy 5010, Affirmative Action and Nondiscrimination
  Proposed rescission of amendments to Policy 5010, Affirmative Action and Nondiscrimination, was provided for approval by the board of directors. In the recent adoption of revised Policy 5010, Affirmative Action and Nondiscrimination, a question was raised about the deletion of marital status and age. Upon further review, it was determined that changes were inadvertently made and the revised April 2013 version was accurate. The superintendent proposes that the policy revisions approved on May 24, 2016 be rescinded and that the policy revert to the April 2013 version.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors rescinds the revisions to Policy 5010, Affirmative Action and Nondiscrimination approved on May 24, 2016.
  Recommendation: That the board of directors approves the Consent Agenda as presented.
