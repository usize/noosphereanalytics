Puyallup School District
Regular Board Meeting
Ballou Junior High
Monday, February 18, 2020
      1. Opening
A. Call to Order
President Kathy Yang called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. In addition to President Yang, also in attendance were Directors Turan Kayaoglu, Michael Keaton, Maddie Names and Joseph Romero.  The superintendent's designee, Dr. Vincent Pecchia, was excused.
B. Pledge of Allegiance
President Yang led the Pledge of Allegiance.
C. Modification of Agenda
None.
  2. Communications
A. District Highlights
Dr. Brian Fox said we have a lot of highlights tonight. We have a tradition of excellence in Puyallup. Tonight we will present specific evidence of excellence. He introduced Dr. Maija Thiel to talk about the CTE Teacher of the Year. Dr. Thiel introduced Rogers High School CTE teacher Janet Jepsen. She invited her family, friends and colleagues to stand to thank them for supporting her. Jepson transitioned from an amazing school nurse to a CTE Health Careers teacher at Rogers High School and Thiel highlighted the many reasons she was selected as CTE Teacher of the Year.

Dr. Fox next introduced our three Classified Employees of the Year:
Lisa Martin from Emerald Ridge High School
Rhonda Knudson from Human Resources
Carla Tyree from Maplewood Elementary
They received a round of applause and their administrators each explained why they were chosen. ERHS Principal Richard Lasso honored lab technician Lisa Martin. Maplewood Elementary Principal Susan Walton honored office manager Carla Tyree. Executive Director of Human Resources Ailene Baxter honored Recruiting Specialist Rhonda Knudsen.

Next the board honored four staff members who successfully completed a rigorous professional certification process to become National Board Certified:
Kathryn Alexander from Dessie Evans Elementary
Anastasia Church from Walker High School
Jacob Johnson from Ferrucci Junior High
Cassandra Zancanella from Firgrove Elementary
Leanne McKillip and Romy Haney, our National Board Certified Teacher Advisors, were also recognized.
  Finally, Dr. Fox said AVID is a program of which we are quite proud. In years past, we have celebrated with Woodland Elementary and Karshner Elementary for earning the title of AVID School of Distinction and tonight we celebrate a third school in our district to have earned this title, Rogers High School. Principal Jason Smith told the board that it is with a tremendous amount of hard work from our staff and support from our district that RHS was awarded the prestigious title of being an ‘AVID school of distinction. They are one of four high schools in the state to earn this award.
  Smith then highlighted individual staff members:
Dr. Cassie Mueller, whose dissertation featured the AVID strategies and it was found that AVID students were quite successful on school tests and in life beyond high school as a direct result of the AVID experience.
Valerie Waring, AVID tutor
Jeet Brar, AVID tutor
Jeriko Iese, AVID tutor
Lori Tierney, RHS counselor
Colleen Pancake, AVID teacher

None.

  4. Second Reading of Policies
A. Executive Administration - Second reading and consideration for approval of Policy 3207 - Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying, and Policy 3211 - Gender-Inclusive Schools; Casey Cox, Assistant Superintendent
Cox presented these to the board for first reading on February 3. Tonight is the second reading. The update to Policy 3207 - Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying basically clarifies language in each policy and provides definitions specific to the HIV policy. The update to Policy 3211 - Gender-Inclusive Schools changes language from "transgender students" to "gender-inclusive schools".
  A motion was made to approve Policy 3207 - Prohibition of Harassment, Intimidation and Bullying, and Policy 3211 - Gender-Inclusive Schools, as presented.
  Motion by Maddie D Names, second by Turan Kayaoglu.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Turan Kayaoglu, Michael D Keaton, Maddie D Names, Joseph Romero, Kathy Yang
  5. Action
A. Executive Administration - 2020-21 Board Meeting schedule; Kathy Yang, Board President
President Yang explained that each year at about this time the board creates the schedule of regular board meetings and work sessions for the following year. Next year's schedule follows the same pattern of Monday evening meetings at 6 p.m. with two regular meetings per month except for the months of April, July and December due to school breaks.
  A motion was made to approve the 2020-21 Board Meeting schedule, as presented.
  Motion by Michael D Keaton, second by Joseph Romero.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Turan Kayaoglu, Michael D Keaton, Maddie D Names, Joseph Romero, Kathy Yang
  6. Board Comments
A. Individual Board Member Comments
Director Romero enjoys seeing the district recognize folks. He had the opportunity to meet three out of four of the Classified Employees of the Year and said we can never get too consumed in our work to not recognize what other people do. He really appreciates being able to see what others. They aren't the only deserving people, and he appreciates what everyone does. Romero said he had the chance to attend the WASA Legislative Conference in Olympia and heard State Superintendent Chris Reykdal share ideas on the future of education and said it is really interesting to see how our state is going to approach education in the future.

Director Keaton said it is great to see how well the people we recognized have done and how they are supported by their family, coworkers and friends tonight.

Director Kayaoglu said he has been thinking about what does public education mean? Schools make us public. He was thinking about this issue while he was watching the election for bonds in our neighboring districts.  Tacoma is so happy they passed theirs. We failed our bond. Thinking of our 'Tradition of Excellence,' he said you cannot support excellence on a shoe string budget. If you are public, if you are a community, you are obliged to provide the best opportunities of our students, to recruit and support the best staff, teachers and administrators that are out there. We need to do better. We cannot just sit back in our houses and hope someone else will pay for our public schools.  These are our schools, and we need to pay for them.  He would also like to acknowledge that our friends in Tacoma did an amazing job and hopefully we will have the courage and the support of the public to pass our bond and the levies in the future, so we will realize our tradition of excellence.
  Director Names thanked the students who are here tonight, and hoped they experienced a really good, meaningful part of what we do as public servants to help serve students and our community.  She gave kudos to administrators and knows it is hard to choose between all the classified staff, but they are amazing, and they are definitely the cream, and they've risen to the top.  
  President Yang said the high school students in the audience came to a good meeting because we got to celebrate some great people in the district, and we have so many of them that it is nice we could take time to celebrate and thank them.
  7. Closing
