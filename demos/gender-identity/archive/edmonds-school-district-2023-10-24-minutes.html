    9. PUBLIC COMMENTS-7:05 pm
  Information: 9.1 Audience Comments
  Melissa Boswell, teacher at Scriber Lake High and ESD parent, spoke to the board about the institutional barriers students at Scriber Lake High School face. She empahsized the importance of the Scriber Lake students having a new school campus that would create equal opportunity for the students.

Isabel Shinnick-Gordon, teacher at Scriber Lake High School, spoke about how the students at Scriber Lake have limited science resources availabe to them. She encouraged the board to reconsider adding Scriber Lake to the proposed bond. 

Anthony Shapiro, community member, posed questions to the board about the proposed bond. He stated that he would like to know more about the construction costs involved and the interest rate on the bonds.

Kory DeMun, community member, spoke in favor of the proposed bond and capital/teechnology levy. He encouraged interested community members to volunteer with the "Yes for Kids" campaign working to pass the bond/levy.

Sarah Dilling, ESD parent, shared that her student performed well on the SBA tests, but both she and her student were unaware of the results. She enouraged the district to review how SBA results are communicated with students.

Jason Moore, community member, encouraged parents to remove students from the school district as he does not believe it serves the will of the people.

  16. BOARD MEMBER COMMENTS
  Information: 16.1 Board Member Comments
  Vivian Guillen, student advisor, thanked the College Place Middle school students for their presentation. She was impressed by how the school was able to make students feel a sense of belonging and engagement through their house and Character Strong programs.

Kevin Seng, student advisor, shared updates about the work of the Robotics Team.

Carter Nguyen, student advisor, shared that he is happy to be here serving as a student advisor.

Magdalena Kedzierski, student advisor, shared that she believes moving 6th graders to middle school is a good idea as 6th graders are much more mature than younger elementary school students.

Amin-Erdene Lkhagvasuren, student advisor, shared why she thinks students learn better when they have better facilities.

Director Smith expressed frustrations that a student was confronted when trying to use a bathroom at the stadium.

Director Noble thanked the students from College Place Middle School for their presentation. He shared that he hopes the bond passes because new facilities are extremely important not only for learning but also for the safety of our students.

Director Chase acknowledged the concerns of the Scriber Lake High School staff and noted that we hear their concerns and want to work together to resolve the issues.

Director Kilgore thanked College Place Middle School for their wonderful presentation. She shared that she also serves as a school board liaison to the NW Interscholastisc Activities Association and is excited for the work that group will be doing this fall.

Director Katims shared that the board was named a "Board of Distinction" by WSSDA. She noted that she visited Meadowdale Middle School today and the students were very engaged with their work and were using advanced technology to learn. She thanked College Place Middle School for their presentation and for sharing data with the board. She also thanked the Facilities Advisory Committee for their work planning the bond. Lastly, she shared that she is working on an advocacy campaign to reach out to our legislators with a request for additional funding for schools.

Dr. Miner reminded everyone to vote on November 7th. She offered to provide an informational presentation to any community groups who are interested in learning more about the bond/ levy. She congratulated the board on being named a board of distinction.
