Board Development and Linkage
Superintendent’s Update
Superintendent Chamberlin began his update by informing the Board of Directors of upcoming technology changes
to the Boardroom that will take place over the next several months. He continued by reminding the Board that every
February, UPSD staff are asked to notify the District of their intent to return for the upcoming school year. This time
of year, there is an increase of resignations and retirements on the consent agenda due to this process. Next
Superintendent Chamberlin notified the Board of Directors that the annual audit is underway. Additionally, the
District continues to meet with the Construction Services Group (CSG) on a proposal to bring to the Board in
preparation of a potential bond in 2026. Lastly, Superintendent Chamberlin invited Jeff Loupas, Assistant
Superintendent, and Willie Keith, Executive Director of Primary Education, to address the Board.
Mr. Loupas and Mr. Keith provided the Board of Directors with a brief presentation about transition kindergarten.
They explained that providing a pre-kindergarten program would provide greater access to District services to
families of young children. They explained recent changes in the legislature that would allow for the funding of
transitional kindergarten and how the new program would co-exist with University Place School District’s current
preschool program. The Board of Directors had an opportunity to ask questions about transition kindergarten.
Board Development
The Board of Directors had the opportunity to share board development opportunities they have recently participated
in or plan to participate in. Marisa Peloquin shared upcoming training opportunities from the Washington State
School Directors Association (WSSDA). She continued by sharing the initial results of recent levy and bond
initiatives in school districts across the state. Director Peloquin thanked the University Place School District staff for
their diligence in maintaining financial health for the organization.
Marisa Peloquin continued by providing a legislative update to the Board of Directors. She referenced a few bills
related to education that are still being discussed in the legislature.
Rick Maloney asked that Board of Directors about a possible study session topic he sent out through email. The
topic of the proposed session is the promotion of STEM education with female students. Director Maloney
continued by sharing that he had been invited to a talk with local Boy Scout Troop 148 about serving on a school
board. The conversation was part of a community service badge the troop is working towards.
