Blaine School District No. 503

Monday, October 28, 2019
  Minutes
  1. Regular Board Meeting
A. Call to Order
President Freal called the meeting to order at 7:00 p.m.
All Directors were in attendance.

President Freal led in the Pledge of Allegiance.
  C. Board Comments on the Agenda
Director McKinney asked to move the BEA MOU from the consent agenda to action items to allow for discussion. Dr. Granger added that it will become the first action item on the agenda.
  2. Good News
A. Recognition Item
Blaine High School's Theater Arts students were recognized for completing the rigors of induction to become an official International Thespian Troupe #8941. Theater Arts Teacher Shari Akers explained the efforts to achieve this recognition which have been made over the past 20 years. Payton Ives described the competition experiences and the personal importance of this accomplishment. President Freal presented Troupe #8941 with certificates from the Board and from the International Thespian Society.
  3. Reports
A. High School Student Report
Students representing Blaine High School's Link Crew attended the meeting to share information about their group. Mackenzie Monogue, Colton Pederson, Odelia Dahl, and Tayler Lee described the purpose of Link Crew and activities for the year. Each shared their reasons for becoming Link Leaders. Their "huge goal" of mentorship was described in detail. Principal Scott Ellis added comments regarding noted improvements in school environment for incoming freshmen.
  B. School Administrator Reports
Brief reports were provided by administrators from the following schools:
Blaine Primary School experienced high attendance at the September 26 Open House. Principal Bakarich reported the positive impact of visiting other campuses in September and October. She shared a "Feeling Buddies" example being used as a Conscious Discipline strategy. The K-5 Blaine Thrives program is starting its third week, and a quote was shared from a student who has benefited from this after school opportunity. Director McKinney asked about attendance, and Ms. Bakarich reported that it has varied from 40 to 75 students.
Blaine Elementary School's Walkathan raised $25,000 for the PTO which provides funding for variety of academic and enrichment opportunities. On October 15, 5th grade students attended the Compass to Campus kickoff at Western Washington University. Fountas and Pinnell materials have arrived, and Principal Baldwin described teachers' enthusiasm for implementing this new curriculum.
Blaine Middle School's Boys Soccer and 8th Grade softball finished the fall season undefeated. The PTSO purchased a 3D printer for the Middle School library which will connect with project based learning through PLTW. Highly Capable training was described and included ten teachers. The leadership club recently hosted a special lunch for students new to Blaine Middle School. Principal Benson described the Friday Food Bags program which provides weekend nutritional support to students in need. 
Blaine High School's October 18 ribbon cutting ceremony was well attended. CEL training in Math and ELA took place in October, as well as Threat Assessment Training for administrators and key staff. College in the High School registration takes place this week. Principal Ellis shared other good news, including the start of Special Olympics Bowling for Life Skills students, and Senior Ingrid Aosved being named a Commended Scholar by the National Merit Scholarship Program. The directors were invited to attend the upcoming Veterans Day assembly, as well as the drama program's production of XANADU in November.
C. Teaching & Learning Report
CTE Director Dawn Cottnair provided the Board with a report on the District's 5-Year CTE Plan. She described the state's mandated requirements for a secondary career and technical education program which include the development of new programs of study. The District's 5-year goals were reviewed in the areas of course offerings, curriculum, and instructional spaces. Expansion of Industry Recognized Certificate coursework is desired. New facilities at Blaine High School offer excellent opportunities for CTE instruction. Goals for increased teacher certification include having conditional CTE certifications become continuing certifications. Director McKinney shared comments regarding Whatcom Community College's pre-engineering and cyber security programs. Ms. Cottnair explained the two-year transition into the full Perkins V, and she described the funding eligibility requirement to continue growth in programs of study which will build the District's capacity to provide these programs for kids.
  D. Enrollment Update
Executive Director of Finance and Operations Amber Porter reported on enrollment for the month of October. Running Start enrollment has caused a decline from last month's total in general K-12 enrollment. Overall, the District remains at 11 FTE above budget, with more than expected in Running Start and 4.7 FTE fewer than expected in the general K-12 count.
  E. Budget Status and Financial Report
Amber Porter provided a review of revenue and expenses for the month of September which remained fairly typical. She explained a correction to the revenue as percent of budget on page 3 of the Financial Report which should be 6.78% for the month of September, with a revenue budget amount of $36,261,749.
  F. Capital Projects Update
Amber Porter and Alan Pomeroy, Maintenance and Operations Supervisor, reviewed Capital Projects progress. Pictures were reviewed of the stadium complex where the roof is nearing completion and underlying structures are almost finished. In the high school's core facility, punch list items continue to be addressed, including electrical odds and ends. FLO Analytics will facilitate a community input session in the District boardroom from 6-8pm on Wednesday, October 30, regarding the purchase of property in Birch Bay.
  G. Superintendent's Report
Dr. Christopher Granger provided an update on current District activities.
  Priority policy updates are being identified, including those related to the District's upcoming migration to Google and related requirements for staff and student boundaries and social media use. Dr. Granger will ask WSSDA to assist the District in a policy review process which will take place over the next two years to identify and prioritize needed policy updates.
  Preparations are underway for the District's transition of email services to Google for Education which is currently scheduled on December 20. This will allow for easier integration of Google Classroom, Google Drive, and Google Calendar. Board members will receive a Google log-in prior to the transition.
  This is a non-budget year for the state legislative session. Supplemental budget proposals are due to the Governor by December 20, and OSPI has made proposals in overarching areas. Dr. Granger described OSPI's request for a redesign of the funding model.

The annual WSSDA Conference will take place next month in Bellevue, and Dr. Granger looks forward to the opportunity to learn together and bring back knowledge to benefit the District.

At the September meeting, Director McKinney asked about ASB/Student ID cards. Dr. Granger reported the following information in response to this request:
The Middle School has not been charging an ASB fee this year and all students in grades 6-8 will receive an ASB card at no cost. There is an annual athletic fee of $60.
The cost of the High School ASB card provides funding support equally to athletics and the general ASB, including the costs of game officials, event workers, dance advisors, assemblies/speakers, care weeks, and clubs. Possession of an ASB card provides the student with discount dance tickets, free admission to home athletic events, and half-price admission at away games. High School athletic fees are $25 first sport, $20 second sport, and $15 third sport (or $8 for free/reduced).
Dr. Granger described WAC 392-145-031 which prohibits bus drivers from distributing anything edible to students unless allowed by District policy. For the 2019-20 school year, bus drivers will be allowed to hand out candy on specific special occasions with the approval of the Transportation supervisor and the superintendent. Rachel Hrutfiord is working on a program for future years, which will be supported by campuses, for “Bus Bucks” with non-food rewards.

Later in this meeting, the Board will take action on approval of the individual recommended for the Public Relations Specialist position. Additional support is still needed for upcoming major transitions in the technical accounting area, which is anticipated to be a 24-hour per week position. Work is also underway on the posting of a K-5 behavior position to help address the needs of students.

The District has proposed purchasing field turf, inclusive of installation, through a State of Washington (KCDA) approved vendor, precluding the need to pursue a formal bid. Later in this meeting, the Board will consider approval of a recommendation authorizing the District to enter into a contract for this purchase. Installation is expected to begin on April 1, 2020 in order to accommodate completion prior to the start of football practices and games in the Fall of 2020. The District's Athletic Department is working with Ferndale High School to coordinate use of their track for practices after April 1st through the Spring sports season. A mock-up image of the field turf design was displayed.
  A summary update was provided on the 2021-2024 Levy initiative which will be placed before voters in the February 11, 2020 Special Election. This levy will replace the current Maintenance & Operations Levy. Because it is now know as an enrichment levy, it will be necessary to provide voter education to be clear that this replacement levy still provides funding in support of 15.4% of the District's budget. At the November meeting, the Board will consider adoption of a levy resolution.

  C. Facility Use Fee Schedule
At the August 26, 2019 meeting, the Board took action to suspend implementation of the 2019-20 Facility Use Fee Schedule pending additional analysis of the new fee schedule's impact. Directors McKinney and Berge participated in the work of the Facility Rental Fees Review Committee which included representation from multiple community user groups. Dr. Granger thanked the committee participants for their time and effort. Based upon the work of the committee, Dr. Granger provided a recommendation for the Board's consideration which he read aloud. The recommendation acknowledged community groups' desire to use the facilities for free, but reiterated the District's obligation to ensure that public funds intended for education are not being used for other purposes. New changes to the fee schedule were reviewed and discussion followed. Gymnasium and field use for the months of August 2019 through December 2019 will be charged for Group C at a flat rate of $150/month, with new rates beginning on January 1, 2020.
  Multiple audience members expressed a desire to comment on this topic, however the opportunity for public comment had passed. Director McKinney asked how the new rate of $3/hour compared to fees paid by users in Group C during the 2018-19 school year. Dr. Granger shared that, out of 10 community groups, 8 groups would see a reduction in fees at the new rate and two groups would see an increase in fees. A scheduling meeting was held earlier this month at which time efforts were made for groups to share gym space and work collaboratively. Committee member Katy Smith spoke up from the audience and indicated disagreement with the recommendation. Dr. Granger indicated that actual costs for facility use should be ascertained before the District revisits this issue.The Board voted unanimously to implement the 2019-20 Facility Use Fee Schedule as recommended by the Superintendent.
Motion by Todd A Berge, second by Laura McKinney
Final Resolution: Motion Carries 5-0
Yea: John Freal, Todd A Berge, Joan Lotze, Charles E Gibson, Laura McKinney
