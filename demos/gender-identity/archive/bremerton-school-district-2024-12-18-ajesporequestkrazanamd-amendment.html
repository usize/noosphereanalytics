For almost 30 years in Washington State, Krazan has been providing services to education facilities
from K-12 to higher educational institutions. We have found that it is best to limit distractions as

Krazan understands that the Bremerton School District has requested these surveys as a matter of
due diligence prior to renovation and demolition activities. The survey for Armin Jahr Elementary
will be based on the proposed complete demolition of the building. The surveys for the remaining
seven facilities will be limited to the proposed HVAC duct work from roof level to the adjoining
spaces below. Current federal, state, and local regulations require that property owners, prior to
renovation and/or demolition work on existing properties, conduct surveys to locate and identify
hazardous or potentially hazardous materials. The scope of these limited hazmat surveys have been
client-defined and is intended to provide additional information to the Bremerton School District as

Limited Hazmat Survey

Krazan proposes to conduct the limited hazmat surveys to identify suspect asbestos-containing
materials (ACM) and lead-based paints (LBP), which may be impacted during any future renovations
and/or demolition. To comply with the current requirements of federal, state, and local regulatory
agencies, Krazan anticipates that the Hazmat surveys will involve destructive sampling techniques
and selective demolition to access hidden or concealed suspect ACM/LBP. However, it is
understood that the sampling will be limited in scope based on the approval of the property owner,
and areas defined by the client. The property owner will verify and authorize selective areas for
destructive sampling. If areas of suspect ACM/LBP are unattainable for sampling, those areas will be
included in the report as presumed ACM/LBP and will require sampling before any renovations and/
or demolition. Repair of selective demolition areas is beyond the scope of these services.

Krazan proposes to perform this work ona time and expense basis. Krazan will collect and submit
samples of all suspect ACM/LBP encountered at the sites to an accredited and approved laboratory.
Upon receipt of laboratory results, Krazan will prepare a written report summarizing findings,
conclusions, and recommendations. Krazan will only include areas of the structures that have been
approved by the owner of the property and defined by the client. These surveys will be conducted
by an Asbestos Hazard Emergency Response Act (AHERA) Building Inspector.

Other Potentially Hazardous Materials

Krazan will conduct a limited visual inspection of the referenced spaces for other hazardous or
potentially hazardous materials that may impact proposed future renovation and/or demolition.
Krazan will inspect a representative number of potentially polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs)-
containing fluorescent light ballasts. Krazan will also visually inspect for potentially PCB-containing
electrical transformers as well as potential freon-containing equipment and mercury-containing
thermostats. Other potentially hazardous building materials will be identified as "suspect" by visual
identification only. No special/specific testing protocols are proposed at this time. This survey

will be conducted by an AHERA Building Inspector experienced in the identification of potentially
hazardous materials.
