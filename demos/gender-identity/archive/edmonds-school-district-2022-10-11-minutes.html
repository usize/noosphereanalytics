    10. PUBLIC COMMENTS- 7:00 pm
  Information: 10.1 Audience Comments
  Jeanne Petty, ESD parent, thanked the board for supporting gender inclusive classrooms, Black Lives Matter and ethnic studies. She reiterated that the Board's policies follow all of OSPI's guidelines and are mandated at the state level.

Bob Fretz, Community member, spoke about the history of Christopher Columbus and slavery in North America.

Tony Shapiro, Community member, spoke about the nature of truth.

Enika Lynch, ESD parent, spoke about her concerns with gender inclusive bathrooms.

Megan Wolfe, ESD parent, thanked the district for their work creating an equitable environment. She encouraged more work around Black Lives Matter, Indigeous students and Pride Month.

Cindy Whiteman, community member, spoke in support of letting students express themselves. She encouraged the board to focus on educating students to become responsible citizens.

Sarah Dilling, ESD parent, spoke in support of the district's inclusive policies. She noted that the district follows the state laws when shaping all of its policies.

Saundra Whittemore, ESD employee and parent, clarified the types of work being done by our school nurses and thanked them for their work.

Kairi Kuritani, ESD employee, noted that gender inclusive school policies are mandated by the state of Washington.

Melissa Von Aschen-Cook, community member, spoke about attending Pride events and how these organizations provide support to those who may not have it elsewhere.

Christy Robertson, community member, discussed inclusive restrooms. She encouraged modifying restrooms so that everyone has the privacy they need.

    11. REPORTS
  Information: 11.1 Smarter Balanced Assessment (SBA) Results- Spring 2022
  Austin Mueller, Director of Assessment, Research and Evaluations presented a report on the district's SBA and WCAS results. He discussed the participation rates and percentage of students meeting standards, both in Edmonds and statewide. The results of students in the Edmonds School district were reviewed in comparison to comparable districts in the state, and they were broken down by demographics.

Helen Joung, Assistant Superintendent talked about how SBA data is used by principals an school staff to target instructional improvements.

Lisa Gonzalez, Executive Director of Student Learning, discussed the work of the student learning department and what they are doing to support teachers through curriculum and professional development.

Director Kilgore expressed her concerns about learning loss during Covid and asked what we are doing to remedy this. Assistant Superintendent Joung discussed the measurable strategic plan goals that the whole district is focused on and how these measurable goals will help us to improve.

Elizabeth Lopez, student advisor to the board, discussed student participation on the SBA tests and how the test is not required for every graduation pathway.

Peter Garcia, student advisor to the board, noted that students don't always understand the value of the tests since they aren't always tied to graduation.

Ohanna Jimenez, student advisor to the board, agreed that students sometimes don't see the value of the tests
