  Director of Instructional Leadership and Athletics, Rick Wells, presented the sports highlights from this past fall season and said we had a number of individuals and teams qualify for state. Team representatives and coaches came forward for recognition.

Fox introduced a special guest student from Brouillet Elementary, Braden Benner, and Mr. Sam Sharpe, Assistant Principal, and . Mr. Sharpe discussed the impact of a special program called Welcoming Schools at Brouillet Elementary. Sharpe said Welcoming Schools is an inclusive approach to addressing family diversity, gender stereotyping and name calling in K-5 learning environments.  The program emphasizes that schools should be places where all children are safe. At Brouillet, they are fostering an environment to encourage and teach students to build positive, respectful and caring relationships with their peers. Over the past two years since the implementation of Welcoming Schools, they have noticed a significant decrease in abusive language, bullying, teasing and other forms of harrassment amongst students through practices in this program, however, sometimes students with medical conditions are treated differently.  Kids can either ignore or mock their differences.  But Braden Benner set an example recently that we are very proud of. While Braden was at recess, he supported a student during a medical situation when he caught a student who was falling, easing his fall, and Braden's quick actions prevented the student from hitting the ground and getting hurt. Braden took his coat off and placed it under the student's head and comforted the student until an adult arrived to help. His act of kindness is a model not only for students but for adults, to decide to help and not be a bystander.  With kids like Braden, we can be a welcoming and caring school where kids choose to do the right thing and support their classmates.  Sharpe said it is a great pleasure to introduced Braden Benner; we are proud of Braden and appreciate your quick action in helping your fellow schoolmate.  Fox thanked Brayden for stepping up and doing the right thing. Braden shook hands with the board members and Dr. Yeomans. Fox also recognized Braden's family and principal Nancy Strobel from the audience.

  7. Reports
A. Operations - Bond Oversight Committee Report #2 - Mario Casello, chief operations officer
Dr. Yeomans introduced Mario Casello to provide an update on the progresso of the Bond Oversight Committee (BOC).  Casello reported the BOC had their third meeting last week. They reviewed the 2014 lifecycle levy projects. This is one of the areas the committee focuses on, not only the 2015 bond projects, the five schools, but also the levy project and any future recommendations. This last meeting they focused on revisiting the 2014 life cycle projects. He presented a list of the schedule and those the committee is recommending to accellerate. Seismic projects at four schools: Shaw Road, Brouillet, Hunt and Zeiger elementaries. Each is identical to the other. Now they need to be upgraded, which involves the walls and some of the piping throughout the school. We don't need to do Shaw Road as it is already being done in the current project. Additionally, also looked at Shaw Road interior improvements. We had planned on replacing carpet at Shaw Road, but the carpet is still in wonderful condition, so the staff would like to change that scope of work and repaint the entire school instead, which is what it needs most. We are recommending to the board to not replace the carpet and to paint instead. The third recommendation is the Karshner Museum HVAC system. There are many times you hear the loud thumping noise of the HVAC system, which is steam-driven. The pipes there are shot, and many of the components of the system are over 100 years old. There are many leaks and issues that are not serviceable or repairable anymore, so the committee is recommending we replace the entire HVAC system in the Karshner Center.  Casello presented some of the facts and data of the museum, including that just this last year we had 371 meetings, had over 4,000 students visit, and over 1,600 guests, so a total of 5,500 folks had meetings, field trips or visited just this year last. We already have 88 meetings scheduled for this year.  The Karshner Center and Museum is used a lot. We have $1.5 million in artifacts stored there, which are in jeopardy if we don't maintain proper storage temperatures.  We are also recommending cooler tower replacements at Waller Road Elementary, Ferrucci Junior High and Mt. View Elementary. These are all over 30 years old and pretty much every day we are sending in a plumber or HVAC technician to try to repair the many issues, so we would like to replace those cooling towers at those three schools. These were on the 2014 Levy Schedule.

Casello asked if there were any questions about these recommendations by the Bond Oversight Committee.  There were no questions at this time.
  B. Business Services - October 31, 2016 Financial Statement: Laura Marcoe, Director of Business Services
Dr. Yeomans again introduced Director of Business Services, Laura Marcoe, who presented the October Financial Statement report. Marcoe reported this month the operating budget has been added to the financial statements. The percentage of budget calculations are now based off the operating budget which is based on actual fall enrollment, rather than the adopted budget. This is now a more accurate reflection of what our spending is for the year. The operating budget is basically all the adjustments we've had to make since the board adopted the budget in the summer, such as revenue and expenditure changes based on actual fall enrollment, any bargaining agreements that may have made an impact, also adjusted revenue if needed for our recertified levy and the recalculation of our levy equalization revenue. Those are just some of the examples but it is basically now a more accurate reflection of what our spending is for the year.
  Looking at the transportation vehicle fund, we've received our first $2 million check from the insurance related to the buses we lost in the fire. This isn't the only check we will receive; we expect to make additional claims.

It is now fall tax collection season. We collected $16.8 million in the General Fund, and $9.4 million in the Debt Service fund. One of the figures that is not on the slide is we received $330,000 in the Capital Projects fund due to our technology levy.  The General Fund will see our fund balance spike and then slowly decline until the spring collection.  In the Debt Service Fund, there is a big spike until we pay our fiscal year's first principal and interest payment on our bond debt, which was December 1.

All funds this fiscal year are operating as expected. No new enrollment figures yet for December, so this is the same graph you saw last time.  She asked if the board members had any questions but there were no questions.

President Looker thanked Marcoe for her presentation.
