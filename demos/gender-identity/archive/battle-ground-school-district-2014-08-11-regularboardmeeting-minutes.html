board sub-committee that was formed on January 13, 2014 by the Board of Directors.

committee is charged to:
1. Provide oversight with the external auditor,
2. Review and approve the superintendent contract, timesheets, reimbursements, cell phone bills,
leave buybacks, and other expenditures,
3. Provide communication to other board members if needed,
4. Provide management a copy of the superintendent's contract for legal and compliance review prior
to finalization,

Public Content
Per Policy 2320, student travel requests require board approval if they are overnight trips. Advisors are
required to submit all required paperwork at least two weeks in advance to Linda Gellings, Director of
Business Services and Risk Management. Advisors must clear the trip with their building principal prior to
submitting the request to Risk Management. Advisors are required to submit itineraries, budget
information, transportation information and learning objectives along with the dates of travel and
information on the group of students traveling with chaperones.

Exceptions to the rule are travel requests for state and national tournaments and competitions. The
procedures for these types of trips is outlined in Procedure 2320P. Often the tournaments come up
quickly and there is not enough time to present the trip to the board prior to the trip. In this case, advisors
must submit all paperwork to the Risk Management Officer prior to the trip for approval. The trip is then
ratified by board members at their next regularly scheduled board meeting.
Student travel requests are attached.

Lynn

Meeting :

Aug 11, 2014 - MINUTES - Regular Board
Meeting

Category :

J. New Business

Type :

Action

Recommended Action :

The Board of Directors approve Regence Blue Cross as a

medical insurance provider for Battle Ground School
District employees.

Public Content
Presenter:
MaryBeth Lynn, Assistant Superintendent of Finance and School Operations
In March 2014, the District went out to bid for medical insurance to offer employees the opportunity to reduce their out-of-pocket
costs of premiums. Bids were received from Regence Blue Cross, Moda, Providence and United Healthcare. In addition,
Premera Blue Cross through WEA notified the district of its intention to increase premiums 7.9% for the 2014-15 school year.
The Districts insurance committee, with representatives from each labor group, met in June and reviewed the proposals with the
Districts insurance broker, Mercer. The insurance committee interviewed Regence Blue Cross because its rates were 1% above
the current 2013-14 Premera Blue Cross rates and mirrored the Premera plan, impacting employees the least with no impact to
the district. The insurance committee members polled each of their groups and all groups elected to select Regence Blue Cross
as the recommended medical insurance provider, instead of WEA Premera Blue Cross.
Kaiser Permanente remains the other medical insurance provider. By switching from WEA Premera to Regence, the district was
able to add an additional Kaiser option to help reduce costs to employees insured by Kaiser.

The Board of Directors approve the bid award for
Warehouse Consumables #1214-09 for the Battle Ground
School District to School Specialty and authorizes MaryBeth
Lynn to sign Contract #1214-09 dated September 01, 2014
through August 31, 2015.

Public Content
The District advertised for bids on June 24, 2014 and July 1, 2014 in the Columbian and Daily Journal of Commerce for
warehouse consumables. Three vendors requested the bid packet and one vendor submitted a bid. The Districts current
Warehouse Consumables Contract #09-1005 with School Specialty was awarded in July 2009 and terminated automatically on
July 31, 2014. School Specialty is honoring the expired contract pricing until a new contract for consumables is awarded by the
District.

This is a one year contract with the option to renew for 4 additional one year periods. There are no price increases at renewal
unless it can be supported by a manufacturers increase in pricing.
This bid is a market basket of the districts most requested warehouse items. Purchasing analyzes the inventory items and
utilizes this bid, the KCDA (King County Directors Association) purchasing cooperative and Office Depot through the TCPN
purchasing cooperative for the majority of the bulk of district consumable purchases to receive the lowest pricing.

Price Exhibit A is lower than market comparisons ranging 2.5% - 27.7% less depending on the consumable.
For some items, the new pricing proposed by School Specialty decreased by as much as 25% from the expiring contract.
Price Exhibit B NW 1500 is the top 1500 items most commonly ordered. The School Specialty response included Price Exhibit BNW1500 in order to maintain the consistency of P-Card purchases. The NW 1500 was adopted under the old contract #09-1005
in order to control P-Card purchases of common items.
