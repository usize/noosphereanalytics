Overview a

The Tukwila School District remains committed to ensuring a
positive and productive educational and working environment that
is free from discrimination, including sexual harassment. This
report summarizes the district’s adherence to Board Policy 5011
and related Student Policy 3205, highlighting key questions,
learnings, and actions to be taken in the upcoming academic year.


Key Questions YF

¢ How effective have our current training programs been in preventing sexual harassment?

° Analysis: To assess the effectiveness of the training programs, feedback was gathered from staff, students, and
volunteers through the Title IX task surveys and informal listening sessions. The goal was to determine whether
the training effectively communicated the nature of sexual harassment and the appropriate channels for
reporting it.

¢ Are all reports of sexual harassment being addressed promptly and thoroughly?

° Analysis: Reviewing the timelines and outcomes of reported incidents helps us understand whether the district is
meeting its commitment to promptly investinate and resolve complaints. This includes an evaluation of the time
taken from the initial report to the final resolution.

¢ What measures are in place to protect individuals from retaliation after reporting sexual
harassment?
¢ Analysis: An evaluation of the district’s anti-retaliation measures was conducted to ensure that those reporting

or involved in investigations feel safe and supported. The assessment included checking for any complaints of
retaliation and the district’s response to such complaints.

¢ How well are staff and students aware of their rights and responsibilities under the sexual
harassment policies?

¢ Analysis: Awareness was measured by the Title IX Task Force and informal listening sessions that assessed the
understanding of sexual harassment policies, the reporting process, and knowledge of available resources.

Training and Awareness

Ongoing training / awareness program through
Safe Schools has been effective to an extent,
with increased understanding and recognition of
what constitutes sexual harassment. However,
feedback indicates a need for more frequent
and interactive updates and refreshers to
reinforce the message and keep up with any
policy changes.

Reporting and Investigation Procedures

While most reports are handled promptly, some
cases have shown delays due to coordination
issues within the investigation process.
Streamlining communication and clearly
defining expectations can improve the efficiency
and responsiveness of investigations.

Retaliation Concerns

There were no reports of retaliation in the past
year, indicating that current protective measures
may be effective. Regardless, continual monitoring
and open communication channels are essential to
maintain trust and safety for all involved parties.

Policy Communication

Although policies are posted in district buildings
and included in staff and parent/guardian
handbooks, not all employees and students are
fully aware of the specific procedures for reporting
sexual harassment. Increasing visibility and
accessibility in accessible language formats of this
information is vital.


Recommendations

Enhance Training Programs: During the 2024-25 school year, HR will implement more frequent training
sessions and develop interactive online modules, as brainstormed by the Title IX Task Force student
group, to ensure ongoing awareness and understanding among all district staff and students.

Increase Visibility of Reporting Procedures: By September 20, 2024 school and department leaders will
clearly display reporting procedures in common areas, distribute informational brochures, and conduct
regular informational sessions to remind staff and students of their rights and responsibilities.

. Streamline Investigation Process: By October 31, 2024, HR will review and improve the internal
processes to minimize delays and ensure a prompt response to all sexual harassment complaints.

Monitor and Evaluate Retaliation Policies: During the 2024-25 school year, HR will continue to monitor
for any signs of retaliation and review the effectiveness of current anti-retaliation measures regularly.

Concluding Ideas A;

This annual report outlines the Tukwila School District’s efforts to
actively enforce policies against sexual harassment and provides a basis
for continued improvement in maintaining a harassment-free
environment. The work production of the Title IX task force from the
2023-24 school year is key in creating and sustaining an environment
that protects against the harm caused by sexual harassment.
