available to answer questions from the Board. Piper Sandler will discuss potential bond scenarios and

Board Policy 1800, OE-9, Communication and Support to the Board,directs the Superintendent to advise the Board of recent changes in
assumptions upon which board policy has been established.

At the Board's regular meeting on September 26, 2024, the Board held a first reading of proposed
revisions to Board Policy 3205: Sexual Harassment of Students Prohibited. The revisions, presented by
Executive Director Erin Battersby, aim to bring the District into compliance with the 2024 Title IX
regulations, applicable to incidents occurring on or after August 1, 2024.
Following Board discussion, it was agreed that additional clarifications were needed regarding staff
reporting requirements. The Board reviewed the revised policy as a second reading on October 10, 2024.
The new procedures that will implement the revised policy were included for clarity as was a summary of
the procedural changes. Director Glowitz expressed an interest in further revisions to the policy, and the
Board agreed by consensus to put a third reading on the agenda at the next regular meeting. Markup and
clean versions of the revised policy are attached for Board review as part of this third reading.
The 2024 Title IX regulation expands the scope of sex-based discrimination to include not only sex
stereotypes and characteristics but also pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity. These
revisions align with Washington state laws prohibiting discrimination based on sexual orientation, gender
identity, and gender expressionchapters 28A.640,28A.642 RCWand 392-190 WAC.
The superintendent recommends formal action to approve the revised policy as presented.

National Merit Semifinalists
TheNational Merit Scholarship Programis an academic competition for recognition and scholarships that began in 1955. Approximately 1.3
million high school students enter the program each year. MIHS students enter the program by taking the Preliminary SAT (PSAT) in
October of their junior year. As a co-sponsor of the PSAT, student score reports are provided to the National Merit Scholarship Corporation.
Approximately 50,000 students earning the highest PSAT Selection Index Scores will qualify for recognition in the National Merit Scholarship
Program. These high-scoring students are notified in September of their senior year if they have qualified as either Commended Students or
Semifinalists. About two-thirds, or 34,000 of the 50,000 high-scoring students, receive Letters of Commendation in recognition of their
outstanding academic promise. The remaining one-third, or about 16,000 students, qualify as semifinalists and are invited to submit
applications to advance to finalist standing.

National PrincipalsMonth
Every year in October, theAmerican Federation of School Administrators(AFSA), theNational Association of Elementary School Principals
(NAESP), and theNational Association of Secondary School Principals(NASSP) join forces to celebrate our nations principals and assistant
/associate principals for their tireless dedication to their students and the schools they serve.National Principals Month gives all of us
anopportunity to say thank you to our District principals and associate principals and to share with the community all the great things that
principals do.Principals are among the hardest working, yet often least recognized, individuals in education. Principals set the academic tone
for their schools, and it is their vision, dedication, and determination that provide the mobilizing force for achieving student success.
On September 26, 2024, Governor Inslee proclaimed October 2024, Principals Month, to honor and recognize the contribution of principals
and assistant principals to the success of each student in Washington schools. Link here to read the proclamation.
Native American Heritage Month
November is recognized as Native American Heritage Month and provides an opportunityto recognize the significant contributions the first
Americans made to the establishment and growth of the United States. Native American Heritage Day will be celebrated on November 29,
2024, the day after Thanksgiving. For more information, including online exhibits andevents, visittheNative American Heritage Monthwebsite
and joinin paying tribute to the rich ancestry and traditions of Native Americans.
Veterans Day
Veterans Day is a national holiday recognized on November 11 honoring veterans of the armed forces and those killed in service to their
country. The observance originated in 1919 on the first anniversary of the 1918 armistice that ended World War I, which occurred on the
11th hour of the 11th day of the 11th month. November 11th became a federal holiday in the United States, celebrated as "Armistice Day,"
through an act of Congress approved on May 10,1938. In 1954, Congress amended the act to recognize "Veteran's Day," and, on June 1,
1954, President Dwight D. Eisenhower signed the legislation. Since that time, November 11th has been a day to honor American veterans of
all wars.
