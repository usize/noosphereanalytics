  3.3 Resolution No: 630 - Academic and Student Well-Being Plan
Superintendent Murphy and members of Cabinet presented to the Board Resolution No. 630 - Academic and Student Well-Being Plan.
  The American Rescue Plan (ARP) includes providing for Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief (ESSER) funds to eligible school districts to address academic recovery and learning loss. The same federal law authorizes the Office of the Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) to distribute such ESSER funds to Washington school districts via the Title I funding formula.
  In addition, to federal requirements for eligibility, House Bill (HB) 1368 – COVID – Federal Funding (2021) Sec 12 requires school districts to submit an Academic and Student Well-Being Recovery Plan to OSPI, using OSPI’s template, by June 1, 2021.
  New Model Resolution
To help districts comply with federal and state requirements and receive these ESSER funds, WSSDA has developed this model resolution. This is a model board resolution tailored to meet the federal and state law requirements and receive your ESSER funds.
  The federal and state laws do not specifically require a board resolution, but they do require that the school board “approve” the Academic and Student Well-Being Recovery Plan. Although the school board could simply vote to approve the plan, WSSDA recommend that the district use the model resolution to adopt the plan. The resolution clarifies that the school district has complied with the federal and state requirements, including that the school board considered and adopted the plan at an Open Public Meeting with the requisite public notice and with the opportunity for public comment. This model resolution helps your district meet the quick turnaround for submitting its plan, avoids complications or delays in receiving your funding, and reinforces the local school board’s authority.
  Academic and Student Well-Being Recovery Plan
  The district's recovery plan must include:
an equity analysis,
an attestation that you’ve posted the plan to your website,
a description of universal supports you’re providing to all students,
identification of the diagnostic assessments you are using or plan to use to assess your students’ academic and well-being needs,
a description of your engagement with students and families,
strategic supports you’re providing to students based on diagnostic assessment results,
how you will continually monitor progress, and
strategies or interventions you have implemented or plan to implement.
  The district will continue to monitor and update the plan as needed over the summer, into the fall, and throughout the 2021–22 school year. OSPI will be asking all districts to update their plan in November 2021 and again in April 2022. This will provide the district with time to collect data, monitor progress, make adjustments, and implement sustained improvements over time.
  Superintendent Murphy and the Board discussed additions to the plan including an addendum in the plan that allows the district to revisit the plan periodically over the summer especially with new guidance; add details specific to reflection and engagement; virtual academy details; individualized intervention, support, and assessment; MTSS specifics; survey data; and community input opportunities.
  The draft plan will be reviewed and discuss with the Board at May 20 Board Work Session.  The plan will be presented for final approval at the May 27 Board Meeting in preparation for the June 1 submission to OSPI.  As a requirement, the district will also post the adopted plan on our district website, making it accessible for those with disabilities and those in the community whose language is one other than English.
