  A. WSSDA 2022 Details & Discussion

  B. Policy 6022: Minimum Fund Balance Requirements Discussion
Dr. Middleton shared the amendments made in October with a goal of a 5% fund balance and shared that we will not meet that goal this year but will be close by about 1.5%. Most districts have the luxury of a generous fund balance, other districts have included in their policy wording in policy that says when they go into negotiations they will not go below 5%. Dr. Middleton explained the wording that already exists and encouraged continuing to make this a top priority.

Director Butler read the policy to remind everyone what it states.

Discussion: Director Cary noted the forecasted fund balance of 3.3% and mentioned this is one of the largest fund balances we have had and we are moving in the right direction. There have been impacts that have prevented us from reaching this goal. This does not mean the people who are in charge of the fiscal responsibility are not doing what they should be doing. This issue came up in our audit and we made it a priority to make sure we meet that goal. We will not prevent going into the negative every year until we have above a 5% fund balance.
Director Butler noted the fund balance being severely negative in the past and we have worked very hard on it and we are going in the right direction. The commitment has not changed.
    C. Board Comments

    3. DISTRICT REPORTS
  A. Business Report -- Prepared by Marshall Kruse
Business and Finance Director Marshall Kruse highlighted the enrollment numbers and reported the increase and shared it will continue for the foreseeable future. There was a lot of extra money spent this year to keep school going because of COVID and the means to meet those needs. It's been very expensive to run school this year.
Director Cary inquired about the fund balance and asked if it was based on the point of time in August. Mr. Kruse noted yes it is and reported we are about 160k off from the budget, transportation is behind in billing us, billing is a little slow this year coming into the district, May and June are the smallest revenue months in the school year so we will dip into negative and reported these are the two most expensive months for our district.
Director Cary asked if there's any opportunity that we can do to make up that ground between now and August and how comfortable Mr. Kruse feels about the fund balance.
Mr. Kruse noted we have already incurred those expenses, so there is not much we can do and he is 75% sure with a 1.2-1.4 range. Mr. Kruse noted there is a little unknown for May and June with payroll, but nothing huge.
Director Cary asked if there was a contingency built into the budget revision. Mr. Kruse explained there is about 800k built in and reported we will make it back once we get our proportionment. Salaries and special ed costs are budgeted in and can fluctuate as well.
Director Cary asked Mr. Kruse to talk about how we can move money from debt services fund to transfer this money to capital projects.
Mr. Kruse explained the board approves a resolution to allow the district to do this once they have money built up and reported the use of this for technology catch-up and other building expenses.
Dr. Middleton also noted the Board and ESD approve the resolution.
    B. Superintendent Report- presented by Dr. Middleton
Dr. Middleton shared two reports. The first were highlights and successes from the school year and the second was a financial report with Marshall Kruse. Dr. Middleton presented a high level of events at each school for the 2021-2022 school year. Dr. M also reported on district funding, IPD and fund balance. Dr. M talked about inflation throughout the nation and concern for our economy as a whole, reported that IPD is a state selected percentage, the IPD forecast shared for the 21-22, 22-23, 23-24 and 24-25, the regionalization factor was talked about and how that correlates to our budget; state funding allocation from the state covered; prototypical school funding model explained; IPD for Granite Falls explained as well as the required IPD salary increases for Granite Falls; Dr. M and Mr. Kruse shared what a 5.5% increase looks like for Granite Falls and explained the required increase versus actual increase with a total unfunded amount breakdown shared. Bargaining contract language from district to district explained that these other districts will be drawing down their fund balance to pay for this increase. Fund balances by district were shared and compared with Granite Falls being the lowest in the area.
Director LeDoux asked how Darrington is able to pay more per student and have a larger fund balance.
Mr. Kruse shared this is a one-year snapshot but these districts have built their fund balance over several years.
Director Cary inquired about the trend by district to build those fund balances.
Director Butler notated our fund balance has been brought up recently.
Director Cary summarized what Marshall Kruse stated and noted we will need to identify some other funding source to provide the 5.5% IPD portion that the state does not cover.
Mr. Kruse reported we will have a study session in July to discuss the budget.
Director Cary asked about the actual required IPD percentages.
Director Veatch reported about 85-90% of our budget goes towards payroll and benefits, 10-15% goes toward our buildings and running the district. The nearly $600,000 has to come from somewhere and this could be the fund balance or staff losing jobs. Veatch expressed there is no way to give a 5.5%, and stated this would put the school district in jeopardy. Veatch inquired where do we cut funding and asked if we can cut jobs. Director Veatch expressed the students deserve better and asked when do students get more than 10% of what we have to offer. Director Veatch also inquired if it would be beneficial to have a board member at these negotiation meetings. The board expressed the desire to be notified and allowed to be present during the negotiations and asked for a projected schedule to see if someone could attend. Director Veatch thanked them for the information and presentation. Director Cary shared if we just got the regionalization money that Sultan got, this would give an additional 1 million dollars to our district. Director LeDoux inquired whether this is even a possibility for the upcoming school year. Director Cary explained the process of review for regionalization and this could happen with a bill being passed.
Director Butler explained that regionalization also plays a factor in those districts being able to build their fund balance and offer more wages to their staff.

Discussion: Director Kincaid shared this policy is incredibly sensitive in the subject and noted the policy is lacking in details. Director LeDoux noted the FERPA law and stated it lists that parents of eligible students have the right to inspect their child's school records. Director Butler noted the state went further in preventing parents from this right. Director LeDoux shared this policy doesn't refer to the OSPI website. Dr. Middleton explained the procedures that districts were directed to have at a minimum were the ones that WSSDA has adopted.
