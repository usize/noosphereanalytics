   “GOOD NEWS”   ANNOUNCEMENTS                           
 President Desmarais and Board members read the following “good news” announcements:
  Nearly 130 Nelsen Middle School students were recently inducted into the school’s National Honor Society at a special ceremony, with keynote speaker Bob Bridge. To be eligible for the honor, students had to maintain a 3.5 grade point average, and receive a 3.5 citizenship mark on their second-quarter report card. Nelsen’s Honor Society now numbers 243 students.
  Two Lakeridge Elementary School students won first place in the annual Skyway Veterans of Foreign Wars essay contest.  The winners are Emmanuel Hebron, a 4th  grader and Zeinab Ali, a 5th grader, received a VFW medal, $50 and were honored at a VFW award dinner for their essays on “What Makes our Country Great.”
  Hazen High School recently celebrated the academic successes of over 300 of its students in the first annual Academic Awards Night. The top 20 percent of each class were recognized as Academic All-Stars based on their first semester grades. Additionally, 36 students were honored with new department awards from each of the school’s twelve departments.
  Renton High School’s award-winning newspaper the Arrow will be featured in a story on National Public Radio. A producer from NPR affiliate KPLU radio is interviewing Renton High student reporters and editors, along with the team’s advisor Derek Smith, for the story that will highlight student work to find and tell stories that are relevant to their readers, layout and design work and meeting deadlines.
  More than 25 Renton School District teachers are working with the Institute for Systems Biology’s Center for Inquiry Science to better teach science in elementary classrooms. Two teachers from each elementary school are being trained to effectively spread best-practice science teaching methods throughout the district, and link science with mathematics, writing and reading. The program is funded by the Howard Hughes Medical Institute.
  Hazen High School ‘s Business Education teacher Ray McCabe, along with DECA students that manage the school’s student store, the Zone, have been recognized by the national Distributive Education Clubs of America organization (DECA).  Hazen is one of only 43 schools in the nation to receive the Gold Level DECA certification for 2010 for school-based enterprises. Hazen’s accomplishment will be recognized at the upcoming International Career Development Conference in Louisville, Kentucky.
  More than 60 Dimmitt Middle School students have joined the school’s Pride Team to be peer leaders for incoming students. The 7th and 8th grade students are instrumental in helping 6th -graders transition into middle school by making them feel welcome, build relationships, reduce anxiety, and improve student engagement. Pride team students buddy-up with 6th -graders for the entire year, helping the lower classmen understand how to monitor grades, and assignments and review attendance online.

  SEATTLE FOUNDATION GRANT - MIDDLE SCHOOL ACADEMIC LIAISONS
Chief Academic Officer for Secondary Education Kathleen Bailey addressed the Board regarding the Seattle Foundation Grant.  She stated that the district is the recipient of a five year grant in which the district will receive $1 million over the next five years.  The grant funds will be used to support three Academic Liaisons at the middle school level. 
  Bailey introduced Caroline Maillard from the Seattle Foundation and thanked her for this support.  Bailey then introduced the district’s three new Middle School Academic Liaisons:
Arty Christianto, M.S. – McKnight Middle School
BobiJo Hatch, MA.Ed – Nelsen Middle School
Emily Leinss, MA.Ed – Dimmitt Middle School
The Liaisons presented the goal of the program which is to collaborate with school, community, parents and district staff to deliver services to students deemed at-risk for school drop out.  Liaison’s reviewed the risk factors and warning signs as criteria they use for referral which are:
Low academic achievement
High number of absences
High number of discipline referrals
Liaisons help students set goals; they monitor grades, attendance and behavior, work with families, teach study skills and collaborate with staff.  The number of students served is reported to Kathleen Bailey weekly.  Liaisons also do an analysis two times each quarter in which the data is used to help refine student plans and goals. 
  Liaison Hatch shared an anecdotal comment about a student who had poor grades and through the support of this program; was able to help this student improve their grades.  Hatch said this is the reason why they are there - to help kids be successful.
  Board directors asked clarifying questions and commented that the program felt like an alternative school imbedded within a school and that it would be nice to have this program in the high schools as well.  Bailey said that the district will continue to look for grant funds to support more of these programs.
  The Board thanked the Seattle Foundation for their support and Bailey and the Liaisons for a great job. 
  POLICY 1225 “SCHOOL DIRECTOR LEGISLATIVE PROGRAM”
Approved with modifications– First Reading
On behalf of the Superintendent’s Office, Executive Director Sheryl Moore presented Policy 1225 “School Director Legislative Program” for the first reading.  Moore stated that this is a new policy that was provided by the Washington State School Directors Association (WSSDA). She said the policy describes the school Boards activities and actions related to state legislature and is a companion to Policy No. 1220 “Board Officers and Duties of Board Members”.
  Directors asked for clarification on the section of policy that states “a legislative representative will assume office July 1 in an even year for a two year period”.  Since July does not match the Renton School Board schedule, Moore will check with WSSDA to see if it is required to include a date and time period for the appointment of a legislative representative within the Policy.  The Board would prefer to exclude the month and time period.  If WSSDA requires that the month be included, the Board would prefer that the policy read “January 1 for a two year period” to reflect Renton’s schedule.
  By motion from Franceschina and second by Merrill the Board approved Policy 1225 “School Director Legislative Program” for the first reading with modifications to be determined at the next meeting of the Board on March 24, 2010.  Vote 5-0
