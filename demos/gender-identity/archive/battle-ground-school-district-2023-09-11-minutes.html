BATTLE GROUND PUBLIC SCHOOLS
BOARD OF DIRECTORS
  Regular Meeting
September 11, 2023
6:00 P.M.
  Lewisville Campus
406 NW Fifth Ave.
Battle Ground, WA
  MINUTES
Members present:
Mary Snitily, Andrew Lawhon, Ted Champine, Mark Watrin, Jackie Maddux
  Student representatives present:
Sora Tolley, Julia Stiffler
  Administrators present:
Superintendent Denny Waters, Deputy Superintendent Shelly Whitten
  Recorder:
Executive Assistant to the Superintendent and Board of Directors Sandy U'Ren
  Patrons/Staff: 25
1. Call to Order
President Maddux called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m.
    2. Pledge of Allegiance
Sora Tolley led the flag salute.
    3. Roll Call
Sandy U'Ren called the roll of the board members. There was a quorum.
    4. Agenda Approval (Board Vote w/Student Voice)
Director Maddux read the agenda categories out loud. There were no comments or questions from the board members.
  Motion by Mary Snitily, The Board of Directors approve the agenda as presented.
Student Advisory Vote: Pro: Sora Tolley, Julia Stiffler
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 5-0.
Aye: Mary Snitily, Andrew Lawhon, Ted Champine, Mark Watrin, Jackie Maddux
    5. Communications
A. Student Voice
Louie Ledbury, ASB representative from Battle Ground High School (BGHS), told about current and upcoming school events.
  Sarah Shoote, BGHS representative, spoke about the Cispus program that students attended over the summer.

BGHS Speech and debate students Marley and Elizabeth spoke about the program.

  B. Committee Reports
Legislative Committee
Director Snitily reported that the WSSDA General Assembly is September 22-23, 2023 in Spokane. She provided information on a resolution to change the bylaws, weighted vote, and a legislative position about location control.

Audit Committee
Director Snitily reported that the committee met and reviewed the superintendent's timesheet, budget reports, vouchers and payroll. She noted that everything was in order.

Battle Ground Education Foundation (BGeF)
Director Lawhon provided highlights from the August 24 BGeF meeting. 

Washington Interscholastic Activities Association (WIAA) Committee
There was not a formal WIAA report.

Washington State School Directors' Association (WSSDA) Updates 
Director Snitily reported that she attended WSSDA's Networking Hour then proceeded to provide highlights from the event. In addition, Director Snitily announced that elections are coming up for WSSDA's Director Area 6.

C. Director Reports
Director Watrin announced that he delivered vegetable seeds to all of the district's elementary buildings. 
  Director Watrin asked the board members to review a document for the Boards of Distinction application.
    6. Superintendent and Staff Updates
A. Superintendent Report 
Denny Waters thanked the district's staff members for a great beginning to the 2023-24 school year.

Mr. Waters reported the district continues to negotiate with the Battle Ground Education Association. He noted the next bargaining sessions are scheduled for September 16 and 26, 2023.

Mr. Waters reported that a settlement with the final defendants in the Juul e-cigarette litigation has been reached. Altria Group and Philip Morris have proposed a settlement. The district should receive a settlement within the next few weeks.   
  B. Teaching and Learning Report
There was not a teaching and learning report.
  C. Human Resources Report 
There was not a human resources report.
  D. Finance Report 
Chief Financial Officer Michelle Scott provided some information on student enrollment. 
  E. Operations Report 
Executive Director of Facilities Kevin Jolma reported that most of the summer projects have been completed. Mr. Jolma is starting to work on the 2023-24 projects.  
  F. Communications Report
Director of Communications and Community Outreach Amanda Richter introduced Jenna Buescher-Hill, communication specialist. Ms. Richter announced that Ms. Buescher-Hill was instrumental in the creation of the branding of the Battle Ground Virtual Academy. Ms. Buescher-Hill proceeded to provide information about the BGVA branding process and its website.

The board members asked clarifying questions.

Ms. Richter played the 2023-24 Back-to-school video.
    7. Citizens' Comments
Director Maddux reviewed the protocol for citizens' comments.

Eunice Ingermanson addressed the board about the Christian based organization "Called to Love" who is a group of parents and family members to better understand and support their LGBTQ+ children.

Jennifer Heine-Withee spoke about the negotiations for the teachers' contract and the General Assembly.

Tiffani Shupe spoke about her granddaughter being bullied.

Kristin Wade, coordinator of developmental disabilities program, with Clark County, invited the board members to the Clark County's 23rd Annual Celebration of National Disability Employment Awareness Month on October 11, 2023. She provided information about the event.

Gretchen Verpoorten spoke about bullying and providing safe environments for the students.
    8. Consent Agenda (Board Vote w/Student Voice)
A. Approval of Consent Agenda
Director Maddux read the Consent Agenda items out loud. There were no comments or questions from the board members.
  B. Approval of Minutes - August 28, 2023 Regular Meeting
C. Business and Operations - August 2023
D. Personnel Reports - Certificated and Classified
E. Approval of the Purchase of Imagine Learning
  Motion by Andrew Lawhon, The Board of Directors approve the Consent Agenda as presented.
Student Advisory Vote: Pro: Sora Tolley, Julia Stiffler
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 5-0.
Aye: Mary Snitily, Andrew Lawhon, Ted Champine, Mark Watrin, Jackie Maddux

  9. Old Business (Board Vote w/Student Voice)
There were no Old Business items.
    10. New Business (Board Vote w/Student Voice)
A. First Reading of Policy 6220 Bid or Request for Proposal Requirement 
Michelle Scott presented the first reading of Policy 6220 Bid or Request for Proposal Requirement to the Board. Ms. Scott stated that she was recommending replacing our current policy with WSSDA's model policy 6220. WSSDA's model policy has improved ease of reference and clarity when procuring with non-federal and federal funds.

The board members asked clarifying questions. There were specific questions that were asked pertaining to Section I. Subsection D. Ms. Scott stated that she would research the answers and report back during the second reading of the policy.
  Motion by Mark Watrin, The Board of Directors approve the first reading of Policy 6220 Bid or Request for Proposal Requirements.
Student Advisory Vote: Pro: Sora Tolley, Julia Stiffler
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 4-1.
Aye: Mary Snitily, Andrew Lawhon, Mark Watrin, Jackie Maddux
Nay: Ted Champine
  B. Revision of Policy 6111 Tuition
Michelle Scott presented the revision of Policy 6111 Tuition to the board members. She explained the revision to the policy includes the need for approval by the district for attending school after the age of 21. An update is also included for authorization to charge tuition and special fees to offset maintenance and operating costs for optional summer school/vacation period programs.
  A board member asked a clarifying question.
  Motion by Ted Champine, The Board of Directors approve the revision of Policy 6111 Tuition.
Student Advisory Vote: Pro: Sora Tolley, Julia Stiffler
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 5-0.
Aye: Mary Snitily, Andrew Lawhon, Ted Champine, Mark Watrin, Jackie Maddux
  C. Prairie High School Softball Field Project 
Kevin Jolma stated that the district has utilized the adjoining Brush Prairie Baptist Church softball field since 1979. In January of 2023, The church notified the district they were considering expanding and would be demolishing the field in the near future. In March of 2023, the church confirmed that this would be the last year the district could use their field and we would need an alternative location for the 2023-24 softball season. 

A board member asked a clarifying question.
  Motion by Mary Snitily, The Board of Directors approve the contract for the Prairie High School softball field.
Student Advisory Vote: Pro: Sora Tolley, Julia Stiffler
Final Resolution: Motion Carries, 5-0.
Aye: Mary Snitily, Andrew Lawhon, Ted Champine, Mark Watrin, Jackie Maddux

  11. Future Agenda Items and Board Events
Denny Waters reminded the board about the Citizens Advisory Committee meeting scheduled for September 14. It was also noted the football game between Prairie and Battle Ground high schools is September 15, 2023.
    12. Adjournment
The board meeting adjourned at 7:19 p.m.
