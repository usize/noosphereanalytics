  2. SUPERINTENDENT UPDATE REGARDING NOVEL CORONA VIRUS (COVID-19)
  2.01 Superintendent Update Regarding Novel Corona Virus (COVID-19)
Superintendent Pattenaude shared two words that he has been sharing throughout these unprecedented times, Change and Flexibility. We know that the context of what we are doing now will change, and we need to be flexible as we navigate through it. Public Health shared today that the numbers are now 92 cases per 100K over the last 14 days, which is once again in the high risk range and is a significant increase from last month when it was at 52 cases per 100K over a 14 day period. Therefore, the district will continue with remote learning while continuing to work towards a hybrid model so that when and if we are able to move back to in person learning, we will be ready. Dr. Pattenaude said we will continue to work with our labor partners and was reticent to set any dates for potential returns due to the dynamic nature of COVID-19

  8.05 GEAR UP Achievers Update
  Kevin Smith, Director of Career & Technical Education and Kirsten Thornton, GEAR UP District Coordinator presented an update on the GEAR UP (Gaining Early Awareness and Readiness for Undergraduate Programs) Achievers grant. The grant is a 7-year grant funded by the Department of Education. The District received $6.5 million in funding and is currently in its 3rd year. The funding will go through the classes of 2024 and 2025. The purpose is to increase the number of students who attend and complete post-secondary programs including T-2-4, apprenticeships, and military. The grant is in collaboration with the University of Washington and three other South King County Districts, Kent, Auburn and Tukwila. The grant provides specific support for first generation, low income, and students of color.
  GEAR UP was previously overseen by Vickie Damon, Chief of Secondary Schools, but for the remainder of the grant it will be overseen by Kevin Smith, Director of CTE (Career and Technical Education), as CTE and GEAR UP share common goals. The grant has three sight coordinators and one district coordinator. The program is located at McKnight Middle school, Hazen High school, Dimmitt Middle school, Renton High school, Nelsen Middle school and Lindbergh High school.
  The grant provides a mentorship program, tutoring, family engagement nights, college exploration & visits, financial aid workshops, Q & A Panels, monthly career speakers and more. AVID is also available in 3 of the 4 middle schools and over 500 families were served this last year.
  This year every Freshman in each comprehensive high school was given an in-home success kit. Students learned about community service and how they can impact their community and Diane Dobson, CEO of the Renton Chamber of Commerce and Renton Alumni presented to over 100 students about her career and her love of Renton.
  8.06 2020-2021 School Year Update
  The 2020-2021 School Year Update was presented by Dr. Shannon Harvey, Deputy Superintendent of Schools, Dr. Fred Maiocco, Assistant Superintendent of Finance & Operations, Debi Christensen, Assistant Superintendent of Human Resources, Ellen Dorr, Chief Technology Officer and Jon Stadler, Chief of Curriculum, Instruction, and Assessment.
  The team presented an update on remote learning in each of the Strategic Framework areas that were adopted on August 26, 2020. The update also included a report from Human Resources, Operations and an update on the district’s planning for in-person learning.
  Family & Community Engagement:
The district has been supporting families by connecting through phone calls, home visits, and home deliveries of materials. Mini tech teams have been set up to support families in collaboration with community-based organizations where they are helping to support families basic needs. The district has also been supporting ELL families by providing interpreters to help families access technology.
  Excellence in Learning and Teaching:
The final October student enrollment numbers were under the projected number by 135.16. Student enrollment at the Kindergarten level was 148.42 under projection, which we are seeing is a national trend. Student engagement is being monitored through attendance. Average attendance right now is at 28% with Middle and High school having a higher absenteeism rate. School teams are working with families of students who are being counted absent to understand and support them with their learning. A large variety of courses for professional Learning has been offered to support educators. Each school has been designated $2,500 for stipends to support Professional Learning in utilizing technology.
  Removing Barriers & Supporting Students:
A 3-step inquiry process is being used to determine whether remote or in-person instruction is needed for students with special needs. Currently there are approximately 20 student IEP teams discussing the need for in-person learning and Five students are receiving in-home services to support progress towards IEP goals. All schools have built in Social-Emotional Learning work groups. School Counselors and staff are focused on engaging students and reaching out to those who are not engaging. Counselors have developed a Virtual Playground. Thrive Thursdays, a support for staff well-being through partnership with FarWest and Alliance for a Healthier Generation, will begin at the end of October.
Nutrition Services continues to provide meals and will begin a pilot evening meal pick up program for families from 4:30 – 5:30 p.m. at 4 locations. They will also be collaborating with Transportation to pilot a bus route meal service and direct deliveries for families with exceptional cases.
  Facilities and Operations provided an update on the steps taken to ensure our buildings are in compliance with guidance received from Public Health.
  Enrollment numbers were shared for childcare provided by Right at School. The program is at three schools Hazelwood – 50 students, Mapelwood Heights – 36 students, Sartori Elementary – 81 students.
  Human Resources provided an update. There are currently 4 vacant certificated positions. Furlough letters went out to Nutrition Services and Transportation staff, for both reduced hours and zero hours. Furlough’s will be effective on November 1, 2020. Healthcare benefits will be effective through 12/31/2020 for staff affected by a furlough. Unemployment benefits and SharedWork program is also available to these staff. Substitutes are being provided professional development opportunities as well as opportunities to work onsite or offsite.
  The district continues to plan for in-person learning with the number one priority being the health and safety of students and staff. Priority will be given to students with disabilities, newcomer ELL students and our youngest learners. Public Health does not recommend returning to in-person learning until the number of cases per 100K during a 14-day period is in the moderate range which is 25-75 per 100K cases. As of October 12th, the number of cases increased to 86 per 100K within a 14-day period.
  In an effort to plan for in-person learning, family surveys were sent out to Preschool through grade 5 to get a sense of how many families would return to in-person learning if the district was able to do so, and how many would opt to remain remote. In preschool 48.7% plan for their child to return to school for in-person learning. 30.1% would like to continue with remote learning and 21.2% were undecided. The K-5 survey data showed 54.2% would like their child to return to in-person learning and 45.8% would like to remain remote. Staff were surveyed as well to see if they plan to work onsite should the district return to in-person learning. Most staff indicated they would return to work onsite.
  Human Resources is beginning Impact Bargaining with all union partners in the coming weeks.
