OPENING ITEMS
Meeting Date and Location:
November 14, 2024 – District Office, 1985 Barkley Blvd., Bellingham, WA.
Call to Order/Land Acknowledgement
Mr. Benjamin called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. He acknowledged that this meeting
is being held on the ancestral homelands of the Lummi people and Nooksack tribe.
Roll Call
Present: Directors Douglas Benjamin (President), Camille Diaz Hackler, Jenn Mason
(Vice President), Scott Ritchey, Superintendent Greg Baker and Sheri O’Day (recorder).
Director Katie Rose was unable to attend the meeting.
Audience:
No public testimony was offered.
Superintendent’s Announcements and Report: Summary of Dr. Baker’s comments:
• We just completed the first quarter of the school year.
• November is Native American Heritage Month, and a Title VI Native American
Family Committee has been formed as we prepare to apply for a Title VI grant that
will support the educational needs of Native American students. The first meeting
was held this week.
• Dr. Baker shared photos from recent district Native American and Hispanic events, as
well as from:
o An 8th grade STEM event at Fairhaven Middle School.
o A joint Bellingham, Sehome and Squalicum High School choir concert.
o The publication of a book written by a Northern Heights paraeducator.
o ILA (Instant Language Assistant) devices, which help to facilitate
conversations in over 200 languages with a high degree of accuracy.
o The finance team’s award for its work with the budget and preparation of the
budget book.
o Executive Director of Communications and Community Relations Jackie
Brawley received WSPRA’s Learning and Liberty Award. Other awards from
WSPRA recognized the district’s Climate Action Plan, the Class of 2024
video and an award of merit for the levy campaign.
o Veterans Day assemblies and recognition of veterans across the district.
• Dr. Baker attended the state cross country tournament in Pasco, Washington to watch
district athletes compete.

Tonight’s consent agenda contains a report on EL-5, which highlights ways the

There is nothing new to report on the budget. The current governor’s budget proposal

INFORMATIONAL ITEMS
GP-7.2 2024-25 Annual Board Agenda, Revised – general review.
School Board Directors’ Reports. Highlights of comments:
• Ms. Diaz Hackler attended a movie night at Parkview Elementary School last week
and enjoyed the recent student roundtable at Bellingham High School. It was good to
talk with students about the cell phone policy.
• Mr. Ritchey shared that the board subcommittee is making progress in preparation for
the board’s retreat. He attended Kulshan Middle School’s fall festival, and it
reaffirmed for him that there are incredible professionals working at the school. The
Pumpkins by the Piano event at the District Office was great. He attended a City of
Bellingham event related to the Civic Complex, to understand how the city is thinking
about the future of the complex and a possible new location for Carl Cozier
Elementary within the complex. The student roundtable at Bellingham High School
was amazing, and he has had many conversations within the community about his
takeaways regarding the cell phone policy.
• Ms. Mason thought the student roundtable regarding the cell phone policy was great.
The student perspective is very different from that of older generations. She agrees
it’s so valuable to hear directly from the students at the roundtables. She appreciated
the email from Dr. Baker following the Nov. 5 election and believes it hit the right
tone in addressing everyone’s diverse perspectives.
• Mr. Benjamin shared that we learn more about our students at each roundtable. He’s
concerned about the mental health crisis and believes we don’t yet see the full picture
regarding cell phone use. He found it interesting that students talked about their own
decision to use their cell phones less. He enjoyed attending the Bellingham Public
Schools Foundation dinner. He is serving on a WSSDA standing committee related to
equity, and he’s wondering if there will be new pressures or a lack of support that will
change our equity work. He has attended some meetings with Dr. Baker over the last
few weeks and has asked questions about an incident that has been receiving press
coverage. Mr. Benjamin appreciates the district’s position to consider student privacy
when a situation arises. He appreciates Chief Finance Officer Kathryn Weilage’s
efforts to secure insurance money related to building damage and deposit it to the
general fund.
