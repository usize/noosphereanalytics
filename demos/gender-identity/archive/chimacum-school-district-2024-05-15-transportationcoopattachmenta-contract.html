Transportation Interlocal Agreement between Port Townsend and Chimacum
School Districts
Attachment A — Budget

A. By May 15th of each year of this agreement, the Operator District shall prepare
a preliminary budget covering the proposed operations and financing needs for
submission to the Administrators and respective Boards of Directors
representing each member District. By June 15, the Operator District shall
prepare a tentative final budget for submission to the Administrators and
respective Boards of Directors representing each member district of the
Cooperative. Each budget shall include the estimated financial obligations
allocated to each member district based on the allocations contained in this
Agreement and its attachments.

B. The host district shall develop a separate account code within their accounting
records to track costs for each party to this Agreement. The account code will
have a separate subprogram account and location account code.

G. Bus Barn(s) and Fire Alarm System .

1. CSD and PTSD have replaced the roof of the current bus barn
and are in the process of installing a fire alarm system with the following
understandings:
a. Costs will be determined by Section H of this Agreement.
b. The roof was completed in 2018-19.
c. The fire alarm system installation is pending.

2. Estimated life span of each component of this project (1.e., the roof and
the fire alarm system) is fifteen years (15)

3. Ifa new bus barn is built and CSD takes over responsibility of the old bus
barn:
a. CSD will credit PTSD for its share of the actual costs of roof
replacement and the fire alarm system.


b. The “share” PTSD will receive shall be calculated by
crediting PTSD with one-half of the total costs of each
project prorated over fifteen years from the completion of
the roof (2018-2019) and from the installation of the fire
alarm system, still remaining as of the date any new bus barn
is effectively in use.

H. Each year CSD will develop a capital budget for the current bus barn facilities
(bus barn and related property) for those improvements necessary to continue
operation of the building. PTSD will pay fifty percent (50%) of actual direct
capital costs plus three percent (3%) of indirect costs related to construction
management.

I. The parties to this agreement have agreed to make needed maintenance
improvements and expend such funds for the modernization of the current
structure pursuant to the terms and conditions of Attachment B—Urgent Capital
Needs, which is attached to this Agreement. Attachment B is made a part of this
Agreement and incorporated by reference.

J. CSD and PTSD recognize that our aging facility needs modernization or
replacement and the as part of a transportation coop, state funding assistance is
available toward construction costs. CSD and PTSD administration will
continue to meet to explore solutions for the facility needs. Results will be
shared at Joint School Board meetings. This will be completed no later than
May 2027 for possible levy in February 2028. If funding will be provided then
this Agreement will be updated to meet OSPI school construction assistance
guidelines including, but not limited to the duration of the Agreement (e.g., ten
years), cost sharing, etc.

K. Ifa new structure is to be built, a separate attachment will identify the parties’
cost share and other obligations.
