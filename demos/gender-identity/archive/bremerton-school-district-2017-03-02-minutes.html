Superintendent's Report
Dr. Leavell followed up on Mr. Pritchard's public input.  He shared that there have been conversations at a couple Board meeting about the importance of protecting all students, and as there has been some anxiety in the community and schools, he has drafted a letter for the Board to review (attached to agenda) in regard especially to "immigration and transgender individuals," and more generally to reaffirm "our commitment to the protection of all of our student and staff from any form of discrimination.  The Board liked the intent and content of the letter and Superintendent Leavell asked that if they had further concerns or comments, he would wait a couple days before sending the letter, and the Board could email him or call him.  He discussed with the Board that he could sign the letter on behalf of the Board, sign along with President Rotter, or have the letter drafted so that each Board member could sign the letter.  Director Evans suggested that perhaps the Board should consider passing a resolution addressing the content in the letter that reaffirms "our commitment to the protection of all of our students and staff from any form of discrimination" and to "providing a safe, respectful and supportive learning environment for each and every student."    Dr. Leavell will draft a resolution to bring back to the Board at the March 16, 2017 meeting.
  Aaron reported on recent and upcoming activities within the district and community, including several fundraising events for student and staff scholarship opportunities.  He thanked our maintenance department for the wonderful job of pressure washing the steps and seating at Memorial Stadium, stating how great it looks for the opening day of spring sports and the upcoming June graduation.

  (Action) Policy 3140; Release of Resident Students (2nd reading)
Assistant Superintendent Caddell presented revised Policy 3140, Release of Resident Students, for second reading.  The changes to this policy include the state requirement for parents to complete the Nonresident paperwork online when students enroll in an online course or an online school that is an OSPI approved provider. Additionally, the revision will include parents' rights to appeal to the School Board and OSPI if the Superintendent denies a student’s application for release.   He responded to questions.  Director Rubie moved to adopt for second reading Policy 3140 as presented.    The motion carried unanimously.
  (Action) Policy 2162; Education of Students with Disabilities Under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 (2nd reading)
Mr. Lynn Caddell, Assistant Superintendent, presented revised Policy 2162 for second reading. The Section 504 policy has been updated to ensure districts are fully considering the effects of mitigating measures relevant to the decisions about whether a student needs a specific accommodation or 504 plan. These mitigating measures can include the impact of medication, medical supplies, low-vision devices, prosthetics and hearing aids. It includes definition of a 504 student.  There were no questions or comments.  Director Dubos moved to adopt on second reading Policy 2162 as presented.  The motion passed unanimously.
  Written Reports
Written Reports which included March Enrollment, Updated Budget/Staffing Timeline for 2017/2018, Enrollment Budget for 2017/18, and Comprehensive School Improvement Plans (from all schools) were presented for questions or comments.  The Board thanked staff for their reports and discussed the CSIP plans briefly.  Dr. Steedman responded to questions around how schools are funded based on their budgeted enrollment vs. actual enrollment, adding that the Business Office does an enrollment check each month to be sure school are receiving adequate building allocation based on their actual enrollment.  Dr. Leavell provided more information on current development happing within our district's boundaries and how this might impact enrollment over the next few years and building capacities.  Crownhill Principal Teneka Morley-Short helped answer some general questions from the Board in regard to CSIP structure and consistency.  The Board agreed they would like a study session with a small group of principals to discuss what information they would like to see on all CSIP reports.
