  4. Policy / Procedure Discussion (6:08)
  Discussion: A. Library Report by Michele Moriarty, CSD Library Specialist
Michele acknowledged the efforts of Chris Alm (CJSHS librarian), Barb Greenwood (CES librarian), and Traci Meacham (currently aiding in the CCP library).
Celebrations include: Weekly library classes with middle school students; an established collaboration with CJSHS staff on Wednesdays; a collective development that supports and represents all students across the district; additions to the CJSHS library that supports the middle school population; all 3-6 graders have a connection to research tools and resources at the Jefferson County Public Library; the addition of Destiny, an online catalogue system; a barcoding system that allows the library to keep track of all textbooks and computers; and the positivity that has been maintained through COVID pandemic.
  Further reports were shared about the ratio of books to students (31.6 books/student) in inventory for grades 3-12. CCP holds about 7000 books. A major goal of the libraries is to continuously work on circulating and weeding out books that are part of the deselection process (taking into account appropriateness, age/state of book, use). 
  Director Mike Aman questioned any collaboration with other libraries. Michele responded there has been connection with Port Townsend Library and Jefferson County Public Library and future plans are to continue with the focus of literacy instruction and collaboration at all grade levels. 
Director Roxanne Hudson commented on the statistics showing increased circulation of graphic novels and questioned the CSD libraries inclusion of audio books and the support of low level readers. Michele reported audio books are able to be obtained at the Jefferson County Library. The cost of audio books is a challenge for CSD; however, digital books are an alternative.
Director Roxanne Hudson inquired about Epic (digital library for kids) and how it is being used? Michelle explained that all students can sign up through the school district and it's been free through pandemic. A challenge is it cuts off at 3:00 PM. Discussions are in progress on how to proceed with its use.
Student Director Eugenia asked on clarification of 19000 copies; Michele confirmed that is total, not different titles. Michele explained distribution works better with the drop-in model; but student drop-in numbers are low. Student Director Ava shared about the library and Chris Alm providing a safe and calm place and she uses the library daily for studying. 
Chair Kristina Mayer questioned if there are specific areas that need more investment? Michele explained that the non fiction section needs the most weeding, but also need books to replace once that occurs.
  Looking to the future, CJSHS has plans of remodel. Superintendent Mauk shared the want of a library that reflects who we are as a community with a vision of Summer of 2023 to have a design team in place to clean up look, make more comfortable and accessible, and the use of mobile units so the configuration is flexible.
  5. Superintendent's Report (6:30)
Discussion: A. Student, Family, Staff Survey
Superintendent Mauk broke board members into three groups to each focus on one part of the survey results: Staff, Students, and Parents. The purpose being to focus on attributes of district and any concerns that would offer constructive insights to aid in strategic planning and community aspirations.
Break out groups discussed for 20 minutes, then returned to report findings to all:
  Staff: Director Roxanne Hudson shared concern of survey conducted back in November and if people would feel the same now in this changing climate of the pandemic. She reported staff were concerned with lack of resources, people, and materials. She shared the word 'caring' came up a lot and the 'ray of hope' vision for the future that people were sharing. Positives shared by staff were the hard work, just the right amount of professional growth offerings, college credits in the high school, the sense of place in the community, and the five-year vision. 51% felt extremely safe at work. Communication across district with different platforms is difficult. Student Director Eugenia added that responses were either all for or all against, none really neutral. She felt the verbiage used in the survey was old fashioned, stagnant. 
  Students: Director Tami Robocker reported on the students' positive comments of the gardens and outdoor education lending to the community hub as well as the concern of getting through this year. In the parent and staff survey, Director Robocker noted the common thread of both wanting more involvement and community dialogue.
Chair Kristina Mayer noted in the student survey that the concept of relationships kept coming up. There were also comments on the healthy food options, the need for more rigorous work and more active learning. The mention of positive of small school and community came up frequently. A concern was the number of times the word bullying came up; although 70% said they felt safe and secure. Behavior towards LGBTQIA and use of correct pronouns was noted on survey. Chair Mayer commented that quite a few responses where kids said: No one cares, asking for help and no one responding; resulting in a dissonance between staff saying how much they care and students saying no one cares. However, on question of what is best thing about school, student response of 'teachers' came up a lot. Chair Mayer summed up the students voice for relationships, more real life work and more hands on work at higher levels. Also reported on how few Covid-related comments there were. Student Director Ava voiced idea of students really thinking teachers don't care or did they get a bad grade. Student Director Eugenia added there's a difference of what students always complain about in school as to what responses in survey are.
  Parents: Student Director Ava reported that the survey showed parents believed there was caring teachers and staff, that they feel children are supported, and the positive of the small school. Concern from parents on feeling removed with Covid restrictions. Concern from parents on discipline; skewing towards not enough discipline. Voice from parents to see more motivation and engagement of children.
Chair Kristina Mayer added there were comments that the things the students were doing didn't really match their interests.
  Superintendent Mauk commented that the survey responses resurfaced on what he heard as he was going through the hiring process; so things are already being chipped away at. 
Mauk shared elementary students response is recess is favorite. Probing into students craving more time to talk and interact and want more interactive project work in class; more social. 
Student Directors Ava and Eugenia expressed how can they be reached to love something more than recess; programs to get students interested. 
Chair Kristina Mayer noted how all responses are useful to implement in strategic planning.
