HEARING SESSION:
Mr. Brady announced we would be going into the hearing session of the meeting. The audience
was reminded of the 3 minutes per speaker. Larry Hiemstra interrupted the meeting and shared
an old document stating they would be given 5 minutes. Mr. Burge clarified that the procedure
had been changed a few months ago. Mr. and Mrs. Hiemstra started to argue about who makes
changes and Mr. Burge said it was not up for discussion.
Linda Hiemstra addressed the board with the following: She played a recording, the first
speaker said he moved to the Zillah for one reason and that is the school district. So I would just
have to say keep up the good job. And Mr. Burge replied I would say James I appreciate that
thank you. There is a faction of our community right now that as it has in many communities
that because of the pandemic as you may know that maybe don’t support some of the things or
they have a political agenda that they think are happening in schools which really are not. So
we are dealing with that in our board meetings, dealing with that in the community but again I
think our school district is a great place. I am a little biased as I have been at the school district
for 28 years so my family knows nothing else. 3 students that are in college or have graduated
from college and are products from the school district. Yeah I am biased and not the guy to talk

to. We are dealing with those things and keep moving forward. Linda turns off recording and
states: Those are the comments made at the January 2022 chamber of commerce ZOOM
meeting to which the superintendent of the Zillah school district Doug Burge called anyone who
comes to the school board and shares their concerns a faction with a political agenda. The
reason I have a recording of these comments is because I am a member of the Zillah Chamber of
Commerce. Doug’s comments at this meeting unfortunately show how closed-minded he is.
When comments like those Doug made I am thinking he is hoping that this will help with his
agenda by just shutting people up. Maybe that is what you all want. I am a constituent, I pay
taxes, my three children attended Zillah schools as well, my family is a part of this diversity of
this community. We have been active participants in our children’s education and
extracurricular activities; in fact our support of the Zillah School’s goes far beyond many of you.
We have supported monetarily and given time to many sporting activities, musical events, ZCenter stage just to name a few. We have a vested interest in this school and community. The
superintendent’s behavior and his words at that chamber meeting were that of an absolute bully.
You harass people until they back off, call people names, a faction is calling somebody names in
case you need to look it up, you put them in a labeled box and do everything you can to
intimidate them in hopes that they go away. This style of leadership alienates anyone who does
not fall in line. I would hope this is not what this board of elected officials would want. We do
not, I do not come…Mr. Burge gave her the 3-minute time up warning. Mrs. Hiemstra said, “I
am not done and continued with the following. Self-seeking, genuine concerns, we are not selfseeking, we want to discuss our concerns, we have said we support education. We support
teachers and educators, our only agenda is to bring valid concerns to our district directors, we
are all a part of this community. I hope you take into account this recording when you go into
executive session today and rather than looking at our concerns as a liability to embrace that we
are those willing to care about our schools and care about the product that we produce. Thank
You. Mr. Brady asked that each person abide by the 3 minutes please.
Mr. Scarlett addressed the board with the following statement: I will be way shorter than her
(Linda Hiemstra). He said next time to have a better place for speaker as he could not hear Mr.
Burge during work session. So RCW 28A.150.230, District School Board Director’s
responsibilities, what you are governed by. Pretty short 1 page, “in conformance with
provisions of Title 28A RCW, as now or hereafter amended, it shall be the responsibility of each
common school district board of directors to adopt policies to:” and there is a-g. I will just read
(g) “evaluate teaching materials, including text books, teaching aids, handouts, or other printed
material, in public hearing upon complaint by parents, guardians, or custodians of students who
consider dissemination of such material to students objectionable.” I want the board members to
ask yourself, if being polite and having your ears open constitutes the idea of this RCW? By
being polite are you actually listening to constituent’s concerns. I sent an email to each of you, I
don’t know if you got it or did not read it. We are not a bunch of haters out here. We want to do
the best for the schools in the school district. But, when we come with legitimate concerns about
legitimate issues that are going around in the country that actually impact Zillah. When we get
blown off which is the impression that we have gotten it is a little disconcerting.
Mrs. Jensen stated the following: First of all I just want to really quickly say we are all here to
listen to your opinion, we have listened to Doug and listened to Justin a lot and that is good but
what we are here to do is listen to your opinion. I would encourage you to give more comments
on what you believe and what your thoughts are because agree or disagree we are here to listen
to you guys, ok. I have been on commission, it was a free exchange of all the commission

members, we talk constantly. I have not seen that here and I would like to. Second, I wanted to
give you some information about a school district over in Lakewood, Clover Park school District
that has instituted a new culturally responsive student discipline policy. This was passed March
14 by a 3-2 Vote. This policy now considers students race and background in their discipline.
This is all in the name of equity, and one of the board members argued stating you have 2
students that did the same crime and they will be potentially disciplined differently based on
their race and background. This policy is going to cause race hate in that school. When I came
here the first time back in the beginning of school I acknowledged that bill 5044 was not
teaching CRT to students however teaching it to teachers and administrators and regulators
such as yourself will change the culture and result in CRT happening in our school, and now we
see it happening in the school on the west side. Today you talk about how you are having your
equity training in April. I know we are not doing this in Zillah, I acknowledge that you have said
we are not doing it in Zillah but I am here to ask you to be on your guard when you are in that
training. To quote Voltair “ those who can make you believe in absurdities can make you
commit atrocities.” Please do not let your training on 5044 from WASA make you believe in
absurdities. Thank You
There was no more comments for the Board tonight so Mr. Brady concluded the hearing session.

B.

Superintendent/Assistant Superintendent Report
1.
Enrollment Report
Mr. Burge reported 1271 FTE for the month of March. The district has averaged
1245 FTE average for the year to date.
2.
Collective Bargaining – ZEA, ZPSE, ZCA, and ZPA
Mr. Burge discussed the district is in negotiations with Collective Bargaining Units.
The ZEA will hopefully be finished up in the next 2 weeks. ZPSE will start process in
May and hoping to wrap it up by August. The ZCA has met a few times with
some language changes and wrap up by May. The ZPA has an opener so we will be
talking with them soon.
3.
Asset Preservation Program-Information
Mr. Burge shared we have to do an annual asset preservation for Hilton and then in
2025 we will have a certified assessor come inspect the building which is done every
6 years. The High School will begin the Asset preservation Program next year.
4.
Legislative Session-Information
Mr. Burge shared legislative update worksheet that is uploaded to One note. The
session is over.

5.
Staffing Openings – Update
Mr. Burge shared updates of staffing changes for the next school year. Noe Gonzalez
(Transitional Kindergarten), Cristian Schmidt (Kindergarten), Abriah Venema (2nd
Grade). The worksheet is attached to One note.
6.
Transitional Kindergarten, Kindergarten Registration
Mr. Burge shared the district sent out the annual flyer for transition and kindergarten
registration. The pre-registration will begin on April 4, 2022.
7.
Health and Safety Protocols – Update
Mr. Burge and Mr. Irion shared the new COVID District site for notification of
positive cases in the district. Masks are not optional and there is new guidance on
time out for positive cases.
8.
Redistricting Data – Board of Directors – Step 3
Mr. Burge discussed we are asking the board to approve the redistricting proposal
sent to us from the Sammamish Data Systems.
9.
Instructional Materials Committee
Mr. Irion shared this is a part of the curriculum adoption process. We are looking at
three areas. We are looking at 9th grade Health, Foreign Language which is Spanish
at the High School and Social Studies. Once the instructional committee agrees then
we make sure it meets policy, then will move on to the Board for public view for
comment.
10.
FCS Training Update
Mr. Irion shared the Fundamental Course of Study training is any staff assistant
working with students need to have this training. We do hire staff assistant yearly
based on student need.
