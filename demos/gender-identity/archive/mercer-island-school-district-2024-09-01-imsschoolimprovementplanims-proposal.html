Key Performance Indicator 2

Teachers will guide students in becoming expert
learners by teaching them the language of the
engagement continuum, and helping them self-
assess and track their academic behaviors using
the continuum language from Reimagining Student
Engagement.

Students will progressively develop the skills to
evaluate their engagement- from identifying where
they are on the continuum to using the continuum
language to articulate their engagement level, and
providing evidence to justify their self-assessment.

Progression in Student Reflection and Goal-
Setting:

Initial Stage: Students will begin by measuring
their engagement level on the continuum,
identifying whether they are participating, investing,
or driving their learning.

Intermediate Stage: Students will learn to explain
their engagement level in their own words, using
the continuum language to reflect on how they are
living into that level (e.g., “l am investing because |
ask questions and engage with my peers’).
Advanced Stage: By the end of the year, students
will provide specific evidence to justify their
engagement level (e.g., “l am driving my learning
because | set personal goals, sought peer
feedback, and used that feedback to improve my
project outcomes’).
