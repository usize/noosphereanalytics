  9.02 Approval of Resolution 1023, Adoption of the Fiscal Year Budget for 2011-12
  RCW 28A.505.040 requires school districts to make their operating budget available for review by July 10 of each year. RCW 28A.505.050 requires board adoption of the fiscal year budget for the ensuing year no later than August 31 of each year. RCW 28A.505.060 requires a public notice and a public hearing for adoption of the fiscal year budget. Consistent with notification in the Daily Herald on June 21, 2011 and June 28, 2011, this agenda item is presented for the purpose of conducting a public hearing for, and formal approval of, Resolution 1023, Adoption of the Fiscal Year Budget for 2011-12.

The first reading and presentation of the contents of the budgets for the district's five major funds including General, Transportation Vehicle, Capital Projects, Debt Service, and Associated Student Body occurred at the June 28, 2011 regular board meeting. The required budget document known as the state report “F-195”, an Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) web-based software structure, in which districts input their unique budget data into a common format was provided. As was the state revenue report F-203.  The F-203 establishes the state's 2011-12 revenue projection for districts to be incorporated into, and serve as a companion to, the F-195. The F-195 has been reviewed by Northwest Educational Service District (ESD 189) and released for adoption by the board.

As indicated by the extensive list of prior actions, General Fund budget development thoroughly engaged the Fiscal Advisory Council (FAC), community, parents, students, and staff to support final development of the Reduced Educational Program (REP) as adopted by the board on April 19, 2011. The REP is the formal action that provides the direction necessary to make significant adjustments to district instructional and operational programs as a basis for final development of the general fund budget.  The provided F-195 budget currently incorporates all of the REP reductions approved by the board. Further, it relies upon utilization of approximately $2 million of General Fund reserves (fund balance) carried forward from the 2010-11 fiscal year. Finally, the F-195 includes resources necessary to fulfill all current local commitments within the district's labor agreements.

At 5:51 p.m. President Petersen recessed the regular meeting and immediately reconvened into a public hearing for discussion on Resolution 1023, Adoption of the Fiscal Year Budget for 2011-12. Executive Director for Finance Jeff Moore used a PowerPoint presentation to share information on the 2011-12 Fiscal Year Budget.   10.02 Director Request: Access to Non-Public, Confidential Records
During the April 19, 2011 board meeting, Director Jessica Olson requested of the superintendent an appointment to inspect unredacted legal invoices maintained by the district. The nature of the request, the history of the board's decisions regarding director requests and access to non-public confidential information (such as personally identifiable student records and unredacted attorney invoices), the minutes of the board, and consultation with the board president provided guidance for this action item. The superintendent's response to Director Olson recommended submission of a request for the next regular board meeting. Director Olson requested of the board president consideration of the request by the board. During the May 10, 2011 board meeting the directors considered the request and adopted unanimously an order stating “that the board of directors approve Director Olson's request to inspect by appointment on a date agreed to with the superintendent, unredacted attorney invoices maintained by the superintendent for director inspection, and expressly prohibit copying, photographing, or otherwise recording, or removing from the custody of the superintendent, such records; and that the president or designee be included in the appointment.” Director Andrews was designated by the board president, and an appointment was set for Friday, June 24, 2011. Directors Olson and Andrews met at the Longfellow Building on Friday, June 24, 2011 to review the non-public, confidential records in the presence of district staff charged by the superintendent with safekeeping of these district records. The board of directors has not waived attorney-client privilege for these records. Attorneys for the district have not reviewed and redacted the invoices to delete attorney-client privileged information. After approximately two hours, during which Director Olson recorded the meeting with district staff and Director Andrews, Director Olson removed the entire notebook of unredacted records from the district offices over the objections of district staff. Director Olson later returned the notebook containing the records, from several of which documents the staples had been removed. Emails documenting the incident are attached for board consideration.

President Petersen asked Director Andrews to update the board on what transpired while reviewing the legal documents with Director Olson on June 24, 2011. Director Andrews said that the event was disturbing. She shared her meeting experience by reading from prepared comments.(E:330/11)  Director Andrews apologized to Jennifer Farmer for her part in the actions that caused what she described as intimidation and harassment of Ms. Farmer by Director Olson. She stated that this incident raised many issues for which the board must take action.

President Petersen asked for Director Olson's comments on the June 24, 2011 meeting. Director Olson responded by stating she respectfully disagreed with Director Andrews. After President Petersen questioned her brief response Director Olson confirmed she had voted for the May 10, 2011 motion where it expressly prohibited copying, photographing, or otherwise recording, or removing from the custody of the superintendent, such records, and that technically she had violated the motion.  She responded further that other directors had violated various motions so it was all relative. Director Olson concluded her statement by saying she had done nothing illegal.

The board discussed Director Olson’s violating the board’s trust and acting in opposition to the May 10 order which was approved by all five board members. The board asked for research and further study, including advice of counsel, on several questions: Is it permissible for a board member to audiotape and/or videotape an employee during a non-public meeting between the board member and an employee?; Should the board give notice to persons who attend board meetings that a board member is audiotaping/videotaping the meeting, including their comments and/or actions, and that such recordings may be published in whole or in part in social media by the board member?; Are the videotapes/audiotapes of a public or private meeting recorded by a board member public records?; Is there a difference in the law regarding videotaping and/or audiotaping public and private meetings?  Is consent required for either?; Does unauthorized removal of non-public, confidential records by a board member violate policies or laws?
