  2.1 Disability and Language Access Policies and Procedure
Director Flores spoke briefly about the Disability and Language Access Policies and Procedure sharing meaningful language access for parents who are multilingual.  The language access policy drafted for the district and posted in BoardDocs is the model policy with a few revisions.  The policy also includes guidance on language
access assessment that was provided by the Department of Justice for all federal agencies on May 2011.  Director Flores would like to ensure that the district makes every effort to provide language access in every way possible to students, parents, and families.  The disability access policy is the WSSDA model policy.  The language 
under effective communication is a bit confusing and should be revised. 

The effective communication policy and procedures are geared towards the student or parent that has communication disabilities aiming for meaningful access and the ability to participate. It is for family parents who are deaf or hard of hearing, who are blind or sight impaired.  The policy and procedure will aid in providing the 
proper accommodations that should be put in place so that parents and families can participate in their student’s education. 

There are a number of student and family-serving local organizations that the district can collaborate with for the engagement plan for language such as CIELO who provides tutoring for language proficient parents.
2.2 Transgender and Gender Non-Conforming Students Policy Review
Olympia School District adopted a Transgender and Gender Non-Conforming Students Policy prior to it being a required policy for all districts.  This shows all students civil rights as a way of background in the 1920 Legislative Session substituting Senate Bill 7689 that required a policy that supports gender and non-conforming and
non-binary students, in part of the requirements in the Anti-Harassment, Intimidation, and Bullying (HIB) law.  The district is attempting to update current policy to include any revisions and current definitions following a national model policy for all school districts to consider for educators, families, and students of the LGBTQ 
community.  Director Flores highlighted multiple definitions in the Gleason Model policy that also provides guidance on identifying a primary contact for family and students should they have any concerns.  District staff will work in collaboration with the Board and stakeholders to update the current policy to ensure it is reflective of
preferred terms and legal requirements.  The district will also look into working with local organizations on an engagement plan for this policy to ensure students feel protected and safe in schools, and to be able to have their rights protected for gender identity and expression.  The policy engagement may be part of the drafting
 process because of the critical nature of engaging staff, students, and families most effected with the policy around the specific language, which would be a little bit of a departure from the usual process to write out a policy and send it out for feedback.
  2.3. Academic Fall Contingency Plan Update
President Seidel mentioned that this is the first time that the Board is being updated about the Academic Fall Contingency Planning Committee’s (ACFPC) work.  The board and leadership is grateful for the correspondence and the engagement of all those who have been dedicated to the work.  The Board and Superintendent have 
received great information from many in regard to school start up plan, public health concerns and ideas on how to keep staff and students safe in schools, etc.  Moving forward will continue to be robust as the district develops a plan for the fall with, but the district will have several different opportunities for the community to reflect 
on the work and give critical feedback. Currently, a fall plan is in development and not yet complete.  The district is creating a plan collaboratively across many different groups of folks.
  Superintendent Murphy began the update with a brief overview of recent information shared by The Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) who released guidance late last week (week of 6/8) that laid a framework for schools to begin planning what their return to school in the fall looks like. The guidance was developed in partnership with the state Department of Health (DOH); the Governor’s Office; the Department of Labor and Industries; and a broad stakeholder group of more than 120 educators, practitioners, parents, community-based organizations, legislators, and students. While face-to-face learning is the goal, the guidance includes three concepts for school districts to consider adapting and building from should they be limited in face-to-face learning in the fall:
· Split or rotating schedules with distance learning,
· Phased-in opening with continuous learning, and
· Continuous learning 2.0, a more effective remote learning system.
  How a district reopens school will be decided in partnership with their students, staff, families, and their local health authority. While reopening is not tied to the Governor’s Safe Start Plan, the Olympia School District and other districts in Phase 1 or Modified Phase 1 must receive approval to reopen from their local health authority.  OPSI Guidance is not descriptive.  There are some guidance from the state and local health department, but much planning is up to the district.

  Committee Charters and Timeline
What will fall 2020 look like in our schools? Planning is underway.  Olympia School District has formed (2) Committees:  Operations Fall Contingency Planning Committee (OFCPC) and the Academic Fall Contingency Planning Committee (AFCPC). Both committees has started to map out needs for different scenarios come fall. Different groups have been assigned to address academic needs, and the other is looking at how the district could safely operate school inside buildings.  Through all planning, the district will continue to follow guidance from the Governor’s Office, the WA State Department of Health and Thurston County Health Department, and education officials.  The public can learn more about the committee work, including committee rosters, meeting agendas/minutes, and documents shared via the district website. Superintendent displayed the district’s webpage that highlighted information the work as well as subpages that specifically address each of the committee’s work.
  The OFCPC provide feedback and recommendations to Olympia School District leadership to ensure a physically safe environment for a return to school that protects the health and well-being of students, families and staff and meets Washington State Department of Health and Center for Disease Control (CDC) safety protocols. The committee is made up of more than 30 members including teachers, support staff, family members, students and district leadership.  The committee is currently discussing, planning, and establishing recommendations for physical distancing in schools (classroom, lunch, assemblies, etc.); cleaning and sanitizing requirements, the use and availability of PPES – masks, pexiglass shields in schools and other district buildings, temperature check screening before/during school etc., and transportation needs and accommodations.  Frank Wilson, Executive Director of Operations and Wendy Couture, Custodial Supervisor provide the Board information on the current safety and cleaning/sanitizing plan for classrooms and building that are in place.
  The AFCPC role is to provide feedback and recommendations to Olympia School District leadership to ensure a high quality, culturally responsive learning experience for students and staff, regardless of the model. The committee is made up of more than 100 members including teachers, support staff, family members, students and district leadership.  There are five subgroups within the full committee that meet in breakout groups during the committee meetings: Whole Child (social-emotional); Primary (lower elementary); Intermediate (upper elementary); Middle School; and High School.  The committee is currently discussing and planning for development/instructional support; recommendation for parent/student training/supports; social emotional supports; equipment and connectivity; resources/materials/curriculum and platforms; hybrid instructional schedule model.  The AFCPC next meeting is scheduled for June 18.
  Visit the district website for more committee information, rooster, meeting agenda, etc.
  Superintendent Murphy will gather both committee’s recommendation to bring to the Board for review and consideration.
