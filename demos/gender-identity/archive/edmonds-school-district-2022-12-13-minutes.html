    11. PUBLIC COMMENTS-7:10 pm
  Information: 11.1 Audience Comments
  Lucille Downey, 3rd grader at Madrona K8, spoke about the challenges of accessing the school during the recent snowfall. The handicapped parking spots were filled with snow and building accessibility was difficult. She also had to leave classes early in order to allow enough time to enter and exit the school. She asked the district to please be more cosiderate of those using a wheelchair when removing snow and make sure the buildings are accessible for all.

Christy Robertson, community member, spoke about the changes she has seen regarding how Christmas is taught in schools. She stated that holidays are not celebrated but LGBT pride is being taught to all.

Claudia Saint Jacque, ESD parent, spoke about the difficulty her son faced when he was trying to enter and exit school from the handicap parking spots during the recent snowfall. She shared photos with the board of the snowy conditions at district schools. She shared concerns about accesibility and equity when students with special needs have difficulty accessing schools.

Anthony Shapiro, community member, spoke about the board's responsibility to focus on the future of our children.

Robert Fretz, community member, spoke about the history of Christmas in the USA and the celebrations of the holiday around the world.

Cindy Whiteman, community member, shared that she recently reviewed the district's curriculum. She shared that she has concerns with the Lucy Caulkins material in the reading curriculum. She encouraged the district to review this curriculum.

Deborah Lobe, community member, shared her belief that safety starts with policy but also depends on the parents. She asked the district to reevaluate bathroom policies.

Nicolai Shymko, student at Scriber Lake High School, spoke on behalf of the Scriber Lake Film Club. He asked the board to approve the club's upcoming trip to the Sundance Film Festival.

Rosa Ibanez, ESD parent, spoke regarding the expansion of the dual language program to Mountlake Terrace Elementary. She spoke about how this program would benefit students who speak Spanish at home and how this will give children valuable tools to succeed in the future.

Sarah Dilling, ESD parent, spoke about the importance of inclusivity in schools regarding holidays.

Lisa Hernandez, community member, spoke in favor of the dual language program expansion and encouraged kindness.

  Peter Garcia, Student Advisor to the board, reflected on the recent snowfall and the upcoming holidays. He encouraged everyone to be mindful of the comments they are making and whether or not they are helping or hurting others.

Ohanna Jimenez, Student Advisor to the board, expressed disappointment in some of the comments tonight. As a trans student, they shared that that some public comments made are hurtful.

He wished everyone a happy holiday season.

Director Noble wished everyone a happy holidays and gave a shoutout to the Lynnwood and Meadowdale High School volleyball teams who both made it to the state tournament.

Director Chase shared that she visited Woodway Center with Dr. Miner this week. The visit made her proud to be a board director and see the learning taking place in our schools. She thanked the student advisors for their insight and perspective.

Director Kilgore thanked the Technology Department for their work on the new sound system. She shared that she attended the WSSDA conference in November and was able to meet other school directors from across the state.

Director Katims shared that any public comments against a group of students are hurtful. She noted that today was the signing of the Respect for Marriage Act. She also shared that she grew up Jewish and she didn't feel included at the time. She expressed that currently religion and holidays have not been removed from schools, but rather we know now that it's important to include a variety of perspectives.

Dr. Miner introduced Andrea Collins, principal at College Place Middle School and superintendent intern. Andrea attended this board meeting, as well as the agenda setting meetings leading up to this. She also shared that district staff met to debrief after the recent snowfall and agreed that they fell short in clearing ADA parking spots. They will be working very hard to do a better job should there be an another snow event. She shared appreciation for our staff, community, students and board members after 6 months of her Interim Superintendent tenure.
