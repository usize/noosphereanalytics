H.E.R.E. Learning Happens
Vision

e The Tukwila School District believes that our students, staff, and families have the ability to achieve at their
highest potential. Our core purpose is to engage students intellectually and socially with authentic and
substantive learning experiences.

e In partnership with families, our students will be successful and productive members of society because of the
academic, social, and emotional skills they have learned with the support of the Tukwila community.

e We recognize, encourage, and capitalize on the strengths, talents, and contributions of our students, staff, and
community.

e Wecelebrate our rich diversity and meaningful partnership with the Tukwila community and our role in making
society stronger and more equitable.

Mission
Tukwila School District Mission is to create a learning community that supports and empowers students, staff, and
families to reach their greatest potential.

Reflections on the Retention Data:
What are the next steps and follow-up on last year’s goals.
e Human Resources will establish and monitor a set of SMART goals centered on hiring
and retaining a workforce that is reflective of the Tukwila School District student
population.

By August 31, 2025, the HR team will demonstrate that the percentage of BIPOC staff working at
all five schools will increase by 3.5% or greater.

Follow-up on last year’s goal: In August of 2023 we set a goal to increase this percentage by
3.75%. The goal was exceeded by 1.15% for Classified staff and shy by .75% for the Certificated
staff.

By August 31, 2025, the HR team will demonstrate that the percentage of turnover has decreased
in all job classifications by 3% or greater.

Follow-up on last year’s goal: In August of 2023 we set a goal of decreasing this percentage by
2%. Due to the reductions in force, we had more turnover than anticipated in all job categories by
an increased average turnover rate of 2.27% greater than the previous year. HR will endeavor to
reduce controllable turnover by implementing the next steps learned from the exit interviews.

By August 31, 2025, the HR team will demonstrate that the percentage of BIPOC certificated
seniority in the 0-1 years of service bracket will increase by 4%.

Follow-up on last year’s goal: In August of 2023 we set a goal to increase this percentage by 2%.
