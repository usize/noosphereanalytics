PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Bond Program continues to make steady progress across multiple projects, building on the
momentum of recent milestones. For Armin Jahr and View Ridge Elementary Schools, we’ve
successfully completed the Validation phase—ensuring that scope, budget, and schedule are on solid
ground—and we’re preparing to begin Schematic Design. Over the holiday break, the full design team
walked both sites and evaluated existing portables for potential reuse in the near term.

Meanwhile, our District-Wide HVAC project has reached a critical juncture by finalizing both preferred
scopes and alternative scenarios. This positions us to enter an expedited design phase, as well as
procure essential long-lead equipment to maintain our tight schedule. The Consolidated Facilities
replacement project has likewise moved forward: we have received Statements of Qualifications
(SOQs) from prospective Design-Build teams and will soon shortlist candidates for interactive
meetings. Finally, while the Renaissance project remains in the planning stage, we are laying the
groundwork to begin discussions on the future of Renaissance High School in the coming months.

Progress Summary

We recently concluded the Validation phase, where the Design-Build team confirmed the scope,
budget, and schedule for both schools. This confirmation helps ensure that we are proceeding with a
viable plan before investing further in design.

In line with that progress, we have formally kicked-off the Schematic Design phase, where architects
and planners will begin shaping the overall look, feel, and function of the new buildings.

During the holiday break, the full design team toured both Armin Jahr and View Ridge to take a fresh,
in-depth look at existing conditions. The team also evaluated current portables for possible reuse
next year, exploring how these might provide short-term solutions to capacity and transition
challenges.

Over the last few Advisory Committee meetings, members participated in exercises to explore
possible site layouts and adjacencies of key spaces. These discussions have shaped early design
concepts for the Schematic Design phase. The committee has provided essential insight into
community needs, and we continue to capture ideas on how to broaden engagement. We will be
forming an internal Design Advisory group with staff representatives from each school, as we proceed
through the design process.

In December, we hosted events at Armin Jahr Elementary and View Ridge Elementary Arts Academy,
connecting directly with staff and families to share updates, answer questions, and collect feedback.
Later this month, we will expand these efforts to include the entire Bremerton community at a planned
Open House on January 27. This will offer a broader look at all Bond Program projects and seek
community input as we move deeper into design.

Later this month, we'll also be forming the Armin Jahr Transition Advisory Working Group, and we’re
seeking participation from Armin Jahr families. This Advisory Working Group will provide valuable
insight as we develop plans for the temporary relocation of the Armin Jahr school community and
work to minimize the disruptions for students and staff during the construction of the new school.

right-of-way work, potential wetland issues at Armin Jahr, and permitting processes. We have been

Progress Summary

Following extensive assessments of current systems, the HVAC project team has finalized a
preferred scope and identified alternative approaches for each school. This due diligence ensures we
have flexible options to accommodate unexpected findings or constraints, while working within our
budget.

Having determined the scope of improvements, the team is now launching into an expedited design
phase. We plan to procure long-lead equipment as soon as possible to minimize delays.

The project remains cost-conscious, but we are also mindful of the importance of selecting
energy-efficient solutions that align with state requirements (such as the Clean Buildings Act) and
meet our long-term operational goals.

Alongside these presentations, our team will be conducting an Energy Audit to help secure incentives
that could help offset construction costs, further maximizing the District’s investment.

Consolidated Facilities

TASK STATUS
PRC Application Process In-Progress
PDB Team Selection Process Not Started

Progress Summary

We received Statements of Qualifications (SOQs) from interested Design-Build teams on January 8.
The Selection Committee will review these to shortlist the most qualified candidates, who will then
participate in interactive meetings.

This multi-step selection process mirrors the approach used for Armin Jahr and View Ridge, ensuring
we collaborate with a Design-Build team that can maximize project value and address potential
complexities.

While we are advertising a tentative construction start date in Spring 2026, we have more flexibility
here than with the school projects, since it is not tied to the academic calendar.

After shortlisting teams, we will conduct a Validation phase (similar to the elementary school projects)
before committing to final design and construction schedules. This ensures budget alignment and
thorough risk identification.
