RIVALS
Athletic Program Report

To: Chimacum and Port Townsend School Board Directors
From: Shelby Box, Athletic Director
Subject: Title IX Report/Athletic Program Overview for ‘23 -’24

Date: May 2, 2024

Context: WIAA Classifications - WIAA just released the new classification numbers. East Jefferson will
remain in the 1A classification as part of the Nisqually League. This classification cycle is for the next 4
years.

e EJ has been part of the Nisqually League since the inception of the combine.
Section | - Progress Towards Goals
e Increase participation numbers at both districts. Increase participation of those students
experiencing poverty. Continue to educate coaches on producing better humans through sport
participation.
e Data Analysis
o Chimacum participants - 147 (63 girls, 84 boys) see appendix 1 for more information
© Port Townsend participants - 297 (137 girls, 160 boys) see appendix 1 for more
information
o Student athletes were given surveys at the end of their seasons, two of my favorite
questions | added were “How does it feel to be coached by your coaches?” and “What
life lessons did your coaches teach you that you can apply outside of sport?” see
appendix 2 for some answers to those questions. Also note that | do not have survey
results for spring sports yet.
© Equity: Chimacum - 42 out of 93 (45%) athletes qualify for free and reduced lunch. Port
Townsend - 48 out of 184 (26%) athletes qualify for free and reduced lunch
© Comparison: Chimacum - 73 athletes last year compared to 93 this year! Port Townsend
- 178 athletes last year compared to 184 this year.

Section Il - Celebrations

e Fall: XC Girls: 2,3 finish at State, Boys Tennis: Reid - District Champ, Swim: 6 athletes
participated at State

e Winter: Girls Basketball: hosted a playoff game and was 1 win away from going to State, Girls
Wrestling: MiAmada - 3rd place at State, Boys Wrestling: Manaseh - competed at State

e Spring: I’m not sure at this time. | know we will have some Track & Field athletes qualify for
State.

e Obstacles: Communication from coaches to families. SportsYou was implemented this year and


that seemed to help. Still need to work on getting all families signed up. Coaches seemed to
adapt well to SportsYou.

Continue to promote coaches education in regards to producing better humans, continue to
promote sportsmanship by coaches, players, and fans (student section - appropriate cheering),
continue to be visible at events so the athletes know | care about them and their program.
Continue to coach coaches on communication with families.

Section Ill — Challenges & Next Steps
Obstacles | am still working to overcome and the steps | will take:
© Promoting a “Purpose Based” athletic program.
= Continue my training through InsideOut
m Work to change the overall culture of our athletic program by “leading from the
middle.”
m Continue to get coaches, administrators, parents, and students on board by
using common language, i.e. Our purpose is to produce better humans.
o ASB Collaboration between the districts
m Have the ASBs meet at the beginning of the year to pre-approve budgets so
both can be on the same page
© Managing the expectations of both districts
m Continue to have open dialogue with administration of both districts
o Managing where practices and competitions will be held. As the MOU states it is up to
my discretion as to where practices and events will be held.
m= Continue to be transparent about what my thoughts are based on scheduling.

e | will provide a memo to both principals at the beginning of the school
year to inform them of where practices and competitions will take place
and my rationale behind those decisions. If | have to change the venue
for a competition | will also write a memo to principals to explain why.

e Last year | scheduled practices and events mostly based on where they
had been held in the past.

e This year | have tried to take both districts’ input into consideration while
also keeping in mind time involved with getting venues ready for
competition.

= Continue to be in contact with both Chimacum and Port Townsend ground crews
and facilities managers.

e Bill and | talk weekly about schedules and expectations

e Chris and | talk weekly about grounds

Section IV - Board Role
Both boards have been supportive of me from the start and | continue to appreciate their
support.
What is the board(s)’s vision for the athletic program?


| have seen some board members at games. That is always good for the athletes to see that
they are being supported. Continue to show up at games and events. Think about showing up
at the less attended events such as swim, tennis, cross country, and golf.

Use the common language of “purpose based athletic program” and “producing better humans’
when talking to families or community members.

| would like to set up a meeting with the WIAA Board Rep, the superintendents, and myself once
a year just to make sure we are all on the same page.

D

Appendix 2 - Athl rvey Resul

When athletes were asked how it feels to be coached by their coaches, here were some responses:

Football: “Like they wanted to teach me, and make me better” and “Fun, very fun.”

Volleyball: “It was fun they told us what we did wrong but didn't make it in a mean way, they pushed us to do
our best and they would get frustrated if we didn't do our best and | really appreciated that because they
actually cared about our performance.”

Cross Country: “Alice and lan truly do care about every single one of their athletes. As the 6th runner | do
not actually get points during meets, nevertheless Alice was always there to celebrate my prs even if it didn't
change the team's outcome. They give good advice and make sure you never push yourself too hard,
something | know other coaches struggle with. They find a way to make everything fun, and the team really
does feel like a huge family.”

Girls Soccer: “I think they are really good coaches. They have taught me a lot throughout all the soccer
seasons, soccer and life skills. | feel comfortable to be around them.”

Swimming: “It felt good and really annoying sometimes which in my opinion is a good thing because they
pushed us to be our best.”

Boys Tennis: “Like | was being coached by a family member.” and “My coach was an amazing individual who
was so kind, helpful, and humble. | really loved having Keith as my coach, and! am sad | will not be coached
by him again.”

Boys Basketball: “I really enjoyed it.” and “I learned a lot, sometimes there were interesting coaching
decisions but overall it was good.”

Girls Basketball: “There were some ups and downs but overall good, | was able to talk with coaches about
problems | was having and get them worked out.” and “It was great. They were nice and it was fun to talk to
both of them. Even if it wasn't about basketball.”

Wrestling: “The coaches were encouraging and pushed us hard to become better.” and “My coaches are
amazing! If it was ever possible a girls wrestling coach would be sick!”

Cheer: “Il enjoyed being able to be coached by the same coach that I’ve had for the last 2 years and being
able to build a closer bond with her.”

When athletes were asked what life lessons they were taught that they can apply outside of sport, here
were some responses:

Football: “Don’t let size be your handicap.” and “Leadership, teamwork, persistence.”

Volleyball: “They taught me that winning isn't all that matters. Sometimes what matters more is the
improvement that a person makes.”

Cross Country: “Don’t be too hard on yourself. Many people have slumps in improvement that can last for
years.” and “Persistence, listening to your body, kindness, leadership.”

Girls Soccer: “They taught me how to achieve my goals or overcome obstacles. They also taught me to be
there for each other, be a better teammate, have better communication and gain leadership skills.”
Swimming: “I was taught so much this season but the one | keep with me the most is knowing when to push
yourself this season | accomplished a lot more than | thought | could because of that! My coaches also bring
in lessons like how to treat people and healthy eating!”

Boys Tennis: “Don’t be a jerk.” and “Seeing my coach at nearly 80 years old running around and being super
healthy was a life lesson within itself!”


Boys Basketball: “Life isn’t fair even when it should be.” and “They taught me to be able to handle my anger
on the court.”

Girls Basketball: “Be kind, have good sportsmanship. Which can help you in life, like even if you may be
upset with how things are going just keep a positive outlook.” and “Don’t be a moldy strawberry.”

Wrestling: “That hard work always pays off, you get what you put in, wrestling is always the hardest sport
you do, and if you don’t enjoy the thing you’re doing you won'’t get better. Float like a butterfly sting like a
bee.” and “Sometimes you gotta fight for things. Hard work and discipline will bring progress and happiness.
Don’t slack off and stand up for yourself.”

Cheer: “We have to work together as a team or group to make things work.” and “To work with some people
you don’t get along with.”
