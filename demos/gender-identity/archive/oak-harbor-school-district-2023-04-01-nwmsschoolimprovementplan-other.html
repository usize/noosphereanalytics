levels below by 5%, as measured by the iReady Reading assessment. Grow the number of students
who are on or above grade level by 5%, as measured by the iReady Reading assessment.

NWMS students who are reading one or more grade levels below by an additional 5%, as measured
by the spring iReady Reading assessment. We will also grow the number of students who are on or
above grade level by an additional 5%, as measured by the spring iReady Reading assessment.

Strategies to leverage district-support initiative(s) to support your school’s reading goal:
e Students will attend teacher-led Advisory class 4 days per week.
e Teachers will implement CharacterStrong Advisory curriculum, which emphasizes relational
activities and creating a trusting atmosphere in the class.
Mental Health counseling will be available for students who need individualized counseling
services.
Teachers will incorporate relational activities into their lesson plans on a weekly basis.
MTSS team will use student voice data to determine effective supportive relationship building
strategies.
MTSS team will identify and train staff on the relationship building strategies.
Monitoring tools and targets:
e Panorama Survey
e CharacterStrong “Temp Checks”

Goal #2 SEL: Improve the sense of self-efficacy for NWMS students from 45% to 48% as measured by
the Panorama Survey.

Teachers will integrate Growth Mindset lessons into advisory.
Advisory teachers will deliver Xello lessons that will help students plan their future and how
education prepares them for that.
MTSS team and staff will define self-efficacy for staff and students.
Admin and AVID team will share self-efficacy strategies from AVID and Character Strong.
Monitoring tools and targets:
e Panorama Survey
e Use “Take the Time Tuesday” for tracking missing work and grades
e Use “Think it Through Thursday” to provide real world problem solving strategies.

Goal #3 Reading Goal: Reduce the number of NWMS students who are reading one grade level or
more below by 5%, as measured by the iReady Reading assessment. Grow the number of students
who are on or above grade level by 5%, as measured by the iReady Reading assessment.

Strategies to leverage district-support initiative(s) to support your school’s reading goal:

e Staff will identify students in need of reading support and enroll them in reading intervention.
ELA teachers will continue with implementation of StudySync ELA curriculum.
ELA teachers use data to make informed instructional decisions about student growth.
Resource teachers will utilize Orton-Gillingham strategies in special education classes.
Teaching and Learning and school staff will make data informed decisions on how to provide
specialized instruction and reading strategies across the curriculum.

Monitoring tools and targets:

e iReady Assessment data from September, January and April

e SBA data

e Classroom-based common assessments

Goal #4 Math Goal: Exceed 100% median progress to Typical Growth between fall and spring, as
measured by the iReady Math assessment.

Strategies that leverage district-support initiative(s) to support your school’s instructional goal:

Staff will identify students in need of math support and enroll them in math intervention.
Math teachers will identify power standards for each grade level.

Math teachers will focus instruction on missing skills based on fall iReady scores.

Math teachers will use Big Ideas supplementary lessons to help accelerate struggling math
students.

Monitoring tools and targets:

iReady Assessment data from September, January and May
SBA data
Classroom-based assessments
