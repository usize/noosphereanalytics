Improvement Plan

Islander Middle School



Multi Tiered System of Support
MTSS

Systematic Support and Expectations: Build a system of
supports that includes three tiers of instruction and support for
academic, behavioral, and social emotional needs. In the 24-25
school year, we will seek to improve Tier 1 (all students) and Tier 2
(more targeted support).

This work is the backdrop and the framework (structural system of
public schools) and all of our goals are integrated into this
structure.

Overarching emphasis - in the middle, we are Building Expert
Learners in our Students and Ourselves!

Expert Learners are people who:

Are ENGAGED contributors in the learning process.

EMBRACE the idea of Feedback and Reflection as the heart of learning.
Understand that EXPECTATIONS MATTER!
COMMUNICATE with passion throughout the learning process.

Os

Influenced by Katie Novak’s work on Expert Learning



Katie Novak’s work on Expert Learning is deeply rooted in
the principles of Universal Design for Learning (UDL), which
emphasizes the development of learners who are purposeful,
motivated, resourceful, and strategic. Novak advocates for
cultivating expert learners who can self-assess and take
ownership of their learning journey. This aligns with her belief
that students should not just acquire knowledge but also
develop the skills to manage their own learning process.

Self-Reported Grades -
Strategy

According to John Hattie, "self-reported grades" is a strategy where
students assess their own work or level of mastery over a given
subject, essentially giving themselves a grade based on their
perception of their performance, which is considered a highly

impactful practice with a large positive effect size on student
achievement.
