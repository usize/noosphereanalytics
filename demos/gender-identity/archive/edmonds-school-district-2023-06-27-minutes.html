  18. LEGISLATIVE UPDATES
  Information: 18.1 Legislative Information
  Director Chase shared that the board will begin working on their legislative priorities for the 2023-24 school year. She encouraged the board to reach out to our 9 local legislators and continue to advocate for funding.

Director Kilgore asked the board members what their strategy is to have difficult conversations with our legislators and how they can inform and persuade our legislators to provide additional education funding. Director Katims shared that she has focused on the flaws and shortfalls of the prototypical schools model. Director Chase spoke about the importance of board members across the state joining together to advocate for school funding that is appropriate.
    19. BOARD MEMBER COMMENTS
  Information: 19.1 Board Member Comments
  Director Smith congratulated students and parents for finishing the school year. He thanked the Technology team for their work in allowing him to attend board meetings remotely. He encouraged the community to vote in the August primaries.

Director Chase shared her excitement for the upcoming climate policy study session in August. She recently attended the climate fair at Maplewood K8 and shared some of the students' ideas. She also shared how much she enjoyed attending graduation ceremonies last week.

Director Kilgore shared that she had a great time at graduation and end of year awards ceremonies. She thanked the Student Services department for their hard work in applying for and being awarded additional safety net funding. She also noted her appreciation to Dr. Vergara and his team for their hard work on the Family Language Access Policy.

Director Katims thanked her colleagues for their hard work this year. She thanked Vince DeMiero and the journalism students for sharing their award with the board. She also shared that the Edmonds City Council will be providing funding for Student Intervention coordinators at three schools in Edmonds. She thanked the council for their partnership and for awarding the district this funding.

Dr. Miner shared that the Edmonds Principals Association, Edmonds Managers Association and the Professional Technical employee groups also gave up funding from their contracts to go toward funding additional Student Intervention Coordinators. She shared how excited she was to attend district graduations and thanked everyone who contributed to student success. She shared that she is honored to continue with the district as permanent superintendent.
