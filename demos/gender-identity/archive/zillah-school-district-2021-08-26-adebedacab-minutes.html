HEARING SESSION:

Elizabeth Jensen read the following letter she prepared in
response from the Board. I received the letter sent to me by the
board on September 3rd, and I would like the opportunity to
respond to several points. Per your letter, “we do not have any
intent of incorporating Critical Race Theory in staff training nor
our student’s education.”
How often do your teachers incorporate a YouTube video or
article that they have found on their own, outside of the
approved curriculum? Have you ever asked? Every time one of
these “non-approved” videos or articles is presented in the
classroom by a respected teacher, it is given credence by the
students. It could be hat this informational tidbit has nothing to
do with Critical race theory or other divisive or racist topics,
but it could be directly on that point. The only proof we have

one way or the other is the vulnerable student that it was given
to, who is depending upon that teacher for a good grade.
Do you see my point? Until you say unequivocally that this
divisive and marginalizing material will not be taught or
tolerated and that diversions from this position will be dealt with
appropriately, you leave a door open to Critical Race Theory.
I would challenge you to give a survey to your teachers, asking
them how often they present a video or article to their students
that has not been approved by the board. I could be wrong in
thinking this happens often, but I think I am no. Each one of
these instances is an opportunity to let CRT or other divisive
and racist material into Zillah.
You also mentioned the process of adopting a curriculum, which
could include parents. I would assume that even though you
have voted to adopt a curriculum, you did no read all of the
materials? You are given a recommendation by a few trusted
individuals, and then you vote based upon those criteria. Who is
giving you those recommendations, and what is their agenda? I
know this sounds like I am being overly suspicious. However, in
July of this year the two biggest teachers’ unions in the nation,
National Education Association (an affiliate of our Washington
Education Association) and the American Federation of
Teachers endorsed teaching Critical Race Theory in the
classroom. The Washington Education Association encouraged
its members in a Q & A session about CRT and other
controversial educational subjects that while they are required
to teach the curriculum approved by their school districts:
Educators have broad leeway to choose how each curriculum is
implemented in the classroom, whether that’s by choosing which
books to read or by selecting which units on which to spend
more or less time.
In regards to parents enquiring about the content that their
children are learning:
We are aware that parents are sending emails to educators
asking questions about what exactly we are teaching regarding
equity, anti-racism, LGBTQ inclusion, sexual health education,
and more. As an educator, you do not have the reply to these.
To clarify, this is the teachers’ union in our state.
And as parents, how are we are supposed to trust these groups
with our children’s education and the curriculum that is
promoted to our school board, and that they are not pushing
Critical Race Theory to our children? The parents need to be
active members in the selection process of the curriculum as the
decision is being made by the board, not just able to comment
before the vote.

Finally, regarding to cameras that you declined to adopt in the
classroom. I do not deny that the teachers are professionals.
However, many professions, including my own, are videoed as
they perform their duties, for their protection and others. I
believe the term is “trust but verify”. I want to trust my
children’s education system, but he only way we will know that
Critical Race Theory or other divisive and racist material is not
being taught in Zillah is to verify. There are cameras in all the
hallways, why would we not extend this to the classroom?
I endorse the stance that was taken by the school board of the
Chehalis School District, which explicitly states that students
will not be taught that people are “guilty or innocent” or less
capable or worthy than others due to their race, period. That is
what our students need in Zillah, and the school board and the
community need to take action to assure they get it.
Will Scarlett read the following letter for Mike Collins who was
not in attendance: I am unable to attend the meeting tonight
because of a prior commitment, but would like to respond to the
letter that I received regarding my presentation at the last
school board meeting. I thank you for your prompt reply, but I
find some of the information in your letter to be unfounded.
I accept your two statements in the second paragraph: 1)”Our
ultimate goal is keeping our students on campuses in person,”
and 2) “We believe we would be jeopardizing the process if we
were unmasked.” However, your next line stating, “Regardless
of personal opinion, indoor masking is truly a health and safety
measure,” I do not accept.
First of all, I didn’t present the information as my personal
opinion. I presented you with an article that had researched
facts, and I also presented data from the Washington
Department of Health. The article was in regard to the
effectiveness of maks, and the data was in regard to actual cases
of covid, hospitalizations and death rates as reported by the
Washington Department of Health. Neither of those were my
“personal opinion.”
Secondly, I would like to see the scientific data on which you
base the claim that masks are a health measure. On the second
page of your letter, you state that health and safety measures
have “been implemented following extensive research by public
health experts.” I would also like to see the “extensive
research” that you refer to regarding the effectiveness of masks.
Keeping with the idea of data and research, you stated,
“Statewide, nationwide, and global data and research show us
that universal masking and widespread vaccinations are the two
most effective measures our schools and utilize to combat the

spread of the virus.” The article that I presented, The Science of
masking Kids at School Remains Uncertain, emphatically
disagrees with your “global data research” statement. Also, I
did not present anything to you about vaccinations. I talked
only about masks. It is contrary to scientific inquiry to use two
variables (masks and vaccinations) to defend one variable
(masks). Also, I would like to see the actual research and data
used to make the statement, “These safety measures work.” I
believe there is no such research and data.
Will then made a statement of his own. “As a member of this
community and with grandkids in the school district, I get that
you guys (board members) are in a really bad/awkward
position. My personal request is that when you respond to the
public you respond with truth. Say the state mandates that we
have to wear masks or the state mandates this or that, or we
could lose funding. Be honest, don’t use oh the studies show
that masking there is so much evidence that it works. There are
no studies that masks prevent an air borne virus. That’s all that
we are asking is to be honest and say we have to implement
masking, we have to enforce that because if we don’t we will
lose state funding. Leave it at that. There is a lot of people in
the community, I am just thinking of myself for one that really
care about what is going on. For a lot of years there has not
been a lot of people at school board meetings and I am sure you
can attest. This is going to change, and I hope for the long term
because as farmers, the squeaky wheel get the grease and I am
going to be the squeaky wheel.
Mr. Brady asked for any other statements and closed the
hearing session of the Meeting.

Mr. Irion discussed we have asked our tech department and
administrators to be ready for any situation in which we would have to go

Mr. Irion shared we are a small district and have to contract out. We
currently contract with BCBA through ESD 105. We have Mountain View

they transition into the community.

Mr. Irion discussed we are having very few substitutes in every positions.
There are a number of COVID issues that have made it difficult and our staff
is stepping up to help keep up going.
