  Regular Meeting of the Board of Directors
Monday, November 19, 2018
      OPENING
  1.1 Call to Order & Pledge of Allegiance
Vice President Wilhelm called the meeting to order at 6:30PM. Avanti High School student Alexis Nevy led the Pledge of Allegiance.
  1.2 Roll Call
Vice President Wilhelm, Directors Leslie Huff, Hilary Seidel and Scott Clifthorne, and Superintendent Murphy responded as present. Student Representatives Anna McClatchey and Grant Erickson joined the meeting in progress at 6:35PM. President Frank Wilson joined the meeting at 6:53PM.
  1.3 Approval of Agenda
The agenda was unanimously approved.
  1.4 Focus on Avanti High School (see Avanti presentation attached to the Agenda)
Principal Michael Velasquez welcomed the Board and introduced Avanti staff who were present.  He highlighted the many programs and projects in place at Avanti, with a focus on student engagement and student achievement. He spoke about achievement data and interventions, and about Avanti graduation rates. Avanti program priorities and sustainability were also included in his presentation.
  1.5 Community Comment
Leah Wells, Helen Kramer and Christy Austin are parents of Avanti students, and they provided very positive perspectives on the Avanti experience.
Julie Voorhies, representing OTPAA office and technical support staff, provided the Board with a reminder of the roles the members of OTPAA fill, and asked the Board and administration to give consideration to their bargaining requests.
  1.6 Superintendent's Report
Superintendent Murphy thanked the Board for attending the WSSDA conference in Spokane, and he noted a large level of disagreement among districts across the state regarding state funding, where the application of the McCleary decision resulted in inequities in the support of districts.  He reported that a committee would be looking into the design of the Board Room in our new facility, and thanked Director Wilhelm for participating with the committee.  He also offered thanks to our many athletes who were part of very successful fall athletic seasons, and specifically to our volleyball teams. 
  CONSENT AGENDA (see the regular agenda for attached reports)
The basic purpose of the consent agenda is to act upon routine matters in an expeditious manner. Items placed on the consent agenda are determined by the Board President in cooperation with the Superintendent, and are those which are considered common to the operation of the District and normally require no special Board discussion or debate. A Board member, however, may request that any item on the consent agenda be removed and inserted at an appropriate place on the regular agenda.

    REGULAR AGENDA
3.1 Draft Student Dress Policy
Superintendent Murphy provided background for the creation of the draft policy, noting that the district does not currently have a district-wide policy. The Board committed to a broader perspective and asked the Superintendent to draft a dress code policy, informed by staff and community feedback.  He said he reviewed information from the Anti Defamation League as well; he noted that adult intervention can sometimes be counter-productive, which can contribute to sending a message that a girl may be responsible for negative reactions she receives. One comment from a parent indicated she was worried about her daughter being distracted from learning by worrying that perhaps the length of her shorts would be questioned. He found that staff and administrators are concerned about being expected to intervene. This policy, or something like it, won't change the fact that most of our students dress appropriately.
  Several community members spoke with the Board to express their views on the proposed dress code. Primarily parents and a teacher, Monica Brown, Kylie Phillips, Mary Jo Rhydholm, Rich Marlatt, Jessica Revelas, Brent Evans, Caleb Backholm, and Mike Santana, offered thoughts, support and suggestions for the proposal. Comments included setting high standards, creating confidence and self-respect, looking at parenting models, and promoting a higher quality of family life. They spoke of gang-related symbols, the pros and cons of hats and hoodies, setting standards that set our kids up for success in life, considering the needs of all students, avoiding standards that place the emphasis on girls' dress, and keeping in mind the budget restraints of all families. 
  The Board thanked the speakers for their comments, and discussion by the Board continued, resulting in agreement to push out the final draft to a wider audience, returning for Board reconsideration on January 22, 2019.
    Following the Dress Code discussion, the student representatives were excused.
  3.2 Student Outcomes
Superintendent Murphy opened a discussion regarding formally adopting student outcomes. After discussion, it was agreed that adoption of Student Outcomes is just step one, with measurements and parameters to then be crafted. Adoption will be requested at the December 10, 2018, Board meeting.
  3.3 Temporary Use of District Land by City of Olympia
Assistant Superintendent Jennifer Priddy presented a proposal by the City of Olympia to use the property adjacent to McLane Elementary School for a dog park. The Board agreed in concept with the use by the City. An ongoing part of the conversation is finding space for gardens/farms for both Capital and Olympia High Schools.
  3.4 2019-2024 Capital Facilities Plan, Impact Fee Adoption, Resolution 589
Assistant Superintendent Jennifer Priddy reported that the City will defer to the Board with regard to an impact fee for the downtown corridor. While the City has experienced significant growth, future growth is not considered significant (in dozens, not hundreds). The building community is sensitive to immediate change and the City would request a period of adjustment and no change until 2020. They will look closely at boundaries.
  Vice President Wilhelm moved to adopt Resolution 589 as presented. Director Seidel seconded. Board, five ayes; motion carried.
  3.5 2019 M&O Tax Collection, Resolution 590, Certification of 2019 Excess Property Taxes
Assistant Superintendent Jennifer Priddy presented this resolution which declares to the county commissioners the amounts to be collected in 2019.
  Director Huff moved to adopt Resolution 590 as presented. Director Seidel seconded. Board, five ayes; motion carried.
    CLOSING
4.1 Board Member Comments
Director Clifthorne thanked Angel Elam at Capital High School for time in her classroom, and he said he was concerned about the need to capture legislative attention on funding for Olympia.
  Director Seidel said she enjoyed the WSSDA conference and particularly those conversations that related back to the Board's work on student outcomes. She also has enjoyed engaging with family members on many topics and receiving their critical feedback.
  Director Huff agreed that the community feedback they are receiving is very much appreciated. She said spoke with students from Marshall Middle School who had been volunteering at the Food Bank about their experiences, and then encountered some of them again during her own volunteering at the Food Bank with her daughter.
  Vice President Wilhelm enjoyed the music and art she witnessed recently at Madison Elementary; she suggested the students be given an opportunity to report to the Board during the opening section of the meetings so they could be free to withdraw when needed; she said she also enjoyed the WSSDA conference and looks forward to seeing their equity statement; and, she noted the success of our farm programs and the grants we have received.
  President Wilson visited Pioneer, Jefferson and Marshall with good discussions at each one; he reminded the Board that the next meeting would be the annual Board reorganization; and, he thanked everyone for good the work they do every day for our kids.
  4.2 Adjourn
The meeting was adjourned at 9:30PM.
