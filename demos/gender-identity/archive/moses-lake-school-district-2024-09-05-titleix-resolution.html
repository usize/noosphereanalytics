RESOLUTION NO. 2025-01

TITLE IX REGULATIONS SUPPORTING FAIRNESS
IN COMPETITION AND STUDENT SAFETY

WHEREAS, Chapter 28A.58 RCU grants each school district board of directors the authority to control,
supervise, and regulate the conduct of interschool athletic activities and other interschool extracurricular activities of an
athletic, cultural, social or recreational nature for students of the district and

WHEREAS, a board of directors may delegate control, supervision and regulation of any such activity to the
Washington Interscholastic Activities Association (WIAA); and

WHEREAS, WIAA’s 2023-24 handbook states that, All students have the opportunity to participate in WIAA
athletics and/or activities in a manner that is consistent with their gender identity’, and

WHEREAS, Title IX was created to promote fairness, safety, and the protection and enhancement of female
athletic opportunities, and

WHEREAS, the U.S. Department of Education released updated final Title IX regulations on April 19, 2024, to
take effect on August 1, 2024, and its implementation is currently subject to a court injunction; and

WHEREAS, The Department of Education’s rulemaking process is still ongoing for a Title IX regulation related to
athletics and the Department intends to issue a separate final rule to address the application of Title IX application to
sex-spearate athletic teams; and

WHEREAS, a U.S. Congressional Research Service legal brief highlights a proposed rule allowing limitations on
participation based on fairness in competition and prevention of injuries.

THERE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Moses Lake School District Board of Directors strongly supports fairness in
competition and student safety; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Moses Lake School District Board of Directors encourages all students
to become involved in extracurricular activities; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Moses Lake School District Board of Dire tors strongly affirms that the
current status of WIAA regulations, Washington State Law, and Federal Law is not working for our students; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Moses Lake School District Board of Directors strongly affirms that
decision makers and elected officials must act to correct our current situation.

ADOPTED by the Board of Directors of Moses Lake School District No. 161, Grant County, Washington, at a
regular open public meeting thereof, held this 5th day of September 2024, the following Directors being present and
voting in favor of the resolution.
