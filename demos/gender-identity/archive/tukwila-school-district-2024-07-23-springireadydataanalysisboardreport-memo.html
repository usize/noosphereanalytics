MEMORANDUM
To: Dr. Concie Pedroza, Superintendent

From: Scott Whitbeck, Chief of Academics and Schools
Date: July 23, 2024

Re: Analysis of i-Ready data, Spring 2024

Attached report prepared by Shelby Abrahamian, Assessment Specialist/Data Analyst II

The attached report provides an in-depth look at the data from our Spring 2024 i-Ready assessments in
reading and math, which is administered in grades K-9. All students K-8 took the assessments in Fall,
Winter, and Spring. All grade 9 students took the test in fall and spring.

Our district’s current assessment protocol is that students in Cascade View’s Dual Language Program
(DLP) take iReady math in Spanish. The reason they do not take the reading test in Spanish is that it is not
adaptive like the math test is. Our i-Ready consultants advise us that the company is working to make the
reading test in Spanish adaptive, which we can use at that time. These students did not take the Spring
diagnostic in reading because they were piloting a separate reading program.

The report and this memorandum provide information about what the data are telling us, incorporating
our racial equity toolkit lens and street data.

Elementary School Data - Spotlighting Higher Levels of Growth at Tukwila Elementary (TUK)
Provided by Ms. Hani Nur, TUK Principal

This year, TUK educators focused on three key areas of improvement:

dl,

Enhancing Professional Learning Communities (PLCs)

They established a structured cycle for teacher collaboration and data analysis, incorporating
regular check-ins with administrators. Despite the significant effort required to establish this
framework, it proved invaluable in maintaining focus and fostering team growth.

Improving core instruction

Their primary goal was enhancing core instruction by implementing universal strategies including
Guided Language Acquisition Design (GLAD) and differentiation based on individual student
needs and readiness to benefit. They specifically targeted support for students receiving special
education services and MLs, ensuring inclusivity in every classroom activity.

Strengthening foundational skills

Their push-in model for differentiated instruction provided students with two dedicated support
sessions: one from push-in teachers and another from their specialized reading, math, and MLL

teachers. This approach effectively expanded their support network within grades K-2, aiming to
narrow achievement gaps as students progress.

One reason for TUK’s higher-than-average student academic progress is that their teachers possess
dedication to a strong growth mindset, facilitating the adoption of new ideas and collaborative
problem-solving as a cohesive team.

Racial Equity Toolkit Questions to Consider

1. Who are the under-represented groups (federal subgroups) affected? What are the potential
impacts?

a. _ All federal groups who are under-represented / historically underserved are represented in
these data. The potential impacts are illustrating which students our system needs to prioritize
in providing opportunities to accelerate their learning.

2. Does this policy, program, practice or decision worsen existing disparities or produce other
unintended impacts?

a. The practice of using i-Ready assessments as diagnostic tools to inform instruction and as
measures of growth throughout the year could worsen disparities if the results were used as
the sole means for determining student success. That is why our educators work hard to use
multiple forms of gauging progress, including street data such as personal conversations with
students and parents.

3. How have you intentionally involved stakeholders who are also members of the communities
affected by this policy, practice or decision?

a. Schools engage students and parents in learning about student progress and areas for growth,
as well as educators gaining insights about each students’ best ways to learn and demonstrate
their understanding of content.

4. What are the barriers to more equitable outcomes?

a. Some barriers are students’ Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs), as described in the website
of the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention - LINK here. Also, students who enter
school with little to no current understanding of English need to be given rigorous learning
opportunities in a welcoming, inclusive environment to accelerate their development of
proficiency in English so they can fully access our assessments.

5. How will you mitigate the negative impacts and address barriers?

a. Our educators work each day to provide social-emotional support to students with ACEs so
that they can better focus on experiencing the excitement of learning toward their potential.

GROWTH BY CATEGORICALS

A series of independent samples t-tests revealed the following about potential relationships
between scale score change and demographics/categorical programs. These results apply to
both the Reading and Mathematics data.

e There was no statistically significant difference in growth between Multilingual and
non-Multilingual students, except for at Showalter Middle School. At Showalter, MLL
students grew more than their peers in reading, and less than their peers in math.
