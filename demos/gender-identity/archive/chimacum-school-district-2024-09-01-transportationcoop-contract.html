INTERLOCAL AGREEMENT
BETWEEN
PORT TOWNSEND SCHOOL DISTRICT AND CHIMACUM SCHOOL
DISTRICT
REGARDING THE
TRANSPORTATION SUPPORT COOPERATIVE

This Interlocal Agreement (“ILA” or “Agreement’’) is entered into this Ist day of
September 2024, between the Port Townsend School District (“PTSD”) and the
Chimacum School District (“CSD”) for the continued operation of the
Transportation Support Cooperative (“the Cooperative”). Each entity may be
referred to individually as a “party” and collectively as the “parties.” Each entity is
also a “member district” of the Cooperative as that term is used in this Agreement
The parties enter into this Agreement pursuant to the authority provided by
Washington law, including the general powers of the parties under Title 283A RCW,
the Washington Interlocal Cooperation Act as codified in RCW Chapter 39.34, and
the provisions of RCW 28A.320.080 and RCW 28A.335.160.

BACKGROUND

WHEREAS, both PTSD and CSD operate school bus transportation fleets to
transport their respective students to each party’s schools pursuant to RCW
28A.160.010;

WHEREAS, PTSD and CSD have a long history of jointly operating a
Transportation Support Cooperative, the benefits of which include, but are not
limited to: (1) saving costs related to management, storage, and maintenance of
their respective transportation departments and school bus fleets; (2) qualifying for
state transportation funding for annual operation costs related to transportation
coops, and (3) qualifying for state construction assistance funding for the current
bus barn facility; and

WHEREAS, the parties further desire to renew, update, modify, and clarify this
ILA for the Transportation Support Cooperative;

Purpose

A. The parties’ Transportation Support Cooperative, first established in
1981, is intended to provide a safe, economical transportation support
program for public school students enrolled in each respective district.
The Cooperative is meant to provide for shared storage, maintenance and
support services to avoid unnecessary duplication of supervisory and
support staff. The parties’ ILA has historically established each district’s
responsibilities and obligations for the Transportation Support
Cooperative, and has provided flexibility in operation to facilitate rapid
program adjustments and meet emergency, special, and new transportation
needs as they arrive.

B. The purpose of this ILA is to (1) renew the parties’ commitment to the
Transportation Support Cooperative, and (2) update and modify the terms
and conditions of the agreement so as to properly allocate responsibility
among the parties for the storage, maintenance, management, and route
dispatch services required for each party’s respective bus fleet.

Duration

This terms and conditions of this ILA will commence on the date set forth
on the Preamble above (“Effective Date”) and will end on August 31, 2028.
Unless notice of an intent to withdraw from or dissolve the ILA is provided
by February 1, 2028, as set forth in Section III. A. below, this agreement will
automatically renew each year unless and until such notice is provided or the
parties mutually agree in writing otherwise.

Termination of Agreement Prior to Expiration

A. Any party may terminate this ILA during the duration of the Agreement
by providing notice of intent to dissolve the Transportation Support
Cooperative no later than February 1 of the school year preceding
withdrawal. Such withdrawal shall become effective on September | of
the succeeding school year. On the effective date of dissolution, any
unencumbered funds, equipment or other assets or property of the
Transportation Support Cooperative (other than real property, buildings,
facilities, permanent structures, improvements, and/or fixtures, as

Location of Transportation Support Cooperative Facility and Offices

The Transportation Support Cooperative facility and offices, hereinafter the
“transportation center”, will be situated in the bus maintenance facility
located at 241 West Valley Road in Chimacum, Washington 98325, and/or
any other sites as the parties may hereafter agree in writing.

Administration of the Transportation Support Cooperative

A. The Transportation Support Cooperative is a joint operation of the parties
pursuant to the authority of Chapter 39.34 RCW for the transportation
needs of public school students, and the Cooperative does not constitute a
separate legal or administrative entity, nor does it constitute a separate
public agency. The Cooperative shall be administered jointly by the
Superintendent of each member district and/or each Superintendent’s
respective designee, hereinafter referred to as “Administrators.” The
Administrators may mutually agree to organize and delegate authority for
planning and formulating procedures to staff committees equally
representing each member district. The Administrators are charged with
receiving, advising, and acting on recommendations from staff,
participating districts, and advisory committees as they deem appropriate.
The Administrators may mutually adopt appropriate rules and regulations

to govern administration of Cooperative activities consistent with this
ILA.

B. The Administrators will consult regularly and not less than once per
fiscal quarter to review the operations of the Transportation Support
Cooperative. During such review the Administrators may consider
whether the resources of the program are sufficient to extend
maintenance, repair, and other services to other school districts and public
agencies, and whether to expand this ILA to include such additional
participants in the Transportation Support Cooperative. Any added
participants to this Cooperative must be accomplished by written
agreement incorporating the terms of this ILA.

C. Day-to-day routine operating decisions of the Transportation Support
Cooperative are delegated to the Chimacum School District as the
“Operator District’? within whose boundaries the Cooperative’s
transportation center is currently located. Operational decisions with
significant impact on the Transportation Support Cooperative will be
referred to the Administrators for approval.

D. The Operator District shall employ as a district employee (1) a
Transportation Supervisor, (2) dispatcher(s), (3) mechanics, (4) additional
support personnel as necessary, and (5) other staff as may be agreed upon
by the Cooperative Administrators to meet the transportation needs of the
Cooperative. The Administrators will participate in and recommend to the
Operator District any final action or any other decisions involving the
hiring, discipline, nonrenewal, or discharge of employees affiliated with
the Transportation Support Cooperative. The costs associated with each
employee supporting the Transportation Support Cooperative—other than
bus drivers, who are addressed specifically below—shall be borne by the
parties proportionately as set forth in Attachment A to this agreement;
however, such staff shall be employees of the Operator District for all
employer-employee purposes.

E. Transportation Supervisor. Under the supervision of the
Administrators, the Transportation Supervisor shall administer the
Cooperative’s maintenance and operations conducted at the transportation
center. Such services shall include maintaining and repairing buses and
other vehicles, preventative maintenance program planning, emergency
assistance, and such other services as agreed upon by the Administrators.

Budget

By May 15, of each year of this Agreement, the Operator District shall
prepare a preliminary budget addressing the proposed operations and
financing needs of the Transportation Support Cooperative. This budget
shall be developed using customary professional standards implementing
sufficiently distinct account codes so that each party to this Agreement will
be able to track costs within their own respective records. Further details
related to the development of a Cooperative budget can be found in
Attachment A.

Cooperative Review

Administration will review this Agreement and its terms and processes,
including billing, auditing, accounting, invoicing, etc., quarterly. These
meetings shall be held (1) November, (2) February, (3) May, and (4) July of
each Fiscal year, unless otherwise agreed to by all parties to this Agreement.
The School Boards will meet annually to review the agreement.

VIII. Equipment

A. The Transportation Support Cooperative may purchase or otherwise

acquire equipment to replace, supplement, or add to the facilities and/or
services available. Such purchases will be shared between the parties on a
prorated cost basis as provided in the budget section of this Agreement. If
equipment costing more than $5,000 and lasting more than one year is
purchased, and if this Agreement should be dissolved by the parties, the

parties to the Agreement will share in and/or be credited for the remaining

Cooperative. Equipment purchased will become the property of the
Cooperative, managed by the Operator District, and no equipment will be
removed from the transportation center facilities without the mutual
consent of the Administrators from the member districts.

IX. Dispute Resolution

A. It is mutually agreed by the parties that whenever an issue arises between
the parties to this ILA concerning the operation of the Transportation
Support Cooperative, it shall be resolved in accordance with the following
procedure:

1. The dispute shall first be presented to the Administrators representing
each member district in an attempt to mutually resolve the dispute.

2. In the event that the dispute cannot be resolved at that level, the matter
shall be submitted to the respective Boards of Directors of each school
district that is a party to this ILA, who shall work together in an effort
to mutually resolve the dispute.

3. In the event that the matter is still not resolved, the parties to this ILA
agree to request support from Olympic Educational Service District
114 in an attempt to resolve the dispute. The cost of OESD 114
support will be divided evenly by the parties involved in the dispute.

4. In the event that the matter is still not resolved, the parties to this ILA
agree to go to mediation in an attempt to resolve the dispute. The cost
of mediation shall be divided evenly by the parties involved in the
dispute.

5. In the event that mediation is not successful either party may bring
suit to resolve the dispute in Jefferson County Superior Court. At all
times the laws of Washington apply to the interpretation and
implementation of this Agreement.

XII.

Admission of New Members

A school district or other governmental entity wishing to become a member
of the Transportation Support Cooperative must submit a written request to
the Operating District. If the application is approved, a formal Agreement
will be prepared for signature and approval by the proposed entity and the
Operating District’s Board of Directors. Upon a new member’s admission,
the member is subject to the Cooperative’s duration, expiration, and
termination provisions contained in Section II and Section III.

The agreement will be attached to the master Agreement. A new member
must agree in writing to adopt and abide by the terms and conditions of this
Agreement, as well as any Attachments that are part of this Agreement.

Records and Audit

To the extent required by law and the Washington Secretary of State’s Local
Government Common Records retention schedule (CORE), as well as the
school district records retention schedule, the parties shall retain all books,
records accounts, reports, files and the like pertaining to the Transportation
Support Cooperative (including related contract documents) and agree to
make such items available for inspection by the respective parties to this
Agreement, as well as available to members of the public, pursuant to the
Washington Public Records Act, Chapter 42.56 RCW.

Indemnity

A. Each party to this ILA (the indemnifying party) shall maintain insurance
or self-insurance coverage for general liability, errors and omissions, and
other appropriate coverage in amounts and terms determined by the
Administrators sufficient to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless other
members of the Cooperative, their officers, elected officials, agents,
employees and volunteers from and against any and all claims, losses,
liability, costs, or expenses (including reasonable attorneys’ fees)
(hereinafter collectively referred to as ‘claims’) arising out of, or in any
way resulting from, the indemnifying party’s negligent acts or omissions
related to activities under the Transportation Support Cooperative ILA.
Each party agrees that its obligations under this subsection extend to any
claim, demand and/or cause of action brought by or on behalf of any of its
employees or agents. For this purpose, each party, by mutual agreement,

hereby waives, with respect to the other party only, any immunity that
would otherwise be available against such claims under the industrial
insurance act provisions of Title 51 RCW. The provisions of this section

B. The member districts of the Transportation Support Cooperative shall
each indemnify and hold harmless each member district from any and all
claims, demands, damages, lawsuits, or other legal or administrative
proceedings brought against a member district resulting from the acts or
omissions of personnel designated as Cooperative personnel or other
persons acting on behalf of the Transportation Cooperative. This duty to
indemnify and hold harmless, however, shall not apply to the extent that a
claim (1) arises from the acts or omissions of bus drivers or other staff
who are not designated as Cooperative personnel and who are employed
by and acting on behalf of an individual member district, including but not
limited to claims from third parties and claims for industrial injury or
employment-related actions; (2) arises out of the Transportation
Supervisors involvement in the hiring, supervision, retention, or any other
employment actions affecting bus drivers or other personnel employed by
the member districts for claims, including, but not limited to, negligent
supervision or retention of such personnel; (3) arises from the acts or
omission of the Transportation Supervisor regarding bus drivers or other
personnel not designated as Cooperative personnel, employed by the
respective member districts; (4) arises from employees or bargaining
organizations of a member district who are not designated as Cooperative
personnel, alleging such employees are joint, dual, or mutual employees
of the Cooperative or of the other member district.

C. The duties of subsection B of this section shall survive termination or
dissolution of this ILA.

XII. Nondiscrimination

In the performance and execution of this Agreement neither party will
discriminate against any individual on the basis of race, creed, religion,
color, national origin, honorably discharged veteran or military status, sexual
orientation including gender expression or identity, the presence of any
sensory, mental, or physical disability, or the use of a trained dog guide or
service animal by a person with a disability.
