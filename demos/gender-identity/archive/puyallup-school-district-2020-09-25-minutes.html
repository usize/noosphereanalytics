A. Equity and Instructional Leadership - A Focused Lens: Equity and Social Justice; Dr. Vincent Pecchia, Assistant Superintendent of Equity and Instructional Leadership
Dr. Pecchia said this morning the board will hear from me, Assistant Superintendent of Equity and Instructional Leadership; Amanda Kraft, Executive Director of Equity and Professional Learning; and Almai Malit, Executive Director of Equity, Assessment/Accountability, and Highly Capable. This study session will highlight our district’s journey of creating an inclusive environment that values hearing from students, parents, and guardians; providing professional learning that focuses on equity and social justice; and creating safe spaces for staff and students called “Safe Zones” in our district. Our presentation will focus on the implementation of specific strategies and activities that address implicit and explicit bias, support marginalized groups of students/staff/families, foster equity and social justice accountability of stakeholders, and identify racial disparities in academic achievement data.

He began the presentation by acknowledging the land in which we reside here in the Puyallup School District. A land acknowledgement is a formal statement that recognizes and respects Indigenous Peoples as traditional stewards of this land and the enduring relationship that exists between Indigenous Peoples and their traditional territories. Starting a meeting or professional learning with land acknowledgement is embedded within our district’s culture and happens frequently in our schools and at district sites before a meeting or professional learning opportunity begins.

Our "what" and "why" as a school district is to partner with our diverse communities to educate and inspire students to reach their full potential. We do this by making an intentional effort to hire staff that reflect our student demographics, providing professional learning focused on equity and social justice, and creating safe and inclusive environments for staff and students. Our purpose is rooted in the belief that high quality professional learning that effectively utilizes leadership and evaluation practices focused on equity and social justice results in school leaders setting meaningful equity goals with staff. The anticipated outcome of setting meaningful equity goals is reducing disproportionality data found between historically marginalized students and discipline and referral data, enrollment in rigorous programs, and overall academic achievement. Our purpose is also aligned with our cultural competency framework of building awareness, increasing knowledge, developing skills, and promoting advocacy. The words displayed on this slide represent a systemic and intentional effort to align our professional learning of administrators, teachers, and classified staff with that of our district’s diversity committee members. The cultural competency framework on the right side of the slide displayed was developed by our District Diversity Committee.

Our district has two distinct and comprehensive nondiscrimination policies. The first is Policy 3210, that focuses on the equal and educational opportunity and treatment for all students and staff. The second is Policy 3211 that specifically recognizes the importance of an inclusive approach to supporting our transgender students. We are proud of our policy work focused on nondiscriminatory practices and inclusivity as this is not always found in other public education institutions. Did you know, it takes four or more meaningful interactions with a person of a different race, culture, sexual orientation, etc. to positively influence one’s mindset or the human brain automatically predisposes itself to negative stereotyping portrayed in the media? Dr. Pecchia said year four was spent confronting privilege and implementing Equity Walks. The intended outcome of confronting privilege as educational leaders was to become more aware of our individual levels of privilege and that privilege is used to advocate for equity and mitigate implicit and explicit acts of oppression within our organization. Equity Walks were implemented as one measure to indicate if professional learning focused on equity and social justice with district and school administrators was transferring to schools and classrooms at large. Equity Walks are a form of professional learning that provides educators with an opportunity to observe instructional practices in a classroom setting. Equity Walks foster data driven discourse amongst all participants. Equity Walks are designed to support our collective understanding of how equity and social justice foster the implementation of culturally responsive teaching practices using high leverage practices, and instructional technology. If administrators engage in Equity Walks and effectively utilize their learning of our district’s instructional framework through the lens of equity and social justice, then:

• Administrators will have opportunities to calibrate a collective understanding of culturally responsive teaching practices;
• Observe the frequency of culturally responsive teaching practices that are applied in classrooms; and
• Engage in critical conversations about observational data, bias, and perceptions related to the Equity Walks.

  Equity Walks will strengthen administrators’ awareness, knowledge, skills, and advocacy of the correlation between student achievement and historically marginalized students. To ensure this outcome, the following is expected:

• School environments are safe and inclusive for all students and families
• Professional learning is focused on culturally responsive teaching practices
• Evaluation process is focused on equitable instructional practices
• Student growth goals focus on closing the opportunity gap of historically marginalized groups of students
