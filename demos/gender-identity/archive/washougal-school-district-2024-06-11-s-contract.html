GENERAL TERMS AND CONDITIONS
Definitions

As used throughout this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meaning set forth
below:

A. “Washougal Schools” shall mean Washougal School District 06-112 Board of
Directors or Superintendent, or the Superintendent’s delegate authorized in
writing to act on behalf of Mt. Pleasant School District.

B. “Mt. Pleasant School” shall mean the Mt. Pleasant School District, Receiving
services(s) under this Agreement.

C. “Service provider” shall mean the classified and certified employees assigned to
perform work at the Mt. Pleasant School site by the Washougal School District
Superintendent or designee.

CONTRACT
INDEPENDENT CAPACITY OF THE WASHOUGAL SCHOOLS

The Washougal School District and its personnel or agents performing under this Agreement
are not employees or agents of the Mt. Pleasant School. The Washougal School District
Employees will not hold themselves out as or claim to be an officer or employee of the Mt.
Pleasant School District by reason of this Agreement, nor will the Washougal School District
Employees make any claim of right, privilege or benefit that would accrue to such employee
under law. Conduct and control of the work will be solely with the Washougal School
District.

LIMITATION OF AUTHORITY

Only the Washougal School District or Mt. Pleasant School District’s delegate by writing
(delegation to be made prior to action) shall have the express, implied or apparent authority
to alter, amend, modify or waive any clause or condition of this Agreement. Furthermore,
any alteration, amendment, modification or waiver or any clause or condition of this
Agreement is not effective or binding unless made in writing and signed by the Washougal
School District.

SEVERABILITY
The provisions of this Agreement are intended to be severable. If any term or provision is

illegal or invalid for any reason whatsoever, such illegality or invalidity shall not affect the
validity of the remainder of the Agreement.


SUBCONTRACTING

Neither the Washougal School District nor any Subcontractor shall enter into subcontracts for
any of the work contemplated under this Agreement without obtaining prior written approval
of the Mt. Pleasant School District. In no event shall the existence of the subcontract operate
to release or reduce the liability to the Washougal School District for any breach in the
performance of the Washougal School District’s duties. This clause does not include
contracts of employment between the Washougal School District and personnel assigned to
work under the Agreement.

Additionally, the Washougal School District is responsible for ensuring that all terms,
conditions, assurances and certifications set forth in this agreement are carried forward to any
subcontracts. Washougal School District and its Subcontractor agree not to release, divulge,
publish, transfer, sell or otherwise make known to unauthorized persons personal information
without the express written consent of the Mt. Pleasant School District or as provided by law.

CHANGE TO CONTRACT

AMENDMENTS

This agreement may be amended by mutual agreement of the parties. Such amendments
shall not be binding unless they are in writing and signed by personnel authorized to bind
each of the parties.

ASSIGNMENT

Neither this Agreement, nor any claim arising under this Agreement, shall be transferred or
assigned by the Washougal School District without prior written consent of the Mt. Pleasant
School District.

NONAPPROPRIATION

In the event funding from state, federal, or other sources is withdrawn, reduced, or limited in
any way after the effective date of this Agreement and prior to normal completion, the Mt.
Pleasant School District may terminate the Agreement under the “Termination for
Convenience” clause, subject to the 30 day requirement, subject to renegotiation at the Mt.
Pleasant School District’s discretion under those new funding limitations and conditions.

COMPLIANCE ISSUES

LICENSING, ACCREDITATION AND REGISTRATION

The Washougal School District shall comply with all applicable local, state and federal
licensing, accreditation and registration requirements/standards, necessary for the
performance of this Agreement.


REGISTRATION WITH DEPARTMENT OF REVENUE

The Washougal School District shall complete registration with the Washington State
Department of Revenue and be responsible for payment of all taxes due on payments made
under this Agreement.

EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY and NONDISCRIMINATION LAWS

The parties agree to comply with all EEO laws that prohibit discrimination on the basis of
race, creed, color, religion, national origin, age, families with children, sex, marital status,
sexual orientation including gender identity and expression, honorably discharged veteran or
military status, physical, sensory or mental disabilities or use of a trained guide dog or
service animal.

These laws include, but may not be limited to the following Acts: Age Discrimination
(ADEA), Americans with Disabilities (ADA), Fair Labor Standards and Equal Pay (EPA)
and the Civil Rights Acts.

CONFIDENTIALITY/SAFEGUARDING OF INFORMATION

The Washougal School District shall not use or disclose any information concerning the Mt.
Pleasant School District, or information that may be classified as confidential, for any
purpose not directly connected with the administration of this Agreement, except with prior
written consent of the Mt. Pleasant School District, or as may be required by law.

PRIVACY

Personal information including, but not limited to, “Personally Identifiable Information”
regarding a student under FERPA and “Protected Health Information,” collected, used or
acquired in connection with this Agreement shall be protected against unauthorized use,
disclosure, modification or loss. Washougal School District shall ensure its directors,
officers, employees, subcontractor or agents use personal information solely for the purposes
of accomplishing the services set forth herein. Washougal School District and its
Subcontractor agree not to release, divulge, publish, transfer, sell or otherwise make known
to unauthorized persons personal information without the express written consent of the Mt.
Pleasant School District or as otherwise required by law.

Any breach of this provision may result in termination of the Agreement and the demand for
return of all personal information. The Washougal School District agrees to indemnify and
hold harmless the Mt. Pleasant School District for any damages related to the Washougal
School District’s unauthorized use of personal information.

PUBLICITY

The Washougal School District agrees to submit to the Mt. Pleasant School District all
advertising and publicity matters relating to this agreement wherein the Mt. Pleasant School
District’s name is mentioned or language used from which the connection of the Mt. Pleasant
School District’s name may, in the Mt. Pleasant School District’s judgment, be inferred or


implied. The Washougal School District agrees not to publish or use such advertising and
publicity matters without the prior written consent of the Mt. Pleasant School District.

RECORDS MAINTENANCE

The Washougal School District shall maintain books, records, documents, data and other
evidence relating to this Agreement and performance of the services described herein,
including but not limited to accounting procedures and practices that sufficiently and
properly reflect all direct and indirect costs of any nature expended in the performance of this
Agreement.

Washougal School District shall retain such records for a period of six years following the
date of final payment. At no additional cost, these records, including materials generated
under the Agreement, shall be subject at all reasonable times to inspection, review or audit by
the Mt. Pleasant School District, personnel duly authorized by the Mt. Pleasant School
District, the Office of the State Auditor, and federal and state officials so authorized by law,
regulation or agreement.

If any litigation, claim or audit is started before the expiration of the six (6) year period, the
records shall be retained until all litigation, claims or audit findings involving the records
have been resolved.

TOBACCO FREE PROPERTY

In accordance with RCW 28.210.310 and board policies adopted in accordance therewith,
Washougal School District, employees, Subcontractor and agents shall not use tobacco
products on the Mt. Pleasant School District property.

BACKGROUND CHECKS

Washougal School District shall conduct criminal background checks on all its employees,
Subcontractor and agents that may have access to children in the course of fulfilling the
obligations under this Agreement. Washougal School District hereby covenants that no
person who has plead guilty to or been convicted of a felony crime defined in Section 1,
Chapter 396 of the Laws of 2009 (Engrossed Substitute House Bill 1741 amending RCS
28A.4003330), who is employed or contracted by, or who is an agent for Washougal School
District will have access to children in the course of fulfilling the obligations under this
Agreement. Should the Mt. Pleasant School District determine, at any time during the
Agreement, including any renewals hereof, that Washougal School District has violated this
provision, the Mt. Pleasant School District may immediately terminate the Agreement.

INSURANCE

The Washougal School District shall provide insurance coverage as set out in this section.
The intent of the required insurance is to protect the Mt. Pleasant School District should there
be any claims, suits, actions, costs, damages or expenses arising from any negligent or
intentional act or omission of the Washougal School District or Subcontractor, or employees
or agents of either, while performing under the terms of this Agreement.


The Washougal School District shall provide insurance coverage, which shall be maintained
in full force and effect during the term of this Agreement, as follows:

1.

Commercial General Liability Insurance Policy. Provide a Commercial General
Liability Insurance Policy, including contractual liability, in adequate quantity to
protect against legal liability arising out of contract activity but no less than
$1,000,000 per occurrence.

subcontractor provide adequate insurance coverage for the activities arising out of
subcontracts.

Automobile Liability. In the event that services delivered pursuant to this
contract involve the use of vehicles, either owned or unowned by the Washougal
School District, automobile liability insurance shall be required. The minimum
limit for automobile liability is:
a. $1,000,000 per occurrence, using a Combined Single Limit for bodily
injury and property damage.

Errors and Omissions, Provide an Errors and Omissions Liability Insurance
Policy, including contractual liability, in adequate quantity to protect against legal
liability arising out of contract activity but no less than $1,000,000 per
occurrence.

The insurance required shall be issued by an insurance company/ies authorized to
do business within the state of Washington, and shall name the Mt. Pleasant
School District, its agents and employees as additional insured’s under the
insurance policy/ies.

All policies shall be primary to any other valid and collectible insurance.
Washougal School District shall instruct the insurers to give Mt. Pleasant School
District thirty (30) calendar days advance notice of any insurance cancellation.

Washougal School District shall submit to Mt. Pleasant School District within fifteen (15)
calendar days of the Agreement effective date, a certificate of insurance that outlines the
coverage and limits defined in the Insurance section. Washougal School District shall submit
renewal certificates as appropriate during the term of the agreement.

this Agreement, and transmit the deducted amount to the Department of Labor and
Industries, (L&I) Division of Insurance Services. This provision does not waive any L&I’s
rights to collect from the Washougal School District.

DISPUTES

Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, when a dispute arises between the parties
and it cannot be resolved by direct negotiation, either party may request a dispute hearing
with an independent third party. The parties shall agree upon the independent third party and
shall pay an equal share of the cost for such party’s services.

1. The request for a dispute hearing must:
a. Be in writing;
b. State the disputed issue(s);
c. State the relative positions of the parties;
d. State the Washougal School District’s name, address, and contact number; and


e. Be mailed to the non-requesting party/respondent within 3 working calendar
days after the parties agree that they cannot resolve the dispute.

2. The respondent shall send a written answer to the requester’s statement within 5
working calendar days.

3. The independent third party shall review the written statements and reply in writing to
both parties within ten (10) working days. The independent third party may extend
this period if necessary by notifying the parties.

4. The parties agree that this dispute process shall precede any action in a judicial or
quasi-judicial tribunal.

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to limit the parties’ choice of a mutually
acceptable alternative dispute resolution method in addition to the dispute resolution
procedure outlined above.
