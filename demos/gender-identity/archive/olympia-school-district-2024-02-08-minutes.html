3. REPORT
3.1 Outcome 5 Monitoring
Superintendent Murphy introduced Executive Director of Secondary Education, James Whitehead; Executive Director of Teaching & Learning, Inger Owen; and Thurgood Marshall Middle School Principal Anthony Brock who presented on Outcome 5 Monitoring. 
Student Representative Dani Farney asked about the second to last slide (state percentages vs OSD percentages).  What will the district do to increase the percentage of Black/African American students who are on track in 9th grade?  Executive Director Owen answered there needs to be more support at the middle school, and the district needs to have students see themselves as content area experts.  Executive Director Whitehead added that everything around the two goals of engagement, inclusionary practices and rigor, is centered towards that.  He said that AVID will assist in the transition to high school as well as Universal Design for Learning, great instructional practices, relationship-building, and restorative practices.  The Professional Development has been, and will be, centered on these aspects.
Director Huffman thanked the presenters.  She asked for details on Professor Stembridge’s residency.  Principal Brock shared that it has been remarkable.  Teachers are collaborating, digging into research, and designing lessons.  They then will walk into their classrooms and teach the lessons. 
Director Clifthorne asked if there is an adult that is part of the AVID process that a student can check back in with.   Executive Director Whitehead explained the requirements:  for AVID programs there needs to be a building administrator who is also the AVID administrator, there needs to be a counselor connected to the program, and if AVID is being offered as an elective class, there needs to be the teacher.  The teacher has additional support with tutors who are usually college students.  He further explained that in middle school, AVID teaches the how-tos and then in high school, students learn how to apply those skills to real-life situations.  Principal Brock said the AVID teacher follows the cohort of students in grades 6-8.  He wonders if AVID & Student Mentorship Programs can work together.  Executive Director Owen highlighted the WICOR (Writing, Inquiry, Collaboration, Organization, and Reading) strategy which focuses on Executive Function skills.  Executive Director Whitehead mentioned that each student taking AVID is encouraged to take a dual-credit/intensive course (Honors, AP, IP, etc.). 
Director Jess asked if there are any students getting left out (if they are not taking a dual credit course).  Executive Director Whitehead answered that this is a suggestion not a requirement.               
4. CLOSING
4.1 Board Member Comments
Student Representative Dani Farney believed there was really good stuff in the Outcome 5 presentation especially the AVID aspect.  She believes it is a program that will promote being a learner, that going through your education learning how to like learning is the best road.  However, she does not believe these outcomes will be successful if schools are closed.  She wants to learn more about alternatives to closing schools and what will happen to these students if their schools closed.     
  Director Jessica Tourtellotte-Palumbo visited Jefferson Middle School & ORLA.  She apologized for comments she made about McKenny Elementary School.  She appreciates the students who showed up about Policy 4311 and those that have emailed about repealing this.  She reminded people about the Public Hearings and added there will be a Public Comment Sign Up for them.           
  Director Darcy Huffman attended the Annual Heritage Awards given to Lincoln Elementary School and Avanti High School by the Olympic Historical Society & the Bigelow House Museum.  She cut the ribbon for the 100th celebration of Avanti High School; there was wonderful student art displayed, including pottery by Student Representative Farney, and plays written, directed, and performed by students.  Director Huffman thanked Representative My-Linh Thai for the Lunar New Year Celebration invite.  It was held at the Governor’s Mansion; she met a couple of board members from Kent School District, and witnessed an amazing dragon dance presented by students.  She wished a Happy New Year to all those that celebrate the Year of the Dragon.  She will be visiting schools in the next week and is looking forward to next week’s Work Sessions.        
  Vice President Scott Clifthorne shared there is a housing shortage for students who participate in the High School Page Program; he asked for volunteers to house Pages.  He also attended the 100th celebration at Avanti High School.  He visited with Principal Brock at the Thurgood Marshall Middle School PTO meeting.  That morning, he walked to Madison Elementary School with a lot of students & families and also chatted with Madison staff.  Director Clifthorne explained that the passage of Policy 4311 by the Board does not state there will definitely be a contract with the Olympia Police Department; it states if, and when, there is a proposal to have a memorandum of understanding with the Olympia Police Department, the proposal will be subject to community feedback and a vote by the Board.  The policy does not state what type of relationship might be had with OPD, and it is not specific about what type of officers the district will work with.  The policy was explicit in stating that the district will consider not only working with commissioned officers but also consider working with a crisis response unit.  The citizens advisory committee has not yet been created which is the next step.  He is thankful for the opportunity and invitation to attend the OSD4ALL Forum on budget solutions.        
     President Hilary Seidel shared that the work session on March 7th will be an opportunity for community members to bring their own research on how the district can grow revenue.  She proposed having a Google form for people to fill out and upload data.  Then the Board will take a week to pull of that information together in preparation for the work session.  People who submit ideas & data may sit at the table to present.  She thanked the staff who support students who are experiencing homelessness & housing/food/income instability.  She thanked the City of Olympia who met with them to discuss planning for the community.  The OSD School Board & the City Council will meet in May for a joint work session and she is looking forward to that conversation.  The Thurston Regional Planning Council’s meeting discussed the nature of where housing is slated to be built and what types of homes are being built if anyone is interested in this, please attend a meeting.  She thanked those that participated in the Policy CAC and is excited to bring the rest of the policies to the board for readings.  She thanked Centennial Elementary School and Olympia High School who welcomed her and Superintendent Murphy to celebrate the Teacher of the Year Awards.  She congratulated the Capital High School Combined Chamber Choir, the Men’s Chamber Choir, the Sailing Ladies Quartet, and Soprano Bethany Boyd who qualified for the state music competition.
