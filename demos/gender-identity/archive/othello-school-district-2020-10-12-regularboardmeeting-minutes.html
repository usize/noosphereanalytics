Public Comment at Board Meetings
The Othello School Board of Directors is committed to hearing and seeking input from the parents, teachers, and the community
at large in a variety of formats. One of these formats is public comment at our regularly scheduled Board meetings.
The Board generally meets on the second and fourth Monday each month at 7:00 p.m. The meeting calendar can be found at
the Othello School Districts website. The meetings are normally held at the District Boardroom, 1025 S 1stAve, in Othello. From
time to time, the Board also meets at school locations. Public comment periods are held at the beginning of each regular meeting.
Tips on preparing to speak to the Board:
Your goal for providing a comment should be to raise a specific topic or issue with the School Board. Our goal is to listen
and learn from the community. We will not respond to testimony that evening. If you would like to speak before the school
Board, please follow these guidelines:
Do not address questions to the Board. The Boards goal for the public comment period is to hear your concerns,
comments and opinions. Questions can be answered through calls or letters to Board members.
Try to resolve any issues with teachers, school principals, or other district personnel before bringing the issue to the
Board.
Recognize that the Board does not discuss personnel matters at Board meetings. Issues or concerns about individual
staff members can be addressed with the Board through letters or phone calls.
Do your homework and understand the basic facts of the issue before addressing the Board.
Prepare an outline of your testimony so that it is well organized and avoids repetition.
Plan to keep your remarks to three minutes. The Board has limited time to hear all of those who sign up to speak.
Process at the meeting:
Members of the community who wish to speak to the Board during the public comment period should sign up before the
meeting starts. A sign-up sheet is available at the back-right side of the boardroom, usually by 6:45 p.m.
Please print your name and complete address legibly on the sign-up sheet.
When the Board is ready, the president will refer to the sign-up sheet and call out the names in the order that they appear.
Addressing the Board:
When your name is called, approach the front table. Speak into the microphone, giving your full name and where you live
for the record.
Please limit your comments to three minutes.
Be respectful of the Board when speaking.
Be ready to respond to questions from the Board if asked.
Address your testimony to the Board members, not the audience.
Do not address questions to the Board. The Boards goal for the public comment period is to hear your concerns,
comments and opinions. Questions can be answered through calls or letters to Board members.
The balance of the Board Meeting after Public Input:
The main purpose of Board meeting is for the Board to conduct its business in public. During that time, the Board will be listening
and learning from each other and those previously invited to provide input. Please refrain from attempting to interrupt the
Boards deliberations to volunteer comments or input during that time frame. Should you have further input based in the
Board deliberations, please contact a member of the Board or the Superintendent after the meeting concludes. Information on
ways to contact a Board member or the Superintendent are found on the Districts website.
