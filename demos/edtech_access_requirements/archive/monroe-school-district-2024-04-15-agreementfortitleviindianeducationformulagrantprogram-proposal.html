Indian Ed Grant Abstract 04/15/2024

Purpose:
The purpose of the Indian Education Grant (OIE/EASIE 2024-2025) is to provide funding

assistance to supplement, not supplant, the work of our Indian Ed liaison who provides ongoing
support to Native American students and families who can access to programmatic
opportunities through the Skykomish Valley Indian Education Consortium. This includes cultural
events, storytelling and art workshops, annual Powwow and Heritage days, as well as some

academic support.

Methods:
Our Indian Ed Liaison acts as our bridge to provide access to our Cultural Center, located in the

Wagner Center, planning for cultural events hosted at the Long House on the Evergreen State
Fairgrounds and elsewhere in the community, and maintains a presence on the parent board
that sponsors the work of the program. The Liaison also supports students academically both
during and after the school day, when possible.

Results:
We measure success by our ability to maintain the total number of students we serve across the

three districts represented by the Skykomish Valley Indian Education Program. They include
students who attend school in Sultan, Monroe, and Snohomish School Districts. We expect to
serve 116 students in the 2024-2025 school year.
