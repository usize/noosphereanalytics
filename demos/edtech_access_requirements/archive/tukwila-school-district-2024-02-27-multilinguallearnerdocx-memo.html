WIDA Assessment Information

When students enroll in school, families complete a home language survey. This survey asks them
questions about the languages spoken in the home, what languages students first learned to speak, and
what is the primary language spoken in the home. The responses to those surveys determine whether
or not staff administer the WIDA screener in order to identify whether or not students qualify for
services.

After students are screened and qualified for Multilingual Services they take an assessment annually
called WIDA Access in order to indicate their progress towards English proficiency. The WIDA Assessment
defines English Language proficiency at 6 levels — Entering (1), Emerging (2), Developing (3), Expanding
(4), Bridging (5), and Reaching (6). Students who receive a composite score of 4.0 at grades K-2 and 4.7
at grades 3-12 has attained the proficiency necessary to no longer qualify for Multilingual Services.

Classroom Supports
The WIDA Standards Framework guides instructional support for multilingual learners. The work of the

framework is anchored by the four Big Ideas:
e Equity of Opportunity and Access
e Integration of Content and Language
e Collaboration among Stakeholders
e Functional Approach to Language Development

There are 5 standards which take a functional approach to language development ensuring that the
standards themselves are connected to core content areas and undergirded by the development of
language for social and instructional purposes. It is possible to learn language and content
simultaneously.

All certificated and classified staff are responsible for the instruction and support of all students,
including multilingual learners. Additionally, Multilingual Department certificated and classified staff
support students and staff at all Tukwila schools through a combination of co-teaching, co-planning,
consultation, stand alone course instruction for beginners at the secondary level, and pull out
interventions at the elementary.

The identification of classroom materials that meet the needs of multilingual learners is a key
component of the selection of curriculum materials for piloting across grade levels. The Tukwila
Curriculum Council has adopted the English Learner Success Forum guidelines for English Language Arts,
Mathematics, and Science. These research-based guidelines center the needs of multilingual learners at
the forefront of curriculum selection and identification.

Professional Learning
In order to meet the needs of multilingual students the Teaching and Learning Department has

supported Learning Labs for staff and school leaders for Cascade View, Thorndyke, Tukwila, and
Showalter. This professional learning sequence is designed to support a cohort of educators that
includes classroom teachers, multilingual learning department staff, and administrators. The sequence
consists of alternating learning labs and planning sessions that concentrate on both math instruction and
multilingual strategies and practices. These labs provide opportunities for educators to collaborate,
explore, and experiment with learning strategies that focus on language outputs alongside content
learning. After each session participating staff provide feedback which informs future session
development.

At Foster High School an internal team of teacher-leaders are facilitating a 3-part series designed to
support content teachers in deepening their understanding of WIDA Standards alongside opportunities
to co-plan and collaborate with multilingual learning department staff. Additionally, two staff meeting


sessions book-end these collaborative sessions so that all staff can work with and learn from one
another.

Tukwila School District staff have been developing MTSS (Multi-Tiered Systems of Support) processes
and learning about Inclusionary Practices through WASA’s Inclusionary Practices Project. The district
wide MTSS team includes teacher leaders from each school and district staff who are working together
to identify core practices in literacy instruction and high leverage teaching practices for all classrooms
that center the needs of multilingual learners. The learning from the Inclusionary Practices Project
supports the deepening knowledge of Universally Designed Instructional Practices which will inform the
ultimate selections and next steps for the MTSS Team.

Outside professional learning is available via workshops, conferences, and endorsement reimbursement.

Dual Language
Tukwila School District’s K-12 Dual Language program launched in the fall of 2023 with a Kindergarten

classroom at Cascade View Elementary. Planning for this program began in 2017 with the first lottery for
enrollment occurring in March of 2023. Through the efforts of the Dual Language Advisory Team and
program mission and vision were identified. These statements are the foundation for the program.

Program Vision Statement

Our vision for the Tukwila School District Dual Language Program is to educate globally focused and
multilingual citizens equipped with the linguistic and sociocultural competence necessary to succeed ina
diverse world.

Heritage Language
A Heritage Language class is composed of students who share a common heritage language, in this case

Spanish, and the content of that class is taught in their heritage language. Students and families
self-select into the course which takes the place of one of their regular core content or elective classes.
Student benefits include increased biliteracy and bilingualism, understanding of core content, and
fostering a sense of belonging all of which connect to the Tukwila Strategic Plan. During the 2022-2023
school year Tukwila School District received a Heritage Language Planning Grant from OSPI. That allowed
teams at both Foster High School and Showalter Middle School to develop plans to implement Heritage
Language classes for the fall of 2024. Both schools are continuing to develop multi-year plans to increase
the number of heritage language courses offered with the goal of merging the heritage and dual
language courses as the Dual Language Program rolls up. At Showalter the 6" grade Ethnic Studies


course is offered in Spanish and at Foster the World History course is offered in Spanish along with an
elective world language class.

Family and Community Engagement

Family engagement is key to a student’s success in school. In Tukwila School District a key way that we
partner with families is through our 7 Community and Family Liaisons. These staff members support
families from the beginning with enrollment all the way through high school graduation through grade
checks, connecting students and families with teachers, and direct academic tutoring.

While each Liaison speaks English and at least one other language they do not limit their support to
students and families who speak only the language they speak. They support school-based events — such
as the Showalter Newcomer Night, Foster High School’s session for families of students with IEPs and
lead their own parent engagement sessions like teaching families how to access Skyward, what state
tests are for, and how to navigate schools in the US.

They are uniquely positioned to support entire families with students across grade levels and schools.
You may see a Community and Family Liaison in an elementary school in the morning checking in with a
family in need of assistance and going to class with a student at the high school in the afternoon to
ensure they understand the material taught so that their support with homework is more effective. In
between they are likely supporting school staff or families with emergent needs.

Each Community and Family Liaison centers their work around the needs of multilingual students and
families and tracks their progress in many cases over several years. This level of engagement keeps
families connected to their school communities and fosters a sense of belonging within the entire
district.

Language Access
Parents also have a right to receive important information from schools in a language they can

understand. School Districts must provide interpretation and/or translation services when needed. The
Tukwila School District has a long history of providing interpreter services for families. Beginning with the
2023-2024 school year, all school districts in Washington State must provide a Language Access
Coordinator who monitors the requests for interpreting services and provides families with the
opportunity to share feedback on their experiences with interpreted conferences. Schools throughout
the district have received training on these new requirements and the TSD Language Access Coordinator
continues to be available for individual or group training requests. During the 2023-2024 school year
Tukwila School District received grant funds from OSPI to offset the costs of interpreting and translation
services to support the new reporting requirements.

Other Grant Funded Opportunities

Tukwila School District has received two other grants this year to specifically support multilingual
refugee students. Through School’s Out Washington Tukwila receives funding for the Refugee School
Impact Program. These funds support in-class support delivered by International Rescue Committee
(IRC) staff for qualifying refugee students at Cascade View and Showalter Middle School and will support
a summer camp at Cascade View Elementary. Tukwila also receives funding directly from OSPI to support
Afghan Refugees. This funding has allowed us to provide Ready for Kindergarten Classes for families of
current preschool students, a staff development session about education in Afghanistan, after school
tutoring at Showalter and Foster for newly arrived students, and parent education sessions for Afghan
families. Tukwila School District Staff from Cascade View and our Afghan Community and Family Liaison

8


have been instrumental in providing meaningful sessions for staff and families. Each of these separate
funding sources has provided Tukwila School District the ability to support newly arrived students and
developed connections between the school district and their communities.

Conclusion & Next Steps
Supporting multilingual learners in Tukwila School District is a complex effort with many layers that
begins with equity and empathy that supports high expectations and excellence for all. In the
development of relationships with students, community, and staff we can engage our learners. Centering
multilingual learners in our efforts will enhance the educational experience for all who learn and work
here. Next steps to support multilingual learners include:

e Continued professional learning opportunities for staff across Tukwila

e Review of annual WIDA Access assessment data when it becomes available in July of 2024
