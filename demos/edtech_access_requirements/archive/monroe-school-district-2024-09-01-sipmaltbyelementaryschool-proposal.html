Needs Assessment Summary
Describe the highest priorities and resource inequities (funding, staffing, materials, resources, etc.) identified through conducting the Comprehensive Needs Assessment that will be addressed in this plan.

Academics - Math and Reading, Sense of Belonging and Attendance.
The data for all goals in the 2024-2025 SIP is based on data from Spring 2024 and includes data from the SmartBalanced Assessment, iReady, and Panorama.

While students are making great progress toward closing the gaps that were created, a need continues for educators and schools to think creatively about possible solutions, continue to further develop effective interventions,
and ensure that all students have quality Tier | Core instructional practices. The other aspects that are needed include establishing the MSD essential standards for math and reading, developing a Multi-Tiered System of Support
(MTSS) model that provides clear Tier |, Tier Il and Tier III practices in the areas of academics, behavior, social emotional, and attendance. The work of our collaborative teams will be an important part of supporting students.

In the spring of 2023, our team first began using Panorama to look at the needs of our students in academics, SEL, attendance, and behavior. In the 2023-2024 school year, we made improvements in all areas of attendance and
will continue our current strategies to increase our attendance rates for the 2024-2025 school year.

Attendance and academic performance are directly tied to a student's sense of belonging. We continue to see students struggling to make friendships, having challenges identifying and managing their feelings, having difficulty
connecting with other students and adults, a lack of problem solving skills, and challenges being able to show respect between students. We have a variety of tools that we used last year around this goal and will continue to
utilize the same strategies.

In order to close gaps and reduce inequalities, we will need to narrow our focus and be intentional about our plan. A lot of the work that is needed has already been happening in our classrooms and school. We have a number
of teams who will support the work.

SY 2024-2025 SMARTIE Goal #1: Math

100% of students will make typical progress and 35% of students will make stretch goals on the iReady Math assessment by the end of the 24-25 school year.
70% of K-5 students will reach standard on iReady Math at the end of the 24-25 school year, up from 63% in the 23-24 school year.

65% of students in 3rd, 4th, and 5th grades will meet standard on the Math SBA by the end of the 24-25 school year, up from 56.8% in the 23-24 school year.

100% of students identified as MLL (27 students) and students with an IEP (63 students) who take the iReady Math assessment will make typical progress by the end of the 24-25 school year.

We will increase the percentage of total students who reached proficiency in the numbers & operations strand as measured by the iReady Math Diagnostic, going from 67% proficient on the SPRING 2024 iReady
Math Diagnostic to 75% proficient on the SPRING 2025 iReady Math Diagnostic.

SY 2024-2025 SMARTIE Goal #3: Sense of Belonging

75% of 3rd, 4th, and 5th grade students will report a positive sense of belonging as measured by the Panorama survey by the end of the 24-25 school year, up from 66% in the 23-24 school year.

Within the Panorama Survey, the lowest area that was identified by students was the respect that other students at their school show to them. In the fall 2023 students answered that 60% had a favorable response about the
amount of respect that other students show them. The response dropped to 50% in the spring of 2024. The goal for the 2024-2025 school year will be to increase the positive response rate to 70%.

60% of families will report positive family engagement by the end of the 24-25 school year, up from 40% in the 22-23 school year. We did not collect family engagement data during the 23-24 school year.

Reconnect with our community and provide a variety of events and opportunities for all families and community members to engage in student learning.

Chronic Absenteeism is when a student is absent 10%-29% or more full days of school. In 2024-2025, our school had 16.5% (32 students) of students in the chronic absenteeism category. Our goal is to decrease the overall
total chronic absenteeism to 15% by the end of 2024-2025 school year.

Goal 3 - Severe Chronic Absenteeism:



Severe Chronic Absenteeism identifies students who were absent 30% or more full school days. In 2024-2025, at our school 2.2% (7 students) of students were recorded as Severe Chronic Absenteeism.Our goal is to reduce

low severe chronic absenteeism by 25% and continue to identify students and families in need and provide support where appropriate.
