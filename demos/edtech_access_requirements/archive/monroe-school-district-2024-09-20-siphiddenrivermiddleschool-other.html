Needs Assessment Summary
Describe the highest priorities and resource inequities (funding, staffing, materials, resources, etc.) identified through conducting the Comprehensive Needs Assessment that will be addressed in this plan.

Our purpose at Hidden River is to ensure that ALL students learn the essential academic, social, and citizenship skills to be successful in middle school and beyond. Our vision at Hidden River is that “All Means All.” To create a
system that meets the needs of all students takes a coordinated effort amongst a community of adults. As such, the vision of our work together at Hidden River is: the needs of the student come first. We will meet those needs
through unsurpassed collaboration. No one is big enough to work independently of others; the combined wisdom of one’s peers is greater than any individual. We will use a teamwork approach, share our insights, and take a
continuous interest in each other's growth. Simply stated, “All means ALL... takes ALL.”

During the 2022-23 school year, Hidden River Middle School's Guiding Coalition, Multi-Tiered Systems of Support (MTSS) Leadership Team, and School Advisory Team went through an intensive process of gathering and
analyzing data from multiple sources to identify a set of strategic priorities to move us toward continuous improvement in pursuit of our mission to meet the needs of all our students, and to provide a guaranteed student
experience that promotes a welcoming, inclusive environment for all our students. The needs assessment included observational data from Continua Consultants; survey and focus group data gathered by our partner Vincent
Perez of Equity Institute; and student perception data through our twice-annual Panorama school climate survey. At the end of the 2022-23 school year, Hidden River's leadership teams developed a strategic plan for improving
our student experience, and for improving our implementation of a guaranteed and viable curriculum that provides high levels of learning and growth for all our students. The foundational information gathering that took place
during the 2022-23 school year set in motion several areas of work that will require multi-year efforts, and as such these initial data sources-- while over a year old-- are still relevant in the improvement work we have
committed to during the 2024-25 school year.

Indeed, during the 2023-24 school year, we continued to monitor our growth in the way we provided a guaranteed student experience that promoted a sense of belonging for all students. We utilized our Panorama Survey
Data, our mid-year student perception data, and quarterly student surveys to monitor our growth in utilizing a set of schoolwide Common Expectations that were student generated, taught, reinforced, and celebrated
throughout the 2023-24 school year. We are celebrating significant growth and progress in our students’ knowledge of our Common Expectations and our staff use of these foundational expectations as high percentages of our
students indicated they not only knew the expectations but also experienced them being integrated meaningfully into our classrooms, including (but not limited to) being formed into co-created classroom agreements in the
majority of our classrooms. Yet we know this is just the foundation of our student experience-- while we demonstrated minimal growth in our overall sense of belonging data (only 40% of our students indicated a “favorable”
response on the Sense of Belonging Domain in our Spring, 2024, Panorama Survey), our MTSS team noted that one specific question on that survey can provide a more specific indicator for our areas of work during the
2024-25 school year: 70% of our students gave a “favorable” response to the question “Do you have a teacher or other adult at school you can count on to help you no matter what?” (An improvement from 63% in Spring,
2023). This question will be used to set our sense of belonging goal for the 2024-25 school year, as it aligns with a known protective factor for students, which is that they have at least one adult at school they can turn to for
help. Additional professional learning to create a strong sense of belonging and to create a higher level of student engagement in our classrooms centered around the foundational implementation of Culturally and Linguistically
Responsive Teaching practices. Using these practices as a baseline and guide indicates continued need for work on implementation of these strategies, which will have the impact of improving our ability to diversify our
pedagogical approaches and honor the diverse cultural and developmental needs of our students to better engage them in the classroom. In support of better understanding our student experience, attendance data was

practices; team-collected Essential Standards data from common assessments; performance data on annual SBA and WCAS state assessments; performance and growth data from iReady Reading and Math benchmark
diagnostics; The highest priority needs facing students and staff at Hidden River Middle School for the 2023-24 school year are as follows:

(1) Improving Academic Achievement: The percentage of students meeting proficiency on state assessments in ELA (reading and writing), Math, and Science are significantly below the outcomes we expect for our
students. Hidden River Middle School staff hold high expectations in our ability to meet the needs of students, and annually aim for 80% or more of our students to meet state academic standards, and when we do not
meet those levels it is an indicator that our core, Tier 1 instructional approaches need to be adjusted and improved.

(2) Reducing Educational Disparities: Achievement gaps between general education students and students with disabilities as well as our Multilingual Learners (MLLs) show a significant discrepancy in performance amongst
these subgroups relative to their same-aged peers. Specific learning and language acquisition strategies must be applied to increase achievement markers for students with disabilities and MLLs relative to our students.
A deeper and wider implementation of both Culturally and Linguistically Responsive Teaching practices (CLR) and Universal Design for Learning (UDL) approaches

(3) Increasing Sense of Belonging at School: The percentage of students expressing a low sense of belonging-- in particular the connection to a trusted adult at school-- represents a significant barrier to the vision for a
safe, welcoming environment for all students that exists at Hidden River Middle School.

(4) Increasing Consistent Attendance: The percentage of students who are below expected attendance levels to make adequate progress in their learning is below our expected levels.

SMARTIE Goals
What specifically are you trying to accomplish? Do your goals meet the criteria of a SMARTIE Goal?

A SMARTIE Goal is specific, measurable, attainable, realistic, time-bound, inclusive, and equitable and should answer the questions:
e What will be improved?
e By how much?
e By when?
e And, for whom/what?

Questions to ask and answer when addressing inclusion and equity:
e Will achieving this goal build success and/or shrink disparity gaps for specific student groups in our learning community?
e Does the goal ensure that traditionally marginalized students have equal access, and is there an element of fairness and justice inherent in the goal?
e Ifthe outcome specified in this goal isn’t specifically promoting equity and inclusion, is the process of achieving this goal going to improve equity and inclusion for all students? How?
e Who have we consulted to check for unintended negative consequences? Who needs to be consulted?
