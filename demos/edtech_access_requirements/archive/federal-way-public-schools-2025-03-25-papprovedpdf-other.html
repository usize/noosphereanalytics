Administrative Policies and Procedures Series: 4000 — Community Relations

DISTRIBUTION OF MATERIALS: 4060P

Distribution of materials by nonprofit organizations and government entities may be requested
by submitting information through the District’s approved flier distribution platform for review
and approval by the Communications Department.

The District will publish its approval process for the posting of e-fliers on the district website and
will verify that submitting organizations have valid nonprofit or governmental status. In order to
be considered for posting, the flier must:

e Promote the academic, vocational, or social/civil/cultural growth of students;

e Contain an express disclaimer that the school and the school district do not
endorse or sponsor the organization promoting the activity;

e Bescreened for the appropriateness of its content, and may not contain any language
proselytizing or disparaging religion;

e Be made available in designated quantities for students who do not have web access upon
request; and

e Prominently display scholarship availability.

The District will provide school newsletter editors with news articles directing students and their
parents to the website for community e-fliers.

Organizations and government entities that approach individual schools with requests to distribute
materials to students will be directed to the District offices.

For-profit companies’ materials will only be distributed to district staff.
The District or the school will not distribute materials that:

A. Are obscene, lewd, or vulgar;

B. Are libelous;

C. Contain language that is intimidating, demeaning, harassing, or threatening on the basis of
race, religion, color, national origin or ancestry, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation,
age, disability, marital, or veteran status, including, but not limited to racial, sexual, or
ethnic slurs;

D. Promote commercial enterprises;

E. Promote the violation of existing laws, regulations or ordinances, or official school policy,
tules, or regulations; or

F. Proselytize or disparage religious beliefs.

Administrative Policies and Procedures Series: 4000 — Community Relations

DISTRIBUTION OF MATERIALS: 4060P

The Communications Department designee will review and determine whether the materials are
approved for distribution to families. Any further review will be made by the
Superintendent/designee whose decision is final.

Annual distribution by non-profit organizations may be obtained on a case-by-case basis.
Distribution to Sites

The District will not deliver non-district fliers/information through its internal mail system. Fliers
and information must be uploaded to the approved district platform.

The building principal has the final authority regarding the distribution of materials at their
building.

District Hold Harmless Statement
All materials distributed must contain this statement:

“This event is not sponsored by Federal Way Public Schools and the District assumes no
responsibility for the conduct or safety during the event. In consideration for the privilege to
distribute this information/hold this event, the organization shall defend, indemnify and hold
Federal Way Public Schools harmless from any and all claims, injuries, damages, losses or suits
including attorney fees arising out of or in connection with this event, except for injuries and
damages caused by the sole negligence of the district.”
