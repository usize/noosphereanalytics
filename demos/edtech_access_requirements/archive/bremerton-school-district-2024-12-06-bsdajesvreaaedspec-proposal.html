3.0 Armin Jahr Elementary School (AJES) &
View Ridge Elementary Arts Academy (VREAA) Overview

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:

Korsmo Construction and TCF Architecture in partnership with OAC Project Management
wish to thank the District Administration and Staff at Armin Jahr Elementary School and
View Ridge Arts Academy for their contributions to the content of this Education
Specification. We had an ambitious agenda and are thankful for their time during the long
days of the Ed Spec Workshops.

It was clear from the outset that input was heavily influenced by Staff’s current
environment. Some Staff were dealing with the issues of an aging building, such as flooring
and mechanical systems being used past their useful lives resulting in poor climate control,
poor security, and finishes that are difficult to maintain. Staff were simply short on storage
or support spaces and made do with storing equipment and Curricular items in hallways or
corners of remote classrooms. Others were in portable classrooms with no weather
protection between their room and the remainder of the School, while citing some positive
aspects of the portables which included their large size rooms and their own toilet rooms.
Common desires of both schools include: connectivity between adjacent classrooms to
create an opportunity for Teachers to support each other, more calming spaces to de-
escalate children within the classroom, dimmable lighting, the need for access to restrooms,
and tackable or magnetic wall opportunities for posters and children’s work to be posted
without damaging walls.

Armin Jahr Elementary School (AJES) and View Ridge Elementary Arts Academy (VREAA) will
have slightly different communities to serve. AJES will cater to a strong dual language
program whereas VERAA will support a progressive performing arts program. While both
Schools have strong Special Education programs, AJES will also have a Headstart program.

Further discussion will happen throughout the design process to capture more specific
needs and to engage the District in larger issues that affect not only these two schools but
all new schools in the District. Such issues include flexibility of classrooms, gender-neutral
restrooms, and school security protocol and hardware.

This Ed Spec is a needed early step in bringing Bremerton new high performing systems and

spaces in safe and secure school buildings. We look forward to continuing development of
these two schools.

PROGRAM OVERVIEW:

Armin Jahr Elementary School: AJES is a Pre-K through 5" Grade elementary located at 800
Dibb St. which is a High Density Residential neighborhood. Julie Fairall is the Principal. The
School is housed in a 41,200 square foot one-story facility built in 1968 and remodeled in
1993 and 2004. The 11 acre site also contains seven portable buildings. AJES provides a
dual language education program.

View Ridge Arts Academy: VREAA Pre-K through 5* Grade elementary located on 3250
Spruce Ave which is a commercially zoned neighborhood. Carol Mendoza is the Principal.
The one-story 50,900 square foot school was constructed in 1956 and remodeled in 1994.
The large 23 acre site also contains ten portables, one of which is a preschool facility. It is
also the site of the East Bremerton Community Gym. VREAA provides an education
program with an emphasis on Performing Arts.

New Construction: The construction of both schools will occur simultaneously. Due to the
site size and constraints of the Wetland Buffers, Armin Jahr’s site is not large enough to
build a new school while keeping the existing school. The new school will need to
approximately be in the same spot as the existing building. This will require relocating
students temporarily, likely in Portables at the much larger View Ridge site.

Armin Jahr Elementary School: The existing school will be demolished to make way for the
new school. The north and west side of this parcel contains wetlands and is wooded. The
existing buildable area is mostly level and accessible.

View Ridge Arts Academy: The existing school will remain in operation for the duration of
construction of a ‘Portable Village’ to house the AJES students as well as construction of the
two new schools. Site locations are currently being studied including the North Playfield,
and the East side of the East Bremerton Community Gym.

Unfavorable soil conditions at the two schools will require special foundations such as pin
piles and/or aggregate piers.

The new schools have been programmed to include:

e Student Capacity: 544 for both schools comprised of 502 students in General
Classrooms and 42 students in Specialty Classrooms.

e Administrative Center: Generally including Reception / Secretaries Area,
Administrative Offices, Conference Room, and the Health Clinic.

e Staff Support Spaces: Such as the Staff Work Room, Staff Lounge, Staff Restrooms,
Central Supply, and similar spaces.

e Library / Media Center: Including the Library, Office / Workroom, and Storage
Room.

DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS:

Through workshops with staff and administration and District administration, the following
design considerations, along with the detailed Educational Specification requirements, have
been developed and used to guide the building and site design.

SITE:

e Separation of Cars and Buses: Car and bus traffic must be separated from each other
for the safety of students, staff and parents.

e Parking: Provide parking on site to provide adequate spaces for the typical school day
as well as special events such as assemblies and community events. Staff and
visitor/parent parking may be separated. Target 100 stalls plus a 20 car parent
pickup/drop off and 8 bus pickup/drop off.

e Site Access: Maximize vehicular queueing space to minimize traffic issues on adjoining
roads.

e Security / Access Control: The main office reception area needs direct line-of-site view
to the pick-up / drop-off and parking, thus the requirement for the front door facing
the parking lot, not the street, in addition to other security considerations such as
controlled access, use of the vestibule, etc.

e Service Access: Service access must be separated from car traffic. It is OK to combine
service access and bus traffic. It must provide service to a yard adjacent to the kitchen
and mechanical areas.

e Emergency Vehicle Access: Code requires that the fire department have access to the
full perimeter of the building from a truck with a 150 foot hose.

e Playfield / Playground Area: Playground should be accessible outside of the Activity
Wing. Provide a soccer field of a size appropriate to the site.

e Outdoor Classroom: Incorporate outdoor learning spaces in courtyards to serve both
small and large groups if feasible.
