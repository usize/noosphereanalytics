PUYALLUP

SCHOOL DISTRICT
A Tradition of Excellence

Business Services — Bond Resolution



At the November 18, 2024, School Board meeting
the Board approved the Bond Advisory
Committee's recommendation and
authorized the drafting of a resolution
to facilitate a bond election on February 11, 2025.


February 11, 2025 Election Results

¢ The election took place on February 11, 2025. With 63.34% approval, we surpassed the
supermajority requirement for the Puyallup School District Bond measure, Proposition 1 - an

incredible reflection of our community’s shared commitment to investing in students and

schools for which we are extremely grateful.

* However, one final requirement remains before the election is certified. In Washington State,
Bonds must also meet a voter validation threshold, requiring a minimum number of ballots
cast. Unfortunately, we were just under 3,000 ballots short of validation.

¢ Therefore, we recommend resubmitting Proposition I on the April 22, 2025 ballot.



Bond Advisoty Committee (BAC)
Recommendation:

This $800M plan, seeks to maintain a tax rate of $4.14 over 21 years (2025-2046) and focuses
on alleviating overcrowding, replacing aging schools, and enhancing safety.

The District’s total property tax rate may be influenced by changes to the school district
funding model or local levy authority made by the state legislature.

The scenario includes seven projects: additions to Emerald Ridge, Puyallup, and Rogers High
Schools; and replacements for Spinning, Waller Road, and Mt. View Elementary Schools, plus

the construction of a new elementary.

This plan leverages a $130M levy rollback and a 10% bond contingency, with a potential
$152.9 million state match collected upon nearing project completion.


Change to Resolution:

(m) Voters of the District approved a six-year capital levy at the February 13, 2024 special election to
restore, improve and replace school infrastructure. To the extent proceeds of the Bonds are used to
accelerate such capital projects as defined and described in Section 2(f), the Board will be authorized to
reduce or eliminate (i.e., “rollback’’) the capital levy for the purpose of maintaining the District’s total
property tax rate at current levels. The District’s total property tax rate may be influenced by changes to
the school district funding model or local levy authority made by the state legislature.
