Refrigeration and equipment repair costs have had a significant impact on our expenditures over the past year.
Many pieces of equipment such as walk-in coolers and freezers, dishwasher machines, ovens and steamers are
over 25 years old and approaching their end-of-life stage, necessitating additional maintenance and
replacement. Additionally, costs for parts, labor, and preventative maintenance continue to increase annually.
To help offset costs and improve the timeliness of repairs, a district refrigeration and kitchen equipment repair
technician position was created and filled in April 2024. This new position has taken on a significant amount of
nutrition services refrigeration and equipment repair responsibilities and greatly reduced the need for hiring
outside vendors, reducing overall costs.

Demographic Changes:
The economic status of our community continues to evolve. Currently, the district free and reduced rate sits at
39%, a decrease of 3% since last year’s high of 42%.

USDA Administrative Review

In November 2024, the Food and Nutrition Services Department had a USDA Administrative Review, which
occurs every five years. Three auditors evaluated all aspects of the child nutrition program including program
compliance, meal access and reimbursement, nutrition requirements and resource management. Auditors
spent time at Edgemont, Walker High School, Northwood, and Sunrise during meal periods where they
experienced first-hand how our meal services operate. After more than a week of review, the Food and
Nutrition Department received high marks for program integrity and quality. In the final report, the lead auditor
wrote “Puyallup School District Food and Nutrition Services continues to operate an excellent program. Thank
you to the Nutrition Services team for their exceptional work.”

Initiatives and Program Enhancements:

The Community Eligibility Provision (CEP) is a federal program that seven of our Puyallup schools qualified for
starting in the 2021-22 school year. The program provides free breakfasts and lunches to 100% of enrolled
students in qualified schools, regardless of family financial status. Federal funds cover 40% of the costs while
Washington State covers the remaining 60%. The seven schools that qualify for the program are Firgrove,
Sunrise, Spinning, Stewart, Wildwood, Waller Road, and Walker High School.

In 2022, the Washington State Legislature passed House Bill 1238. This program provides free breakfasts and
lunches to all students in elementary schools with over 30% of the student population qualifying for free or
reduced meal benefits. Our district has qualified 15 elementary schools this year in addition to the seven schools
under the CEP program. The only elementary school that doesn’t qualify for free meals for all students is
Fruitland Elementary with a free and reduced meal rate of 26%.

In September 2024, a new menu planning software system called Health E Pro was introduced in the school
district. This program allows community access to menus on the district website, or a mobile app. Features
include easy access to crucial meal information including daily food options, ingredient lists, food labels,
nutrition facts and food allergens. Additionally, menus can be translated into 180 different languages.


In the spring of 2024, the Food and Nutrition Services Department began working with the Tacoma Pierce
County Health Department (TPCHD) to reevaluate the meal service options at Walker High School. After
gathering feedback and working with the capital projects team, small structural changes were implemented in
the Walker High School kitchen and service area to allow for more meal service flexibility. This fall, a self-serve
salad bar was installed to allow for more fruit and vegetable options. Additionally, hot foods are now assembled
onsite allowing for increased meal entree options.

Upcoming Legislation

The Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) is currently proposing changes to WAC 392-157-125,
which would mandate a minimum seated lunch time of 20 minutes. Many school districts currently allow 20
minutes or less for lunch periods, including the time it takes for students to obtain their lunch and sit down to
eat. Fortunately, the Puyallup School District has a standard lunch period of 30 minutes at all schools, with most
schools already providing at least 20 minutes of seated lunch time.

Another proposal gaining momentum in recent weeks is House Bill 1404. This bill would ensure that all students
in Washington State, not just elementary students, receive free breakfast and lunch regardless of income status.
Governor Ferguson has identified this bill as a priority for his new administration, and it has garnered bipartisan
support. If enacted, the bill would take effect in the 2026-2027 school year.
