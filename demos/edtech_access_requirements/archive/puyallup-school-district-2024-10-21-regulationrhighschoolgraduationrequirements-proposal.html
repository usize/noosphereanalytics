1. High School graduation requirements for Puyallup School District students include requirements in the
following areas:
A. Credits and subject areas requirements
B. Non-credit requirements
C. Pathways to Graduation requirements

2. Earning Credit in the Puyallup School District
For the Classes of 2022 and beyond, students will be expected to earn a total of 24 credits in order to
complete graduation requirements.

A. High school credit will be awarded for successful completion of a specified unit of study, Successful
completion of a specified unit of study means:

1. Earning a passing grade according to the district’s grading policy; and/or

2. Demonstrating proficiency/mastery of content standards as determined by the district;
and/or

3. Successfully completing an established number of hours of planned instructional activities to
be determined by the district.

B. All credits which have been earned by students transferring from an accredited public or private
school will be accepted by all schools in the Puyallup School District. Letter grades will be posted
and credit granted to the extent the credit matches District graduation requirements.

C. Although 9th grade is housed in junior high buildings in the Puyallup School District, it is considered
the first year of high school and all credits earned in 9th grade count toward high school graduation.

D. A student’s diploma or transcript may be withheld until the student pays for any school property
that has been lost or willfully damaged. Upon payment for damages, or the equivalency through
voluntary work, the diploma or transcript will be released.

3. Guidelines for Earning Alternative Credit
A. Equivalency Credit
Puyallup School District recognizes the need to help students pursue individual career goals as they
comply with graduation requirements. The District provides the opportunity for students to fulfill


required credit with equivalent credit from other courses. Courses will be established as equivalent
based on similar objectives and content through the Department of Equity and Instructional
Leadership.

Credit may be granted provided the following requirements are met:
1. Prior permission has been granted by the principal or designee.
2. The program fits the educational plan submitted by the student.
3. Credit is granted from approved schools. The following is a list of approved schools:
a. Schools approved by the National University Extension Association
b. Community and technical colleges, four-year colleges and universities, state-approved
private schools in Washington State, and state-approved online course providers.
c. Other schools or institutions approved by the Puyallup School District after evaluation
for a particular course offering.
Credit may also be granted and transcripted to allow for core classes that students have earned
through Puyallup School District courses that meet both graduation and college entrance
requirements.

4. Demonstrating Mastery/Proficiency of Content
The district will manage the assessment process so that students seeking mastery-based credit can
demonstrate mastery/proficiency in various subject areas. Mastery-based credit can be used either for
awarding credit in place of a traditional course or for credit recovery purposes.

Students may recover credit by demonstrating mastery in the following ways:

A. State Assessments:

A. The student may recover up to 3.0 credits for identified subjects following a failed or incomplete
course if the student meets standard on a state assessment in the equivalent subject;

B. The student may recover 1.0 credit following a failed or incomplete course if the student meets
standard on another approved state alternative that meets the graduation requirement.

C. The student may recover up to 2.0 credits for missing identified courses if the student meets
standard on a state assessment in the equivalent subject. Application of this to replace graduation
requirements in advance is not permissible.

B. District-Created Assessments:

The student may obtain up to 1.0 credits in all subject areas by passing a district-created assessment that
is aligned to state learning standards. Students do not need to have attempted and failed a course
before being eligible for the following options:

e Locally created written or oral test;

e Written report by the student;

e Student-designed portfolio of work;

e Student presentation or oral defense of their learning in the course;

e Hands on demonstration of knowledge and skills; or

e A combination of assessment approaches as defined by the district.

Next higher-level course: Credit may be awarded for a course when the student successfully completes the
next higher-level course in a sequence with a C- or higher that includes a natural progression of the state
learning standards from the previous course. State or locally determined learning standards will be used as
the guide when making decisions regarding what courses should qualify.

A. First Waiver (.5 PE credit) - The student must meet proficiency/competency at 70% or higher in one
of the following:
1. OSPI-developed fitness assessment: Concepts of Health and Fitness
2. District-approved fitness assessment (cognitive assessment on fitness education) - OR
B. Second Waiver (.5 PE credit) - The student must meet proficiency/competency at 70% or higher in
one of the following:
1. OSPI-developed fitness assessment: Fitness Planning
2. District-approved fitness assessment (cognitive assessment on fitness education that is
different than First Waiver) - OR
C. Third Waiver (.5 PE credit) - The student must meet proficiency/competency in one of the following:
1. District-approved fitness plan/portfolio at 80% or higher
2. District-approved fitness assessment (cognitive assessment on fitness education that is
different than Second Waiver) at 70% or higher

6. Waiver of District Graduation Requirements
High school diplomas will be granted to those students who have met all graduation requirements. The
District recognizes that in
exceptional situations the best interest of individual students may be served by waiving a graduation
requirement in favor of a specified
requirement that more suitably meets the needs of the student. In such instances the following shall
apply:

A. Upon consultation with the counselor, a formal request for a waiver of a graduation requirement
shall be initiated by the student or the parent/guardian.

B. The principal shall make a written determination based upon input provided by the student,
parent/guardian, counselor, and/or any other person as invited by any of these parties.

C. The following should be considered in establishing the “good cause” basis for this decision:

1. the student’s four-year and post-high school plan,

2. evidence that a waiver of the graduation requirements is in the best interest of the student,

3. any persuasive evidence that specific educational and/or career harm would be incurred by
the student should the waiver not be granted, and

4. fulfillment of state statutory requirements.

D. At the time any graduation requirement is waived, the principal shall substitute a personalized
pathway requirement that is directly related to the student’s educational and/or career plans. Where
applicable, every effort shall be made to establish such individualized graduation requirements that
will assist the student to achieve as many as possible of the competencies of the waived course.

E. When a course requirement is waived, the principal will ensure that it is recorded in the student’s
permanent record.

Students earning a Washington State diploma must pass a course of study in Washington’s History and
Government OR another state’s history and government. Washington State History taken in the 7th or 8th
grade may be counted to help fulfill the requirement in high school, but will not be counted as a high
school credit. In the Puyallup School District, Pacific Northwest Studies is taken in 7th grade to fulfill this
requirement. If a student has transferred in from another state and has completed that state's history and
government course, the requirement for Washington State History and Government will have been
satisfied. This class will show up as a "P" (pass) and will not be included in the student's high school GPA.
In essence, Washington State History and Government is a non-credit bearing high school graduation
requirement.

Students, who have not met the requirement at another school or in another state, may fulfill this
requirement through an alternative learning experience approved by the Department of Instruction,
Learning, Curriculum, and Assessment.

Twelfth grade students who transfer from another state, and who have or will have earned 2.5 credits in
social studies at graduation, may have the Washington state history requirement waived by their principal
if without such a waiver they will not be able to graduate with their class.

. 12th Grade Transfer Students

A 12th grade student transferring from another high school may be permitted to graduate with fewer
credits provided minimum state-course and credit requirements are satisfied and the student successfully
passes a full schedule of classes during the 12th grade.

Granting High School Graduation to Students with Special Educational Needs

No student shall be denied the opportunity to earn a high school diploma solely because of limitations on
the student's ability. Pursuant to WAC 180-51-115, and upon principal approval, students with special
education needs may:

A. Remain in school up to and including the school year in which a student reaches 21 years of age.

B. Be granted special accommodations, or in lieu thereof, be granted exemption from any requirement
if such requirement impedes the student’s progress toward graduation and there is a direct
relationship between the failure to meet the requirement and the student’s limitation.

C. Be placed in a special education program in accordance with Chapter 28A.13RC W if the student is
eligible.

Granting High School Credit to 7th and 8th Grade Students (Fast Start Credit)
A student who has completed high school (grades 9-12) courses while in 7th and 8th grade shall be given
high school credit which shall be applied to fulfilling graduation requirements if:
A. The course was taken with high school (grades 9-12) students, or course name, content, and rigor
that has been designated by policy as a high school course, and
B. The student successfully passed the course by completing the same course requirements and
examinations as high school students.
In order to opt-out of receiving high school credit for Fast Start coursework, 2400 Form Fi must be
completed prior to the last school day in May of the requesting student’s senior year. Students prior to the
graduation class of 2024 will continue to complete Form 2400F1 prior to graduation in order to opt-in to
earning high school credit.

High School and Beyond Plans

Beginning in the eighth grade, each student will develop a plan for satisfying the state and district’s high
school graduation requirements for their first year after high school completion. The plan should be
developed in collaboration with the student, parent, and district staff. The plan should include how the
student will satisfy the district’s academic credit requirements, preparation for successfully completing the
statewide required assessments, a description of the student’s community connections and the student's
goals for the year following graduation. Each student plan should be reviewed annually to assess student
progress, to adjust the plan, and to advise the student on steps necessary for successful completion of the
plan. The plan will also provide information on dual credit programs and the college

bound scholarship program. Copies of the High School and Beyond Plan form will be provided to students’
parents or guardians in their native language if that language is one of the two most frequently spoken
non-English languages of students in the district.
