Public Comment Procedure
The School Board welcomes and values public comment on educational issues and recognizes the
importance of the opportunity for members of the public to express their thoughts to the School Board.
It is the policy of the Monroe School District to promote mutual respect, civility, and orderly conduct
among District employees, parents, and the public. We kindly ask that you refrain from comments that
violate school district policy.
Individuals are asked to limit comments to three (3) minutes.
A designated speaker for a group is asked to keep comments to five (5) minutes.
The Board does not respond to public comments during the business meeting. The Boards silence does not
signal agreement or endorsement of the speakers remarks. The president may interrupt or terminate an
individual's statement when it is too lengthy, personally directed, abusive, obscene, irrelevant, or targets a
protected class. The board president will determine the appropriateness of all such rulings.
You may provide public comments virtually if you are planning on attending the meeting via Zoom.
Information regarding public comments at school board meetings can be found here:
https://www.monroe.wednet.edu/about/school-board/public-comments

3.06 OpenSciEd Middle School Science Instructional
Materials Adoption (Second Reading)

Meeting :

Jul 15, 2024 - Regular Board Meeting

Type :

Action

Recommended Action :

The Monroe School District Board of Directors adopt and
approve the OpenSciEd Middle School Science
Instructional Materials

Public Content
Description:
It has been over 10 years since our last science adoption and our current middle school
science materials are not aligned to the Next Generation Science Standards. Teachers
have been working diligently to curate materials or repurpose existing materials to better
align them to the standards. This adoption will support them in working more efficiently and
effectively to meet the needs of students. In addition, it will support our efforts in becoming
a district-wide PLC and aligning both materials and practices across buildings.
We are asking the board to review the OpenSciEd Instructional Materials for grades 6-8 as
put forth by the Instructional Materials Committee. These are Inquiry-based curricular
materials aligned to the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS). These instructional
resources are part of the Creative Commons license which means a continued low cost
(online resources are free) for yearly access to resources. Teacher editions and supplies
to support the curriculum are needed to fully implement the curriculum. This includes
science kits that contain both consumable and non-consumable products. Consumable

products will have to be replenished annually. Kendall Hunt has teacher editions as well as
student workbooks but these will not need to be re-purchased on an annual basis. This will
be the first reading of the materials and there are many links embedded in the slide
presentation for the board to look at before and after the presentation.

Motion & Voting
The Monroe School District Board of Directors adopt and approve the OpenSciEd Middle School
Science Instructional Materials
Motion by Molly Barnes, second by Jeremiah Campbell.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Jennifer Bumpus, Jeremiah Campbell, Molly Barnes, James Etzkorn
