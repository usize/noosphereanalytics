Introduction

College Board created the AP African American Studies course and began a two-
year pilot with the full course becoming available during the 2024-2025 school
year. This will be new course offered in the Puyallup School District in the 2025-
2026 school year to bring more opportunities to our students. The African-
American Odyssey text will support student in the AP African American Studies
course.

History

e In 2022, 60 schools piloted this course that was released by the College
Board. The following year, the pilot expanded to 700 U.S. High Schools. In
the 2024/2025 school year it was made widely available to all U.S. Schools.
The Board approved the course for adoption in Puyallup this past fall, and
we are now at the point of adopting a textbook. Students who score a a 3
or above on the exam may be eligible for up to 5 humanities credits at the
college level.

Rational for Recommending

e Standards Alignment
o The course AP African American Studies includes multiple disciplines
such as history, economic, political, art, literature and music. This text
focuses on creating the historical context including the role of various
leaders. This aligns well with the content in the College Board Course
and Exam Description (CED) for AP African American Studies.
e Organization and Presentation of Materials


o The CED for AP African American Studies Curriculum is separated into
4 units. The African American Odyssey Text includes 6 units, each
made of 3-5 chapters. The text includes maps, figures, tables, and key
vocabulary highlighted throughout, and review questions.
e Support Material and References
o The curriculum includes teaching and learning materials. They have
an eText that includes audio, study plans, and online primary sources
and videos.
e Focus on Citizenship
o The textbook explores the theme of citizenship throughout American
history, from the struggles of enslaved people to gain freedom for
civil rights and beyond, with a focus on: Reconstruction and the fight
for rights for African Americans to gain full citizenship through the
13%, 14 and 15 Amendments to the Constitution; the Civil Rights
Movement that led to landmark legislation of the Civil Rights Act of
1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965 which further reinforced
African American citizenship rights; and the importance of
institutions fostering civic engagement and leadership such as
churches, schools, and community organizations.
e Quality of Information
o The College Board is a long-standing, well respected organization that
delivers rigorous Advanced Placement (honors) curricula to students.
Contributors, developers and editors include Professors from various Ivy
League institutions, including Harvard, Georgetown, Columbia, and MIT.
The Senior Director for the project, Brandi Waters, holds a Ph.D from
Yale. Henry Louis Gates, a top scholar in the field, was consulted on the
project, as were other top scholars from around the Country.

Bias Screening

Teachers completed bias screening on December 9, 2024. During this review we
found the curriculum to meet most areas of the bias screening well. There were a
few exceptions, which due to the historical nature of the content, may happen.
While the text does include diverse voices and roles, there is still a larger emphasis
on males in leadership roles, and less inclusion of persons with disabilities. The full
course from College Board includes a number of required sources that will add to


the types of peoples and perspectives discussed in the course. We appreciated the
neutral tones and multiple perspectives used to discuss different points of views
and events.

Community/Student Review

The community review was available during the 3 curriculum fairs at the high
schools. There were samples of the text, online access, and a QR or paper survey
available. The high schools held these on February 4, 5, and 6 respectively.
Students were able to access the curriculum online during social studies classes
and provided feedback between January 27-February 7, 2025.

“Is there anything else you'd like to share with us about the materials you
reviewed?”

had nothing to add.

e Of the 35 respondents who commented on this question, 27 (77%)
responded positively to the content and/or the layout of the textbook and 8
(23%) responded negatively to the content and/or the layout of the
