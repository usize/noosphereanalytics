Regular Board Meeting
October 22, 2024

Responses to the Board’s questions/comments on Agenda Item 9.D — Capital Projects and Winterization Update for the
October 22, 2024 Regular Board Meeting:

1. Agenda Item 9.D — Capital Projects and Winterization Update

1. On page 10, how much of the levy dollars will be spent on these projects??
Liliana Cardenas: The estimated total levy dollars spent will be $2,267,000. The PAC lighting project will
begin in the summer 2025, and is a planned combination of 24-25 and 25-26 levy dollars.

Generator Replacement: Foster, Tukwila and Thorndyke $130,000

$198,000
Gas Fired AC Units at Service Center: AC-01, AC-02, AC-03

$160,000
Tukwila and Thorndyke: Cooling Towers and Electrical Upgrades

Service Center: Roof Retrofit
$199,000

PAC Lighting $800,000

Foster and Showalter: Locker Room Upgrades $780,000

2. On page 10, what are the projected projects that will be funded by the levy in 25-26 and how much are
they estimated to cost?
Liliana Cardenas: Levy projects for 2025-26 are estimated to cost $1,549,254 and will include Foster HVAC
Upgrades, PAC Lighting (continued from 2024-25), Showalter Gym and Commons interior Painting, Tukwila
Elementary Water Heater Replacement, Tukwila Elementary Interior Painting, Ongoing Energy Management
(McKinstry), Foster ASHRAE II Audit (related to HB-1257).

3. On page 10, what process is used to determine which projects are to be funded in 24-25?
Liliana Cardenas: The process takes into consideration a combination of several factors including the budget,
safety, classroom impact, life expectancy of equipment, facility and condition planning, and project priorities.

4. On page 6, are any high priority projects named as part of the DOE Energy Efficiency Grant?
Liliana Cardenas: No. The DOE Energy Efficiency Grant has specific requirements and restrictions. TSD
submitted an application for the replacement of the natural gas-fired kitchen Make-Up Air Unit (MAU) and
Air Handler Unit (AHU) at the Showalter kitchen. This project will improve energy efficiency for compliance
with HB-1257.
