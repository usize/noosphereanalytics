Purposes and Intended Outcomes

1. Aligning the district around the vision of our Race &
Equity Team.
¢ Creating district-wide vision for race and equity
(based on RE committee/Equity Leads).
¢ Infusing anti-racist approaches across Strategic Plan,
School Improvement Plans, district policies, and in
daily practice at sites.

2. Disrupting inconsistent and racially predictive disciplinary
practices.
¢ Implementation of cross-district restorative practices.


Summary Of Our Work So Far

Formed in 2014 as Diversity Committee

Policy draft and adoption 2016 (Renamed by students)

Policy reworked and adopted again 2017

Race and Equity retreat to write procedures 2018 (facilitation taken over by
educators due to administrative turnover)

Procedures under construction 2018-current

2019 introduction of building team leads

2020 began consultation with PSESD 3 year plan

2020 actual equity tool kit referenced in current CBA adopted - LINK

e 2024 onward: Work with school leads with the goal of facilitating all-staff
professional development on anti-racism embedded in all PD



Progress In Key Topics


SWOT Analysis: Strengths

What's going well, where we are making progress:

e District-level Race & Equity Lead Team members are
well established; continued work being done at sites
and across district.

e Race and Equity teams are written into the CBA, along
with compensation for members.

e District opened with Race and Equity Summit in August
2023 to set the tone.

e There is now aclear anti-racist vision with intended
outcomes for our entire district.



SWOT Analysis: Threats/Challenges

What has been difficult in the process of advancing anti-racism
at Tukwila schools:

e Burnout / high turnover in administration and staff has
resulted in inconsistencies and incomplete initiatives.

e Lack of accountability for carrying out district expectations:
o Continued actions demonstrate White fragility,
anti-Blackness, and racism.
o Tolerance of racist behaviors that go on despite
corrective action or that seem to go on uncorrected.
o Anti-racism work does not filter across district.



SWOT Analysis: Threats/Challenges

Barriers to making the needed progress & reaching our
intended outcomes, and what we will do about it:

e Lack of required professional development time dedicated
to anti-racism.

e District anti-racism policy needs to be updated

e Race and Equity Leads are a team, but not every meeting
at departments and sites are centered on anti-racism.

e District budget situation



SWOT Analysis: Opportunities

Areas in Tukwila schools where we are poised to make improvements in
anti-racism this year and going forward:

e Continued required professional development on anti-racism.

e New Board members bring new ideas and perspectives that can help
move us forward with anti-racism.

e More clearly share the elements of each school's SIPs and use asa
guiding tool throughout the year.

o SIPs will be 1) collaboratively developed; 2) used as action items;
3) infused with concrete anti-racist goals and approaches.

e District policy requires district-wide racial equity plan with clear
accountability and metrics.

o This racial equity plan should be implemented through Strategic
Plan and SIPs.

o Equity plan should include restorative approaches and equity audits
for all schools and buildings
