INTER-LOCAL AGREEMENT

Between

MONROE SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 103
and

CITY OF MONROE POLICE DEPARTMENT

THIS AGREEMENT is made and entered into this _28 th day of _May , 2024, by and between
the MONROE SCHOOL DISTRICT #103 (“District”), and the CITY OF MONROE
(“City”) by and through the MONROE POLICE DEPARTMENT ("MPD"), regarding School
Resource Officer Services for the 2024-2025 school year. This agreement shall supersede any
preceding agreements in place between the District and the MPD with respect to the District’s
School Resource Officer program.

WHEREAS, it is the intent and desire of the District and the MPD to maintain student,
staff, and patron safety; and

WHEREAS, the District desires a full-time police officer, hereafter called a School
Resource Officer (“SRO”), to provide services to Monroe School District at those times school
is in-session; and

WHEREAS, the MPD has full-time police officers and desires to provide services to
the District as set forth herein; and

WHEREAS, Chapter 39.34 RCW, the Interlocal Cooperation Act, provides authority
to enter into such agreements; and

WHEREAS, the District and the MPD have previously executed and operated under
prior agreements providing for SRO services; and

WHEREAS, the District and the MPD mutually desire to update their contractual
relationship to reflect and ensure compliance with applicable state law.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual agreements set forth herein, the
District and the MPD agree as follows:

A. Mission and Purpose

The mission of the SRO program is to improve school safety and the educational climate at the
school. The purpose of this Agreement is to fulfill the requirements of RCW 28A.320.124(2),
which requires an agreement between school districts and the local law enforcement agency for

B. SRO ROLE and LIMITATIONS

The role of the SRO on campus typically involves three parts: educator, informal counselor, and
law enforcer. The focus of any SRO working in the District is to keep students out of the
criminal justice system when reasonably possible. The District shall integrate the SRO into the
school community through participation in faculty and student meetings and assemblies as
appropriate. SROs shall support a positive school climate by developing positive relationships
with students, parents, and staff, and by helping to promote a safe, inclusive, and positive
learning environment. The SRO is a valuable team member of School Based Threat Assessment
Teams, which are preventative in purpose, and SRO is encouraged to participate consistent
applicable District policy and procedure.

The primary responsibility for maintaining proper order and conduct in the schools resides with
school principals or their designee, with the support of other school staff. This may include
minor violations of the law occurring during school hours or at school activities. The SRO
program does not diminish the District’s authority. Principals or their designee maintain order
and handle all student discipline matters consistent with District policy and procedure. Where
appropriate and practicable, the SRO will interact with students informally to reinforce school
tules.

Teachers and school administrators may ask an SRO to intervene if a student’s presence poses an
immediate and continuing danger to others or an immediate and continuing threat of material and
substantial disruption of the educational process or in other emergency circumstances. SROs do
not need to be asked before intervening in emergencies.

As a general rule, law enforcement activity should take place at a location other than school
premises. However, there are circumstances where formal law enforcement intervention/activity
at school is warranted and may be conducted by an SRO. These law enforcement activities may
include but are not limited to interviews and interrogations; search of a student’s person,
possessions, or locker; citations, filing of delinquency petitions, referrals to a probation officer,
actual arrests, and other referrals to the juvenile justice system, consistent District policy and
procedure.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, nothing in this Section B, or any other provision of this
Agreement, shall be construed in any manner as limiting, constraining, or otherwise superseding
the law enforcement functions of the SRO, which functions shall be exercised in the SRO’s

exclusive professional judgment as a Washington peace officer. For the avoidance of doubt, due
to the SRO's peace officer certification and employment with the MPD, the District expressly
acknowledges that the SRO possesses arrest authority and has the discretion to make law
enforcement decisions independently, without requiring consultation with District staff or
administrators.

Per RCW 10.93.160, the SRO duties do not extend to immigration enforcement and the SRO will
not inquire into or collect information about an individual’s immigration or citizenship status, or
place of birth. Neither will the SRO provide information pursuant to notification requests from
federal immigration authorities for the purposes of civil immigration enforcement, except as
required by law, consistent with District policy and procedure.

C. THE MONROE SCHOOL DISTRICT AGREES TO:

Provide the MPD with copies of all applicable District policies and procedures that are
pertinent to and reasonably necessary to perform the SRO’s functions. Without limitation of the
foregoing, the District has adopted a policy and procedure governing school safety and security
staff pursuant to RCW 28A.320.124(1) entitled 4311, School Safety and Security Services
Program, and 4311P, School Safety and Security Services Program. A copy has been provided
to the MPD. In the event of any further or future amendments, the District will promptly
provide a full and complete copy to the MPD.

Notify in a reasonable manner and timeframe the SRO or the MPD of credible serious incidents
of violence or sexual violence, and/or incidents of possession of weapons on District property
or at school-related activities, brought to the attention of a school administrator.

Abstain from obstructing or otherwise interfering with the exercise of the SRO’s law
enforcement function.

Give full cooperation and reasonable access to District property or school functions to police
officials responding to a request for the investigation of reported acts of violence, physical
intimidation, or possession of weapons on District property or at school functions within the
city limits of Monroe. This will be allowed consistent with applicable law and District policy.

Permit reasonable access to District property for a law enforcement response to a civil disorder,
riot, or for arrest of students, staff, or patrons whom probable cause for an arrest exists.

Provide the SRO reasonable access and training to Monroe School District Skyward to
obtain the names, addresses and telephone numbers of students and parents subject to
Federal and State laws, including the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)
(20 U.S.C. Sec. 1232g) and applicable District policy. Other than directory information, no
other information will be accessed or memorialized absent emergency safety circumstances.

D. The Monroe Police Department Agrees to:

Select the SRO with input from the District Superintendent or designee, based upon criteria
mutually agreed by the MPD and the District.

Retain the authority and responsibility for training the SRO. By signing this Agreement, the MPD
confirms that any SRO assigned to the School District will, complete within 6 months of being
assigned in his or her capacity as SRO, have received training sufficient to satisfy the mandate of
RCW 28A.320.1242(1)(a)(ii1), including training on the following topics:

e Constitutional and civil rights of children in schools, including state law governing search
and interrogation of youth in schools;

e Child and adolescent development;
e Trauma-informed approaches to working with youth;
e Recognizing and responding to youth mental health issues;

e Educational rights of students with disabilities, the relationship of disability to behavior,
and best practices for interacting with students with disabilities;

e Bias free policing and cultural competency, including best practices for interacting with
students from particular backgrounds, including English learner, LGBTQ, immigrant,
female, and nonbinary students;

e Local and national disparities in the use of force and arrests of children;

e Collateral consequences of arrest, referral for prosecution, and court involvement;

e Resources available in the community that serve as alternatives to arrest and prosecution
and pathways for youth to access services without court or criminal justice involvement;

e De-escalation techniques when working with youth or groups of youth;
e State law regarding restraint and isolation in schools, including RCW 28A.600.485;

e The federal Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (20 U.S.C. Sec. 1232g)

Director of Risk and Safety and/or school administration for performance and program
feedback.

E. UNUSUAL OCCURRENCE CONSIDERATIONS, REGULAR DUTY HOURS, AND
SUPERVISION OF THE SCHOOL RESOURCE OFFICER:

Due to unusual occurrences in the District, such as pandemics and natural disasters, along with
the developing role of remote, hybrid and in-person student learning in the District, the SRO
may not be needed during certain days and times when some or all students are not engaged in
in-person learning. Therefore, during those days and hours that the District determines an SRO
is not needed due to quarantine, remote learning or other unforeseen circumstances related to
unusual occurrences, the SRO will not be assigned to the District. The District will notify the
MPD at least 24 hours in advance, or as soon as possible, for those days and times the District
determines an SRO is not necessary. The District will be entitled to a prorated refund for those
days and hours the SRO is not assigned to the District.

During a normal schedule, to facilitate the exposure and value of the SRO position, the District
desires the SRO to be available for regular school duty on those days and during those hours
that school is in session for on-site learning. The Monroe Chief of Police or designee, as
needed, will make reasonable efforts to replace SRO absences during the school day due to
scheduling conflicts, training, vacation, or other long-term duty assignments with other

{JZ2L4889-0591-1177;3/13011.900000/}


appropriately trained commissioned officers. The SRO shall notify the District designee in
writing regarding the hour(s) or day(s) that they will be absent and, if possible, the name and
title of their replacement, as soon as reasonably possible.

Regular hours may be modified as needed upon mutual agreement. The MPD shall make
reasonable attempts to ensure that any SRO assigned to the District to fill any absences will
be trained consistent with this Agreement and reasonably support efforts by the District to
increase immediate understanding of the replacement SRO of such obligations.

The Chief of Police or designee shall oversee the SRO, perform scheduled and unscheduled
visits to the schools, and, without limitation of the District’s obligations under Section C, shall
work cooperatively with the District in coordinating, implementing, and developing the SRO
program. If there is a specific District concern regarding SRO performance or suitability, the
District will timely share this with the Chief of Police and the Chief of Police and District
Director of Risk and Safety shall meaningfully confer over resolution of the District’s concern. If
concerns over the fitness of a particular SRO to be present around District facilities or students
come to the attention of the Chief of Police, including any issues of professional boundaries,
such concerns shall be brought to the immediate attention of the District Director of Risk and
Safety.

The District designee or school principal, while having no direct authority over the SRO, may
request that the SRO assist in additional duties that are mutually agreed upon by the
Superintendent or designee and the Chief of Police or designee as long as they do not violate
the terms of this Agreement. During those hours that school is in regular session, the SRO may
also be assigned related duties on or off campus as determined by the Chief of Police.

The MPD shall send the SRO’s expected work schedule 30 days in advance to the Director
of Risk/Safety. The SRO will notify District and building administrators and necessary
office staff at Monroe High School, Park Place Middle School and Leaders in Learning
when on leave or unavailable to respond to calls at the school.

With the exception of “hot pursuit,” the SRO and other law enforcement officers will
check in at each school building’s main office when arriving on campus.

For law enforcement service requests outside of regular school hours the regular processes
for scheduling off-duty police officers will apply.

G. ADDITIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE SCHOOL RESOURCE OFFICER:

The SRO shall, where practicable and warranted in the SRO’s professional judgment:

1.

Take appropriate law enforcement action at the District’s request against intruders and
unwanted guests who may appear at the school and at related school functions to the
extent that the SRO may do so under authority of law.

Attend parent/faculty meetings to solicit support and understanding of the SRO program,
as well as to assist parents and faculty members in law enforcement related problems
involving students, when requested and approved by the Chief of Police or designee.
Be available for student, parent, and faculty member conferences in order to assist with
the identification and resolution of problems of law enforcement or crime related in
nature.

Attend the District’s Student Threat Assessment meetings to review, participate and
develop student safety support plans, including offering input on threat assessment
consistent with the SRO’s training as a law enforcement professional.

Become familiar with a variety of community agencies that offer assistance to children,
youths and their families (e.g., mental health clinics, drug treatment centers, etc.) and
shall make referrals when appropriate.

. Assist the school principal and/or building, or District’s Emergency Management Team,

in developing emergency plans and strategies to prevent and/or minimize dangerous
situations.

. Provide assistance to other law enforcement officers and agencies in matters related to

the SRO's assigned duties.

Participate in or attend school related functions whenever possible and as approved by
the Chief of Police or designee, including appropriate staff training as mutually agreed
upon by the Superintendent or designee and the Chief of Police or designee.

The SRO is not to be used for regularly assigned lunchroom duties, bus duty, hall monitoring, as
a crossing guard, or for continuing vehicle traffic control. If there is a temporary problem or
emergency, however, the SRO may assist the school until the problem or emergency is resolved.

H. AUTHORIZED USE OF TECHNOLOGIES:

The District maintains a Video Management System (VMS) and Rapid Responder (RR)
coordination and information tool, to promote the safety and security of students and staff. The
VMS system currently has over 100 cameras placed in areas of the District where there is not a
reasonable expectation of privacy. Some of these cameras may also provide visibility into public
areas around district facilities, including joint-use fields, intersections, bus stops, etc. that may be
of interest to law enforcement. The RR system is currently utilized as the primary
communication and coordination tool for all incidents in the District. The District Superintendent
has granted access to RR and the VMS to authorized District staff to actively monitor and/or
retrieve footage related to safety or security incidents. To further promote school safety and
security, the SRO will be granted access to the MSD VMS and RR tools.

This routine access to RR and the VMS by an SRO is for the purpose of reacting to criminal

incidents and addressing safety issues at District facilities. The SRO shall not use RR or the

VMS for anything other than said purposes. Any viewing of confidential student information
shall comply with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act - FERPA.

If a critical incident occurs and the MPD retrieves recorded footage, it shall be provided to the
District to ensure that said footage is securely preserved per Washington State Records Retention
Schedules. Retained footage shall not be transferred outside of Districts systems via email,
upload, disk, flash drives, etc. without acknowledgement by the District of a subpoena, court
order, or the express permission of the District Superintendent or designee, unless otherwise
required by the Public Records Act.

The District will provide two District VMS access licenses to the Monroe Police Department to
be expressly used by the SRO and Incident Command during incidents deemed critical in nature.

The District reserves the right to limit MPD’s access to RR and the VMS depending upon usage
and system capacity. MPD understands that the use of the VMS will be monitored. The District
will maintain final approval of any and all access provided to MPD.

J. COMPLAINTS & DISMISSAL:

Members of the public may file a complaint concerning the SRO’s performance under this
agreement by submitting said complaint in writing to the Superintendent or designee, or the
Chief of Police or designee. All such complaints will be reasonably responded to by the District
and the MPD, and/or investigated in accordance with each agency’s policies, procedures, and
labor agreements. Provided, that nothing in this Section J shall be construed as: (1) creating any
cause of action against or liability to the SRO, the MPD, or the City beyond that existing under
Federal and State law; (11) superseding or otherwise modifying any applicable City policy or
collective bargaining agreement; or (111) limiting the City’s exclusive authority to discipline,
retain, compensate, terminate, or otherwise supervise the SRO.

To dismiss an SRO from the duties described in this agreement, the Superintendent or designee
shall communicate in writing to the MPD a request to change or terminate the SRO or this
agreement. The District will outline reasons for the requested change. Absent agreement by the
parties to resolve a change, the SRO shall be replaced as soon as reasonably possible depending
on the training needs and availability of a replacement.
