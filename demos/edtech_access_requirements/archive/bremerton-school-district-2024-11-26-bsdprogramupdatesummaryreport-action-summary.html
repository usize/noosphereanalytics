PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Over the past month, we’ve reached significant milestones on several Bond Program projects, while
proactively planning for community engagement and managing key risks.

We've successfully completed the Validation phase for the Armin Jahr and View Ridge elementary
school replacements, paving the way for the Schematic Design phase to begin. Our Advisory
Committee has provided vital feedback to shape these projects, and we’ve begun hosting initial
outreach events for staff and families.

For the District-Wide HVAC upgrades, a closer look at our existing infrastructure revealed additional
challenges that we'll navigate thoughtfully to ensure long-term efficiency and compliance with
evolving standards.

Our Consolidated Facilities project has gained approval to proceed with a Design-Build delivery, and
we’re moving forward with the selection process.

This past month has been pivotal in transitioning from planning and validation to design and
engagement. Whether advancing design at Armin Jahr and View Ridge, assessing complex HVAC
upgrades, initiating Design-Build procurement for Consolidated Facilities, or preparing for the
Renaissance project, the district remains focused on thoughtful decision-making, transparent
communication, and strategic community involvement. We look forward to continuing this momentum
and collaboration as we shape the future of our school facilities.

Although the Renaissance project hasn’t officially begun, we’re already considering how to leverage
resources effectively once it does. Across all projects, prudent budget management, robust

stakeholder engagement, and strategic planning remain our top priorities.

PROJ ECT SUMMARIES

Armin Jahr ES & View Ridge EAA

TASK STATUS
Pre-Design Due Diligence Wrapping Up
Validation Phase Completed
Education Specifications Finalizing
Schematic Design Phase Beginning Now

Progress Summary

We are presenting the final Validation Package to the School Board at this meeting. This package
confirms the project’s target budget, scope, and schedule, as well as the Design-Build team’s
validation that we have a solid foundation to proceed.

While we initially planned to seek immediate approval of the Ed Specs, we may defer this approval to
ensure they fully align with the validated program. Taking additional time will help secure the
necessary buy-in and guarantee that the Ed Specs reflect the scope established during the Validation
phase and the needs of the District.

With Validation complete, we’re moving into Schematic Design. Over the next few months, the team
will start shaping the overall look, feel, and layout of the schools based on feedback gathered so far.
This includes integrating information from due diligence studies (soil conditions, site constraints,
wetland delineation, and permitting considerations) to mitigate potential risks early.

We continue to track uncertainties such as soil conditions, potential right-of-way work, wetland
constraints at Armin Jahr, and the permitting process. We recently met with the Mayor, who
committed to prioritizing these school projects, which should help streamline the permitting timeline.

The Advisory Committee’s last two meetings offered valuable input on site arrangements, key space
adjacencies, and considerations for broader community involvement. After an intense initial period,
the committee will now meet monthly, helping us incorporate their insights into outreach efforts. In
November, the design team engaged the committee in scenario exercises, receiving feedback that
will shape initial design concepts.

In recent weeks, we’ve engaged staff at both Armin Jahr and View Ridge, as well as families at Armin
Jahr’s Family Movie Night. Next week, we’ll meet with View Ridge families, continuing to gather
insights on priorities and concerns, especially regarding interim housing, playground features, and
representation in decision-making.

In January, we'll expand community engagement with an open house event and enhanced online
resources, including project-specific web pages, open comment boxes, and monthly engagement
opportunities.

HVAC Upgrades

TASK STATUS
Initial System Evaluation and Project Scoping In Progress
Expedited Design (Schematic Design and Design Development) Starting Soon

Progress Summary

Our HVAC team has nearly completed assessments at each school, evaluating how best to add
cooling and improve system efficiency. We've discovered that four out of five schools may require
electrical upgrades, likely involving Puget Sound Energy transformer replacements.

Due to these electrical constraints, a phased implementation over the next couple of years appears
necessary. We’re also considering the implications of building age, future system demands, and the
requirements of the Clean Buildings Act. To support these decisions, we’ll conduct a full energy audit
and investigate incentive programs.

While we remain mindful of costs, our approach is not purely cost-driven. We aim to balance financial
considerations with long-term sustainability and effectiveness, ensuring we deliver meaningful
improvements that align with future district needs.

Consolidated Facilities

TASK STATUS
PRC Application Process In-Progress
PDB Team Selection Process Not Started

Progress Summary

The Project Review Committee unanimously approved our application for a Design-Build delivery
model. Their support underscores our approach to complex, budget-sensitive projects.

We've launched the selection process for the Design-Build team, expecting initial proposals by
January 8. The final selection is targeted for the second Board meeting in February. Like the
elementary replacements, we'll begin with a Validation phase before moving into design.

Unlike the school projects, the Consolidated Facilities replacement isn’t tied to the academic
calendar, allowing more flexibility. This adaptability supports maximizing our budget’s impact,
delivering the best possible outcome for the district’s operational facilities.
