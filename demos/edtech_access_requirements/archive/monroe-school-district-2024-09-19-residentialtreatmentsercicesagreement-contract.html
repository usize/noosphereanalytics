The PAYER agrees to the rate of $829.30 per diem for room and board, education, supervision
and social services rendered to _E.E. during their treatment at Copper Hills Youth Center, The
anticipated duration of placement will be 6 -9 months, or the length of stay that is deemed
medically necessary by the physician and the child’s treatment team. Copper Hills Youth Center
is exempt from seeking funding from any other insurance provider that may cover the child
(including Medicaid).

Invoicing will be submitted to Adina Givens (accountspayable@monroe,wednet.edu ) on a
monthly basis. Payment shall be rendered within 45 days of receipt of the invoice with checks
made payable to Copper Hills Youth Center. Transportation services for the patient’s admission,
discharge and any home or family visits will be arranged and paid by the PAYER as this expense
is not covered by the daily rate. In addition, if the PAYER requests a therapeutic home pass
during treatment that requires the patient to be gone overnight, the PAYER agrees to pay the full
$829.30 per diem to maintain the bed for the youth’s continued treatment once returned from the
home pass. If outside medical services are required, arrangements will need to be made with the
guardian for payment.

Insurance.

CHYC shall provide PAYER the necessary material to keep on file with documentation which
establishes that CHYC has, in effect, current insurance policies with respect to the following: A.)
General and Professional Liability: CHYC shall provide general liability insurance for all its
employees at a rate of $3,000,000 per occurrence. B.) Professional liability set at $5,000,000 per
occurrence, C.) Worker’s Compensation set at $2,000,000 per occurrence.

Background Checks.

CHYC performs criminal background checks prior to commencement of services by any new
Personnel. CHYC pays any costs associated with the record check. All employees are also
entered in a system called DACS, maintained by the State of Utah, Dept of Health and Human
Services. The DACS system conducts continuous monitoring for new criminal activity. If there
is anew charge, then DACS take the appropriate action and notifies the facility.

CHYC shall keep and maintain record check files and make said information available to the
PAYER upon request.

CHYC shall prohibit any Personnel from working in contact with student if the Personnel has
pled guilty to or been convicted of a felony crime involving the physical neglect of a child under
chapter 94.42 RCW, the physical injury or death ofa child under chapter 9A.32 or 94.36 RCW
(except motor vehicle violations under chapter 46.61 RCW), sexual exploitation of a child under
chapter 9.68A RCW, sexual offenses under chapter 9A.44 RCW where a minor is the victim,
promoting prostitution of a minor under chapter 9A.88 RCW, the sale or purchase of a minor
child under RCW 9A.64.030, or violation of similar laws of another jurisdiction.

Failure to comply with this section shall be grounds for the PAYER to immediately terminate
this Contract. Termination based on this section shall supersede any other termination clause
within this Agreement.

Termination.

In the event that either party violates any of the terms of this Agreement, the other party may
serve written notice of its intention to terminate the Agreement. Unless within ten (10) days after
the serving of such notice upon the party such violation shall cease and satisfactory arrangement
for correction be made, the Agreement shall, upon expiration of said ten (10) days, cease and
terminate by providing written notice to the other party.

Fither party may terminate the Agreement with or without cause before the end of its term by
giving thirty (30) days written notice to the designated contact person. If the Agreement is
terminated upon thirty (30) days’ notice, the parties shall be liable only for the performance
rendered prior to the termination date.
