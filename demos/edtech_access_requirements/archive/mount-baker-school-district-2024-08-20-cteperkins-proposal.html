provide students with the Programs of Study. We developed short videos about all classes we teach to show during registration week. We show our class videos

taking to junior high lunches and possibly high school lunches this winter and early spring before registration. We are also adding certification opportunities in all
classes through YouScience.

The term “Program of Study” means a coordinated, non-duplicative sequence of academic and technical content at the secondary and postsecondary level that:

* Incorporates challenging state academic standards including those adopted by a state uncler section 1111(b)(1) of the Elementary and Secondary
Education Act of 1965,

® Progresses in specificity (beginning with all aspects of an industry or career cluster and leading to a more occupation-specific instruction;

® Has multiple entry and exit points that incorporate credentialing; and

* Culminates in the attainment of a recognized postsecondary credential.

281: Acacemic Proficiency In Reading/Language Arts

Describe in detail the specific action(s) the district/STEC will take In the 2024—25 SY to irnprove performance for Indicator 281 (considering an analysis of the
disaggregated data found in the Tableau Perkins Concentrator Dashboard). If the indicator was met by at least 90% of the state larget, type N/A.

In order to improve performance for this indicator, Mount Bakers taking several steps. First of all, because the high school is in such close proximity with the
junior high school and can easily collaborate, the English department is implementing a full year of English in 8th grade instead of receiving English instruction for
2 out of 3 trimesters. The goal of this intervention is to improve reading and writing early on and increase SBA scores in 8th grade before students enter high
school. In addition, the department made the choice to detrack 10th grade English because we know that research shows that heterogeneous classrooms result
in higher achievement of all students across sub groups. Since students take the SBA in 10th grade, we suspect this will help all students make progress towards
proficiency. Finally, through the school's work with the Center for High School Success, we have added a support class for at-risk 9th and 10th graders who
would not traditionally receive extra support within the school day from something like an IEP. The goal of this class is to help these students make progress
toward standard in their classes and ultimately on the SBA.
