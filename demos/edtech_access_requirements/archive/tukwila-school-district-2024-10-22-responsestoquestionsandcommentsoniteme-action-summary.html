Regular Board Meeting
October 22, 2024

Responses to the Board’s questions/comments on Agenda Item 9.E — Technology Plan for the October 22, 2024 Regular
Board Meeting:

1. Agenda Item 9.E — Technology Plan

1. Does the district have enough devices so that every student can have their own while in a classroom?
Aaron Draganov: Yes. In classrooms, carts have a number of Chromebooks that will cover the maximum
number of students at any period.

2. Whatare the different devices that students use throughout the district?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: Kindergarten has about 220 students General Education, Dual Lanquage, and
LRC 2 services. We have 246 iPad Mini 6's. We are 1:1 iPads in general education and dual language. LRC 2
spaces are using the needs of the student to guide the need for a device at a 1:1 ratio. 1st - 12th have
Chromebooks that will accommodate the max number of students at any period. Kindergartens have 2-1
iPads in classrooms. There is a floating cart with iPads so the classroom can have 1-1 iPads when needed.
Libraries, STEM labs have Dell All-in-One desktop computers and ten iPads. Foster CTE has i9 desktops and i7
laptops, both with separate video cards for video processing. There are also 15 iPads in the media lab.

3. On Page 9, what schools have an after-school elementary Robotics program?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: We regret to inform you that none of the elementary schools currently have
a program. However, we need to promote this opportunity for students. The district and association have
established a cost structure for the program; now we need adult participation.

4. On Page 9, where is the Media Lab?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: | apologize for the lack of clarity. This refers to the media lab at Showalter
Middle School.

5. On Page 9, what are the plans for a Middle School Robotics program?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: One middle school educator has expressed interest, but they are currently
committed to coaching sports. We are hopeful that more educators will get involved. The district and
association have agreed on a compensation structure to support the robotics program—now the focus is on
finding additional adult participation.

6. What are the plans to secure eRate funding?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: Our C2 budget will renew at funding year 2026-2027. That means we can
start applying around this time next year. Currently, we plan to use C2 funding to update our internal fiber
connection to Single-Mode fiber. We have been using C1 funding to support our internet connections and
connections between elementary schools and Foster (out network hub). We are planning to apply for Self-
Provisioned Fiber (dark fiber) in the near future when the timing is right.

7. On Page 12, how is the district measuring the impact of this program?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: The current measurement is anecdotal, based on student feedback, which
has been overwhelmingly positive. We track which students have devices and how long they've had them,
with the first devices issued in February 2024. We've met with city staff and have been invited to attend a
testimonial event at OSPI in 2025. Although we've requested clarification on the necessary data, we have not
yet received specifics. We may review achievement data later this year, though it is not required by the
grant. The main focus is ensuring that students have access to devices and receive support if any issues arise.

8. On Page 12, does the district ensure any costs for this program?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: There are no direct financial costs to the district for the Chromebook
partnership program between the city and the school district. However, librarian and technology staff time is

On Page 12, how many Chromebooks have been lost?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: Eleven devices have been reported lost or stolen.

Can students take home any devices other than the ones in the City and District Chromebook partnership
program?

Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: Currently, only city-issued devices can be taken home. Some students keep
them at home, while others bring them to and from school.

On Page 13, is this technology spending covered entirely by the Tech and Capital Projects Levy?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: Yes, these spending projections are intended to be fully funded by the levy
categories.

On Page 7, how will the district determine how to use technology to close existing achievement and
opportunity gaps in STEM fields?

Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: We believe it’s crucial to expose students to the careers of the future,
including those that may not even exist yet. This exposure can ignite their passion for STEM and encourage
them to explore innovative pathways. We are excited about the potential these future careers hold and are
committed to preparing our students for the evolving job landscape. To determine how to effectively use
technology in this endeavor, the district will assess current achievement and opportunity gaps, gather
feedback from educators, and analyze data to identify areas where targeted technology integration can
make a significant impact. This approach will ensure that our efforts are both strategic and responsive to the
needs of our students. We are looking forward to supporting an environment where all students have access
to the resources and support they need to thrive in STEM fields.

On Page 9, how will the district “adjust” Creative Learning Labs for equity across all elementary schools?
Aaron Draganov/Hsian-Yu Kuo: In the tech department, we maintain an anti-racist lens to ensure that our
services are equitable and based on the specific needs of our students. We believe that student success and
the overall success of the program will depend significantly on the training provided to the teachers
facilitating this work. This summer, educators received training from our tech staff, and we have a plan to
continue this training to support their growth through a PLC model. Also, our tech team meets weekly to
discuss problems of practice through an anti-racist lens, including needs in the CLLs. This collaborative
approach helps us ensure that our decisions and responses are equitable.
