TUKWILA SCHOOL DISTRICT TECHNOLOGY PLAN

Mission

Tukwila School District's mission is to create a learning
community that supports and empowers students, staff, and
families to reach their greatest potential. This technology plan
aligns with that mission by integrating technology to enhance
educational opportunities, improve teaching practices, embed
anti-racist practices, and foster equity and accessibility.

Technology Plan Input

Input on this plan was gathered from written department
records, discussions with members of the technology
committee, students and staff from Tukwila School District,
parents and guardians, technology department personnel, and
community partners. The 2024-25 Technology Committee will
commence meetings for the year in November 2024.

CITY AND DISTRICT CHROMEBOOK PARTNERSHIP UPDATE

Currently, 157 of the partnership Chromebook devices
have been deployed to students who live in city hotspot
zones. Students qualify for these devices if they receive
MLL services or are in McKinney-Vento status. The
current deployment has benefited 8th to 12th graders at
Showalter and Foster. We are planning to expand the
deployment to include 5th to 7th graders in the next few
months. The district currently has 5/7 devices on hand,
which are being used as replacements in case a
Chromebook is lost or needs repair. The warranty on
these devices is excellent, so we encourage students to
return Chromebooks if they are experiencing issues. The
second phase of the grant will provide about 150
additional Chromebooks, which we intend to deploy to
students in the younger grades.

Specifics

To enhance support for Special Education, Tukwila School District will equip all Special Education classrooms with adaptive technology, including enhanced audio systems,
interactive panels, and specialized software tailored to meet the diverse needs of students. By June 2025, the district aims for every Special Education classroom to be fully
equipped with these tools, with staff satisfaction measured by an annual survey, targeting a 90% satisfaction rate. This goal is achievable through collaboration with technology
vendors and by utilizing Tech Levy funds to ensure resources are available on schedule. This initiative aligns with the district’s mission to support inclusivity by making technology
accessible for all learning needs. Progress reviews will occur semi-annually, starting in January 2025, to ensure that the timeline is met. The district will also provide training to
ensure staff can effectively use these tools, promoting equitable access for all students

The district will undertake significant upgrades to its LAN/WAN and server infrastructure, aiming to enhance network reliability and performance district-wide. By June 2026,
these upgrades should result in a 50% improvement in network speed and a 75% reduction in downtime, based on network performance evaluations. The upgrade process will
be phased, starting with school sites most in need according to current performance data. This aligns with the mission to create a reliable and technology-dependent instructional
environment. The timeline will commence in September 2024, with a completion goal of June 2026. An inclusive approach will ensure that all schools, especially those in
underserved areas, receive upgrades on schedule. Furthermore, schools with historically poorer network performance will be prioritized, ensuring equity across the district.

Tukwila School District will implement an internet safety education curriculum for all K-|2 students, incorporating age-appropriate lessons and resources. By June 2026, the
district aims for 90% of students to complete the internet safety training, with pre- and post-assessments indicating a 70% improvement in understanding. This is achievable with
the support of the Technology Advisory Committee, which will assist in creating and delivering the curriculum. This goal aligns with the district's mission to empower students
with skills to safely navigate the digital world. The curriculum will be rolled out by February 2025, with initial training expected to be completed by October 2025. Lessons will be
designed to be culturally responsive and accessible to students with varying levels of digital literacy. Additionally, materials will be differentiated to address the needs of diverse
learners, including students with disabilities, to ensure equity in access.

The district will expand the BootUP Coding Curriculum for K-5 students and develop a comprehensive Computer Science Education Program for grades 6-8. By June 2027, all K-
8 students will participate in at least one semester of computer science, with a target of 75% proficiency as indicated by assessments. This goal will be supported by collaborating
with the Puget Sound Educational Service District for resources and by securing funds for curriculum development. This aligns with the district's mission by promoting digital
literacy and fostering critical thinking across all grade levels. The expanded program will begin in January 2025, with full implementation expected by June 2027. To ensure
inclusivity, the curriculum materials will be accessible to all students, including those needing accommodations. Additionally, the program will be introduced first in schools with
limited access to computer science resources, to promote equitable learning opportunities across the district.
