Title I, Part A, Targeted Assistance Program Requirement: Answer the following questions to satisfy the requirement of Component Two -
Identification of Students

a. Please describe how you select students for the program based on multiple criteria, e.g., Student Data, Teacher Referral, Previous
Placement, etc.
Students are selected for support through Title 1, Part A, through the use of a number of criteria. First and foremost, students are
assessed through DIBELS, Imagine Learning/Galileo, Math Forefront, Quick Phonics Screener, and WIDA at the beginning of each
year. These assessments are repeated throughout the year as well. Students who are below grade-level in these areas are at risk of
not meeting standard at the end of the year, and thus are selected for support through Title 1, Part A. These students, along with all
non-qualifying students are progress monitored on a weekly basis throughout the year. Reading and math interventionists also join
weekly PLC’s with classroom teachers in order to monitor and discuss progress of all students. When skill-gaps are identified, these
students are supported through the use of Title 1 funds.

Describe the process used to create the rank-order list of students identified to receive services, e.g., grade level, age, failing or at
risk of failing, not meeting standards, etc.

As stated above, students are frequently monitored throughout the year using a variety of assessments. Any students demonstrating
that they are below grade-level standard are identified as needing Title 1 support. Staff also meet twice per year to analyze School
Improvement goals and data, and further analyze the progress of each student. Each student is tracked based on AIM lines of where
they should be on grade-level standards and grouped according to area of need. Targeted interventions in math, reading, and
language are implemented and adjusted based on specific student needs and skill-gaps.

How will you use student data to monitor progress, at what intervals will you monitor progress, and how will student data be used
to determine if a student is ready to exit services?

Students are monitored through weekly progress monitoring, as well as CFA’s completed around 6-week data cycles. Students are
grouped by skill deficits and are then monitored on progress toward each specific skill during the 6-week data cycle. Students are then
re-grouped at the end of 6 weeks based on new areas of need.

Educators

What professional learning and support have you identified that the school’s staff (e.g., administrators, educators, counselors,
paraprofessionals, support staff, etc.) need to strengthen the implementation of evidence-based practices for both teaching and learning, as
well as intervention supports (e.g., positive behavior interventions)?

As noted above, we have identified that the PLC process is stronger in some areas than others, and there is a need to improve school-wide

areas. While we have school-wide data meetings to address academic gaps, and we have PBIS tier 1 and 2 systems to address behaviors, we

What professional learning and support have you already implemented that is proving to be powerful and effective? What are your metrics
for identifying them as successful?

We have implemented support in math with number talks, ELA with dyslexia supports, PBIS tier 2 and SEL supports, and how to work with
special-needs students on the autism spectrum. These supports have helped with growth in a number of areas.

From a cultural aspect, we hold many cultural celebrations, have a counselor on site that not only supports our students, but is also

How did your school identify these areas of strengths and improvement?

We analyzed school-wide benchmark assessment data, discipline data, attendance data, and SEL data to determine areas of strength and
areas of improvement

How well do school and community systems interact to assure continuity of supports for students? Provide at least one example.
We do a lot of work in connecting students who struggle with social-emotional needs to outside agencies. For example, we have a Yakama

Nation Behavioral Health counselor that comes to our building every Thursday to work with students on their case load. When we are
working with a family who is struggling, we offer this support and help them get connected so that they can begin receiving services.

What areas have you identified as areas of strength, and where do you hope to strengthen and build further student, family and community

banquets. We hope to improve in attendance at PTO meetings where we present information to help gather community feedback.

Title I, Part A, Targeted Assistance Program Requirement: Answer the following questions to satisfy the requirement of Component 4 -
Coordination and Transitions
a. How does your targeted assistance program coordinate with core and additional programs in the school?

Our program works closely with other programs such as LAP and Bilingual. We have a number of staff members who support Title 1
students in ensuring that learning gaps are closed, while also working to support the language needs of those students in the
bilingual program. The same is true for our Migrant students, who may receive migrant support in mathematics, while being
supported in reading deficiencies through Title 1 funded support staff. All of these programs work to support the core program
within the building. Time is scheduled daily for core ELA and Math, while time is also set aside for strategic intervention work
outside of core time. This work is completed by certificated staff, as well as classified.

How have you aligned your targeted supports to ensure students falling in WSIF identified student groups are receiving required
services to ensure growth and proficiency?

All students, including those ML learners, migrant students, and SPED students receive support that they need through Title 1, if needs are
indicated through the various assessments and screeners utilized at Simcoe Elementary. ML students struggling with language acquisition, as
determined through WIDA, receive additional support through Bilingual paraeducators, under the instruction of EL endorsed teachers. All

math are screened and supported through reading and math intervention programs in order to fill learning gaps and support student academic

How do you support transitions between grade spans?
Cross-grade-level communications occur during analyzation of SIP data, multiple times throughout the year, along with

cross-grade-level PLC’s in spring in order to communicate grade-level and specific student needs. Reading and math
interventionists also engage in PLC work with grade-levels during Spring to share spring assessment data that is used for the
following year's planning. These allow for grade-level teams to have initial baseline data to begin targeted interventions to begin the

school-year.

Are the students in your targeted assistance program able to participate in electives/enrichment time that pique their interest?

All students at Simcoe Elementary, including those in the targeted assistance program, are able to participate in electives and
enrichment throughout the year. Every student at Simcoe Elementary receives weekly P.E., music, and STEL (Science Technology
Engineering & Library.) Additional enrichment opportunities are given to each student who needs it within the classroom. This
planning is part of our PLC process, with question 4 of DuFour's guiding questions being “What do we do once students have learned
the targeted skill?” When a student has reached their goal, classroom teachers find innovative ways to extend student thinking and

learning.
7. Title |, Part A, Targeted Assistance Program Requirement: Answer the following questions to satisfy the requirement of Component 5 - Parent

and Family Engagement
a. How does your parent and family engagement strategy align to your targeted assistance practices and strategies?

Parent and family engagement plays a large part in our targeted assistance practices and strategies at Simcoe Elementary. In order to engage
families and provide them support in how to best support their students, we hold multiple family events throughout the year. These include
family math night, ELA night, and SEL night. Here, students and families engage in various games and activities, but also leave with
resources and strategies to help teach and support their students at home in the given content area. These planned games and activities all
directly align with grade-level and skill-level specific content which students are engaging in while at school.

How will you evaluate your parent and family engagement strategies? How will you know if they are working?

We evaluate these through analyzing the amount of participation, as well as through parent feedback on surveys such as the CEE. Families
answer a variety of questions about the school and how the school is doing in terms of supporting them and their students. We have seen an
increase in satisfaction in these areas, and continue to review results through our Learning Improvement Team, as well as through whole-staff

meetings throughout the year to analyze SIP progress and data.

Will achieving this goal build success and/or shrink disparity gaps for specific student groups in our learning community?

If the outcome specified in this goal isn’t specifically promoting equity and inclusion, is the process of achieving this goal going to improve
equity and inclusion for all students? How?
