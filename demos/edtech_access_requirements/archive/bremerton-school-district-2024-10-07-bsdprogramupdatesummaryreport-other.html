PROGRAM OVERVIEW

We are pleased to report that the selection process for the Design-Build team for the replacement of
Armin Jahr Elementary School and View Ridge Elementary Arts Academy has been completed. After
careful consideration of multiple highly qualified teams, we have identified Korsmo Construction and
TCF Architecture as the preferred and recommended team. The competition was very close, and we
are confident that the selected team will be an excellent partner in delivering these key projects.

Early site evaluations are currently underway at both project locations. Geotechnical engineers have
been conducting soil tests, and land surveys have been completed to ensure we have a thorough
understanding of site conditions as we move forward. These preliminary assessments are essential
for guiding the next steps in design and construction.

Additionally, we are preparing for the October 7th kickoff of our Replacement Elementary Advisory
Committee. This committee, consisting of community representatives, including teachers and parents,
will provide valuable input and help ensure ongoing community involvement in the project.

On the district-wide HVAC upgrades, we have completed the selection of the design team and
commissioning agent, and have started the contractor procurement process engaging a contractor
early in the process to support successful delivery. This project presents significant challenges due to
the complexity of retrofitting older school buildings, but we are assembling a highly capable team to
manage it.

Lastly, we will soon begin the process of assembling a team to support the replacement of the
Consolidated Facilities building, with more details to follow in the coming months.

In terms of site activities, several important steps are underway:

e Land Surveys: The survey for View Ridge is complete, and the survey for Armin Jahr should
be finished by the end of this week. These surveys provide critical data for the design team to
ensure we have a full understanding of each site.

e Geotechnical Testing: Geotechnical boring, which helps us assess soil conditions, has been
completed at View Ridge and is underway at Armin Jahr. This information will guide foundation
design and ensure site stability for the new buildings.

e Environmental and Biological Reviews: A wetland on one of the sites was identified earlier
this year, and our biologist has now completed the delineation, confirming its location.
Fortunately, the feasibility assessment ruled out any wetlands on the second site. These
findings will be included in the forthcoming survey plans. Additionally, our arborist has
completed their identification of trees on the site, ensuring that we are mindful of the natural
landscape during the design process.

e Traffic Studies: Our team has begun discussions with local agencies to scope the
requirements for traffic studies. These studies are necessary to ensure that the new schools’
designs accommodate traffic flow and safety needs for the surrounding community.

Looking ahead, we’ll be focusing on our permitting strategy, particularly in coordination with the City
of Bremerton, and we will be scheduling a meeting to review this process in detail. It’s crucial that we
align our timeline with both permitting needs and long lead items such as mechanical, electrical, and
elevator components, as these can impact our overall schedule.

Finally, the Replacement Elementary Advisory Committee will kick off on October 7th. This group of
community members, including teachers and parents, will play an important role in ensuring
transparency and providing feedback throughout the project’s progress. We'll also be inviting our
Design-Build team into this process.

We're excited to be moving into this next phase and look forward to keeping the Board updated as we

move ahead.

HVAC Upgrades

TASK STATUS
Design Team Selection Complete
PRC Application Process Complete
GCCM Selection Process In-Progress

Progress Summary

We are pleased to share that we are finalizing contract negotiations with our preferred design team
for the district-wide HVAC upgrades project. The selected team is Design West Engineering,
partnered with Rice Fergus Miller. Importantly, the majority of the design team members are local,
which aligns well with our goals of supporting the local community and bringing a deep understanding
of regional needs to the project.

We've also received approval from the Project Review Committee (PRC) for our application to use
the General Contractor/Construction Manager (GC/CM) process. This is a significant step forward, as
the GC/CM process allows us to engage a contractor early in the project to help manage budget and
schedule risks. We issued the Request for Qualifications (RFQ) for the GC/CM on Friday, September
27, and we’ve already seen positive responses from the mechanical contractor community. We
expect some strong teams to submit proposals for this important project.

There are certainly challenges ahead, particularly with the budget and schedule. However, by working
closely with our design team and the selected GC/CM, we are confident that we can navigate these
challenges effectively. Additionally, we have selected a commissioning agent to support the entire
bond program. Having this expertise onboard from the beginning will be critical in ensuring the HVAC
upgrades are delivered smoothly and perform as expected.
