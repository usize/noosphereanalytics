Policy Review Talking Points
Presented by: Richard Lasso, Assistant Superintendent

First Reading: February 3, 2025 — Kessler Center and Zoom
Second Reading: February 18, 2025 — Kessler Center and Zoom

1, POLICY 3210 — NONDISCRIMINATION
The following revisions were made to this policy:

e Removed marital status from the list of protected class designations to align with state law.

e Added language regarding the district’s obligation to adopt the OSPI Model Student
Handbook Language as required by RCW.28A.300.286

e Added language regarding the district’s obligation to adopt a complaint procedure in
accordance with chapter 392-190 WAC.

e Reorganized structure and updated formatting to make it more user and reader friendly.

2. POLICY 5010 —- NONDISCRIMINATION AND AFFIRMATIVE ACTION
The following revisions were made to this policy:

e Updated the definition and examples of discriminatory harassment.

e Updated language in Affirmative Action section to align with WAC 392-190-0592.

e Added language regarding the district’s obligation to adopt a complaint procedure in
accordance with chapter 392-190 WAC.

e Added service animals as a permissible reasonable accommodation for a qualified applicant
or employee.

INFORMATION ONLY — No Action Required
Policy Technical Changes and Regulation/Form Updates

1, REGULATION 2400R — HIGH SCHOOL GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS
In District-Created Assessments section, changed grade requirement for awarding credit for a
course when the student successfully completes the next higher-level course in a sequence to C-
(minus) or higher; in Health and Fitness section, removed language referring to the class of 2016
and increased the number semesters of Fitness that may be waived from two (2) to three (3), or 1.5
credits.

2. FORM 2400F2 — PHYSICAL FITNESS ACTIVITY WAIVER REQUEST
Added “Directed Athletics” and 1.5 credits as options for requesting a PE waiver.

3. REGULATION 3210R - NONDISCRIMINATION
Reorganized content to facilitate better understanding of the regulation and complaint resolution
process, including a clearer distinction between the formal and informal complaint processes, as
well as appeal options.

4. REGULATION 5010R — NONDISCRIMINATION AND AFFIRMATIVE ACTION
Removed language specifying affirmative action plans, staff goals, and internal monitoring, as this
information is included in the district affirmative action plan; updated complaint procedures,
including formal and informal complaints, the appeal process, mediation, and recordkeeping.
