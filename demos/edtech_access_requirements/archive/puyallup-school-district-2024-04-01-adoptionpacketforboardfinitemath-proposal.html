Introduction

In the spring of 2024, Finite Math was proposed as a new course in Puyallup
School District. This course is part of an academically rigorous third year math
credit sequence designed as an alternative to the completion of the traditional
Algebra || course. Students will learn to understand and apply mathematical
models and methods to solve real-world problems, enhancing their analytical and
critical thinking skills. Core topics emphasize practical applications, problem-
solving strategies, and the use of technology and software tools for computation
and visualization. By the end of the course, students will have a strong foundation
in finite mathematics, preparing them for college-level coursework and equipping
them with skills for practical problem-solving in future careers. This course
highlights the relevance of mathematics in real-life situations, fostering
appreciation and motivation for further study.

History

e To increase course options for students in pursuit of graduation and
personal pathway requirements, a new course, Finite Math, was proposed
to and approved by the Board in the spring of 2024. A small team of
teachers and Teaching and Learning staff began the process of designing the
new course and reviewing materials in the fall of 2024. This new semester-
long course will be offered in the Puyallup School District in the 2025-2026
school year to expand options for our students in continuing their
engagement with mathematics. Finite Mathematics and Its Applications,
featuring an e-text and digital platform, will support students in the Finite
Math course.


Rational for Recommendation

A Rigorous and Flexible Mathematics Textbook

This textbook provides high-level academic content that extends students’
mathematical learning trajectory. Designed for flexibility, it supports both
semester-long and year-long courses, ensuring adaptability to different
instructional models. The content fosters a deep exploration of
mathematical concepts while remaining accessible to a variety of learners.
Modular Structure for Accessibility

Organized into discrete, modular units, the textbook allows educators to
use content in a flexible, customizable manner. This structure supports
differentiated instruction, varied pacing, and scaffolded learning, ensuring
that students can engage with the material at an appropriate entry point
while offering opportunities for extension and enrichment.

Real-World Relevance and Cultural Responsiveness

Grounded in real-life data sets and engaging scenarios, the textbook
connects mathematical concepts to meaningful, real-world applications. It
incorporates diverse cultural perspectives and identities, making
mathematics more relevant and inclusive for all students. By integrating
multiple viewpoints, the textbook enhances engagement and helps
students see themselves reflected in the content.

Comprehensive Digital and Assessment Support

Supported by a robust digital platform, the textbook provides interactive
tools, instructional resources, and a variety of assessment options. These
assessments offer meaningful feedback, support student growth, and
accommodate different learning styles, ensuring a well-rounded approach
to mathematical understanding.

Fostering Mathematical Mindsets and Identities

Beyond delivering strong mathematical content, the digital platform
emphasizes the development of students’ mathematical identities and
mindsets. Through the MyLab tool, students can engage in metacognitive
learning activities around mindset and study skills. Perseverance,
confidence, and problem-solving skills are reinforced, as is the importance
of both conceptual understanding and procedural fluency. By aligning with


the standards for mathematical practice, the textbook supports a
comprehensive approach to learning mathematics.

Bias Screening

Our workgroup completed bias screening in February of 2025. During this review,
there were many criteria for which no evidence was found. This text was originally
written for the post-secondary market, and has been adapted for a high school
audience. As such, there are no pieces of stock art or “filler” illustrations. Any
diagrams or models in the text are directly related to content and used to
explicate problems. Names and situations used in contextualized problems do
represent a variety of languages of origin and are representative of the globally
diverse application of the mathematics being studied. A broad variety of
occupations, life experiences, and socio-economic indicators are used as examples
throughout the text. Some of the data sets used do reflect disparity or
disproportionality between demographic groups, but the data is cited in an
academically appropriate way.
