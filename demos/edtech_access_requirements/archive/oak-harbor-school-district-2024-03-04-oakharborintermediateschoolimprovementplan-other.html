Oak Harbor Public Schools

Learning for Life

Oak Harbor Intermediate School

Public Schools School Improvement Plan 2023-2024
—— COVID-19 Pandemic Teaching & Learning

OHI Vision: Educate each student to value learning and to gain personal and academic
growth.

Mission: To create an inclusive learning community that develops students’
social-emotional and academic growth.

Core Values: Respectful, Responsible, & Ready

Pandemic Learning Conditions:

e 3rd Year Full In-person Learning: We reduced our staff by six teachers and class sizes

are closer to contractual class caps, around 28 or 29 students per class. With
increased stability in staffing, we have increased success with our PBIS initiatives that
contributed to a smooth transition from summer into the new school year.

Modifications to Schedule: OHI modified its master schedule by moving the SEL time
from the PM to the AM based on staff/student feedback and MTSS Consultant advice
for students to start their day with SEL check-in activities. We also use this time to
provide school wide AVID instruction that includes agendas with learning logs and
binder checks with assignment trackers. We also reduced our lunches from four to
three per day to reduce impact to our PE instructional space and to increase lunch
seat time from 20 minutes to 30 minutes. Lastly, based on the new schedule, we now
push our paras into the classrooms with increased minutes as teachers provide recess
supervision.

SECTION 1: ANNUAL GOALS

Goal #1 Social Emotional Learning Goal:
e Panorama Student Survey: For the SY 23-24, using the Panorama Survey (Student

Perception Survey) with a focus on Supportive Relationships category question, “Do
you have a teacher or other adult from school who you can count on to help you, no
matter what?” will increase from 75% to 85% by the end of the school year.

Panorama Staff Survey (35 of 109 respondents): For the SY 23-24, using the
Panorama Survey (Staff Perception Survey) with a focus on the BELONGING category
question, “How connected do you feel to other adults at your school will increase from
75% to 85% in the “somewhat connected” to the “extremely connected” range by the
end of the school year.
