Needs Assessment Summary
Describe the highest priorities and resource inequities (funding, staffing, materials, resources, etc.) identified through conducting the Comprehensive Needs Assessment that will be addressed in this plan.

SVEC has become a more diverse population as we grow in size. Anecdotally, many families seem to be turning to “homeschooling” for a number of reasons, and from diverse backgrounds and experience levels in
homeschooling. This diversity of families and diversity of needs makes bringing our community together through a shared sense of belonging even more important. Ironically, some of the data that could help drive our decision
making is hard to come by. We experienced a huge drop in SBA participation and participation in district-wide surveys in recent years, but are trying to change that. In an effort to embed this work into the PLC process, PLC
groups will be self-selecting the area of need they feel they could most impact and form teams around that need.

All of this brings us to the conclusion that are biggest needs at SVEC are:

Improving participation in SBA and surveys to improve our ability to make more data driven decisions

Continue to improve SBA participation school-wide from 49% in SY 22-23, to 66% in SY 23-24, and accomplishing our original target goal this school year of 75% participation

Expanding opportunities for students and families to feel a sense of belonging in our school community.
Increase our on-time class attendance school wide and continue to achieve over 95% weekly contact. This is always an area of focus in an ALE because funding is tied to weekly contact, among other things.

Our SIP goals will focus on making improvements in the above needs, as well as our PLC groups and their goals.
