» April 25 & 26, Spokane

— Mr. Doffing and Mr. Kratzig joined me with Heidi Ypma from
Bellingham Technical College to represent our larger team. Not
attending was Heather Powell, CEO of the Boys & Girls Club of
Whatcom County. They were hosting their annual fundraising Gala on
April 25".

— The team consists of Meridian School District, Bellingham Technical
College, and the Boys & Girls Club of Whatcom County. Emphasis of
this convening was to gauge progress, share problems of practice
and increase networking to share ideas across the state to affect an
increase of FAFSA completions for seniors and to increase the post-
secondary going culture in Washington. The grant covers travel
expenses for convenings.

» April 25 & 26, Spokane

- Most problems of practice, panels, and activities involved increasing
access to colleges and the college-going culture. Ideas shared
revolved around college advising activities, campus visits, FAFSA
family events, and similar endeavors.

— In contrast, our focus is more on the establishment of developing
coursework and opportunities to increase understanding of
opportunities for a whole host of post-graduation options: military, 4 &
2-year college programs, trades by engaging in CTE courses,
developing exploratory courses, industry & trade opportunities (Stem
Like Me and career fair at MMS) and alignment with future skill center
programming. Our emphasis is to increase understanding of post-
graduate options and increase relevance for both MHS and future
course work. Our students need to know what is available to them!

WA-CCER Spring Conference

» April 27, Pasco Th WA -CCER |

- The Washington Association for Career Counseling and Employment
Readiness (WA-CCER) is a subsection of the Washington Association
for Career and Technical Education (WA-ACTE). This one-day
conference focused on collaboration with community and trade
colleges and their outreach to K-12 systems, along with CTE
coursework, apprenticeship/internship, career days and work-based
learning opportunities.

— Emphasis was also placed on the messaging provided by counselors
to students to recognize the validity of CTE K-12 and Post K-12
industry work in the trades. Many of our teachers and counselors
across the state are products of the university culture and do not
always recognize a bias they have away from CTE courses of study
and post-secondary opportunities. Again, our purpose in Meridian is to
combine student interest and aptitude to build skills and position our
students to thrive beyond graduation.

WA-CCER Spring Conference

» April 27, Pasco thi WA -CCER |

— We see several students who receive special education supports
pushed into CTE programs and skill centers as a means to provide
hands-on, viable opportunities for them to grow and build skills across
the state. Also embedded in this effort is the discouragement of said
students to engage in academic endeavors and a perception of CTE
programs being “less than.”

— This must be countered with support and higher engagement in
supporting students in areas they are interested and possess an
aptitude. Offering opportunities to explore, visit, and engage in
conversations with trusted adults who share the same values will help
our students leave us with strong options. All students must have the
opportunity to engage and not be deterred.
