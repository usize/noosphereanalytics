Vision

e The Tukwila School District believes that our students, staff, and families have the ability to
achieve at their highest potential. Our core purpose is to engage students intellectually and
socially with authentic and substantive learning experiences.

e Inpartnership with families, our students will be successful and productive members of society
because of the academic, social, and emotional skills they have learned with the support of the
Tukwila community.

e Werecognize, encourage, and capitalize on the strengths, talents, and contributions of our
students, staff, and community.

e Wecelebrate our rich diversity and meaningful partnership with the Tukwila community and our
role in making society stronger and more equitable.

Mission
Tukwila School District Mission is to create a learning community that supports and empowers
students, staff, and families to reach their greatest potential.

High Expectations & Excellence for All

Desired Outcome: Student achievement increases as a result of high-quality instruction and high
expectations by staff.

Percentage of students meeting standard on assessments in English Language Arts and
Mathematics
e SBA(Smarter Balanced Assessment)
o Allstudents will increase by 10% in each core area (ELA, Mathematics and
Science) as reported on the OSPI report card for 23-24 and each subsequent
year. (OSPI data not yet released)

Percentage of 3 and 7" grade students meeting their stretch goal in iReady reading and
math
e iReady Assessment
o The number of 3 and 7" grade students reaching their stretch goal in
reading and math will increase by 10 percentage points each year.

Percentage of 9" grade students who are on track to graduate
e On-Track to Graduation
o The percentage of 9" grade students who are on track to graduate will
increase from 73.7% to 78.7% in 2023-2024 and increase 5% each
subsequent year.

Percentage of positive scores on staff EES - High Standards and Expectations
e EES (Educational Effectiveness Survey) Staff - High Standards and Expectations
o The percentage of staff who answer positively on the EES Survey in the area
of High Standards and Expectations will increase from 66% in 2022-2023 to
71% in 2023-2024.

Engaged Learners

Desired Outcome: Student learning increases as a result of students taking an active role in their
own goal setting and progress monitoring.

Percentage of students setting personal academic growth goals
e iReady student growth goals
o 100% of students in grades K-8 will set student growth goals in reading and
math after they have take the iReady diagnostic in the fall.

Desired Outcome: Students feel safe and supported at school and have positive relationships
with peers and teachers.

Percentage of positive scores on EES
e EES (Educational Effectiveness Survey) Student - Collaboration & Interpersonal
Skills


o The percentage of 4th - 12th students responding positively on the EES
student survey in the area of Collaboration and Interpersonal Skills will
improve from 65% to more than 78%.

Discipline data
e Documented student discipline

o Thenumber of documented student discipline reports will be reduced in all
five schools by 3% in the 2023-2024 school year. The focus area is the
Inappropriate Behavior category. The number of documented student
discipline reports for Inappropriate Behavior will be reduced from 74 in
2022-2023 to 60 in 2023-2024, and at least 1.0% reduction each following
year.

Desired Outcome: Students consistently attend their classes and are actively engaged with
learning by planning for their futures.

Attendance data
e Percentage of students regularly attending school
o The percentage of students regularly attending school will increase from
63% in 2022-2023 to 73% in 2023-2024 and will increase by 5% every year
after.

High School and Beyond Plan
e Percentage of 11° grade students who have completed their High School &
Beyond Plan
o 90% percent of 11" grade students who complete their High School &
Beyond Plan during the 2023-2024 school year. This will increase to 95%
during the 2024-2025 school year and 100% during the 2025-2026 school
year.

Relationships

Desired Outcome: Students will feel connected to and supported by their school community and
accepted for who they are.

Percentage of positive scores on EES
e EES (Educational Effectiveness Survey) Student - Belonging and Identity
o The percentage of students responding positively on the EES student survey
inthe area of Belonging and Identity will improve from 72% in 2021-2022 to

Percentage of positive scores in Healthy Youth Survey
e Healthy Youth Survey responses
o The number of positive responses to question 168 (“There are people from my
school who will help me if | need it.”) will increase from 66.2% in 2022-2023 to
71.2% in 2023-2024.


o The number of positive responses to question 228 (“There are lots of chances
for students in my school to talk with a teacher one-on-one.”) will increase from
87% in 2022-2023 to 92% in 2023-2024.

o The number of positive responses to question 232 (“My teacher(s) notices
when | am doing a good job and lets me know about it.”) will increase from 72.2%
in 2022-2023 to 77.2% in 2023-2024.

o Wewill align representation of students with IEPs, multilingual learners, and
