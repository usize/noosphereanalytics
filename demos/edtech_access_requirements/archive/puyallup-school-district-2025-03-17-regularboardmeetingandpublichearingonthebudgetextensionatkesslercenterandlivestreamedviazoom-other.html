2024-2025

Center and livestreamed via Zoom

Category : 4. Consent Agenda
Type : Action (Consent)
Recommended Action : Approve attached interdistrict agreement for 2024-

2025 as presented.

Public Content

Presen
Karen Mool, Executive Director of Special Education
Issue:

Add Steilacoom Historical School District to Special Education Interdistrict Agreements for the 2024-2025
school year.

Background:

Each year districts must have interdistrict agreements in place that allow for special education services to
be provided to out-of-district students on a contractual basis. In general, these students have disabilities
that are considered to be high cost, low incidence. Many of the smaller districts in our area do not have
appropriate programs for these students in their resident district. Most of the students receiving services
in Puyallup are identified as qualifying under the funding category of Deaf/Hard of Hearing.

Di ion:


Puyallup provides services to approximately nineteen non-resident special education students on a
contractual basis. At the same time, Puyallup contracts outside the district for seven students. The district
bills for all excess costs that are incurred for providing special education services to these students.
Approval of the resolution as well as school board signatures on agreements is required on an annual
basis. The district included in Resolution #102 2024-25 is Steilacoom Historical School District.

If you have questions or comments regarding this item, please contact Karen Mool, Executive Director of Special Education at

Subject : K. Operations - Asset Preservation Program, Resolution
#103 2024-25
Meeting : Mar 17, 2025 - Regular Board Meeting and Public

Center and livestreamed via Zoom

Category : 4. Consent Agenda
Type : Action (Consent)
Recommended Action : Approve Resolution #103 2024-25 to accept the Asset

Preservation Program as presented.

Public Content

Presented by:

Dr. Vincent Pecchia, Assistant Superintendent of Operations and School Support

Issue:

The school district is committed to preserve the districts facilities in a safe, healthful, and educationally effective manner that is
compliant with applicable codes and requirements. We are asking that the board consider approval of the attached Resolution
#103 2024-25.

Background:

The State of Washington requires that the superintendent or his designee will oversee the development of an Asset Preservation
System (APS) with supporting policies and procedures to comply with all requirements of the Asset Preservation Rule (APR)
(WAC 392-347-023). This requires the APS to provide for the preservation of the district facilities by employing a system of
processes to include, but not limited to, predictive and preventative repairs, maintenance, and re-conditioning.

Di ion:

The Asset Preservation System (APS - Maintenance Plan) is a part of the overall Asset Preservation Program (APP). Further,
Board Policy #6800 was revised in June of 2017 and commits PSD to compliance with the Asset Preservation Program (APP).
As per OSPI, a board resolution is a requirement of the overall Asset Preservation Program (APP) and becomes an official
document, to OSPI, that the district has committed to the APP and APS.

n Information:

If you have questions or comments regarding this item, please contact Dr. Vincent Pecchia, Assistant Superintendent of

Operations and School Support, at pecchivj@ puyallupsd.org or 253-841-8772, or Phillip Anderson, Director of Maintenance, at
anderspj@ puyallupsd.org at 253-841-8777.

Issue:

settings of thePuyallup School District until a future capital bond program was considered and funded. The board approved an
update to the SCUT Charter on June 17, 2024, to provide ongoing annual review and updates.

Background:

SCUT held three meetings this school year on October 29, January 28, and March 10. The primary topic for each meeting were:
* October 29, 2024: Review of projected enrollment vs actual enrollment (October Headcount)

January 28, 2025: Enrollment projection update with October 2024 headcount basis

* March 10, 2025: Review and Update of SCUT recommendations

Di ion:

SCUT incorporated current enrollment projections and school capacity data to best understand schoolsthat may experience
building-related capacity challenges within the next four years. The task force has reviewed its current recommendations
approved by the Board of Directors on June 17, 2024,and is presenting updates to those recommendations based on the
informationprepared and reviewed this year by the task force. The board will be asked to consider acting on approving the School
