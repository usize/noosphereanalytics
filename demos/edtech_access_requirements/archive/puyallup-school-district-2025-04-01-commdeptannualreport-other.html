Challenges/Opportunities:

ParentSquare and StudentSquare

ParentSquare has become integral to the district's communication strategy, serving as our primary broadcasting
tool. Its multilingual capabilities, efficiency, and user-friendly interface have contributed to significant growth in
usage across the board. Comparing February 2025 to the entire 2023-2024 school year, we see growth in nearly
every metric. However, with increased engagement comes the need for a disciplined and structured approach to
maintain effective communication. High-volume messaging risks overwhelming families and may lead to
disengagement. StudentSquare, launched in the 2024-2025 school year as a student-focused extension of
ParentSquare, has gained traction through marketing materials and messaging efforts. As a result, over 900
students have activated their accounts, with 27% of high school students downloading the app. Since September
26, students have received their own version of Connectivity, our weekly family newsletter, with student-
specific content featured regularly since early January.

Website:
Challenges:

o Increasing page views and user sessions: While there has been significant growth in page views and
user sessions, continuing to improve website content branding and usability can further boost metrics.

o Engaging new users: With a notable increase in new users, ensuring the website provides clear
information, optimized for search engines, and easy to navigate is crucial for maintaining their
engagement.

o Catering to mobile users: The significant growth in mobile users highlights the importance of optimizing
the website for mobile devices to ensure an optimal user experience.

Opportunities:

o Increasing user engagement: We will continue to implement strategies such as adding more interactive
content, improving site navigation, and optimizing page load times to encourage users to explore the
website further and return more frequently.

o Ensuring Website Accessibility: Making sure our webpage is accessible will enable users with disabilities
to have equitable access and experiences. As part of our commitment to inclusivity, we are prioritizing
the conversion of PDFs into HTML, which ensures important information remains easily accessible for
everyone. Additionally, creating accessible PDFs will further support our efforts in maintaining a fully
inclusive platform.

o Leveraging mobile optimization: Enhancing the mobile experience can attract even more users who
prefer browsing on smartphones or tablets, further increasing website traffic and engagement.

Social Media
Challenges:

o Decline in Facebook followers: A decrease in new followers on Facebook suggests the need to reassess
content strategies and potentially explore new methods for attracting followers.

o Modest growth on Instagram: While Instagram shows growth across most metrics, maintaining a steady
increase in followers and reach will require consistent, engaging content and adapting to platform
trends.

Opportunities:

o Increasing website traffic: A significant increase in website users and new web users from both
Facebook and Instagram indicates that our social media platforms are successfully driving traffic to our
website. Maintaining and optimizing this trend can enhance our overall online presence.

Increased Social Media Engagement: Notable increases in followers and engagement across platforms like
Facebook, LinkedIn, and Instagram, showing a heightened public interest in district updates and capital projects.
Expanded reach on social media has allowed for more meaningful connections with the community, helping to
spread awareness and garner support.

Improved Website Traffic: A rise in website visits demonstrates that more stakeholders are seeking information
on capital projects, which reflects increased public awareness and engagement.

Positive Public Sentiment: Sentiment analysis reveals increased positive feedback regarding the district's
transparency, project execution, and financial stewardship.

Challenges:

Managing concerns about the scope and impact of capital projects, particularly in communities where change is
met with skepticism, remains an ongoing challenge.

It’s crucial to address and balance community expectations while ensuring that timelines, budgets, and project
deliverables are clearly communicated and understood by the public.

Construction and development can occasionally lead to temporary disruptions, such as permitting delays
beyond our control. To mitigate any negative impact, we must proactively manage these challenges by
maintaining transparent communication and providing regular updates to keep the community informed.

Opportunities:

There is an opportunity to strengthen and foster more collaborative relationships with contracted businesses,
builders, and other key stakeholders involved in our school projects, ensuring ongoing engagement, open
communication, and valuable feedback throughout the process.

By enhancing outreach efforts, particularly through digital platforms and community forums, we can continue to
increase public awareness, support, and engagement.

There is an opportunity to strengthen the connection between communication efforts and the teams
responsible for capital projects. By fostering a closer relationship, we can ensure that the updates and narratives
surrounding these projects are more directly aligned with the day-to-day progress, creating a more cohesive and
effective communication strategy.

As we focus on transparent communication, we can further improve the district’s reputation as a responsible
and accountable steward of public funds, which will continue to inspire trust and confidence in future

projects.

Moving Forward:

Moving forward, the department will continue to prioritize transparent and engaging communication about capital
investments through ‘Foundations For Our Future.’ We will actively seek and incorporate community input, ensuring
that our stakeholders feel heard and valued. By refining our communication strategies, leveraging data-driven decision-
making, and maintaining a focus on building trust and support, we will work to secure the resources necessary for the
district's growth and improvement.
