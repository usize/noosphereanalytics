SCHOOL DISTRICT REVENUES

1000 - Local Tax
This revenue consists of tax receipts received from the approved local maintenance and
operations levies.

2000 - Local Non-tax
This revenue is made up of student lunch receipts, sales from vocational programs, interest
earned from district accounts, building rentals and several other small sources.

3000 - State General Purpose

This revenue consists of 3100 - basic education apportionment, 3121 - special education
apportionment and 3300 - Local Effort Assistance (LEA). Basic education apportionment refers
to the funds received through a state funding formula, based on the average number of
students served. Basic education apportionment also includes funds for vocational-technical
education and running start. Local Effort Assistance (LEA) revenue is provided by the state as
a means of equalizing local levy rates for districts with a proportionally lower assessed
valuation tax base.

4000 - State Special Purpose

This revenue includes funding for the following programs: Special Education, Learning
Assistance, Multilingual Education, Highly Capable, Food Services and Transportation. The
state supports each program based upon the district’s student enrollment or participation
through a funding formula specific to each program. This category also includes funds for one-
time allocations or special grant activities.

5000 - Federal General Purpose

This revenue consists of funds related to federal forest lands within the district’s boundaries
and payments received for qualified bond interest credits on certain qualified tax credit bonds
issued by the district.

6000 - Federal Special Purpose

This revenue is provided by the federal government to support programs for special needs
students such as students with disabilities, students with limited English skills and low-income
students needing help with reading and math skills. This category also includes funds to
provide free and reduced lunch and breakfast programs for low-income students.

7000 - Revenue from Other Districts

This reflects reimbursements received for special education services rendered to students
from other school districts. Services are provided to special education students where facilities
and/or staff are not available in their resident district to provide the required services.

8000 - Revenue from Other Governmental Entities
This revenue is related to agreements and partnerships with governmental agencies like the
educational service district (ESD).

9000 - Other Financing Sources

This revenue includes proceeds from bond sales, sales of surplus equipment and proceeds
from long-term financing.

SCHOOL DISTRICT EXPENDITURES by ACTIVITY GROUPS

Central Administration

This category includes the expenses for the school board, the superintendent's office, the
business office, human resources, public relations and for supervision of the following
activities: instruction, food services, student transportation and maintenance.

Building Administration

This category includes the expenses associated with management and coordination of the
school building. Examples of such things would include the duties of the principal, assistant
principal, vice principal and their secretarial and clerical assistants.

Teaching Support

This category includes the salary and benefits of pupil security personnel, playground, bus,
and lunchroom assistants, librarians, counselors, nurses, psychologists, communication
disorders specialists, therapists and their related expenses for instructional professional
development and technology, supplies, equipment and travel as well as the costs of books
and equipment in the school library.

Teaching Activities

This category includes the salary and benefits for teachers and teacher assistants and their
related expenses for supplies, equipment and travel. It also includes extracurricular expenses
and payments to other school districts.

Other Support Activities

This category includes the operational expenses for student transportation, food services,
maintenance and grounds, custodial, utilities, insurance, data processing, warehousing and
public activities.
