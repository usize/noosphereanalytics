Policy Review Talking Points
Presented by: Richard Lasso, Assistant Superintendent

First Reading: March 17, 2025 — Kessler Center and Zoom
Second Reading: April 1, 2025 — Kessler Center and Zoom

1, POLICY 2028 — STUDENT SERVICE ANIMAL ACCOMMODATIONS

The following revisions were made to this policy:
e Updated title to reflect that it applies only to animal accommodations for students.
e Removed references to adults or employees with disabilities.
e Updated the definition of service animal, to specify any dog or miniature horse that is
trained to perform tasks for the benefit of an individual with a disability.
e Clarified what does not constitute a “task”, added that it is a civil infraction to misrepresent
an animal as a service animal, and updated the cross references section.

POLICY 2029 — ANIMALS AS PART OF THE INSTRUCTIONAL PROGRAM
The following revisions were made to this policy:

e Added that requests to include animals in the instructional program shall be submitted in
writing to the building principal and sent to the Executive Director of Elementary or
Secondary Education for final approval.

e Specified that the policy does not apply to service animals for students or employees who
are disabled and updated the cross references section.

POLICY 5028 — EMPLOYEE SERVICE ANIMAL ACCOMMODATIONS (NEW)
Because policy 2028 was revised to address service animal accommodations specifically for
students, it was necessary to create policy 5028 to address service animal accommodations for
district employees. This policy:
e Acknowledges the district’s responsibility to permit adults with disabilities to be accompanied
by a service animal as required by federal laws and state laws against discrimination.
e Provides a definition for the term “service animal.”

POLICY 5400 — PERSONNEL LEAVES
The following revisions were made to this policy (as a reminder, this policy applies to all employees,
unless a particular employee’s CBA provides otherwise):

e Added section titles to provide more clarity.

e Created a new section for “unpaid leaves”.

Policy Technical Changes and Regulation/Form Updates

REGULATION 2028R — STUDENT SERVICE ANIMAL ACCOMMODATIONS

Updated title to reflect that it applies only to animal accommodations for students; removed
references to employees with disabilities; added that a written request will be submitted to the
building administrator who will forward the request to the 504 Coordinator, and the request must
be received at least 10 school days prior to bringing the service animal to school or school function.

REGULATION 2029R — ANIMALS AS PART OF THE INSTRUCTIONAL PROGRAM

Added that a written request to include animals in the school’s instructional program must be
made to the building principal using form 2029F, and the principal will consult with the Executive
Director of Elementary or Secondary Education; added requirements for the approval of fish and
dogs; added section for handler responsibility/liability and school responsibility; added appeal
process for requests that have been denied.

FORM 2029F1 — REQUEST FOR APPROVAL OF AGENCY ANIMAL ON DISTRICT PROPERTY
New form created for the purpose of requesting in writing an animal provided by a vendor to be
used as part of the instructional program.

FORM 2029F2 — STAFF REQUEST FOR APPROVAL OF ANIMAL ON DISTRICT PROPERTY
New form created for the purpose of requesting in writing an animal provided by a district
employee to be used as part of the instructional program.

REGULATION 3124R — REMOVAL/RELEASE OF STUDENT DURING SCHOOL HOURS

Added that when a student will be transported by ambulance to a doctor or hospital, when
feasible, the principal or designee should accompany the student during transport, and notification
of student transport by EMS should be made as soon as possible to the appropriate district
administrator.

Management, Corrective Actions or Punishment”).
