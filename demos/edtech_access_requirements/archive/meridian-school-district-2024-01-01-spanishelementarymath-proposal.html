Spanish and Elementary Math


Overview of Core Curriculum Adoption Process:

Board Policy 2020 outlines criteria for the selection of instructional
materials

e Identification committees or task forces of content experts study best
practice and identify instructional materials which are proposed to the
Meridian Instructional Materials Committee (IMC).

e The IMC reviews proposals for evidence of alignment to the Meridian
School Board Criteria for Selection of Core Instructional Materials.

e The Meridian School board determines final approval of materials.


Core instructional materials shall be selected based upon the degree
to which they:

A. Demonstrate likelihood of impact as shown by scientific or evidence-based research;

B. Enable implementation of the district's developed curriculum and meet state standards and College
Readiness requirements;

C. Provide sufficient flexibility to meet the varied needs and abilities of the students served;

D. Provide clear and appropriate differentiation components for English Language Learners, special
education students, students with academic opportunity gaps, and highly capable students;

E. Where appropriate, present balanced but differing views of issues, controversial or
otherwise, in order that students may develop critical analysis and informed decision-making
skills;

F. Demonstrate consideration of appropriate format(s) (including technological, visual, and/or auditory
components);

G. Support an equitable access to learning and learning materials for all students; including
the provision of appropriate, high-quality accessible instructional materials to all students
with disabilities who require them; and

H. Are free of stereotyping and gender, race, class, and other forms of bias

Highlighted Curriculum Strengths

A focus on student discussion, real world math, and application of the standards for
mathematical practice supports student understanding.

Modules are intentionally organized to build connections between concepts.

Structure of the lessons allows for different entry points for students based on their
prior knowledge

Universal Design for Learning strategies are specifically called out and supported
within lessons

Math language routines are a core element of the instruction, these support all
students and ML students in particular.

Challenge and support activities are embedded within lessons and lessons are able to
be differentiated based on student need.

Rich problems and discussion help students to develop critical thinking skills in
mathematics.

Highlighted Curriculum Strengths

Each unit is aligned with the 5 C’s in the World Language Standards: Communication,
Cultures, Connections, Comparisons, Communities.

Research based unit structure has students learning language through immersion in
the target language.

Students practice speaking, writing and listening in authentic contexts.

Materials are authentic and include real-world topics and examples that are accessible
for students at a variety of levels.

The materials demonstrate research based practices for world language instruction.
The material’s use of proficiency rubrics is aligned with growth practice

Readings and activities can be shared digitally for students that need to use Google
Chrome accessibility tools.

Lesson materials include video, audio, visual resources.
