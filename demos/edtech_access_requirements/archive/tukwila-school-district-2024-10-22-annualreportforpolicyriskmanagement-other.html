Introduction

- Purpose:
The Board of Directors believes the district must
identify and measure risks of loss.

- Areas of Concern:
- Damage or destruction of district property

- Claims against the district (e.g., harm caused by
district actions or inaction)

- Policy Objective:
To implement a risk management program that
reduces and eliminates risk.



Key Components of Risk Management

Risk Identification & Measurement:
The district must assess potential risks in property damage and liability.

Risk Mitigation:
The goal is to reduce or eliminate risks whenever possible.

Risk Assumption:
Determining the level of risk the district can afford to handle.

Risk Transfer:
Transferring certain risks to insurance companies when they are too costly for the district to
assume directly. At the awareness stage only in Tukwila School District.

Conclusion

The Tukwila School District administration is aware
of the recent increase in property damage due to
inclement weather. We are actively collaborating
with the WSRMIP claims agent to develop an action
and preparedness plan aimed at mitigating future
losses and minimizing impacts on our buildings and
community. Additionally, we are closely monitoring
automotive loss run data. We are committed to
addressing other areas highlighted by the loss run
data and will implement strategies to reduce and
eliminate risk and exposure throughout the 2024-25
school year.
