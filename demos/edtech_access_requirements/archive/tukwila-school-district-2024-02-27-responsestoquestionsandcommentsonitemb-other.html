Board Meeting
February 27, 2024

Responses to the Board’s questions/comments on Agenda Item 9.B — 23-24 Forecasted Revenue and Expenditures for the
February 27, 2024 Board Meeting:

1. Agenda Item 9.B — 23-24 Forecasted Revenue and Expenditures

1. On Page 6, Program 88, why is After School Dinner included with ECEAP?
Amy Fleming: The forecast spreadsheet has been updated for the future to only say ECEAP.

2. How can categorical programs be overspent? Who is responsible for ensuring that charges to categorical
programs do not exceed the amount the district receives as revenue?
Amy Fleming: We do not receive adequate funding from the state for programs such as Special Education
and Transportation. Further research and cost containment measures will be reviewed and implemented for
ECEAP.

3. How much of the 23-24 deficits are the result of known expenditures that were not accounted for in the
23-24 budget?
Amy Fleming: Information will be provided in a future update.

4. How much of the 23-24 deficits are the result of known expenditures that were not accounted for in the
23-24 budget?
Amy Fleming: Information will be provided in a future update.

5. On page 5, has the $1.3 million been repaid to the Capital Projects fund or is this a current liability? Does
this include interest?
Amy Fleming: The $1.3 million is an operating transfer that does not coincide with borrowing cash. | believe
this transfer was included in the budget as a placeholder only. The updated projection shows this amount as

SO.

6. On Page 10, why is the assumption about revenue growth at 2%, but expenditure growth is 3.2%?
Amy Fleming: The state does not fully fund basic education. Historically, revenue growth has been less than
expenditure growth.

7. On Page 9, isn’t the goal for 24-25 to get to a 4% fund balance and 6% by 25-26?
Amy Fleming: The letter from OSPI was in draft form. This letter is being finalized this week and has been
updated to reflect a SO fund balance for the end of 24-25 and a 4% fund balance reserve for 25-26.
