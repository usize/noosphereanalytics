Our Vision: We believe all students can learn at high levels
Our Mission: Preparing students for success in the global economy

GUIDING PRINCIPLE:

We believe that all staff should feel safe and respected, Therefore we must commit to clear communication, effective problem solving, and
respectful conflict resolution. We engage in non-judgemental analysis, demonstrating honesty and integrity in all our interactions.
We believe that all staff should feel valued and supported. Therefore, we hold each other accountable to our commitments, provide space for
reflection when tensions arise, and remain open to new perspectives. We are dedicated to making compromises for the well-being of our school
community.

a. Please describe how you select students for the program based on multiple criteria, e.g., Student Data, Teacher Referral, Previous

e Academic
o During PLC, classroom teachers identify students who may need Tier II interventions as per their standards
classroom assessments. Galileo Benchmark data also provide classroom teachers direction towards achieving
standards.
For Tier Ill, all students are given the QPS and placed accordingly in their intervention group. Tier Ill students receive
instruction with the UFLI curriculum and those students who test out are placed in Reward or Rewards+ groups.
Students were given the Math Screener to all students. Tier Ill students were sent to the Math Interventionist and
team of para educators and Specialists to provide appropriate instruction. The rest of the students who were more
at a Strategic and Benchmark level were assigned to grade level teachers. Classroom teachers are expected to use
WSD approved resources such as Illustrative Math centers, Imagine Math, & Number Talks.
e Behavior
o Classroom teachers were asked to screen their students using a Behavior Tier II Screener. Once those students were
identified, classroom teachers then needed to provide additional information regarding the needs of the students.
The counseling department (Counselor and Social Worker) along with the Dean of Students provided students
appropriate support such as Check Ins and Lunch Bunches.
e Attendance
o An attendance protocol was in place for our students. Students who had 2+ absences were identified and our
Counseling Department reached out to parents/families regarding the absences. Monthly meetings were also set up
with our WSD Attendance Liaison to discuss students who were chronically absent.
