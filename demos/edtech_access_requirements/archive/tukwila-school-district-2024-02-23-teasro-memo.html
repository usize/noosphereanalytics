To: Tukwila School District Board of Directors Date: February 23, 2024
From: Debbie Aldous, Tukwila Association President

Topic: School Resource Officers

It has been at least four years since Tukwila Education Association (TEA) members have been asked their
opinion of School Resource Officers (SROs). Knowing this is a current interest of the School Board, and hearing
questions from staff at both Showalter and Foster about what was happening with the program, we surveyed
our members recently; the results can be seen below:

“I am in favor of having SROs from the Tukwila Police Department at Showalter and Foster.”

65% Agree or Strongly Agree
Member voice:

“Our students are being impacted by increased violence in our community and schools. Staff and students feel
unsafe and often view each other as a threat. Is this THE answer? No. Iam agreeing we need SROs because I
believe support staff need additional help to support students in a different way.”

“I think that if we bring back SROs we also need to address community violence as staff and faculty. There has
been too much gun violence, loss, and death that has gone unaddressed by folks in power in our school district.
We need to work at all levels to address how and what we respond to with student behavior and relationships
with parents and families. Simply bringing back and SRO will not solve what happened outside of the walls of
our school buildings.”

29.5% Disagree or Strongly Disagree
Member voice:

“We need more counselors to support students. I do not feel comfortable with someone in the building who is
armed with a gun, regardless of their training with a firearm. This will only add to anxiety. Our schools lack
safety because they lack systems, resources and effective leadership that can bring about repair from harm
and preventative measures to protect students from harm.”

When last asked four years ago, TEA members were overall not in favor of SROs in our secondary schools. The
results of this most recent survey represent a significant change in the thoughts of our members, and we
thought it was important for the Tukwila Board of Directors to have this information to inform on this topic.
