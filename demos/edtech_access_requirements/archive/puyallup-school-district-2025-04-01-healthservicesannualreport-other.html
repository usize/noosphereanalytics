Health Services Annual Report



This school year, we have continued to notice the increase in student medical needs requiring licensed
nursing support as well as the elevated costs of health room supplies. Moreover, an additional
foreseeable cost is replacing all expiring Narcan which is due to expire by July 2025. Health Services
was able to secure eight boxes of donated Narcan from Tacoma Pierce County Health Department: two
for each High School. Additionally, House Bill 1273 requires school districts to provide access to
menstrual products for all students. This bill aims to increase access to menstrual products for all
students, thus increasing the need for more inventory at each school building level.

In alignment with our goal of maintaining consistency in all health rooms, Health Services aims to
purchase new emergency medication storage equipment. This new method of storing emergency
medication will increase the health and safety of our students and ensure a faster, more efficient
response from all Health Services staff. We have partnered with Green River Community College LPN
program and will utilize their LPN students to assist with our annual hearing and vision screening.

Health Services has been able to maintain a balanced budget by providing health rooms with individual
budgets for unique school purchases, empowering staff to make informed, tailored decisions. This
strategy has enhanced the efficiency and effectiveness of Health Services while fostering a sense of
ownership and responsibility among the staff. This approach has resulted in a well-structured and
financially sound Health Services program.

Program Challenges:

The challenges we have encountered over the last few years continue to evolve. The increasing
number of student Emergency Action Plans (EAPs) highlights the growing complexity and volume of
health issues among our students. The noted complex medical needs of our students (i.e., students
with diabetes, seizure disorders, students requiring urinary catheterization) illustrate the increased
need for licensed nursing support.

Staffing shortages remain an ongoing challenge within the department. Securing qualified candidates,
particularly licensed candidates, continues to be a high priority. We have recently partnered with
Green River Community College’s LPN program in the hopes of identifying qualified candidates. We
have also partnered with PLU and WGU nursing programs, where we have successfully recruited and
maintained RNs.

Supply costs remain elevated, thus creating a significant impact on our expenditures over the last few
years. Additionally, some of our equipment, such as audiometers and AEDs, are over 8-10 years old
and will need to be replaced soon.

Demographic Changes (include data if applicable such as number of emergency action plans, medical
needs, etc.):
The medical needs of our student population continue to evolve. Each year, the number of students


with EAPs increases. These trends illustrate the growing needs and complexity of our student
population, therefore requiring licensed nursing support. In 2024, the total number of EAPs increased
by 13%, Life-threatening Allergy (LTA) EAPs increased by 9%, Life-threatening Asthma EAPs increased
by 8.5%, Seizure EAPs increased by 4.5%, and all other EAPs increased by 22%. This year, the total
number of EAPs increased by 18%, Diabetic EAPs increased by 39%, LTA EAPs increased by 8%, Seizure
EAPs increased by 18%, and all other EAPs increased by 38%. As of March 2025, 86 emergency 911 calls
were made, with 55 of those resulting in transportation to the hospital by EMS.

Despite the overall increase in EAPs and medical needs of our student population, we have been able
to maintain student vaccine compliance at 99%. This number is directly related to the hard work of our
health room teams. Each Health Services staff member has been very diligent with speaking with
parents and guardians as well as providing resources to obtain those missing vaccines.

Initiatives and Program Enhancements:

The Health Services department continues to grow and evolve, ensuring we are maintaining best
practice to align with WA Board of Nursing, OSPI, and WA Department of Health standards of care. We
implement Evidence-Based Practice in Nursing which focuses on using the best available evidence to
create the most effective care plan for the individual student.

Prior to the start of every school year, all Health Services staff participate in annual skills validation.
The primary goal of annual skills validation is to ensure that staff possess the necessary knowledge,
skills, and abilities to provide safe and effective student care, aligning with organizational and nursing
plan of care requirements. Skills validation plays a crucial role in maintaining the quality of nursing
care and ensuring student safety.

In alignment with our goal of achieving standardization throughout our health rooms, Health Services
implemented new Emergency Go-Bags this school year. This quality improvement project was a year
in the making, involving extensive data collection and surveys across all health rooms in the district.
The new Emergency Go-Bags are in full compliance with WAC 110-301-0230, which governs Emergency
Go-Bags in school settings. With this project, the district now has a standardized item across all health
rooms, marking a significant step forward in enhancing the health and safety of our students and staff.
To ensure student health and safety, Health Services has also implemented Quality Control (QC) checks
for each health room. These QCs are utilized to help identify areas where more focus or training may
be needed as it is important that student care, medical devices/equipment, and health room
expectations are consistent, and continue to meet a high standard in service to our students and
families.

In accordance with Senate Bill 5804, Health Services has obtained Narcan that is accessible in all 34
school buildings throughout the district. Each school building has a minimum of three boxes (six doses)
of Narcan available. Secondary schools will have more Narcan onsite as they have more AEDs on
campus. All health services staff, building administrators, and security officers are trained annually in


the use of Narcan.

Other additional program highlights include increased professional learning, such as mock codes and
emergency preparedness/readiness. Health Services recently participated in performing mock code
drills. Mock code drills are simulated emergency situations, like a cardiac arrest, designed to train and
enhance the skills, knowledge, and teamwork of Health Services staff in a safe, controlled
environment. Mock codes aim to improve technical skills such as practicing CPR, airway management,
defibrillation, and other resuscitation techniques as well as staff knowledge base such as
understanding emergency protocols, equipment, and medications. Mock codes also help improve
critical thinking skills such as analyzing emergency situations and making quick, informed decisions.
Additionally, mock codes build up teamwork by encouraging staff to coordinate their efforts with other
Health Services staff during an emergency. Mock codes also improve staff morale by reducing anxiety
and building confidence in handling real-life emergencies.

Lastly, Health Services has created wonderful partnerships with community members. We have
partnered with Tacoma Pierce County Health Department (TPCHD) to offer teen Mental Health First
Aid (t(MHFA). We piloted this program at Walker High School last school year, which was very well
received by students and staff alike. This year, we extended tMHFA to Ferrucci Junior High where it has
been taught to all 9* grade students through their Family Health class. Our goal is to extend this
program to all Junior Highs with the intention of providing this instruction to all 9" graders through
their Family Health class.

A new partnership this year provides free vision screenings and eye exams to students who receive a
vision referral because of a failed vision screening at school. Dr. Santa Elena is a pediatric optometrist
who works in Puyallup and is one of the few pediatric optometrists in this area. It is crucial to correct
any visual acuity concerns early as visual conditions that impair vision ultimately affect the learning and
development of children. In fact, in school-age children, the inability to focus their eyes or move their
eyes inward can cause difficulty in doing near-related tasks, such as reading. 80% of a child’s learning is
obtained through vision. More than 1 in 20 preschool-age children and 1 in 4 school-age children have
a vision disorder. Through this new partnership, Dr. Santa Elena and her team have identified many
students who needed a more in-depth vision screening.

Immunization clinics are crucial for public health, offering vital protection against preventable diseases
by providing vaccinations that strengthen the immune system and reduce the risk of serious illness and
outbreaks. Health Services has partnered with Acts Pharmacy to offer a free immunization clinic on
April 21%. We will be offering all childhood vaccines, including MMR and Tdap, free of charge for all
Puyallup School District students.

Health Services has also partnered with Central Pierce Fire and Rescue (CPFR) to ensure that all
policies, protocols and procedures are understood and valued. Though both Health Services and CPFR
serve our communities, we are held to different regulations and restrictions. It is important to come
together to understand and collaborate on how to best serve students and families utilizing the tools


and resources we each provide.

Health Services has also built a great partnership with a few nursing programs at local colleges where
we provide nurse mentoring and clinical site experience as part of their coursework. Nurse preceptors
bridge the gap between theoretical learning and clinical practice. They guide students in meeting
clinical objectives and delivering safe and quality student care. Health Services has been in partnership
with nursing students from WGU as well as PLU’s Bachelor of Science in Nursing and Master of Science
in Nursing students. This relationship has been quite reciprocal in that we have provided a wonderful
clinical experience for their students, and we have recruited nurses to work with us once they have
completed and passed Boards.

We have recently entered a new collaboration with Green River Community College LPN program. Our
LPN positions have the highest turnover rate and have historically been the most difficult position to
fill, for various reasons. By partnering with Green River, our hope is to offer a productive and exciting
clinical experience and potentially recruit new LPN students to work with us once they pass Boards.
Additionally, we will utilize the LPN nursing students to assist with our annual vision and hearing
screening. In doing so, this not only provides students with a hands-on opportunity of providing care
and service to students, but it will also help to offset the expense of health screening staff.

Upcoming Legislation:

SB 5790: Mandates that schools in the state of Washington, beginning in the 2026-27 school year,
maintain easily accessible bleeding control equipment on each school campus for use in case of
traumatic injuries involving blood loss. This new legislation also requires annual training in the
identification and treatment of a traumatic injury as well as the use of the equipment. SB 5790 requires
that each school has a minimum of two employees per school who have completed training using the
Stop the Bleed (STB) kits. Moreover, if a school has more than 1000 students, it must have one trained
employee per 500 students.

The Puyallup School District has had Stop the Bleed Trauma kits in all 34 health rooms for three years
and all Health Services staff have received annual Stop the Bleed/Traumatic Wound Training for the
last three years. As a result of this upcoming legislation, the Director of Health Services and Health
Services Manager became certified as Stop the Bleed instructors. Though this new legislation does not
go into effect until the 2026-27 school year, the Puyallup School District is prepared to handle
emergencies that may require the use of the Stop the Bleed trauma kit.
