Snohomish Education Foundation Presentation

Ms. Erica Cenci came to give the Board a presentation about the Snohomish Education Foundation
(SEF). The SEF connects the community with our schools by supporting and enhancing learning
experiences for all students. They are the only entity in the community investing in district-wide
support for students, teachers, and programs, as well as post-secondary resources. They want to
ensure that every student, regardless of their background or financial circumstances, has access to
opportunities and the resources they need to succeed.

SEF has given nearly $300,000 worth of scholarships distributed to nearly 200 graduating seniors
across the district. They have provided $13,300 towards shoes and clothing to students in need
through the Hanson Shoe Fund. They have provided $79,596 in grants to teachers for their
classrooms. In 2023, SEF donated $66,000 toward district-wide summer programs.

This past year they completed a Strategic Plan that focused on:
1. Grow and invest in the organization.

2. Expand community awareness and engagement.

3. Cultivate partnerships.

4. Create and enhance impactful program.

The SEF annual action is their primary fundraiser. It is November 9, 2024, at the Tulalip Resort.
There will be a live and silent auction.

Enrollment Report

Mr. Laufmann gave the Board the latest date for enrollment. As of October 1, 2024, the district is
63 FTE above our budget and 136FTE last June’s enrollment. High school enrollment is well above
budget while elementary and middle schools are both below budget. Historically, we gain
elementary enrollment throughout the school year. Middle School stays relatively flat and High
School traditionally decreases by the end of the year.

Looking at elementary grades, kindergarten is over budget by 21 FTE while most of the other
grades are below budget. Cathcart is 49FTE above budget. Ms. Ballas asked if we have Running
Start numbers yet? Mr. Laufmann said that we don’t have Running Start numbers yet, but that he
will provide them to the Board once we have them.

Elementary Comprehensive Literacy

Ms. Bauer was excited to share the important literacy work the district has been focused on over
the past two years. The Board was provided a document outlining the Comprehensive Language
and Literacy Instructional Model. Teaching and Learning used this to create curriculum maps for
our teachers.

In the model, there is a description called the Gradual Release of Responsibility. This ensures that
all of our elementary students engage in meaningful and consistent learning experiences to
strengthen and deepen their literacy skills. Since reading is the foundation of all learning, we
recognize that students who are strong readers will be more successful not only in language arts,
but also in math, science, social studies and beyond. Our ultimate goal is to develop independent
readers who can confidently tackle more complex texts as they progress. We take a comprehensive
approach to literacy, incorporating foundational skills such as phonics, guided reading, vocabulary
development, and book clubs to create a well-rounded educational experience.

To ensure that we use our curricular resources effectively and provide high-quality instruction, we
formed a diverse team of teachers from every grade level to develop curriculum maps. In August
2023, we brought together all elementary schools to introduce this instructional model and share
the curriculum maps. Thanks to ongoing feedback from our teachers and curriculum mapping
team, we continually refined the maps to better meet our educators’ needs. We also convened
another team to address the inconsistencies in how foundational skills like phonics and phonemic
awareness were being taught. The district moved from over forty different programs being used
to a unified approach. Now, all elementary schools are consistently using the same materials for
foundational skills and U Fly and Lexia for intervention and extension opportunities.

This past August, we reconvened to reintroduce our updated curriculum maps and provided
professional development with an emphasis on intentional and responsive teaching at each grade
level. We integrated the new foundational skill tools into our comprehensive instruction. Ms.
Bauer shared the curriculum maps with the Board. Ms. Bauer said we always start with the learning
standards, then unit themes, then learning targets. The learning targets are critical for intentional
and responsive teaching. The components of a well-rounded language and literacy instruction as
outline in our instructional model.

None of this work would have been possible without the work of our principals. Every elementary
building principal has committed to creating space for regular collaboration time for grade-level
teams to use the maps effectively. She also acknowledged the tremendous contributions of our
teachers along with the leadership of Shana Fogg and David Greene, who have been instrumental
in organizing and guiding these efforts. Ms. Bauer is proud of the work accomplished and believes
that it aligns with the Strategic Plan. It is foundational to our schools being more alike than
different.
