Board Resolution 01-24

Request for Grades K-12 Waiver of Minimum 180-Day School Year
(2024/2025, 2025/2026, 2026/2027)

WHEREAS, the Meridian School District is committed to the improvement of student learning; and

WHEREAS, School Improvement Plans and training activities are in place in each of the District's schools
and are designed to facilitate implementation of the State's and District's goals; and

WHEREAS, each School's Leadership Team, composed of representative staff with input from parents
and community members has participated in the development of the District’s Strategic Plan; and

WHEREAS, the District will provide professional learning and collaborative planning time to improve
academic achievement through effective instructional practices, social emotional learning, and
development of a positive culture; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Directors of Meridian School District No. 505 recognizes that planning and
training time is needed for staff members to implement identified goals, to align various curricula with
Washington State learning goals and benchmarks, to develop and implement aligned curricula, and to
implement the District's Strategic Plan and individual school improvement plans; and

WHEREAS, the Meridian School District currently utilizes three (3) "Waiver Days" as allowed by the State
Board of Education waiver of WAC 180-16-215 to provide staff members with additional time for
learning improvement and training; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Directors believes that the three (3) "Waiver Days" are critical for planning and
training; and

WHEREAS, the 177-day school year for students will have total program hour offerings that meet or
exceed the minimum requirements prescribed in RCW 28A.150.220(3); and

WHEREAS, WAC 180-18-040 allows a school district to apply to the Office of Superintendent of Public
Instruction (OSPI) for a waiver from the provisions of the minimum one hundred eight (180) day school
year requirement.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Board of Directors of the Meridian School District #505
hereby requests OSPI to waive the one hundred eight (180) day school day requirement for the
2024/2025, 2025/2026, and 2026/2027 school years so that three (3) days each year may be devoted to
planning and training that is linked to improvement of learning; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the District will use the following means to determine if the learning
improvement occurs:

° State test results

e Interim assessments

e Formative assessments


BE IT FINALLY RESOLVED that the Board of Directors is committed to provide continuing opportunities
for staff members, parents, and community members to work together to improve student learning
through such means that include but are not necessarily limited to the schools' Leadership Team,
advertisement meetings in which parents and community members may offer input about curriculum,
and committees that may be established to facilitate achievement of the goals described herein.

ADOPTED BY THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS of the Meridian School District 505, at a regular meeting
thereof, held March 6, 2024 with the following Directors being present and voting:

Waiver Application:
180-day School Year Waiver

As part of the basic education requirements in Washington State, each school district must
make a minimum of 180 school days available to students each school year. In addition, school
districts must offer at least 1,027 instructional hours each school year, as prescribed in RCW
28A.150.220.

The Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI) may grant certain waivers for the 180-
day school year requirement to school districts, in order for the district to implement a local plan
to provide for all students an effective education system designed to improve student
achievement by enhancing the educational program for all students in the district or for
individual schools in the district. Waivers may be granted for up to three school years.

Applications for the regular 180-Day School Year Waiver (formerly known as “Option One”) must
be submitted to OSPI prior to implementation. Once the completed waiver application packet Is
received, OSPI will review and notify the requesting district of the decision. In general,
applications submitted to OSPI by the 5th of the month will have a decision by the 10th of the
following month. For example, applications received by OSPI before January 5 will receive a
decision by February 10.

Please note: Waivers for parent-teacher conferences (up to five school days) or emergency school
closures follow different processes. For more information, please visit OSPI’s waiver page or email
waivers @k12.W4d.uS.

To be considered for the regular 180-day School Year Waiver, please submit the
following documents to OSPI: Documents should be in PDF or Word format. Do not send weblinks.
1. Completed 180-day School Year Waiver Application form (see next page).
2. Proposed school calendar(s) for each of the years for which the waiver is requested.

3. A resolution adopted and signed by the school district board of directors requesting the
waiver. The resolution must identify/provide:

The basic education program requirement for which the waiver is requested.

The school year(s) for which the waiver is requested.

The number of days in each school year for which the waiver is requested.

Information on how the waiver will support improving student achievement.

A statement attesting that the district will meet the minimum instructional hours
requirement of RCW 28A.150.220(2) under the waiver plan.

180-day School Year Waiver Application

Overview: In a few sentences, summarize the district's proposed waiver plan (purpose, goals).

>The Meridian School District will provide professional development and systematic
collaborative planning time to work toward our strategic plan vision to empower all
learners to thrive academically, socially, and emotionally as valued members of our
local and global communities. Professional learning will be focused on building
capacity to impact our strategic commitments to belonging, support and learning.
We will have a specific focus on reducing systemic barriers resulting in a reduction in
disproportionality in student outcomes within our district.

School Improvement Plans: Describe, in detail, how the district's waiver plan is aligned with
school improvement plans under WAC 180-16-220 and any district improvement plan.

Our district and school improvement plans target a reduction in the discrepancy
between the reading and math achievement and on-track to graduate status of all
students and the achievement of students with disabilities and multilingual learners. Our
waiver day plan is aligned with these goals by focusing on strengthening and increasing
the accessibility of tier 1 instruction, improving our multi-tiered systems of support, and
addressing barriers to belonging.

Student Achievement Goals: Describe, in detail, how the district's waiver plan is related to
measurable and attainable goals of the waiver for student achievement.

=> The professional development and systematic collaborative planning that will occur
during the waiver days will contribute to the Meridian School District's goal of a 10%
reduction in the discrepancy between the achievement of all students and the
achievement of students with disabilities and multilingual learners by:

e increasing the implementation of effective instructional strategies in tier 1,
including a continued focus on Universal Design for Learning.

e improving the effectiveness of our muti-tiered systems of support, with a
particular focus on MTSS systems for reading, math, attendance, and academic
and social behaviors.

e implementing strategies to increase student belonging.

e improving processes for systematic data analysis so that we can identify and
address student needs.

on the proposed waiver days and how these activities are likely to result in attainment of the

180-day School Year Waiver Application

Participation in Developing Plan: Describe, in detail, the participation of administrators,
teachers, other district staff, parents, and the community in the development of the waiver
plan.

Teachers, staff, administrators, community members and families were involved in the
creation of the Meridian School District strategic plan and the identification of the focus
areas of learning, support and belonging. Staff, families, students, and administrators
participated in the calendar committee and developing a proposal that included waiver
days in the district calendar as a mechanism for professional learning. Based on feedback
from staff, families, students and the community, the inclusion of waiver days in the
school year calendar has been positively received. Administrators also reviewed data and
worked with teachers to identify content focus areas based on student needs.

Equity: Describe how the district considered equity in developing the waiver plan. This may

include, an equity analysis, community feedback, or other means to assess the consequences

of the waiver.

>In a review of district data and through building and district level needs assessments
we identified a persistent disproportionality in our student achievement and on-track to
graduate status for multilingual learners and students with disabilities. Focusing the
waiver days on both professional learning and systematic planning will help us to
address instructional and systematic barriers to achievement for these student groups.
The focus on increasing belonging for all students was a need identified by students,
staff and families in surveys and other input opportunities.

In planning the waiver days to address potential inequities we preserved full-length
school days for instruction and placed waiver days on our shorter, late start school days.
We will work with food services to plan for maintaining access to meals for students on
waiver days.

180-day School Year Waiver Application

For Renewals, please provide the following additional information:

Describe the effectiveness of the implemented waiver plan activities in achieving the goals
of the plan for student achievement.

=> Our first waiver focused on implementation of Universal design for learning with an
emphasis of increasing access for our students with disabilities. As a result, we have
increased the number of students with disabilities included with their peers in core
instruction and decreased barriers to access in grade level instruction. Targeting
Universal Design for Learning strategies and in particular support for multilingual
learners has resulted in an increase in growth on the WIDA Access for Multilingual
learners in grades K-8.

Describe how the effectiveness of the district's waiver plan is measured.

=>We have done a review of rates of inclusion in general education setting for students
with disabilities and WIDA access growth for students. We have also reviewed iReady
assessment data for reading and mathematics.

Describe any proposed changes in the plan to achieve the stated waiver plan goals.

= In addition to professional learning, we have added a focus on systemic planning for
MTSS structures. This will allow us to address tier 1 core instruction while also
strengthening our systems to respond when students need additional support.

Our iReady academic growth has been inconsistent. We have shifted our professional
development plans to narrow our academic focus areas, limiting the areas we will target
each year and beginning with the areas of greatest need for our multilingual learners
and students with disabilities.

Describe support or concerns by administrators, teachers, other district staff, and the
community for continuation of the waiver plan.

= Staff and administration are in support of the waiver days as a mechanism to support
professional learning. Surveys by the calendar committee show community support for
maintaining these days as a part of the school year calendar.
