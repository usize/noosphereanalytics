On Page 9, any ideas as to why the 90% goal was not quite reached?

Jessica Proctor: We know that some of our students in some of our programs did not complete them and we
need to analyze where the gaps are in the system. Also, different districts have different systems so
sometimes transfer student data doesn’t get input.

On Page 12, how many of the 60 students were graduating seniors?
Shelby Abrahamian: There were 44 Seals of Biliteracy awarded to graduating seniors.

On Page 11, any ideas on how to close the MLL, Black and Latinx disproportionalities?

Concie Pedroza: This is why we are now strategizing on a targeted universalism, meaning resources and
services need to be targeted. This will include tutoring, case management, MTSS/interventions strategies
and progress monitoring.

On Page 12, how many of the 49 MLL students had been in MLL status for more than 5 years?
Shelby Abrahamian: 24 students.

On Page 12, any ideas on how to get more students out of MLL status?

Concie Pedroza / Jessica Proctor: This is year two of our coaching and co-teaching model with our MLLs; this
has been a shift to the system and the school was also like the other schools, overwhelmed with newcomers
last year. MLL is intentionally co-teaching our scholars. Students who are more than four years old will be
reviewed through the MTSS system to analyze further interventions. Also, we have a new MLL advisory
committee this year based on the needs for guidance and support and they will offer suggestions to improve.

Does Foster have a policy on the use of homework?
LaTanya Parker: We do not have a homework policy; there is a grading policy.

How many Foster students have take-home Chromebooks and Google Classroom as part of the City of
Tukwila program? What impact has this program had on those students?

Aaron Draganov: There are currently 132 students in grades 10-12 who have been assigned a Chromebook
through the Tukwila City/District partnership. Additionally, nine 12th-grade students who graduated in 2024
were part of the program. The initiative is expanding to include middle school students and potentially those
in 5th grade as well. Students have reported that having a Chromebook helps them stay closely connected to
their classes, particularly for assignments on Google Classroom. They also benefit from access to research
websites and word-processing tools, which aid in preparing essays, narratives, and reports.
