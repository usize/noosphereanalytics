Notice of Action for the Sparks Stadium Parking
Improvements SEPA Determination.

Public Content

Presented by:

Dr. Vincent Pecchia, Assistant Superintendent of Operations and School Support

Issue:

We are asking the board to consider approval of the attached Resolution #98 2024-25 to proceed with the Notice of Action for
the Sparks Stadium Parking Improvements project based on the findings of the projects Determination of Non-significance
(DNS).

Background:

Puyallup School District acted as SEPA Lead Agency for this project. To fulfill its responsibilities under the State Environmental
Policy Act (SEPA) in an expeditious fashion, board action, relying on the SEPA determination, is needed to initiate an appeal


period. By reference, Board Policy No. 6890 requires appeals to be commenced on or before the 21st day after the second
consecutive publication of the Notice of Action in a newspaper of general circulation.

The SEPA process for this project has entailed two 15-day public comment periods associated with the following:
® Publication of the project Expanded SEPA checklist and supporting documents.
* Publication of the Determination of Non-significance (DNS) and supporting documents.

Each comment period included the following notification efforts following Board Policy 6890:

Published on Washington State Department of Ecologys SEPA register.
Advertisement in the Tacoma News Tribune

Email notification to local agency partners

Public Notice sign posted onsite throughout duration of public comment periods
Posting of project SEPA documents on PSDs SEPA website.

Discussion:

There were no comments received during either of the comment periods objecting or asking for substantive changes to the
project as proposed.

Operations and School Support, at pecchivj@ puyallupsd.org or 253-841-8772 or Brian Devereux, Director of Facilities Planning,
at deverebj @ puyallupsd.org or 253-841-8772.

Dr. Vincent Pecchia, Assistant Superintendent of Operations and School Support


Joe Byme, Director of Nutrition Services and Logistics Support
Issue:

This report will provide the board of directors with information pertaining to the districts Nutrition Services Program. The report will
include student participation rates, Community Eligibility Provision (CEP), staffing, challenges, and future initiatives.

Background:

The mission of Nutrition Services is to deliver high-quality, nutritionally sound food service programs that support student
learning and align with the Puyallup School Districts strategic plan, all while maintaining financial solvency.

The primary goal of Nutrition Services is to enhance each students learning ability by providing nutritious, appealing, and tasty
meals. A diverse array of food choices is offered to educate students on developing healthy, lifelong eating habits. Additionally,
Nutrition Services supports schools, Parent Teacher Association (PTA) groups, community organizations, parents/guardians,
and teachers by offering a variety of catering services, barbecues, and special event support.

Nutrition Services staff prepare healthy school meals using scratch-cooking techniques. They utilize basic ingredients to create
menu items in the districts central kitchen. This facility is designed and equipped for cooking with raw and unprocessed food
ingredients, which have shorter shelf lives and fewer added preservatives, resulting in more nutritious meal options for students.

Di ion:

This report will present the board of directors with comprehensive information regarding the districts Nutrition Services Program.
It will cover student participation rates, CEP, staffing, challenges, and future initiatives.

Ryan Hahn, Executive Director of Business Services

Issue:

Resolution No. 99 2024-25 for Bonds to Alleviate overcrowding, replace aging schools, and enhance safety is presented for
board consideration and action to facilitate a bond election on April 22, 2025.

Background:

The Bond Advisory Committee (BAC) was formed to address the pressing infrastructure needs within the Puyallup School
District. The committee held its first meeting on September 12, 2024, and concluded its work for the 2024-2025 school year on
November 7, 2024. On November 18, 2024, the school board approved the BAC's recommendation, known as Scenario 1, which
aims to:

¢ Expand and improve Emerald Ridge, Puyallup, and Rogers High Schools to reduce overcrowding and reliance on

portable classrooms
° Replace and expand Spinning, Waller Road, and Mt. View Elementary Schools to address failing infrastructure and

ensure safe, secure learning environments
* Build anew elementary school in the southeast region of the district to accommodate projected growth and enrollment,

reducing overcrowding in neighborhood schools

The board authorized the drafting of a bond resolution to facilitate a bond election on February 11, 2025. The proposed bond
resolution seeks to generate funds for these critical projects, focusing on improving the district's educational facilities to better
serve its students and the community.

The election took place on February 11, 2025.With 63.35% approval, we have surpassed the supermajority requirement for the
Puyallup School District Bond measure, Proposition 1 - an incredible reflection of our communitys shared commitment to
investing in students and schools for which we are extremely grateful.

However, one final requirement remains before the election is certified. In Washington State, Bonds must also meet a voter
validation threshold, requiring a minimum number of ballots cast. Unfortunately, we were approximately 3,000 ballots short of
validation.

Therefore, the district is recommending we run the bond election again on April 22, 2025.
