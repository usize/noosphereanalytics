Regular Board Meeting
October 22, 2024

Responses to the Board’s questions/comments on Agenda Item 10.B — Approval of School Improvement Plans for the
October 22, 2024 Regular Board Meeting:

1. Agenda Item 10.B — Approval of School Improvement Plans

1. Whyis the Board being asked to approve drafts? Shouldn’t this be a Board Discussion item?
Scott Whitbeck: School Improvement Plans/SIPs are considered living documents that can be revised during
the year based on progress monitoring. Through that lens, these documents can be reviewed as Drafts for
the 10/22/24 meeting. From Board input at that meeting, the SIPs can be revised and presented again as
“Final” documents for Board approval at the 11/26/24 meeting. The revision process can include an analysis
of progress toward 23-24 goals, as referenced in question #3, below.

2. Whatis the purpose of these documents? Are they required as part of the OSSI process?
Scott Whitbeck: Yes, they are required by the Office of the Superintendent of Public Instruction/OSPI for all
schools they have assigned to the school improvement program based on multiple metrics. Foster HS is our
district’s only school not OSPI-mandated to participate because they have not met the criteria for required
participation. This document details how OSPI determines which schools are mandated to participate in
school improvement. Their criteria and metrics (p.1 1) are:

Grades1 = “K-12” Includes all high schools that have Multilingual Learners.

SBA2 = PROFICIENCY rates on Smarter Balanced Assessment/SBA.

Other3 = GROWTH rates on SBA.

Grad4 = Graduation rate after 4 years of high school.

ELP5 = Exit rate of students who are on track to transition out of Multilingual Learners/ML services
within six years.

e Quality6 = K-8 Attendance; HS combined Attendance & 9th grade on track to graduate.

3. Where is the analysis of last year’s (23-24_ goals?
Scott Whitbeck: Please see response #1, above.

4. Are schools able to braid/consolidate funds from LAP, Title 1, 2, 3, 4 and OSSI to meet the goals?
Scott Whitbeck: Yes, schools are able to braid those funds.
