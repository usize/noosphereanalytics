This report aims to inform the board of directors about the district's Health Services Department. It will cover key aspects such as

Background:

The Health Services Department is dedicated to enhancing student health and safety. Over the past three years, consistent
practices have been implemented, such as new medication books, trauma bags, and biweekly staff meetings. Annual skills
validation ensures staff are well-trained in medication management, emergency preparedness, and diabetic care. The goal is to
convert health assistant hours to Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) hours to meet rising health needs, with data showing an
increase in health plans and visits. Recruitment remains a priority. Additionally, professional learning opportunities, including
training in wound care, emergency preparedness, and diabetic care, are promoted to ensure consistent practice across the
district.

Discussion:

This report will provide the board of directors with a comprehensive overview of the district's Health Services Department. It will
delve into various key aspects, including detailed department statistics that highlight performance metrics and trends. Financial
information will be presented to offer insights into budget allocations and expenditures. The report will also address the
challenges faced by the department, such as staffing issues, resource limitations, and evolving health needs. Demographic shifts
within the student population will be analyzed to understand their impact on health services. Additionally, the report will outline
initiatives and program enhancements aimed at improving health outcomes and service delivery. Other relevant details, such as
policy changes, strategic goals, and future plans, will be included to provide a holistic view of the department's operations and its
role in supporting student well-being.
