Needs Assessment Summary
Park Place Middle is a diverse middle school serving students from various backgrounds. In the fall of 2024, 760 students are enrolled. Our staff has a deep commitment to our mission which states:
ALL Pumas learn at high levels. In this context, and based upon a review of the data, it is essential to identify the specific needs of our students with disabilities and multilingual learners to provide
equitable educational opportunities and support to bridge achievement gaps.

A comprehensive review of various data sources has been conducted to include:

1. Panorama Platform: The Panorama Survey provides insights into the perceptions and experiences of our students, parents, and staff. The survey results indicate that while there are areas of strength,
there are also significant challenges that need to be addressed particularly in the survey area, Sense of Belonging, and student success data platform outlining attendance.

2. SBA (Smarter Balanced Assessment) Scores: The analysis of SBA scores reveals disparities in academic achievement, with students with disabilities and multilingual learners consistently scoring
below their peers. These scores indicate a critical gap in their educational attainment. Park Place also seeks to increase overall achievement for all students in both English language arts (ELA) and
math.

3. iReady Diagnostic and Growth Results (Reading and Math): The iReady assessments offer valuable insights into student progress in both reading and math. The data highlight several trends:

e Students with disabilities (SWD) often demonstrate below-average performance in reading and math.
e Multilingual learners (MLL), while making progress, often require additional support to reach grade-level expectations.
e Limited growth for these student populations signifies the need for more targeted interventions.

4. World-class Instructional Design & Assessment (WIDA): Multilingual learners in Washington State are screened and tested annually using the WIDA assessment. Park Place analyzes this data to
ensure our multilingual learners are progressing and are provided equitable access in their academic pathways.

5. Review of Student Behavior Data: The analysis of students behaviors includes both minor and major behaviors, and exclusionary data. This data helps inform school wide decisions to support the
learning environment and all students. Data was also reviewed to examine discrepancies among our underserved populations, including students with disabilities.

In recognizing the academic disparities among students with disabilities and multilingual learners, Park Place is committed to creating a more inclusive and equitable learning environment. By

addressing the identified needs and implementing strategic solutions, we aim to ensure that every student has the opportunity to excel academically and personally, regardless of their background or
abilities.

What will be improved?

By how much?

By when?

And, for whom/what?
