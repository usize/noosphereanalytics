Purpose

The Tukwila School District Board of Directors is committed to creating a warm, welcoming, and inclusive
educational environment that supports and celebrates diversity. This policy is designed to promote equity
and excellence for all students and families, regardless of race, home language(s), special education
status, citizenship status, gender identity, or sexual identity. It aims to ensure that every individual is
treated with dignity and respect and provided equitable access to educational opportunities.

Equity, Anti-Racism, and Inclusive Family Engagement

Equity and Non-Discrimination

The Tukwila School District recognizes the increasing complexity of ensuring that all students receive
equitable access to education. Every student, school employee, parent, and community member has the
right to be treated equitably, free from prejudice, bigotry, and discrimination. The district is committed to
eliminating systemic barriers that lead to disparate outcomes for students based on race, ethnicity,
language, special education status, gender identity, sexual identity, citizenship status, or other identity
markers. We actively oppose and work to undo racism, discriminatory practices, and institutional bias.

The district defines equity as the process of providing targeted resources and support to meet the unique
needs of each individual, ensuring fair access, opportunity, and outcomes for all. We commit to using a
targeted universalism approach, addressing systemic inequities that impede student success by
implementing strategies that support those furthest from opportunity while benefiting the entire school
community.

Culturally Inclusive School-Family Partnerships

The Tukwila School District is dedicated to fostering partnerships with families that are culturally inclusive,
respectful of diverse backgrounds, and key to student success. Families play a critical role in their
children’s education, both at home and in school. The district encourages and supports family
engagement across all settings—in homes, schools, communities, and school governance.

The district recognizes the diversity of family structures, circumstances, and cultural backgrounds, and


respects families as important decision-makers for their children’s education. Our goal is to create and
maintain a district-wide climate that welcomes and encourages active family participation and supports
student learning and development. To ensure a warm and welcoming school environment, schools must
develop and sustain positive, respectful partnerships with families and the broader community.

High Expectations for All Students

Tukwila School District is committed to providing high-quality educational experiences for every student,
regardless of their background. High expectations are essential to fostering student success, and all
students will be encouraged to reach their fullest potential. The district is committed to eliminating
disparities in access to academic and extracurricular programs and addressing inequities in discipline
practices among various groups.

Schools will offer learning opportunities that reflect the racial, ethnic, cultural, and linguistic diversity of our
community, helping students thrive in an increasingly diverse world. These efforts will equip students to
navigate and contribute to a multicultural society and economy.

Professional Development and Diverse Staffing

The district recognizes the importance of professional development in enhancing educators’
understanding of the diverse cultural and linguistic backgrounds of our students and families. Tukwila
School District will provide ongoing training for staff to promote equity and address the specific needs of
diverse learners.

We are also committed to recruiting and retaining a diverse staff reflective of our student population,
acknowledging the underrepresentation of certain groups in education, including people of color, women,
veterans, individuals with disabilities, and others (RCW 49.74.005).

Supportive School Environment and Advocacy

A supportive school environment is vital to student success, and each student should have an advocate—
whether a family member, guardian, or community partner—who is empowered by district policies to
engage meaningfully in the educational process. Schools must create spaces that make families feel
welcomed, valued, and heard, and develop practices that inform and empower families to actively
participate in their children’s education.

The district will provide resources and guidance to parents and teachers in planning and implementing
family engagement initiatives that support student achievement and well-being. These efforts will be
evaluated regularly to ensure they meet the needs of students and families, with a focus on enhancing
school-family partnerships.

Implementation and Accountability

The superintendent will develop procedures to implement this policy. Copies of this policy will be
distributed to all schools, staff, families, and community members. The district will evaluate its equity and
family engagement efforts regularly, using the results to strengthen these partnerships and address
identified areas for improvement.

Tukwila School District will continue to engage in full partnership with families and community members to


ensure that every student is given the tools and support necessary to thrive academically, socially, and
emotionally. Through this commitment to equity, anti-racism, and family engagement, we reaffirm our
dedication to providing a world-class education for all students.
