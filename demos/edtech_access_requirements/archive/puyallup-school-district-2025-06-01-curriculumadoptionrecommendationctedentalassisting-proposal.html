Introduction

The Dental Assisting course prepares students to provide patient care, take dental radiographs,
prepare patients and equipment for dental procedures, and discharge office administration
function under the supervision of dentists and dental hygienists. The course includes instruction
in medical record keeping, general office duties, reception and patient intake, scheduling,
equipment maintenance and sterilization, basic radiography, pre- and post-operative patient
care and instruction, chair side assisting, taking tooth and mouth impressions, supervised
practice, and the opportunity to become Nationally Board Certified.

History

Dental Assisting teacher, CTE staff, and CTE advisory collaborated to select Dental Assisting
course curriculum offerings for the Puyallup High School program for the 2025-2026 school
year. The recommended curriculum, Dental Assisting: A Comprehensive Approach, 6th edition,
published by Cengage was reviewed alongside two other options. Adopting this curriculum will
support the alignment of industry standards, state core academic standards, and medical-
related Career and Technical Education opportunities for students.

Rationale for Recommending the Dental Assisting Curriculum:

> Standards Alignment
© The Dental Assisting curriculum is aligned to the OSPI state approved framework,
state core academic standards, and industry standards. It is also aligned to the
Dental Assisting National Board (DANB) and National Entry Level Dental Assistant
(NELDA) certification.

> Organization and Presentation of Materials

© The Dental Assisting: A Comprehensive Approach, 6th edition curriculum breaks
down core concepts in dental assisting with updated content covering basic and
advanced clinical skills along with the latest information on key practices,
procedures, equipment and patient and employee safety standards. Highlighted
topics are related to infection control, safety and legal issues, while Patient
Dialogue, Documentation and Profession Encounters features give students
insights into real-world practice.


© MindTap online teacher resource and student supplemental materials scaffold
the necessary dental assisting skills; encourage an understanding of the
importance of core concepts of dental assisting and provides a simulated,
immersive lab environment that engages users with a real-life approach.

© Each unit is broken down by suggested hours and aligned to industry and state
standards through academic lessons, modules, and scenarios.

© Instructional materials align with what is expected by the Washington Career
and Technical Dental assisting Association standards.

> Support Material and References
© MindTap is the accompanying digital learning solution allowing teachers control
of the digital aspect of the dental assisting course.
© Teachers can customize the interactive syllabi, emphasize the most important
topics, use various interactive elements to boost student engagement and add
material or notes in the eBook.

> Student Interest
© Real-world workplace scenarios and connections to dental assisting professional
practices.
© Engaging online supplemental simulations.
© Extensive exploration of related career opportunities.

> Quality of Information

© This curriculum aligns with the state approved frameworks and provides
opportunities for assessment in a variety of methods.

© The authors of Dental Assisting: A Comprehensive Approach, 6th edition include
experienced teachers, leading experts in the field of dental assisting, athletics,
and physical fitness training.

© Students will gain knowledge, skills, and practices required for careers in health
care related fields. Students will continue to apply their skills and advance their
learning as they apply their knowledge in a real-world setting.

Bias Screening

Each evaluator completed the district bias screening tool. Evaluators found that the Dental
Assisting: A Comprehensive Approach curriculum was aligned with both industry and state
standards. Some of the criterion in the bias screening process were not present due to the
technical nature of the curriculum; however, screeners found a balanced representation of
race, ethnicity, and gender in the photos included throughout the text. Persons with disabilities,
gender identity, family structures, and socio-economic status were partially represented
throughout the text and these topics will be addressed through direct instruction, classroom


discussion, and videos provided in the lessons to highlight career-relevant factors related to
inclusion.

Cost

Dental Assisting: A Comprehensive Approach, 6th edition curriculum provides a teacher
textbook, teacher supplemental resources, student textbook, and web-based support tools.
Student textbook cost is $251.95. The addition of Cengage MindTap and eBook/Textbook is at
the cost of $164.99 per student. Student paperback workbooks are $103.95. Total cost of the
Dental Assisting: A Comprehensive Approach, 6th edition curriculum for 30 students and one
teacher is approximately $16,043.64, which is comparable to the other options but offers
increased rigor, student engagement, and online resources align with our current learning
management system.

Community Review

The community was invited to review the materials in person at the district office and in the
main office of Puyallup High School from February 5 to February 16, 2024. No requests or
concerns were provided to the district. Comments from 23 individuals were received during
the teacher, student, and parent/guardian preview and included: 1) intentional information
layout, 2) straight forward and easy to follow, and 3) detailed information. Their feedback
resulted in the selection of Dental Assisting: A Comprehensive Approach, 6" edition because
the textbook was very engaging and easy to navigate with good accessibility and career
representation.
