e Technology department staff work regularly with the Director of Student Services to support the District's Universal Design for
Learning (UDL) initiative. We’ve replaced older IPADs with current generation models with assistive software and deployed more
than 80 touch enabled chromebooks to grades K-2 at Chautauqua. In addition we’ve installed UDL accessibility tools like LAMP
“words for life,” (an aid to give non-verbal individuals a method to develop independent and spontaneous communication)
LessonPix (image communication card creator), Google Read and Write (a literacy support tool that offers help with everyday tasks
like reading text out loud, understanding unfamiliar words, researching assignments and proofing written work. )

e Wecurrently have two budget codes that are used to support UDL and another for pilot projects. We continue to support the
Director of Student Services with implementation and troubleshooting during training PDD’s.
