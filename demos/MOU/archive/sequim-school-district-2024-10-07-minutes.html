Superintendent Report
October is resiliency and principal month-our principals are some of the most resilient people. Constantly reflecting on ways to improve.
10/2 School Custodian Day – many have worked on Capital Project initiatives-thank you for your hard work
10/14 Indigenous Peoples Day
10/16 Anti-bullying and Unity Day
10/15-10/21 – School bus safety – spoke with transportation department this morning, they did an amazing job with the accident navigation last week in Blyn. The SMARTAG system was deployed, and parents were notified efficiently.
10/30-10/31 Parent Teacher conferences
11/1 Professional Learning Day- No school
Erin Fox SHS Principal – SHS received a $10k grant for a 9th grade success team from OSPI – Doug the coach has been fantastic. The goal is to increase the 9th grade success rate. 9th grade success is the #1 predictor of graduation. Students are three times more likely to graduate if they finish their 9th grade year. SHS is tracking data and has put initiatives in place, organizational, and with extended learning one hour a week to work on essential standards. Thank you to the Port Angeles School District for recommending Sequim for the grant. Port Angeles is in their 2nd year with the program.
  LRFPG – As of today six presentations have been completed. The Safety fair was an unplanned opportunity this last Saturday to share information with attendees. We were able to speak with people who do not have students in our schools.
The first public forum will be at Helen Haller on October 8 at 5pm in the gym/cafeteria.
The Policy committee met and is reviewing discipline changes to Policy 3241.
After the regional superintendent meeting, the group toured the Northwater academy in North Kitsap. It is a new behavioral school.
Habitat Breakfast this morning – held in PA. Workforce development is a goal for so many organizations.
Outdoor education for 5th grade will take place this month, OPA held their own very successful version of outdoor education when they went to Ramblewood.
Look for Kaleidoscope will be out on Friday

       Operating Guidelines
Be civil, polite, respectful, and courteous.
Be on time, honor agenda time limits and content.
Be fully present.
Self-monitor for an appropriate balance of talking and listening.
Expect and accept non-closure; work is long term.
Speak the truth and put the elephant on the table; be forthright.
Keep calm and don't boil the ocean.
Presume positive intent - be mindful of how the message is received.
Questions are ok - "gotcha" questions are to be avoided.
Keep in mind that we are role models for students, district staff, families, and our community.
We do prioritize when we come together, we do so in a way that is highly effective.
