Addison's creativity shines in multiple ways.

One of the first things that comes to mind is her ability to bea
creative problem solver. It doesn't matter what it is, she just loves to
help, and her ideas are often something we did not think of.

Addison is a creative thinker, which to me, means she is thinking
beyond what is right in front of her. She connects classroom learning
to real life issues and experiences.

Her inquisitive nature drives her to ask questions and wonder about
things, but she does not stop there. Addison will come up with a
creative and possible answer to her question.

Addison's creativity with her artwork is evident and she takes special

Thank you Addison for sharing your creativity with us!

We have many compassionate students at Gold Bar Elementary. When | heard we were able
to recognize a student during the school board meeting, | immediately thought of Salem
Brown. Salem is one of our 2nd graders and he is one of the most compassionate students |
have worked with. Salem notices when others are having a hard time. He doesn't hesitate to
do what he can to help. He will share, give something he has to others if it will bring a smile
to their face, and is always willing to help. He includes everyone. Salem quietly helps others
or fixes a problem whenever he can. Not only do | notice this, but so do his classmates.
Everyone wants to spend time with Salem, sit near him, and work with him. He exudes
kindness and compassion.

Thank you Salem for being you- for showing others kindness, compassion and
understanding. You are noticed, appreciated and cared for - just as you care for others.

Heather Rummel

2" Grade Teacher at GBE
