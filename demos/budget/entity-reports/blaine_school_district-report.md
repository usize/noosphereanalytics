# Blaine School District Budget Planning Analysis: 2024-2025

## Executive Summary
This report analyzes the budget planning trends for the Blaine School District between the fiscal years 2024 and 2025. The district is facing significant financial challenges, necessitating a projected budget reduction of approximately $2.7 million for the 2024-2025 school year. Key areas of growth include the need for additional funding through local levies and grants, while cuts are primarily focused on staffing and operational expenses. The analysis highlights the critical importance of the Educational Programs and Operations (EP&O) levy, which, if not passed, could exacerbate the district's financial shortfall to over $9 million. This report synthesizes insights from various board meetings and financial reports to provide a comprehensive overview of the district's budgetary landscape.

## Budget Trends Overview
### Projected Reductions
- **Budget Shortfall**: The district anticipates a budget reduction of **$2.7 million** for the 2024-2025 school year, which is critical for maintaining financial stability. This figure is contingent upon the successful passage of the EP&O levy (January 11, 2024).
- **Staffing Cuts**: Significant staffing reductions are expected, with discussions indicating that the district may need to notify staff of potential layoffs due to budget constraints (April 22, 2024). The board has acknowledged that these cuts will impact classroom support and overall educational quality.

### Areas of Growth
- **Local Levy Importance**: The EP&O levy is a crucial revenue stream, contributing approximately **17%** of the district's total revenue. If the levy fails, the district could face a **24% cut** in programs that can be reduced (January 11, 2024).
- **Grant Funding**: There is a push for hiring a grant writer to secure additional funding, although concerns have been raised about diverting funds from classroom support (January 22, 2024). The district's leadership recognizes the necessity of exploring alternative funding sources to mitigate budget cuts.

### Legislative Impact
- **State Funding Challenges**: The district has faced challenges due to state funding formulas, particularly regarding special education and local levy collections. The recent legislative session has seen bills that could either positively or negatively impact funding, with the board expressing concerns about state mandates affecting local control (March 25, 2024).
- **MSOC Funding**: The district is projected to spend more than the state’s allocated MSOC (Materials, Supplies, and Operating Costs) funds, indicating a mismatch between state funding and actual operational costs (July 23, 2024).

## Detailed Analysis
### Budget Planning Meetings
1. **January 11, 2024**: The board discussed the necessity of a **$2.7 million** reduction, emphasizing the importance of the EP&O levy for financial stability. Director Lazarus noted that staffing expenses have increased significantly compared to student funding revenue, highlighting the unsustainable nature of current funding levels.
   
2. **April 22, 2024**: The board unanimously acknowledged the painful nature of proposed reductions, with Director Galbraith emphasizing the need for collaboration among stakeholders to address funding issues at the state level. The board's discussions reflected a consensus on the urgency of the situation and the need for community engagement.

3. **July 23, 2024**: The district's financial reports indicated a projected **6% General Fund balance**, with discussions around maintaining this balance while addressing staffing reductions. The board reviewed the implications of declining enrollment on future budgets, noting that enrollment trends have been steadily downward since the 2019-2020 school year.

### Community Engagement
The board has recognized the importance of community feedback in the budget planning process. Meetings such as "Supper with the Superintendent" have been established to facilitate dialogue between the board and community members regarding budget priorities and potential cuts (April 22, 2024).

### Conclusion
The Blaine School District is navigating a challenging financial landscape as it prepares for the 2024-2025 budget. The projected reductions, primarily in staffing and operational costs, underscore the critical need for community support and the successful passage of the EP&O levy. The district's leadership is actively seeking alternative funding sources and engaging with stakeholders to address these challenges. Moving forward, it will be essential for the district to maintain transparency and foster collaboration to ensure the sustainability of educational programs and services.
