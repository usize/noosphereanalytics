# Renton School District Budget Planning Analysis: 2024-2025

## Executive Summary
The Renton School District is facing significant financial challenges as it transitions from the 2024 to the 2025 fiscal year. The district's budget planning reflects a trend of increasing cuts, particularly in staffing and operational costs, while grappling with declining state funding and rising expenditures. The overall analysis indicates a strategic shift towards reducing administrative costs and reallocating resources to maintain essential services, particularly in special education. The district's financial health is under scrutiny, with a goal to restore the fund balance to meet board requirements.

## Year-over-Year Budget Trends

### Financial Challenges
1. **Declining State Funding**: Since the 2019-20 fiscal year, state funding for K-12 education has decreased from 52.4% to 43.1% in 2024, exacerbating budgetary constraints (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-26).
2. **Increased Expenditures**: The district's expenditures are outpacing last year's figures in nearly all areas, particularly in special education, which has seen a significant rise in costs due to increased enrollment and unmet funding needs (Meeting Minutes, 2025-02-12).
3. **Enrollment Declines**: The district has experienced a decline of approximately 1,200 students over time, resulting in a loss of $13.7 million in revenue (Meeting Minutes, 2025-02-12).

### Areas of Growth
1. **Special Education Funding**: The district's Special Education team has submitted an application for a potential reimbursement of over $6 million, indicating a proactive approach to securing additional funding (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-26).
2. **Enrollment Growth**: Despite overall declines, there has been a slight uptick in enrollment recognized in recent months, attributed to new housing developments (Meeting Minutes, 2025-02-12).

### Areas of Cuts
1. **Staff Reductions**: The district has implemented significant staff reductions totaling approximately $21 million or 7% of the budget for the 2024-25 fiscal year. This includes cuts to central administration, assistant principals, and various non-school based positions (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-26).
2. **Operational Budget Cuts**: The district has enacted a strategic hiring freeze, reduced non-student travel, and cut allocations for Materials, Supplies, and Operating Costs (MSOC) wherever possible (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-26).

### Fund Balance Projections
- The ending fund balance has decreased significantly, from -$15,218,937 in January 2024 to -$7,390,426 in January 2025. The board has set a minimum unrestricted fund balance goal of 4%, which remains unmet (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-26).

### Proposed Reductions for 2025-2026
The district has outlined proposed reductions for the 2025-2026 fiscal year, totaling $15,215,571, which includes cuts across various departments and school levels (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-26). Notable reductions include:
- District-wide: $6,260,077
- Elementary Schools: $3,164,990
- Middle Schools: $1,082,660
- High Schools: $606,299

## Conclusion
The Renton School District is navigating a complex financial landscape characterized by declining state support, rising costs, and significant budget cuts. The district's strategic focus on reducing administrative costs while attempting to protect student services is commendable, yet the ongoing financial challenges necessitate careful planning and community engagement. The upcoming budget hearings and legislative discussions regarding potential changes to the levy cap may provide avenues for improved funding, but immediate actions are critical to stabilize the district's financial health. 

The district's leadership is committed to restoring the fund balance and ensuring that the educational experience for students remains a priority amidst these challenges.
