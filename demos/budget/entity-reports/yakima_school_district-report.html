Yakima School District Budget Planning Report: Year Over Year Analysis (2024-2025)

Executive Summary

This report analyzes the budget planning trends for the Yakima School District between the fiscal years 2024 and 2025. The district is facing significant financial challenges, including a projected budget deficit and ongoing reductions in funding. Key areas of growth include special education services, while substantial cuts are anticipated in staffing and operational budgets. The analysis highlights the district’s strategic responses to these challenges, including a focus on community engagement and long-term financial planning.

Budget Overview

2024 Budget Context

  • The Yakima School District is currently navigating a $20 million budget reduction as confirmed in the January 8, 2024 board meeting. Assistant Superintendent of Finance, Jake Kuper, emphasized the impact of the Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) on the budget, stating, “We are working our way up from the bottom” (January 8, 2024).
  • The district’s financial situation has been exacerbated by declining enrollment, with projections indicating a decrease of 1,000 students over the next five years (January 8, 2024).

2025 Budget Projections

  • The preliminary budget for 2024-25 presented on July 30, 2024, indicated a $3 million deficit. Kuper noted that the district must adopt a balanced budget by August 31, 2024, despite the ongoing financial constraints (July 30, 2024).
  • The board is expected to approve a Program Reduction in Force (RIF) to address the budget shortfall, with a focus on minimizing the impact on essential services (July 30, 2024).

Areas of Growth

Special Education Services

  • The district has committed to enhancing its special education services, with a focus on compliance and support for students with diverse needs. K.C. Mitchell, Executive Director of Special Education, reported that the district served 2,431 students with diverse abilities during the 2023-2024 school year (July 30, 2024).
  • Plans for the 2024-2025 school year include increased inclusion in general education classes and improved training for teachers in behavior management and inclusive practices (July 30, 2024).

Community Engagement

  • The district is actively seeking community feedback on budget decisions, with surveys indicating significant opposition to proposed reductions (August 20, 2024). This engagement is crucial for maintaining transparency and trust within the community.

Areas of Cuts

Staffing Reductions

  • The district is implementing significant staffing cuts, including a 39 FTE reduction as part of the RIF process. This decision has raised concerns among board members regarding the potential impact on student support services (July 9, 2024).
  • Frances Guerrero, YEA President, highlighted the detrimental effects of cuts to custodians and paraprofessionals, stating that these reductions hinder teaching and support for students (August 5, 2024).

Operational Budget Constraints

  • The district is facing ongoing restrictions on spending, particularly in the areas of maintenance and operations (MSOC). Kuper noted that financial protocols have been established to control monthly spending, including renegotiating contracts with vendors (July 9, 2024).

Conclusion

The Yakima School District is at a critical juncture as it prepares for the 2024-2025 fiscal year. While there are areas of growth, particularly in special education services, the district must navigate significant budget cuts and a projected deficit. The emphasis on community engagement and strategic financial planning will be essential in addressing these challenges and ensuring the continued support of students and staff. The board’s commitment to transparency and proactive communication will play a vital role in fostering community trust and collaboration moving forward.
