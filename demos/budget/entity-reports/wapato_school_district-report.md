# Wapato School District Budget Planning Analysis: 2024-2025

## Executive Summary
This report analyzes the budget planning trends for the Wapato School District between the years 2024 and 2025. The analysis reveals a cautious approach to budgeting, with a focus on managing costs amid declining enrollment projections and rising project expenses. Key areas of growth include capital projects and infrastructure improvements, while significant cuts are anticipated in areas related to staffing and program funding due to lower enrollment figures. The overall financial strategy appears to prioritize essential services while seeking to mitigate budget overruns in ongoing projects.

## Year-over-Year Budget Trends

### 1. Enrollment Projections
The Wapato School District is facing a trend of declining enrollment, which has direct implications for budget planning. In a meeting on **February 12, 2024**, Sylvia Bazan, Director of Fiscal Services, presented a conservative enrollment projection of **2,959** for the 2024-2025 school year, noting, "the trend the past few years is that enrollment has decreased" (2024-02-12). This decline is expected to impact funding and necessitate careful staffing considerations.

### 2. Capital Projects and Infrastructure
Despite the challenges posed by declining enrollment, the district is committed to capital projects, particularly the PACE Construction Project. During a meeting on **December 16, 2024**, it was reported that the project is currently over budget, with costs estimated to be **$50-70 per square foot higher** than originally projected (2024-12-16). The Board discussed various cost-reduction options but emphasized the importance of maintaining project integrity. The need for continued exploration of alternatives to reduce costs without compromising objectives was highlighted.

### 3. Areas of Growth
- **Capital Projects**: The district is actively pursuing funding for capital projects, including infrastructure improvements. The **June 26, 2024** meeting noted a budget extension for capital projects to account for revenue from the sale of WHS bonds (2024-06-26).
- **Technology and Communication**: Investments in communication infrastructure, such as handheld radios, are being funded through ESSER funds, indicating a commitment to enhancing operational efficiency (2024-06-26).

### 4. Areas of Cuts
- **Staffing and Programs**: The declining enrollment is expected to lead to cuts in staffing and program funding. The **February 12, 2024** meeting indicated that the district did not meet its projected enrollment funding for the current year, which will influence hiring decisions, particularly in Special Education and Career and Technical Education (CTE) (2024-02-12).
- **Budget Management**: The Board's discussions reflect a cautious approach to budget management, with a focus on avoiding future overruns similar to those experienced in the PACE project. The emphasis on cost management strategies suggests that non-essential expenditures may be scrutinized more closely.

### 5. Financial Reporting
In the **February 24, 2025** meeting, the financial report presented by Sylvia Bazan indicated no significant changes in expenditures and revenue across various fund categories, suggesting a stable but cautious financial outlook (2025-02-24). The Board's engagement in discussions regarding property tax increases and their impact on district funding further underscores the need for careful financial planning.

## Conclusion
The Wapato School District's budget planning for 2024-2025 reflects a strategic response to declining enrollment and rising project costs. While there are areas of growth, particularly in capital projects and infrastructure, the district must navigate potential cuts in staffing and program funding. The emphasis on cost management and careful financial oversight will be crucial in maintaining the district's operational integrity while addressing the challenges ahead. The Board's proactive discussions and decisions will play a vital role in shaping the district's financial landscape in the coming years.
