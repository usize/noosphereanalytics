Yelm Community Schools Budget Analysis Report (2024-2025)

Executive Summary

This report analyzes the budget planning trends for Yelm Community Schools between the fiscal years 2024 and 2025. The analysis highlights areas of growth and cuts, providing insights into the financial health and strategic priorities of the district. Key findings indicate a significant reduction in student enrollment, which has led to budgetary constraints and necessitated cuts in various programs. However, certain areas, particularly special education and technology upgrades, have seen increased funding. The overall financial outlook suggests a need for careful management and strategic planning to navigate the challenges ahead.

Year-over-Year Budget Trends

Enrollment Decline

  • March 2025 Enrollment: 5,524 students (5389.78 FTE), a decrease from March 2024 enrollment of 5,664 students. This represents a loss of 140 students, which directly impacts funding and resource allocation.
  • Implication: The decline in enrollment is a critical factor influencing budget decisions, as funding is often tied to student numbers.

Revenue Analysis

  • General Fund Revenues: For 2025, the projected revenues are $88,617,555, with $46,269,852 collected by March 2025, indicating a collection rate of 52.2%.
  • Comparison to 2024: The previous year’s revenues were $45,055,213 by the same point, showing a slight increase in revenue collection efficiency.
  • Key Revenue Sources:
    • Local Taxes: $5,437,902 (2025) vs. $5,449,890 (2024)
    • State Special Purpose: $18,106,179 (2025) vs. $8,565,453 (2024), indicating a significant increase in funding for special education.

Expenditure Trends

  • Total Expenditures: The total budgeted expenditures for 2025 are $95,553,818, with $45,903,104 spent by March 2025 (48.0% of the budget).
  • Areas of Growth:
    • Special Education: Increased funding to $17,511,322, with $8,537,246 spent (48.8% of budget).
    • Technology Upgrades: Allocated funds for technology improvements, reflecting a strategic focus on enhancing educational resources.
  • Areas of Cuts:
    • Regular Instruction: Budgeted at $49,472,525, with expenditures at $23,064,964 (46.6% of budget), indicating potential cuts in instructional staff or resources.
    • Vocational Programs: Budgeted at $4,514,972, with only $1,871,000 spent (41.4% of budget), suggesting a reduction in vocational training opportunities.

Budget Challenges and Strategic Responses

  • Budget Cuts: Superintendent Chris Woods noted that “district labor groups have inquired about the budget cuts that will be implemented for next year,” indicating an awareness of the financial challenges ahead.
  • Strategic Planning: The district is considering “rolling over cuts made this year to next year” and is actively monitoring legislative developments to maximize revenue streams.
  • Efficiency Improvements: Engagement with school and district administration is ongoing to identify areas for improved efficiency, which is crucial given the current budget constraints.

Conclusion

The budget planning for Yelm Community Schools between 2024 and 2025 reflects a complex landscape of declining enrollment, increased funding in specific areas like special education, and necessary cuts in others. The district’s ability to navigate these challenges will depend on strategic planning, efficient resource allocation, and proactive engagement with stakeholders. As the district prepares for the upcoming fiscal year, careful consideration of these trends will be essential for maintaining educational quality and financial stability.
