# White River School District Budget Planning Analysis: 2024 vs. 2025

## Executive Summary
This report analyzes the budget planning trends for the White River School District between the years 2024 and 2025. The analysis reveals a pattern of stable spending in the General Fund, with a slight increase in expenditures compared to the previous year. Notably, there is a focus on capital projects and educational programs, while certain areas, such as transportation and debt service, show minimal activity. The overall financial health of the district appears stable, with strategic investments in educational resources and infrastructure.

## Year-over-Year Budget Trends

### General Fund Expenditures
- As of January 2025, the district has spent **39.8%** of its budgeted expenditures, compared to **38.8%** at the same time in 2024. This indicates a trend of **level spending** with a slight increase in expenditure rates year-over-year. The district emphasizes that it builds capacity into its budget, suggesting that full expenditure of budgeted amounts is unlikely. 

  > "As a result, we will (very likely) never spend our fully budgeted expenditures." (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-12)

### Capital Projects
- The expenditures in the Capital Projects fund during January 2025 were directed towards energy upgrades and technology improvements at White River High School. This reflects a commitment to enhancing educational facilities and resources.

### Transportation and Debt Service Funds
- Both the Transportation Vehicle Fund and the Debt Service Fund reported **no significant activity** in January 2025. This indicates a potential area of concern, as it suggests limited investment in transportation infrastructure and debt management.

  > "There was no significant activity in the Debt Service Fund in January." (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-12)

### Payroll and Personnel
- The payroll issued in February 2025 amounted to **$5,299,295.48**, reflecting the district's ongoing commitment to its staff and educational personnel. This figure is crucial for maintaining operational stability and supporting educational programs.

### Areas of Growth
- The district is investing in its **Career and Technical Education (CTE)** programs, as evidenced by the introduction of new tools and technologies, such as the AR machine for welding. This investment aims to enhance student engagement and skill development in vocational areas.

  > "They shared information on a new AR machine for welding... a nice tool to ease people into welding." (Meeting Minutes, 2025-03-12)

### Areas of Cuts
- While there are no explicit cuts mentioned in the budget for 2025, the lack of activity in the Transportation Vehicle Fund and Debt Service Fund could imply a strategic decision to limit expenditures in these areas. This may reflect a prioritization of funds towards more immediate educational needs and capital improvements.

## Conclusion
The budget planning for the White River School District between 2024 and 2025 shows a trend of stable and slightly increasing expenditures in the General Fund, with strategic investments in capital projects and educational programs. However, the lack of activity in transportation and debt service funds raises questions about future planning in these areas. Overall, the district appears to be focusing on enhancing educational resources while maintaining fiscal responsibility.
