Call to Order/Land Acknowledgment: Mr. Benjamin (President) called the meeting to order at 6:00 p.m. He acknowledged that this meeting is being held on ancestral homelands of the Lummi people and Nooksack tribe. Roll Call: Present: Directors Douglas Benjamin (President), Camille Diaz Hackler, Scott Ritchey, Katie Rose, Superintendent Greg Baker and Sheri O’Day (recorder). Director Jennifer Mason was unable to attend the meeting. Audience: No public testimony was offered. Superintendent’s Announcements and Report: Summary of Dr. Baker’s comments: • Dr. Baker recently met with the state superintendent and a group of superintendents from across the state. He displayed slides from that meeting showing voter priority issues in 2023 and 2024. Other information shared included K-12 spending over the past 10 years, K-12 spending with inflation adjustments and per-pupil state funding over the past 10 years. The percentage of K-12 education funding in the state’s operating budget has decreased over time. It’s expected that the best estimate of final budget numbers will be coming from the state this week, and the district is working to finalize the 2024-25 budget. It’s expected that 2024-25 will be a “hold steady” year for the budget, other than salaries. A proposed final budget will be presented to the board in June or July. Looking further out, the 2025-26 budget year is likely to be another challenging year, and it’s expected that there will be a lot of advocacy for K12 education funding. • Promise Awards have been presented over the past two weeks, and Dr. Baker shared photos of recipients at the award presentations. • High school students have been participating in exciting competitions, including music, bridge building and robotics. • Dr. Baker shared photos from the March 26 CTE school board linkage. • Registration for Promise K and kindergarten is happening now, with the elementary schools hosting open houses for future students and families. • A Since Time Immemorial Family and Community Night will be held 5:30-8 p.m. on April 24 at Whatcom Community College.

Superintendent’s Announcements and Report: Summary of Dr. Baker’s comments: • Summer programs are in progress, some in person and some online. Dr. Baker noted that the draft budget for 2024-25 has been shared with the community. He has been asked what can be done to influence the governor and the legislature to change the school funding model. It has been

recommended by the Washington Association of School Administrators (WASA) that districts in the State of Washington have common talking points, which include fully funding special education, pupil transportation and MSOCs (Materials, Supplies and Operating Costs). Local levy money is used for these three areas, and WASA has asked that those three areas be emphasized. Other areas that need to be addressed by the state include: o Increased costs of utilities and insurance. o An update to the staffing model. o Fixing the regionalization formula. o Addressing the experience factor for staff.

•

Dr. Baker shared a draft of a tool to use with legislators and the community and responded to questions from the board. At the next regular school board meeting the board will be asked to adopt the 2024-25 budget. Please direct any questions about the proposed budget to Dr. Baker.
