# Pasco School District Budget Analysis: 2024-2025

## Executive Summary
This report analyzes the budget planning trends for the Pasco School District between the fiscal years 2024 and 2025. The district is facing significant financial challenges, including rising costs that outpace revenue growth, necessitating a budget freeze and potential cuts in various areas. The analysis highlights areas of revenue growth, particularly in restricted funding categories, while also identifying significant expenditure challenges, especially in special education and operational costs. The overall financial outlook suggests a need for strategic adjustments to maintain fiscal stability.

## Budget Overview
The estimated total budget for the Pasco School District for the 2024-2025 fiscal year is projected at **$332,959,738**. The budget reflects a forecasted expenditure of **$337,183,575**, resulting in a projected deficit of **$(4,823,837)** (Pasco School District, March 11, 2025).

### Revenue Trends
1. **Revenue Growth**: The district anticipates an increase of **$1.8 million** in revenue compared to previous projections. However, this increase is primarily in restricted funding categories such as transportation and state and federal programs, which do not alleviate the financial pressures on basic education funding (Pasco School District, March 25, 2025).
   
2. **Special Education Funding**: The district's special education revenues are projected at **$39,346,969**, while expenditures are expected to reach **$40,426,726**, resulting in a shortfall of **$(1,079,757)**. The district is also anticipating qualifying for additional special education safety-net funding, which could range from **$500,000 to $1,500,000** (Pasco School District, March 11, 2025).

### Expenditure Challenges
1. **Rising Costs**: The district is grappling with rising operational costs, including an anticipated **15% increase in insurance premiums** and overall inflation affecting salaries and materials. These costs are rising faster than the district's revenue growth, leading to a projected **$54.8 million** over budget if spending habits do not adjust (Pasco School District, March 25, 2025).

2. **Budget Freeze**: To address the financial challenges, the district has implemented a budget freeze, which restricts spending and requires additional approvals for expenditures. This measure is intended to stabilize the district's financial condition and prevent further depletion of the fund balance (Pasco School District, March 11, 2025).

### Areas of Growth and Cuts
- **Growth Areas**: The most notable growth in revenue is seen in restricted funding categories, particularly for transportation and special education programs. However, these funds are not flexible enough to cover the general operational costs of the district.
  
- **Cuts and Reductions**: The district is preparing for potential cuts in various operational areas due to the budget freeze and the need to align expenditures with the reduced revenue forecast. This includes monitoring staffing levels and operational costs closely (Pasco School District, March 25, 2025).

## Conclusion
The Pasco School District is navigating a complex financial landscape characterized by rising costs and stagnant revenue growth. The implementation of a budget freeze is a critical step in managing the district's financial health, but it may lead to cuts in essential services and programs. Moving forward, the district must engage in strategic planning, including potential levy elections to replace expiring levies and ensure sustainable funding for future operations. The financial outlook remains challenging, necessitating ongoing monitoring and adjustments to maintain fiscal stability.
