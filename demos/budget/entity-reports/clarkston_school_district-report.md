# Clarkston School District Budget Analysis Report: 2024-2025

## Executive Summary
This report analyzes the budget planning trends for the Clarkston School District between the fiscal years 2024 and 2025. The district is facing significant challenges, including declining enrollment and the expiration of federal funding sources, which have necessitated budget cuts and adjustments. Key findings indicate a projected budget reduction of $2 million, primarily through staffing adjustments and the use of fund balance reserves. Areas of growth include legislative support and community contributions, while the steepest cuts are observed in staffing and administrative support. The overall analysis highlights the district's commitment to preserving educational quality amidst financial constraints.

## Budget Trends Overview
### Enrollment Decline
- The Clarkston School District has experienced a **5% decline in enrollment** since the 2019-2020 school year, reflecting a broader statewide trend post-COVID-19. Specifically, high school enrollment has decreased by **169 students** over the past five years, with a notable drop of **100 students** in grades 9-12 for the 2024-2025 school year (Clarkston School District, March 25, 2024).

### Funding Challenges
- The per-student funding allocation is set at **$11,500**, which translates to over **$1 million** in total funding. However, without budget adjustments, the district anticipates a fund balance of less than **1%** (Clarkston School District, March 25, 2024).
- The expiration of **ESSER funds** at the end of the fiscal year has further complicated the financial landscape, as these funds previously supported staffing and operational costs (Clarkston School District, August 26, 2024).

### Proposed Budget Adjustments
- To address the projected deficit, the district plans to utilize **$700,000** from its fund balance, which will reduce the fund balance to **3.5%**, below the board's adopted floor of **5%** (Clarkston School District, March 25, 2024).
- Proposed adjustments include:
  - **Staffing reductions** through retirements and non-renewals, estimated to save **$1,165,000**.
  - **Administrative support cuts** and non-staffing budget reviews, projected to save **$150,000**.
  - Anticipated legislative support of **$300,000** to help mitigate funding losses (Clarkston School District, March 25, 2024).

### Areas of Growth
- Despite the cuts, there are areas of potential growth:
  - Legislative support is expected to provide additional funding, particularly in transportation and operational support (Clarkston School District, March 25, 2024).
  - Community contributions and grants, such as a **$12 million modernization grant**, could enhance facility upgrades and support educational initiatives (Clarkston School District, August 26, 2024).

### Staffing and Program Adjustments
- The district is exploring changes to the high school schedule, transitioning from a block schedule to a **6-period day** to accommodate budget constraints. This change will require further staffing adjustments and may impact credit recovery programs (Clarkston School District, March 25, 2024).
- The board has authorized a study to evaluate the need for a **reduced educational program** for the 2024-2025 school year, emphasizing the importance of careful spending to preserve educational quality (Clarkston School District, February 26, 2024).

## Conclusion
The Clarkston School District is navigating a challenging financial landscape characterized by declining enrollment and the expiration of critical funding sources. The proposed budget adjustments reflect a strategic approach to mitigate deficits while striving to maintain educational standards. The district's focus on legislative support and community engagement will be crucial in addressing these financial challenges moving forward. As the district prepares for the 2024-2025 fiscal year, ongoing monitoring and adjustments will be essential to ensure sustainability and educational effectiveness.
