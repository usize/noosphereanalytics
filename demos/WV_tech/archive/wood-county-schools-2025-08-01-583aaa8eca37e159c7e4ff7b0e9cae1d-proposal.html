3) How does this plan provide learning options for students without access to internet service
or electronic devices?

Instructional materials and directions will be provided in multiple formats. Teachers may
share assignments through Schoology and/or WVDE Office365. Students will be able to
download instructional materials and directions onto their county-provided iPads.
Additionally, hard copies of instructional materials and directions (NTID Packets) will be
provided to all elementary students and to secondary students as needed/requested.

How many instructional days do students have to complete the work they are assigned to do

during school closure?

Students will have three days to complete and return assignments.

How does the plan address the needs of students who do not have adequate access to
nutrition during prolonged stays at home?

Prepackaged meal boxes will be available at centralized pick-up locations.

Briefly describe how the instructional materials and tasks align with the curriculum that
students would cover if they were in school.

Teachers will be expected to use county-adopted curricular materials to provide
assignments that address grade appropriate West Virginia College and Career Readiness
Standards. NTID assignments present an opportunity for review of previously learned
materials.

Briefly describe how the lessons or tasks support critical thinking/problem solving.

The West Virginia College and Career-Readiness Standards establish a set of knowledge
and skills that individuals need to transition into higher education or the workplace, and the
standards reflect a rigorous and challenging curriculum. Therefore, assignments designed
to address the standards encourage and expect critical thinking and problem solving.

Do the lessons or tasks cover the four primary content areas (ELA, mathematics, science,
and social studies)?

Yes

10) How are schools ensuring special education minutes are being met on NTI days?

Schools will ensure that IEP services are delivered to students with disabilities. IEP teams
may meet in advance of NTI days to discuss and approve how services will be managed.
This decision will be referenced in the student’s IEP as an accommodation. Additionally,
remote instruction may be provided using technology or other methods, which may include
delivery of service minutes through virtual interaction between students and the special
educator.


Assessment/Accountability

1. Which of the following will you use to demonstrate that students have participated in the
required schoolwork?

Teachers will design assignments that allow students to demonstrate completion of NTID
school work. These may include reports, papers, essays, review, fictional pieces,
experiment design (science), problem design (math), problem/solution (math/science),

PBL design, classroom discussion when students return to school, and other assignments
as appropriate.

2. Will the evidence of learning be graded? If yes, please provide a brief description of
grading criteria.

YES. Assignments will be graded following the Wood County Schools grading policy.

Parent and Community Non-Traditional Instructional Day Survey for 2025-2026

As part of our ongoing efforts to improve educational experiences and explore innovative approaches to learning, we are
considering the implementation of non-traditional instructional (NTI) days in our district. These days would allow for
different types of learning experiences, including online learning, project-based learning, or other flexible schedules.

When school is canceled due to weather or other events, a school district may use up to five (5) non-traditional
instructional days. Work assigned and completed for NTI days count as instructional time that would not be made up at
the end of the school calendar.
