JACKSON COUNTY PRE-K AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT, effective for the 2024-2025 school year, between the JACKSON
COUNTY BOARD OF EDUCATION (“The Board”) and PATCH.

WHEREAS, PATCH operates a Center for children ages three (3) months old through twelve
(12) years old in Ripley, WV; and

WHEREAS, the parties have agreed to collaborate in the delivery of one (1) Pre-School
Classrooms servicing for up to twenty (20) children at the Center in the program known as “Pre-
School’;

The Jackson County Board of Education agrees to provide the following supplements,
services, and staff:

1. Three thousand eight hundred dollars ($3,800) per eligible child in the preschool
classroom, as enrolled in the West Virginia Education Information System (WVEIS), by
October 1, 2024, and verified by the West Virginia Department of Education (WVDE), for
up to 20 children to be prorated based on full time equivalency allowed by the WVDE. This
money is to be utilized to provide a quality preschool program that meets the requirements
of Policy 2525. Preschool services will be offered from 8:00 a.m. — 2:15 p.m. at no expense
to parents. Daily attendance is based on the most current data available through DHHR’s
color coded map, Jackson County School's re-entry plan and child care licensure rules.
Payments will be scheduled three times a year: One-half the amount due will be paid in
October. One-fourth will be paid in January and the remaining one-fourth to be paid in
April.

MEAL SERVICE

Whereas federal regulations require Pre-K children enrolled in public schools and housed
off-site in a child care center be provided meal services, the Jackson County Board of Education
and PATCH agree to the following meal service provisions:

(a) In order for the school system to claim meals for federal reimbursement, the
PATCH will report daily attendance and meal count to the school secretary no later
than 2:00 p.m. each school day.

(b) The Jackson County Board of Education will provide reimbursement for breakfast
and lunch for each child enrolled. The reimbursement provided will be based on
the actual meals served and the actual reimbursement received by Jackson
County Schools based on the Community Eligibility Provision (CEP) rates for 2024-
2025 school year.

(e) Jackson County Schools will provide training and/or communications on the
National School Lunch Program (NLSP) and the Child and Adult Care Food
Program (CACFP).

(f) PATCH is responsible for meeting the guidelines set forth by CACFP which
includes, but is not limited to, nutritious meals that meet the meal pattern
requirements, conducting an accurate point-of-service, and providing the meal
counts to the school where the Pre-K students are enrolled. Jackson County
Schools is required to monitor the meal program a minimum of once annually prior
to February 15.


(g) PATCH will invoice Jackson County Schools by the tenth (10) of each month for
the federal reimbursement and student charge for each meal served.

GENERAL CONDITIONS

1.Assignment: Neither party shall assign transfer or otherwise change the Agreement or any
right or duty created hereunder without the prior written consent of the other party.

2.Term: The term of this Agreement shall be for a period commencing on July 19, 2024 and
ending June 30, 2025. Jackson County Schools or PATCH may terminate and/or amend this
agreement in the event of insufficient student enrollment in the preschool classroom.

3.Modification: The parties may mutually amend this Agreement. Any Amendments shall be
placed in writing, signed by both parties and attached hereto.

4.Entire Agreement: This Agreement, including all attachments, embodies the entire agreement
of the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof. In the event of a conflict between the
terms of any attachment, or appendix, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail. There are no
promises, terms, conditions or obligations other than those contained herein, and this Agreement
supersedes all previous communications, representations or agreement, either verbal or written,
between the parties hereto. It is understood by both parties to this collaborative agreement that
the supplements, services, and staff provided by Jackson County Schools are exclusively for the
use in the Universal Pre-K Collaborative Classroom.

5.Impasse: In the event a conflict should arise, the parties shall follow the Jackson
County Collaborative Impasse Procedures to resolve the issue and come to consensus.

authorized representative as of the date set forth.
