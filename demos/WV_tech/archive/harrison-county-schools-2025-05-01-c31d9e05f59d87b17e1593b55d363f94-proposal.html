The curriculum review committee members consisted of two school classroom teachers, two
preschool special needs teachers, a child care curriculum specialist, and a Head Start teacher.
The review team used the state provided rubrics to evaluate both the general and specific
criteria from each of the approved vendors. After the rubrics were completed, committee
members as well as the Core Team members were invited to attend a vendor fair. Each of the
vendors outlined their curriculum and answered any questions from committee. After the
vendor fair, the curriculum review team was asked to present the pros and cons of each of the
curriculum choices to the Core Team. The Core Team then met and discussed the curriculums.
With a majority the Core Team is proposing Learning Beyond Paper as the curriculum choice for
Harrison County’s Universal Preschool Program.

The total for the proposed Harrison Universal Preschool Program is $188,980.00 for Year 1.
We respectfully recommend that the preschool curriculum adoption be approved as presented.

As a result of the process, the committee recommends that Learning Beyond Paper for use in all
collaborative preschoo! classroom in Harrison County.
