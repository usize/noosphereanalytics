LOTUS RECOVERY CENTERS
MEMORANDUM OF AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT, made this _ day of_, 2024, by and between, LOTUS
RECOVERY CENTERS, hereafter referred to as the Health Agency and BOONE
CAREER and TECHINICAL CENTER hereafter referred to as the Boone Center.

WHEREAS, the Health Agency and the Boone Center have agreed to jomtly
participate in a program in which students from the School of Practical Nursing may
acquire experience in the Health Agency.

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:
: |. This agreement may be terminated by either party within thirty (30) days written notice.

2. The program will be jointly evaluated from time to time and amendments may be made
to this agreement. Any amendments will be reduced to written memorandum and
attached hereto.

3. Neither monetary considerations nor any other benefits will be provided to the students
assigned to the Health Agency.

4. This agreement and the implementation thereof shall be in accordance with all the
nondiscriminatory principals and regulations of the federal government as administered
by the Office of Equal Opportunity and other such agencies.

5. The number of students assigned to the Health Agency will not exceed two (2) health
occupations students.

6. The students understand that due to occupational exposure to blood or other infectious
materials they may be at risk of acquiring hepatitis B virus (HBV) infection of human
immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection, which causes AIDS. The students are mandated
to be vaccinated against hepatitis. The students have been instructed in the use and
requirement for this protective equipment as well as all standard precautions, including
hand washing techniques, disposal of infectious wastes, the HIPAA laws, ethics, and
confidentiality of all information they have learned about patients.

2024-2025


The Boone Center agrees:

1. To submit goals and objectives for each student rotation.

2. To provide all didactic instructions to the students.

3, That the student will be officially registered at the Boone Center in the nursing Programs.

4. To provide the Health Agency with evidence that each student is covered with malpractice

insurance.

5. To provide the Health Agency with evidence that each student has passed a recent physical
examination before beginning clinical rotation.

6. To require students to observe and obey all rules, regulations, policies and procedures of the

Health Agency.

7, Students will be subject to testing for drugs of abuse prior to entering the clinical facility. The
school may conduct random drug tests periodically during the students’ clinical experiences.

The Health Agency agrees:

1. To provide adequate facilities to carry out the program including clinical facilities available; to
include laboratory, examination rooms, reception desk, business office and/or home visits.

2. To help plan and implement meaningful and appropriate learning experience aimed at the
achievement of the objectives of clinical preparation.
