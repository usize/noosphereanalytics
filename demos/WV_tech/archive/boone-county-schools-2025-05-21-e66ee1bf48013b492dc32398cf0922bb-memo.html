Members of the Boone County Board of Education,
I respectfully submit a correction to a previous board agenda item regarding the purchase of a

Practical Assessment Exploration System (PAES) lab. The original submission incorrectly listed
the sole source vendor. The correct vendor is PAES Productions, LLC.

Lam requesting approval to_proceed-with the purchase of the PAES lab using funds from the —

The PAES lab will provide students with intellectual disabilities an invaluable opportunity to
develop essential work-related skills in a structured, hands-on environment. This aligns with our
district’s mission to support transition services and expand work-based learning opportunities.

The PAES lab is a unique and comprehensive curriculum designed to simulate a real-world work
environment. It allows students to explore and build competencies across multiple job skill areas
while promoting independence and work readiness.

Due to the proprietary and specialized nature of the PAES system, this purchase qualifies as a

A corrected sole-source letter is attached to reflect this information.

This purchase aligns with the Guidepost to Graduation grant objectives and directly supports our
efforts to prepare students for meaningful post-secondary outcomes.

Sole Source Justification for PAES Lab

(Practical Assessment Exploration System)
Vendor: PAES Productions, LLC
Product: Practical Assessment Exploration System (PAES Lab)

Description of the Product:

The PAES Lab is a structured, hands-on assessment and training system used to support the
development and evaluation of functional and vocational skills for individuals with disabilities. It

includes-task-based-activities-across five -vocational domains: Business/Marketing,
Construction/Industrial, Processing/Production, Consumer/Service, and Computer/Technology.
The system simulates a work environment to help identify students’ skill levels, interests, and
areas for growth.

Market Research Summary:

To determine whether PAES Lab qualifies for sole source procurement, a review of other
vocational assessment and training resources was conducted. The following products and
vendors were evaluated:

¢ Brigance Transition Skills Inventory — Provides academic and life skills assessments
but does not include hands-on work simulation.

e SkillsUSA Career Essentials — Offers a digital curriculum for employability skills, with
a focus on soft skill development rather than task-based performance.

¢ Project Discovery (Education Associates) — Includes career exploration kits, but the
delivery format and assessment tools differ from the PAES model.

¢ Attainment Company’s Vocational Assessment Tools — Offers general vocational tools
and curricula, but lacks the integrated lab setup and structured assessment components of
PAES.

Based on available information and consultation with colleagues in the field, no alternative was

identified that combines structured task-based training, cross-domain coverage, and work
simulation in the same format.


Research included vendor website reviews, product brochures, peer feedback, and informal
discussions with transition coordinators in other districts.

Justification for Sole Source Designation:

1. Vendor-Specific Design: PAES Productions, LLC is the sole provider of the PAES Lab
and associated training.

2. Limited Comparable Options: Reviewed products differ in delivery format or content
structure and may not support the intended program model.

3. Implementation Consistency: Using the full PAES Lab system supports
standardization, staff training, and long-term program continuity.

Conclusion:

Based on the research conducted_and the specific program needs, it is recommended that PAES
Productions, LLC be approved as a sole-source vendor for this purchase.
