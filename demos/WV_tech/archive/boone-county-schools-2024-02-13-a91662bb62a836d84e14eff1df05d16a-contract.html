WHEREAS, the Health Agency and the Boone Center have agreed to
jointly participate in a program in which students from the Boone Center's Allied Health
Programs may acquire experience in the Health Agency.

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:
1. This agreement may be terminated by either party within thirty (30) days written notice.

2. The program will be jointly evaluated from time to time and amendments may be made to
this agreement. Any amendments will be reduced to written memorandum and attached

hereto.

3. Neither monetary considerations nor any other benefits will be provided to the students
assigned to the Health Agency.

4. This agreement and the implementation thereof shall be in accordance with all the non-
discriminatory principals and regulations of the federal government as administered by the
Office of Equal Opportunity and other such agencies.

5, The number of students assigned to the Health Agency will not exceed two {2) health
occupations students.

6. The students understand that due to occupational exposure to blood or other infectious
materials they may be at risk of acquiring hepatitis B virus (HBV) infection of human
immunodeficiency virus (HIV} infection, which causes aids. The students were told to be
vaccinated against hepatitis by taking the Hepatitis B vaccine at their own cost. However, if
they decline, they understand that by declining this vaccine they continue to be at risk of
acquiring any infectious disease while in a clinical situation. The students have been
instructed in the use and requirement for this protective equipment as well as all standard
precautions, including hand washing techniques, disposal of infectious wastes, the HIPPA
laws, ethics, and confidentiality of all information they have learned about patients.
