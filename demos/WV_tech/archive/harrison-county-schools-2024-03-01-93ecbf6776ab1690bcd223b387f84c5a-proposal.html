The Harrison County Schools Social Studies Instructional Materials Adoption Committee for grades 6-
12 consisted of teachers from each grade level and all schools were represented. All secondary social
studies teachers were encouraged to preview the materials, review the WVDE instructional materials
rubrics and the information shared with them from their committee members. Each committee
member was asked to collaborate and work with their school colleagues in narrowing down to one
series that meets both student and teacher needs.

Secondary Social Studies Resource Adoption Process Timeline

e December/January

Each school administrator selected a committee member. Teachers are notified and a
Social Studies Resource Adoption Process Committee is formed.

e February

The Secondary Curriculum Coordinator, Secondary Curriculum Specialist, and Technology
Curriculum Specialist participated in the WVDE 2024-25 Social Studies Instructional
Resources Textbook Caravan at which time textbook vendors shared their resources.
Committee members attended an in-person meeting, reviewed the WVDE instructional
materials rubrics, studied sample copies, digital resources, and supplemental materials for
each of the Social Studies programs considered.

Committee members were provided a OneDrive folder with instructional resources,
content standard alignments, and rubric reviews provided by WVDE. Committee members
looked at each company to determine if they met the state and county criteria for social
studies curriculum.

Three vendors, two from middle school and one from high school were removed by the
committee as they did not meet the criteria leaving a remaining three vendors for middle
school 6" & 7" grade, two for 8'* grade, and four vendors for high school.

Committee members were instructed to review the information from the meeting with all
social studies teachers at their school for analysis.

e March

A link was sent out to each middle school and high school teacher and they were asked to
reduce the choices to two vendors at each level.

Committee members attended a second in-person meeting and were informed of the
results from the county-wide reduction vote and reviewed program presentations from
the remaining vendors.

Committee members and teachers were asked to use all resources (digital access,
samples, state review, and committee information) to take a “Deep Dive” into the
remaining vendors.

© April

A link was sent out for each school’s teachers to determine their school’s top pick for a
social studies curriculum.

A link was sent to each school’s Committee Member to finalize the school’s top pick.
(Please see Attachment A)

e May

After Harrison County Board approval, submit to WVDE.
