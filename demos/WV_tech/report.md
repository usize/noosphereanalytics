School districts across the state are accelerating the shift toward cloud-based, subscription-style educational technology. Four big patterns emerge:

1. **Platform Consolidation** – Districts are standardising on 2–3 “anchor” systems (LMS, adaptive assessment suite, productivity suite) and layering point solutions on top.
2. **Personalised / Adaptive Learning** – Math and ELA platforms that generate individual learning paths (e.g., *i-Ready, ALEKS, SuccessMaker, IXL, ST Math*) are now mainstream and often required at every building.
3. **AI Exploration & Guard-Rails** – Pilot use of generative-AI teaching aides (e.g., *MagicSchool.ai*) is rising, accompanied by new AI-ethics bylaws and classroom-use policies.
4. **SaaS for Operations** – Back-office functions (health, maintenance, finance, messaging) are being moved to specialised SaaS platforms (*SNAP Health Center, Asset Essentials, SchoolFunds Online, 10DLC SMS, Thrillshare/Apptegy*).

## Emerging Trends in Detail

### LMS & Single-Sign-On Ecosystems

* **Canvas** is still provisioned statewide, but several counties (Boone, Wood, Jackson) layered **Schoology** on top for deeper grade-book, SIS and parent-portal features.
* **Federated Identity & Rostering** – Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Apple School Manager and Clever Rostering are explicitly referenced, signalling a shift toward automated identity management and data-privacy compliance (FERPA/COPPA).

### Personalised Learning & Data-Driven Instruction

* *i-Ready* renewals (often > \$90 k) highlight districts’ willingness to pay for integrated diagnostics + instruction that satisfy WV’s Third-Grade Success Act.
* Math differentiation is fragmenting by level: elementary = i-Ready/Carnegie Learning, middle = **ALEKS**, HS = **Reveal Math** + ALEKS add-on licenses.
* Multi-year digital bundles with built-in PD (Reveal Math 101/102, HMH “Implementation Success Plans”) aim to guarantee adoption fidelity.

### AI Adoption & Governance

* Jefferson County’s AI guidance notes have formalised permissible uses (research, question generation) and stress ethical constraints.
* Teachers report spontaneous student access to **Microsoft Copilot** on managed laptops, prompting “best-practice” reminders to disable AI during summative writing tasks.
* Early pilots (MagicSchool.ai, QuestionWell) focus on teacher efficiency (lesson-plan scaffolding, item banks) rather than direct student interaction.

### Administrative SaaS Modernisation

* **SNAP Health Center** (Marion, Boone) replaces paper nurse logs and integrates Medicaid export features.
* **Asset Essentials** (Randolph) and **SchoolFunds Online** renewals reveal growing use of cloud CMMS and accounting.
* Substitute, payroll and HR flows are shifting to PowerSchool SmartFind, Brightly, or KEV Group POS devices.

### Funding & Procurement Patterns

* **ESSER, Title I/II/IV, Step 7, IDEA** and **ARP** funds are repeatedly cited for technology purchases, especially those tied to learning-loss recovery and special-education support.
* Districts are negotiating 5-year digital licenses to lock in pricing before ESSER sunsets in 2026.
